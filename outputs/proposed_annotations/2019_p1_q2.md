# Proposed annotation for 2019 Paper 1 Question 2

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 5-6, 7-8 and 9-10 are paired by country. Each pair is from a different country.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin and grape variety(ies) as closely as possible. (6 x 12 marks)

b) What are the key winemaking techniques used in the wine's production? (6 x 7 marks)

c) Comment on the quality. (6 x 6 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W5: Rioja Blanco, Allende, 2015. Rioja, Spain. (13.5%)
- W6: Albariño, Santiago Ruiz, 2017. Rías Baixas, Spain. (13%)
- W7: Grillo, Isola della Fiamma, 2018. Sicily, Italy. (12.5%)
- W8: Moscato d'Asti, Elio Perrone, 2017. Piedmont, Italy. (5.5%)
- W9: Watervale Riesling, Mt Horrocks, 2018. Clare Valley, Australia. (12.5%)
- W10: M3 Chardonnay, Shaw and Smith, 2016. Adelaide Hills, Australia. (13%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of paired-country white wines across Spain, Italy and Australia, and the ability to match very different styles within the same country. The pairing clue is a huge help — it means you can reason about the pair as a unit. Spain is a natural pair because you have Rioja Blanco and Albariño from Rías Baixas, two totally different styles but both recognisably Spanish if you know your whites. Italy is a slightly trickier pair because Sicilian Grillo (dry, medium-weight) is paired with Moscato d'Asti (sweet, frothy, 5.5% ABV) — that 5.5% alcohol is an immediate giveaway for Piedmont Moscato. The Australian pair is probably the most identifiable if you know Clare Valley Riesling: tight, lime-driven, high acid versus the Shaw and Smith M3 which is barrel-fermented, textured, cool-climate Adelaide Hills Chardonnay.

The winemaking question (b) is really the point here. The examiner wants you to contrast tank vs barrel, flor vs no flor, reductive vs oxidative, arrested fermentation vs full dry. The quality question (c) is 6 marks — not a huge allocation, so be decisive rather than exhaustive.

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - Paired-by-country structure tells you to reason in pairs, not six isolated wines
  - Six whites in Paper 1 mid-section usually means an international sweep of styles

- Universe I considered:
  - Spain: Albariño (Rías Baixas), white Rioja (Viura/oak), Txakoli, Godello
  - Italy: Soave, Pinot Grigio, Vermentino, Grillo, Moscato d'Asti, Falanghina
  - Australia: Clare/Eden Riesling, Adelaide Hills Chardonnay, Hunter Semillon
- Rule-outs:
  - Spain is confirmed by the two distinct styles; Txakoli would be even more extreme in ABV; Godello possible but less likely at this quality tier
  - Italy: the 5.5% ABV wine is unmistakably Moscato d'Asti once you see it in a Paper 1 flight
  - Australia: Riesling/Chardonnay from SA is the most examined Australian white pairing in the MW corpus
- Conclusion: Spain (Rioja Blanco + Albariño), Italy (Grillo + Moscato d'Asti), Australia (Clare Riesling + Adelaide Hills Chardonnay)
