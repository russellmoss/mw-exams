# Proposed annotation for 2022 Paper 1 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 6-8 come from the same country. a) Identify the origin and grape variety of each wine. (3x15) b) Comment on the style and quality of each wine and the likelihood of market success. (3x10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W6: Pinot Grigio delle Venezie, Tessari, 2020. Veneto, Italy. (12.0%)
- W7: Gewurztraminer, Erste+Neue, 2020. Alto Adige, Italy. (14.0%)
- W8: Etna Bianco di Sei, Palmento Costanzo, 2018. Sicily, Italy. (12.0%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Italian white wines and the country's remarkable diversity of styles. Three wines, same country — Italy is actually one of the harder countries to pin as the answer when you don't know what's in the glass, because its whites range so dramatically from light commercial Pinot Grigio in the northeast to intensely aromatic alpine Gewurztraminer to volcanic, mineral Etna Bianco in the south.

The most recognizable wine here should be W7 — Alto Adige Gewurztraminer is as unmistakable as it gets, with rose petals, lychee and spice making it one of the easiest varietal IDs in the exam. Once you land on that, the "same country" clue is confirmed as Italy and that helps anchor the others. W6 is commercial Pinot Grigio from the Veneto, light and clean, probably the easiest stylistically but hardest to get to the sub-region. W8 is the curveball — Etna Bianco (Carricante-dominant) is tensile, mineral and saline with no oak, reading more like an aged Mosel in structure than a Mediterranean white.

Part b) asking about market success is interesting because these three wines sit in very different commercial positions: Pinot Grigio has enormous global volume; Alto Adige Gewurztraminer has strong on-trade appeal especially in northern Europe and North America; Etna Bianco is a premium terroir-driven wine with a much smaller but rapidly growing fine-wine following.
