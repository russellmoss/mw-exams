# Proposed annotation for 2021 Paper 2 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-3 are all from the same region and producer. Each wine is from a different vintage. a) Identify the region, referencing each wine (15) b) Identify the vintage and comment on quality in context of vintage conditions (3x10) c) [additional sub-question on commercial position/maturity]

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W1: Ducru-Beaucaillou, 2013. St Julien. (13%)
- W2: Ducru-Beaucaillou, 2015. St Julien. (13.5%)
- W3: Ducru-Beaucaillou, 2005. St Julien. (13%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Bordeaux vintages and your ability to age and quality-tier a wine from the same producer across different years. "Same region and producer, different vintages" on Paper 2 means almost certainly Bordeaux — it's the region where vintage matters most, where the examiner can draw from a deep bench of well-documented years, and where a single classified growth at multiple ages makes pedagogical sense. You could argue Burgundy, Barolo, or Rioja, but Bordeaux is by far the most likely.

The key skill here is vintage knowledge. 2005 Bordeaux is a legendary year — deeply concentrated, tannic, long aging curve. 2015 is excellent — ripe, generous, approachable sooner than 2005. 2013 is the curveball — a notoriously difficult year in Bordeaux, cool and wet, lighter extraction, probably drinking sooner than either. At eight years old in the tasting room, the 2013 should show more evolution than the 2015 and much more than the 2005.

Ducru-Beaucaillou is a second growth from St Julien — elegant, cedary, classic left bank Cabernet Sauvignon-dominant. Knowing the producer isn't strictly required, but recognizing St Julien (relatively restraint vs Pauillac power, more elegance than Margaux) helps. The market question will want you to discuss auction value of the 2005 vs current-drinking appeal of the 2013.

- Conclusion: Bordeaux confirmed, St Julien second growth. 2005/2015/2013 is an ideal teaching flight — great, good, and difficult year from the same house.
