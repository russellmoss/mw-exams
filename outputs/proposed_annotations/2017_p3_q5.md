# Proposed annotation for 2017 Paper 3 Question 5

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 9-10 are from the same country and region. a) ID origin (2x8) b) Likely vintage (2x7) c) Quality/potential to improve (2x10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W9: Côte de Nuits Villages VV, David Clark, 2012. Burgundy. (13%)
- W10: NSG 1er Cru Clos des Argillières, Rion, 2011. Burgundy. (13%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Burgundy Pinot Noir, and specifically your ability to distinguish quality levels within Burgundy — village vs. Premier Cru — and to assess vintage character and drinking windows. Same country and region (Burgundy), same grape (Pinot Noir), but different appellations and different vintages. Both at 13% ABV.

The origin sub-question (8 marks each) wants you to get to Burgundy at minimum, and ideally to Côte de Nuits or the specific villages. These are two different levels of the Burgundy hierarchy and the quality/potential sub-question (10 marks each) rewards you for knowing that difference — CdNV (village appellation) has a shorter aging curve, while a Nuits-St-Georges 1er Cru has real cellaring potential.

The vintage sub-question (7 marks each) is interesting because the two wines are from different vintages — 2012 and 2011. At time of exam (2017), the 2012 is five years old and the 2011 is six years old. Knowing that 2012 Burgundy was a low-yield, concentrated vintage while 2011 was a more elegant, accessible year is the specific knowledge being tested. A candidate who can talk about what each vintage was like in Burgundy, and how that affects where each wine sits in its drinking window, will score well on that sub-question.

The quality comparison also tests whether you can recognize the structural differences between village and Premier Cru Pinot Noir blind — tannin quality, complexity, length.
