# Proposed annotation for 2023 Paper 3 Question 2

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 5-7 are all made using different methods of production.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin and grape variety/ies as closely as possible. (3 x 10 marks)

b) Discuss quality and commercial potential. (3 x 8 marks)

c) Identify the key production techniques that have influenced the style. (3 x 7 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W5: Rosé Brut, Laurent-Perrier, NV. Champagne, France. (12.0%)
- W6: Rosé Les Clans, Château d'Esclans, 2021. Côtes de Provence, France. (14.0%)
- W7: Rosé, Mateus, NV. Portugal. (11.0%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of rosé wine across three very different production methods and quality levels. "All made using different methods of production" is the key signal — the examiner is specifically asking you to distinguish how each of these rosés is made, not just what they are.

The three major rosé production methods are: saignée/maceration (used for serious Champagne rosé), direct pressing or short skin contact (used for Provence still rosé), and the semi-sweet tank method (used for mass-market rosés like Mateus). You could also throw in the addition of red wine to white base (which is actually how Laurent-Perrier LP rosé is made — it's a blended Champagne rosé). So in this flight you've got: a premium NV Champagne rosé made by blending red wine into white base (LP's signature method), a Provence Cru Classé-level still rosé made by direct press, and Mateus which is semi-sparkling, semi-sweet and made at massive commercial scale.

Quality and commercial potential really sorts these wines out. LP Brut Rosé is a global premium brand, sold in fine dining worldwide with strong commercial momentum behind prestige rosé. Château d'Esclans Les Clans is at the luxury end of Provence — serious still rosé with fine dining positioning and ageing potential. Mateus is the opposite of both: enormous volume, immediate and simple, with almost purely mass-market appeal. The question rewards candidates who can describe those commercial profiles precisely rather than just saying "good" or "bad."
