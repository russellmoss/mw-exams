# Proposed annotation for 2016 Paper 1 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1 and 2 are from the same country. They are both blends.

For each wine:

a) Identify the region of origin as closely as possible, comment with reference to the grape varieties used. (2 x 10 marks)

b) Comment on quality within the context of the region of origin. (2 x 10 marks)

c) Discuss the key winemaking techniques used to arrive at this style. (2 x 5 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W1: Château Brown, 2013. Pessac Léognan, Bordeaux, France. (13.5%)
- W2: Châteauneuf du Pape, Domaine Vieux Telegraph Blanc, 2014. Rhône Valley, France. (13.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of white blends from France. The "same country, both blends" stem immediately rules out most of the wine world — you need a country that produces multiple distinct white blends at a recognisable quality level. France is the obvious answer: the two main white blend traditions are Bordeaux Blanc (Sauvignon Blanc/Sémillon, barrel fermented, dry) and southern Rhône white (Grenache Blanc, Clairette, Roussanne, Bourboulenc, broad and aromatic). Those are really the only two well-known French white blend styles that could appear together at this level. Australia could technically work (Semillon/Chardonnay, Marsanne/Roussanne) but it's a stretch.

So you're really just deciding: is this Bordeaux white vs Rhône white, or could one be Alsace? Alsace is essentially single-variety so that's out. Languedoc could produce Roussanne/Marsanne blends but they're not iconic enough for a P1 MW question.

In practice you'd identify wine 1 as Pessac-Léognan — barrel-fermented Sauvignon/Sémillon, herbaceous lift, waxy body, some toasty oak — and wine 2 as southern Rhône white: richer, more aromatic, broader, warm climate, garrigue register. The winemaking sub-question wants you to talk about barrel fermentation vs foudre/old oak and the role of malo (likely suppressed in the Pessac, irrelevant for the Rhône). Quality sub-question rewards knowledge of Pessac-Léognan's prestige relative to generic Bordeaux Blanc.
