# Proposed annotation for 2018 Paper 2 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 9 and 10 are made from the same single grape variety and come from the same region. a) Identify the variety and origin (20) b) Comment on the quality of each wine in context (2x8) c) Comment on the winemaking of each wine and whether the style is appropriate for the variety and origin (2x?)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W9: Syrah, Te Mata Estate, 2016. Hawke's Bay, New Zealand. (12.5%)
- W10: Homage, Trinity Hill, 2014. Hawke's Bay, New Zealand. (13.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Syrah from New Zealand, which is a pretty specific ask. Same variety, same region — so you only have to name one variety and one region for 20 marks. The exercise then becomes quality and winemaking differentiation within that regional identity. What two-wine same variety/same region combinations work as an exam question? You need one that shows genuine quality or style contrast. Hawke's Bay Syrah works perfectly: a lighter, more fragrant entry-level expression (Te Mata, 12.5%) versus a flagship wine with more extraction and oak (Trinity Hill Homage, 13.5%).

The 12.5% ABV on wine 9 is a significant signal — that's on the low end for a red wine and suggests either a cool climate, a difficult vintage, or a deliberate style choice. New Zealand Syrah from Hawke's Bay is known for its cool-climate elegance, black pepper, violet, and savory character that resembles Northern Rhône more than warm-climate Australian Shiraz. If you're expecting plum jam and 14.5% you'd be wrong. The Homage is a more ambitious wine — Gimblett Gravels sourcing, more oak, more structure, more concentration. Together these two test whether you can read quality differences within the same regional identity and discuss how winemaking choices (extraction, oak, yield) impact the result.
