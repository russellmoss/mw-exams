# Proposed annotation for 2021 Paper 1 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 10-12 are from the same country. a) Identify the country (15) b) Identify the origin and variety of each wine (3x10) c) Describe the key winemaking of each wine (3x5) d) Comment on the market position of each wine (3x5)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W10: Moscato, Gallo Family, 2019. California. (9%)
- W11: Chardonnay Hyde Vineyard, Patz & Hall, 2016. Carneros. (14.5%)
- W12: Pinot Gris, Ponzi, 2019. Willamette Valley. (13.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of white wines from the United States and your ability to navigate the dramatic quality and style range within American wine. The "same country" clue with three wines asks you to find the common thread across what could be very different styles. The US is a strong candidate here because you can plausibly get a cheap sweet wine (Moscato), a serious Napa/Sonoma Chardonnay, and an Oregon aromatic all under one flag without it feeling contrived. France could do this too but would likely lead with more cohesive regional signals.

W10 is the easy identification if you know Gallo Family Moscato — low alcohol (9%), slightly sweet, simple aromatic, mass-market California. W11 is Carneros Chardonnay at 14.5%, serious barrel-fermented wine from a prestigious single vineyard (Hyde). W12 is Willamette Valley Pinot Gris from Ponzi, one of Oregon's founding families — drier and more textured than an Alsatian version, but still aromatic.

The market position sub-question is really the point: these three wines land in completely different commercial spaces — supermarket/off-trade for Gallo, fine dining/specialist retail for Patz & Hall Hyde, and artisan/on-trade for Ponzi. The examiner wants you to articulate all three tiers.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Same country" + three wines + market position asked = wide quality range likely, probably USA
  - Winemaking asked (3x5) = there will be something interesting about at least one production method (e.g. arrested fermentation for the Moscato)
- Universe I considered:
  - USA: can span sweet/dry/still white across CA and OR easily
  - France: possible but tends toward more coherent regional groupings
  - Australia: possible (Riesling, Chardonnay, Semillon) but less obvious for a Moscato equivalent
- Rule-outs:
  - Italy: hard to span three such different styles under one country flag
  - Germany: could do Spatlese/Auslese/GG but usually from same region
- Conclusion: USA confirmed. The Gallo Moscato is the key identifier — mass-market, low ABV, sweet. Once you have that alongside a premium Carneros Chardonnay, the USA answer locks in.
