# Proposed annotation for 2019 Paper 1 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 11-12 are from two different Old World countries.

With reference to each wine:

a) Comment on the winemaking. (2 x 10 marks)

b) Discuss the wine's style, quality and commercial potential. Do not spend time thinking about the wine's specific origin. (2 x 15 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W11: Chinuri, Iago, 2016. Kartli, Georgia. (12.5%)
- W12: Vecchio Samperi, Marco de Bartoli, NV. Sicily, Italy. (16.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of unconventional Old World white winemaking styles — specifically the ability to recognise skin-contact/orange wine production and oxidative ageing wine production, and to assess commercial potential for both without needing to pin the origin precisely. The question actually tells you not to chase specific origin, which is a signal that the wines are going to be hard to place exactly and that winemaking recognition and commercial thinking are the real marks here.

The two styles being tested are: (1) traditional qvevri skin-contact white (amber wine with phenolic grip, tannin, tea-like weight) — almost certainly going to be Georgian or possibly Friuli/Slovenian amphora-style; and (2) an oxidative, unfortified or lightly fortified dry Sicilian/Marsala-adjacent wine with rancio, walnut and nutty concentration. For part a) the examiner wants you to name the method: extended skin maceration vs fractional oxidative cask ageing. For part b) they want a realistic read on where these wines sit commercially — both are niche, both are artisan, neither is a supermarket wine.

This question is sort of a grab bag of "interesting alternative" whites, and the instruction to skip origin guessing is a kindness to the candidate.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Old World" — rules out Georgia only if you consider it New World, but Georgia IS Old World (it's the oldest wine culture)
  - "Do not spend time thinking about specific origin" — unusual instruction, confirms these are hard to place
  - Winemaking = 10 marks each, style/quality/commercial = 15 marks each — the commercial question is worth the most
- Universe I considered:
  - Orange/skin-contact: Georgia (qvevri), Friuli (Radikon, Gravner), Slovenian amphorae
  - Oxidative dry white: Jura (vin jaune), Sherry (fino/manzanilla), traditional Marsala/Vecchio Samperi style
- Rule-outs:
  - If the wine is not fortified but still 16.5% ABV and oxidative/nutty — that's Vecchio Samperi style (unfortified but concentrated through natural oxidative ageing)
  - Skin-contact wine with 12.5% ABV and phenolic grip is more likely Georgian qvevri than Friuli given the amber depth
- Conclusion: Georgian qvevri amber wine (W11) + oxidative unfortified Sicilian solera-adjacent wine (W12)
