# Proposed annotation for 2017 Paper 1 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 6-7 are from Italy and are made from different grape varieties. a) ID origin, grape variety (2x12) b) Style/quality; trade success (2x13)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W6: Soave Classico, Inama, 2016. Veneto. (12%)
- W7: Roero Arneis, Cornarea, 2016. Piemonte. (13.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Italian white grape varieties. The stem confirms Italy and different varieties for the two wines. So the question is: which Italian whites are famous enough to appear on an MW exam? There are really only a handful: Soave/Garganega from the Veneto, Pinot Grigio (from Alto Adige or Veneto), Vermentino (Sardinia), Arneis from Piemonte, Gavi/Cortese, Fiano, Greco di Tufo (Campania), and Verdicchio (Marche). You're probably not going to get the more obscure southern varieties at this level without more context clues.

The quality and trade sub-question is the heavy one here (13 marks each), suggesting the examiner wants you to go beyond variety/origin and actually discuss how these wines perform commercially and what their stylistic positioning is. Soave Classico is an interesting case — generic Soave is supermarket commodity stuff, but Soave Classico from a quality producer like Inama is a serious, terroir-expressive wine. That tension between the appellation's bad reputation and the quality tier is exactly what an MW question wants you to discuss. Arneis is a niche grape with decent domestic Italian market and some export presence, but it doesn't have the same global recognition as Soave.

Both wines are 2016 from Italy — same vintage, different regions and grapes, which simplifies the vintage discussion and keeps the focus on style contrast.
