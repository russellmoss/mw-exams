# Proposed annotation for 2016 Paper 2 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1 and 2 come from the same region and are made from the same single grape variety.

With reference to both wines:

a) Identify the grape variety and origin as closely as possible. (18 marks)

b) Compare the winemaking styles. (18 marks)

c) Compare quality, with particular reference to both wines' potential evolution. (14 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W1: Morgon, Domaine Lapierre, 2014. Beaujolais, France. (12%)
- W2: Moulin À Vent Clos de Rochegrès, Château des Jacques Domaine Louis Jadot, 2014. Beaujolais, France. (13%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Gamay and cru Beaujolais, and more specifically your ability to distinguish two winemaking philosophies within the same region. The "same region, same single variety" stem on Paper 2 reds really narrows the universe. The most likely candidates are: Beaujolais (Gamay — two crus with contrasting styles), Burgundy (Pinot Noir — different village vs premier cru), Barossa/Rhône (Grenache or Shiraz from different appellations), or Rioja (Tempranillo). 

Beaujolais is the best fit because the variety question is worth 18 marks and Gamay is not as immediately obvious as Cabernet or Pinot — the examiner is rewarding candidates who can identify it correctly. Also the winemaking comparison at 18 marks is a big clue: the examiner wants you to compare carbonic/semi-carbonic Lapierre-style natural low-intervention winemaking against the more structured, Burgundian-influenced Château des Jacques approach. That's a rich winemaking discussion that rewards deep regional knowledge.

Quality and evolution at 14 marks: Moulin-à-Vent with its granite soils and more structured tannin is capable of aging more like Burgundy, while Morgon is typically more floral and approachable earlier. Lapierre at 12% ABV vs Château des Jacques at 13% already signals the difference in style and extraction.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Same region, same single variety" = need a region with internal stylistic contrast
  - Winemaking comparison = a big deal here, 18 marks

  - Beaujolais (Gamay): carbonic vs traditional/Burgundian styles — very strong fit
  - Burgundy (Pinot Noir): different cru expressions possible but no clear winemaking contrast
  - Barossa (Shiraz): possible but winemaking less contrast within sub-region
- Rule-outs:
  - Burgundy: would typically show different crus, not strong winemaking contrast
  - Rioja: similar winemaking philosophy across sub-zones
- Conclusion: Gamay / Beaujolais — Lapierre Morgon (natural/carbonic) vs Château des Jacques Moulin-à-Vent (Burgundian/structured)
