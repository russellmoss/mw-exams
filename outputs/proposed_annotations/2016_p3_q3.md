# Proposed annotation for 2016 Paper 3 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 9 and 10 are from different countries.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin as closely as possible, with particular reference to the grape varieties used. (2 x 10 marks)

b) Discuss the key winemaking techniques used to arrive at this style. (2 x 10 marks)

c) Comment on quality and maturity. (2 x 5 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W9: Tokaji Aszú, 6 Puttonyos, Royal Tokay Wine Company, 2011. Tokaj, Hungary. (9%)
- W10: Château Guiraud, 1er Cru Classé, 2001. Sauternes, Bordeaux, France. (13.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of botrytis sweet wines from two different countries. Two sweet whites on Paper 3 from different countries — the universe is the classic European noble rot wines: Sauternes (France), Tokaji (Hungary), Beerenauslese or Trockenbeerenauslese (Germany), and MAYBE a late-harvest from Austria. The different countries clue and the emphasis on grape varieties used as part of the origin question point toward Sauternes (Sémillon/Sauvignon Blanc/Muscadelle blend) and Tokaji (Furmint/Hárslevelű), since both involve botrytis but from totally different grape varieties.

The 9% ABV on the Tokaji is a significant signal in blind tasting — it's unusually low for a wine, caused by the natural stopping of fermentation under osmotic pressure from the aszú sugars. The Sauternes at 13.5% is a very different beast — richer, more alcoholic, oaked, broader. These two wines are a natural comparison: both are world-class noble-rot sweet wines, both are old enough (2011 and 2001) to show maturity and complexity, but they're made from completely different grapes by completely different methods.

Winemaking sub-question: Tokaji = aszú berry selection, maceration of aszú paste into base wine, puttonyos classification system, aging in used Hungarian oak (furmint fermeted dry base + aszú paste + aging); Sauternes = selective harvesting (tries), Sémillon/Sauvignon blend, barrel fermentation and aging in new French oak, long maceration. Quality question is enhanced by the vintage context: 2011 Tokaji was a good warm vintage, 2001 Sauternes is one of the great modern Sauternes vintages.
