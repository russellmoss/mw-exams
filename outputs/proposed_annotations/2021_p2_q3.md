# Proposed annotation for 2021 Paper 2 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 7-8 are made from different single grape varieties. Each wine is from a different country in the Americas. a) Compare the styles and consumer appeal of each wine (30) b) Identify the variety and origin of each wine (2x10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W7: Petite Sirah, Bogle, 2018. California. (14.5%)
- W8: Terrunyo Carmenere Block 27, Concha y Toro, 2018. Cachapoal, Chile. (14.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of lesser-known South and North American red varieties and their commercial contexts. "Different variety, different country in the Americas" on Paper 2 is somewhat unusual — the more common framing is different continents. Narrowing to the Americas is a hint that the answer isn't Cabernet Sauvignon from Napa and Malbec from Mendoza (too obvious), but something more specific.

The style comparison (30 marks, asked first) is really the point here. Both wines are at 14.5%, both single variety, both 2018 — so the examiner wants you to articulate the character of each. Petite Sirah is a curveball — deeply colored, very tannic, plummy, almost inky. It's not well-known outside California, and Bogle is a reliably consistent, value-priced producer. Carmenere is the classic Chile identity variety — herbaceous (capsicum, tobacco), medium body, soft tannins, very different from what many Europeans expect.

The consumer appeal question will want you to contrast the niche, tannic Petite Sirah (mainly domestic US market, BBQ and steak crowd) with Carmenere which has built an international identity as Chile's signature grape.

Variety ID is worth only 10 marks each, which signals these won't be obvious grapes — they want you to demonstrate you can articulate what's in the glass even if you can't name the exact variety with certainty.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Different variety, different country in the Americas" = not the obvious Cab/Malbec combo
  - Style comparison weighted at 30 marks = character description is primary
  - Variety ID at 10 marks each = probably not the most famous grapes
- Universe I considered:
  - USA: Zinfandel, Petite Sirah, Cabernet, Pinot Noir (CA or OR)
  - Chile: Carmenere, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, País
  - Argentina: Malbec, Bonarda, Cabernet
- Rule-outs:
  - Cabernet Sauvignon: too obvious given only 10 marks for variety ID
  - Malbec/Merlot: less interesting stylistically for an MW comparison question
- Conclusion: Petite Sirah + Carmenere confirmed. Both are varietally very specific, stylistically very different, and both reward someone who knows the category rather than just guessing Cab.
