# Proposed annotation for 2022 Paper 1 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1 and 2 are from the same producer and the same vintage. a) Identify the origin of each wine. (2x10) b) Compare the quality, maturity, use of oak and market position of wines 1 and 2. (30)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W1: Meursault Les Chevalieres, Xavier Monnot, 2019. Burgundy. (13.0%)
- W2: Corton Charlemagne, Xavier Monnot, 2019. Burgundy. (13.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of white Burgundy hierarchy. The stem tells you two wines, same producer, same vintage — that's a massive clue that they want you to differentiate within one estate, almost certainly by appellation tier. In white Burgundy the obvious comparison is village vs premier cru, or village vs grand cru, and in this case it's Meursault village against Corton-Charlemagne Grand Cru from Xavier Monnot.

On the origin identification side you need to get both to at least village level. Meursault should be identifiable from texture and hazelnut richness; Corton-Charlemagne should show more scale, flint and vertical drive. The 0.5% difference in ABV is a small clue toward the grand cru being riper.

The big marks (30) are in part b — comparing quality, maturity, oak and market. That's where you really need to know the hierarchy: a village Meursault from a good producer sits at maybe £30-40 retail, while Corton-Charlemagne from a serious domaine is £80-120+. Oak use is likely similar in approach at Monnot but the grand cru should integrate it better. Maturity: 2019 is still relatively young for Corton-Charlemagne, drinking well for Meursault. This is basically a lesson in appellation hierarchy within a single producer.
