# Proposed annotation for 2021 Paper 2 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 9-10 and wines 11-12 are two pairs. Each pair is from a different region of origin. a) Identify the region, referencing each wine (2x10) b) Compare the style, quality and production of each pair (2x20) c) [additional sub-question]

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W9: CdP, Clos des Papes, 2015. Rhône. (15%)
- W10: CdR, Guigal, 2017. Rhône. (14.5%)
- W11: La Montesa Crianza, Palacios Remondo, 2017. Rioja. (14.5%)
- W12: Castillo Ygay Gran Reserva, Murrieta, 2010. Rioja. (14%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of GSM/Grenache-dominant blends from the Rhone and Rioja, and specifically your ability to distinguish quality tiers within each region. The "two pairs, different regions" format is really a within-region quality comparison wrapped in a cross-region identification exercise.

The Rhone pair is Clos des Papes CdP 2015 vs Guigal CdR 2017 — one of the top estates in Châteauneuf paired with a generic southern Rhône. The quality gap should be obvious: the CdP is structured, complex, built to age; the Guigal CdR is fruit-forward, approachable, and relatively simple. The Rioja pair has the same architecture: La Montesa Crianza 2017 (accessible, less oak, Palacios Remondo's more commercial tier) vs Castillo Ygay Gran Reserva 2010 (a classic traditional-style Rioja with years of oak and bottle age).

The really interesting MW angle here is the cross-pair comparison — both regions are Grenache/Garnacha-dominant blends in the same style family, and the examiner wants to know if you can articulate what distinguishes Rhone from Rioja in the glass (the herbal/garrigue character of the south Rhone vs the vanilla/dried fruit of old-school Rioja). And then within each pair, whether you can articulate the quality and aging difference.
