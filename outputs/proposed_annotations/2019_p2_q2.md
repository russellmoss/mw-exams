# Proposed annotation for 2019 Paper 2 Question 2

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 5-8 come from four different countries in North and South America.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin and variety as closely as possible. (4 x 15 marks)

b) Comment on the quality and the likely market position. (4 x 10 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W5: Au Bon Climat Pinot Noir, 2016. Santa Maria Valley, California, USA. (13.5%)
- W6: Pulenta Estate Malbec, 2016. Mendoza, Argentina. (14%)
- W7: Montes Alpha Carménère, 2016. Colchagua, Chile. (14.5%)
- W8: Bodega Garzón Single Vineyard Tannat, 2017. Maldonado, Uruguay. (14.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of the major red grape varieties of North and South America — it's a continental sweep where each country gets one wine and each wine is a different variety. That's a strong structure and fairly generous because if you can identify the varieties you can largely work out the countries: Pinot Noir from a cool California spot, Malbec from Argentina, Carménère from Chile and Tannat from Uruguay. The question allocates 15 marks to origin + variety — the biggest allocation in the question — which confirms that variety ID is the primary task here.

The curveball is W8 (Tannat, Uruguay). Most tasters will recognize massively tannic dark wine and probably reach for Madiran or possibly a robust Douro but Uruguay is genuinely unexpected. If you can place it there, that's an impressive call. Realistically you might get "probably Tannat" right on variety even if you miss the country.

W7 Carménère is slightly tricky because it can be confused with Merlot or Cabernet Franc, but the hallmark herbal/graphite/green pepper character plus Colchagua Chile is well-known territory. W5 ABC Pinot Noir from Santa Maria Valley is recognizably elegant, Burgundy-adjacent California Pinot — low oak, bright acidity, 13.5% — a useful contrast to the bigger South American wines.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Four different countries in North and South America" — one wine per country, all different
  - "Variety" singular for most wines — suggests mostly single-varietal wines
  - 15 marks for origin + variety per wine — this is the headline task
- Universe I considered:
  - USA: California Pinot Noir, Zinfandel, Napa Cab, Oregon Pinot Noir
  - Argentina: Malbec (overwhelmingly dominant choice), Torrontés (white, ruled out)
  - Chile: Carménère, Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah
  - Uruguay: Tannat (the national variety)
  - Canada: possible but rare at MW level for reds
- Rule-outs:
  - USA: Pinot Noir fits because it's lighter and cooler (13.5%) versus the big South American wines
  - Argentina: Malbec is the only real candidate
  - Chile: Carménère is the obvious choice if the wine shows dark fruit + herbal/pyrazine notes
  - Uruguay: Tannat is the only well-known Uruguayan red variety at MW level
- Conclusion: USA Pinot Noir (Santa Maria), Argentina Malbec (Mendoza), Chile Carménère (Colchagua), Uruguay Tannat (Maldonado)
