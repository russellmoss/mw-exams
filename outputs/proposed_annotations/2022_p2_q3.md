## Question (verbatim)
Wines 7 and 8 are from the same region but different sub-regions. a) Identify the sub-region of each wine. (2x10) b) Comment on the key winemaking decisions for each wine. (2x5) c) Comment on the style and quality of each wine. (2x10)

- W8: Clos Vougeot, Gros Frère et Soeur, 2017. Côte de Nuits, Burgundy, France. (14.0%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Burgundy Pinot Noir hierarchy. Like Q2 before it, the "same region, different sub-regions" format sets you up to compare appellations — here it's red Burgundy, and the spread is significant: a Hautes Côtes de Beaune at regional appellation level versus Clos Vougeot Grand Cru.

The two wines actually share the same structure format as Q3 in Paper 2, so you're doing the same sub-region ID, winemaking and quality assessment twice in the same paper — once for Tuscany, once for Burgundy. The skill being tested is the same: can you tell quality level from the glass and explain why?

Wine 7 (Hautes Côtes) should be lighter, more fragrant and simpler — raspberry, cherry, light earth — with lower ABV (12.5%) and less oak influence. Wine 8 (Clos Vougeot) should be darker, richer, more complex with more grip, more structure and obvious quality depth at 14.0%. The 1.5% ABV difference reflects both the site advantage of a Côte de Nuits grand cru and the different vintages (2017 vs 2019, with 2017 being warmer than the fresh 2019 from Hautes Côtes).

Winemaking wise, the Hautes Côtes wine is likely shorter elevage in used oak or tank, where Clos Vougeot at a serious domaine like Gros Frère et Soeur will be new-oak raised with longer barrel time. The quality gap should be obvious in the glass.
