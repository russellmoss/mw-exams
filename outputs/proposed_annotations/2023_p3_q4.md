# Proposed annotation for 2023 Paper 3 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 10-12 are from three different countries. All are made from the same single grape variety.

With reference to all three wines:

a) Identify the grape variety. (18 marks)

Then for each wine:

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (3 x 7 marks)

c) Discuss the production methods. (3 x 5 marks)

d) Comment on quality and commercial potential. (3 x 8 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W10: Maury, Thunevin-Calvet, 1998. Roussillon, France. (16.5%)
- W11: Garnacha Old Vines Cabrida, Celler de Capçanes, 2018. Montsant, Spain. (15.0%)
- W12: Grenache Yount Mill Vineyard, Newfound, 2019. Napa Valley, USA. (13.0%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Grenache/Garnacha across three very different expressions and styles, from a 25-year-old fortified Maury to a powerful old-vine dry Spanish Garnacha to a modern California Grenache. The grape variety identification at 18 marks is the single biggest question in this set, which tells you the examiner views getting the grape right as the whole foundation.

The key challenge in identifying a single grape across three such different wine styles is that the wines don't look the same. A 1998 fortified Maury is going to be deeply amber-tawny from oxidative ageing, high alcohol, sweet and nutty. A 15% old-vine Montsant Garnacha is going to be full-bodied, powerful, dark red-fruited and firmly tannic. A Napa Grenache at 13% is going to be light, perfumed, lifted red-berry, almost Burgundian. Getting the thread that connects them is the skill — the red-berry fruit character, the potential for high alcohol, and the spicy, garrigue signature when in warm climates.

This question is really testing whether you can recognize Grenache in unusual formats. Most candidates would nail the Napa Grenache or the Spanish Garnacha, but the fortified Maury may throw people. Maury is one of a handful of French VDN (Vin Doux Naturel) appellations made from Grenache Noir — the others being Banyuls. If you know that Grenache is the basis for Maury, you can put it together. Production method discussion is important here: fortified/mutage in the Maury, traditional dry red winemaking for the Montsant and Napa.
