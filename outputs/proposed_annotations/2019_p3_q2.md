# Proposed annotation for 2019 Paper 3 Question 2

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 3-4 are both from the same region.

For both wines:

a) Identify the region. (10 marks)

b) Compare and contrast the style and quality of the two wines. (30 marks)

c) Discuss the commercial appeal of the two wines. (10 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W3: Giustino B. Prosecco Superiore, Ruggeri, 2017. Valdobbiadene DOCG, Veneto. (11.5%)
- W4: Prosecco, La Marca, NV. Veneto DOC. (11%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Prosecco quality tiers — specifically DOCG Valdobbiadene versus commercial DOC Prosecco, and what the style and quality differences look like in the glass. The "same region" clue for two light, gently sparkling, off-dry wines with pear and apple aromatics is going to point pretty quickly to Prosecco. Both wines are Glera-based, both are Charmat method, both are off-dry (Extra Dry designation means about 12–17 g/L RS), both are around 11% ABV. The examiner is testing whether you can perceive quality differences within a category that many tasters dismiss as homogeneous.

The style and quality question (part b) is worth 30 marks — by far the biggest allocation in this question — which confirms that the real skill being tested is perceiving the difference between a premium millesimato DOCG wine from hillside Valdobbiadene (Ruggeri Giustino B.) and a commodity NV DOC wine designed for the US market at volume (La Marca/Gallo). That difference shows up in perlage quality, aromatic complexity, depth on the palate and length.

Part c) on commercial appeal is interesting because these two wines have totally different commercial stories: La Marca is one of the bestselling Proseccos in the US; Ruggeri Giustino B. is a quality estate wine with limited distribution. Very different channels and price points.
