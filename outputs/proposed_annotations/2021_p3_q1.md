# Proposed annotation for 2021 Paper 3 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-3 are all sparkling wines. a) Identify the origin and variety of each wine (3x10) b) Describe the method of production of each wine (3x5) c) Comment on the quality and commercial position of each wine (3x10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W1: Brut Imperial, Moët, NV. Champagne. (12%)
- W2: Pol Roger, 2012. Champagne. (12.5%)
- W3: Brut Quartet, Roederer Estate, NV. Anderson Valley. (12%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Champagne styles and your ability to distinguish between non-vintage, vintage, and New World traditional method sparkling wines. Opening Paper 3 with three sparkling wines is common — it's a chance to test the full sparkling spectrum from entry-level NV to vintage Champagne to premium non-Champagne fizz.

The key discriminations here are: NV vs vintage (W1 vs W2), and Champagne vs non-Champagne (W2/W1 vs W3). Moët Brut Imperial NV is the world's most-sold Champagne — fine but not profound, crisp, bready, accessible. Pol Roger 2012 vintage Champagne is a step up in both age and complexity — more autolytic development, more structure, more precise. Roederer Estate Brut Quartet is the California outpost of Louis Roederer, made in Anderson Valley where the cool climate produces something genuinely close to a Champagne quality level, but it will read fractionally riper and more fruit-forward.

The commercial question will want you to distinguish: Moët is global volume, available everywhere, mass-market luxury. Pol Roger is fine wine with a loyal following and strong fine dining trade. Roederer Estate is the classic "gateway" California sparkling — premium but more accessible than Champagne in price and fruit character.

Production: all traditional method, but the NV uses reserve wines, the vintage does not, and the California wine may use a slightly different assemblage of Pinot Noir, Chardonnay and Pinot Meunier.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "All sparkling" on Paper 3 Q1 = likely a Champagne-anchored flight with at least one non-Champagne
  - Variety and origin (3x10) = they want you to know it's Pinot Noir/Chardonnay/Meunier blends vs perhaps single variety for W3
  - Method (3x5) = all traditional method, but with differences in reserve wine use
- Universe I considered:
  - Champagne NV: very likely in a sparkling flight
  - Champagne vintage: frequently used to show aging potential
  - California traditional method: Roederer Estate is exactly the kind of wine MW examiners use
  - Cava / Prosecco: unlikely to appear at this level without something saying "tank method" or "Mediterranean"
- Rule-outs:
  - Prosecco/Cava: less likely at Q1 of Paper 3 which tends to use higher quality benchmarks
- Conclusion: Three traditional method sparkling wines confirmed. The Roederer Estate is the one where candidates might hesitate — it's good enough to look like Champagne but slightly riper.
