# Proposed annotation for 2017 Paper 3 Question 6

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 11-12 are both fortified. a) ID origin (2x9) b) Method of production re quality (2x10) c) Who would buy? (2x6)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W11: Palo Cortado Viejo CP, Valdespino, NV. Jerez. (20%)
- W12: Maury Grande Reserve, Pouderoux, NV. Roussillon. (15.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of fortified wines, and specifically your ability to distinguish different fortified styles by origin and production method. Both are NV and both are aged oxidative styles — but one is Spanish dry Sherry and one is French sweet VDN. That contrast is the whole point of the question.

The Palo Cortado is the rarest and most intellectually complex Sherry style — it begins its life under flor (like a Fino or Amontillado) but then the flor dies and it develops oxidatively (like an Oloroso). It has the hazelnut/almond character of an Amontillado combined with the richness and walnut of an Oloroso. At 20% ABV it's clearly fortified. Palo Cortado is the kind of wine the examiners love because it tests specific knowledge of Sherry sub-categories.

The Maury Grande Réserve is a Vin Doux Naturel from Roussillon — Grenache Noir, mutage (arrested fermentation with grape spirit), and extended oxidative ageing in old barrels or bonbonnes. At 15.5% ABV it's significantly lower than the Sherry, and it's sweet rather than dry. The "Grande Réserve" designation signals extended aging which would show tawny/rancio character.

The method of production sub-question (10 marks each) wants solera vs. VDN mutage comparison, flor biology for the Sherry, and oxidative aging for both. The "who would buy" question (6 marks each) is a softer commercial question — these are both niche, specialist, fine wine market products, not supermarket stuff.
