# Proposed annotation for 2022 Paper 2 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 9 and 10 are made from the same single grape variety and come from the same region. a) Identify the grape variety and region. (20) b) Compare the style and quality of the two wines. (20) c) Compare the market potential of the two wines, with reference to current trends. (20)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W9: Malbec Serie A, Zuccardi, 2020. Uco Valley, Mendoza, Argentina. (13.9%)
- W10: Malbec Reserve Old Vine, Altocedro, 2018. Uco Valley, Mendoza, Argentina. (14.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Argentine Malbec and the ability to distinguish tiers of quality within the same region and variety. "Same grape variety, same region" at this mark weighting (20 marks just for the ID) means the examiner expects you to be confident and specific — country, region, variety, and probably sub-region.

Malbec from Argentina should be fairly legible: deep purple, black fruit, violet, plush texture, soft tannins, generally ripe and approachable. The key question is which country — Malbec also grows in Cahors (France) and in other parts of the New World, but nowhere in the world does it reach the same critical mass as Mendoza Argentina. Within Mendoza, Uco Valley is the premium sub-region at altitude.

The interesting part is wines 9 and 10 are clearly at different quality levels: Zuccardi Serie A is the more accessible, polished-commercial style at 13.9%, while Altocedro Old Vine Reserve at 14.5% and an older vintage is denser, more structured and more age-worthy. The "reserve" and "old vine" nomenclature should signal that wine 10 is the step-up.

Market potential (c) is where you can show examiner-pleasing knowledge about Argentina's Malbec boom — the shift from cheap supermarket Malbec to a tiered, terroir-led category with Uco Valley at the top. Wine 9 plays in the £15-25 sector with broad retail distribution; wine 10 is fine-wine channel, restaurants, collectors.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - Same variety, same region — narrows heavily
  - Market potential and current trends explicitly asked — suggests a category with a commercial story
  - ABV difference (13.9% vs 14.5%) and vintage gap (2020 vs 2018) suggest different quality tiers
- Universe I considered:
  - Argentine Malbec (Mendoza/Uco Valley) — by far the most likely
  - Cahors Malbec (France) — possible but much less commercially prominent at MW level
  - Chilean Malbec — exists but minor
- Rule-outs:
  - "Current trends" and "market potential" practically telegraphs Argentina's rising Malbec story
  - Old Vine Reserve framing is very New World in style
- Conclusion: Argentine Malbec, Uco Valley, Mendoza — Zuccardi Serie A (approachable tier) vs Altocedro Old Vine Reserve (premium tier)
