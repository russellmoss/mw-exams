# Proposed annotation for 2019 Paper 3 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 9-10 come from two different countries.

For each wine:

a) Comment on the method of production. (2 x 8 marks)

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (2 x 8 marks)

c) Comment on the style, quality, and commercial positioning. (2 x 9 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W9: Manzanilla Papirusa, Lustau, NV. Sanlúcar de Barrameda, Spain. (15%)
- W10: Cuvée Spéciale, Domaine de Montbourgeau, 2012. L'Étoile, Jura, France. (13%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of oxidative white wine styles that are made under or near yeast veils — specifically Manzanilla (aged under flor in Sanlúcar) and Jura sous voile white wine (aged in old barrels without topping, developing a partial flor cover). These are two of the only wine styles in the world where biological ageing under yeast plays a central role in the white wine's character, so putting them together in a pair is a deliberately focused test of that knowledge.

The method of production question (8 marks each) is where the most precise knowledge is needed: you need to describe flor yeast, biological ageing in the solera system, Palomino Fino grape, albariza soils and the Atlantic-humidity conditions of Sanlúcar for the Manzanilla; and for the Jura you need to describe sous voile ageing in old barrels, Savagnin or Chardonnay, and how the partial yeast veil develops oxidative complexity without the strict 6 years / 3 months requirement of vin jaune.

The commercial question is interesting — Manzanilla and Jura white wines both face commercial headwinds because they are niche, polarizing and hard to sell to the general consumer, but both have committed specialist followings. These are not wines you'd find in a supermarket and that's worth discussing.
