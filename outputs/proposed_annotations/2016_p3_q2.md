# Proposed annotation for 2016 Paper 3 Question 2

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 6-8 are all fortified.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (3 x 10 marks)

b) Discuss the key winemaking techniques used to arrive at this style. (3 x 10 marks)

c) Comment on quality and maturity. (3 x 5 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W6: Palo Cortado, Cayetano del Pino y Cia, NV. Jerez, Spain. (20%)
- W7: 20 Year Old Tawny Port, Graham's. Douro, Portugal. (20%)
- W8: 10 Year Old Sercial, Henriques & Henriques. Madeira, Portugal. (20%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of the three major European fortified wine traditions. "All fortified" on Paper 3 with three wines essentially gives you the major checklist: Sherry, Port, and Madeira are the three canonical fortified styles the MW expects you to master. All three are at 20% ABV here which is a helpful visual/analytic signal. The question is not about identifying the variety (which may or may not be obvious) but about placing the origin and explaining the distinct production method for each.

The hardest identification is probably the Palo Cortado — it's a rare sherry category that combines the aromatic finesse of an amontillado nose with the body of an oloroso palate. Many candidates would correctly identify Sherry but might struggle to name Palo Cortado specifically. Tawny Port at 20 years is very recognisable — tawny amber colour, walnut, toffee, orange peel, sweet. Sercial Madeira is perhaps the most challenging because it's dry despite being fortified, with elevated acidity and maderised character; without knowing Madeira varieties candidates might correctly identify Madeira but not the Sercial.

Winemaking sub-question is rich: Palo Cortado = solera system, oxidative aging, fortification, the mystery of the Palo Cortado classification path (starting biologically, losing flor); Tawny Port = fortification during fermentation (to retain sweetness), long oxidative cask aging (20 years average), blending to style; Sercial Madeira = canteiro aging (not estufagem at this tier), fortification, maderisation through heat and oxidation, very high natural acidity from Sercial grape.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "All fortified" = the big three: Sherry, Port, Madeira
  - 20% ABV on all three = all fully fortified
  - Quality + maturity = wines with age-indicator categories (20yr Tawny, 10yr Sercial, VOS Palo Cortado)
- Universe I considered:
  - Australia makes fortified too (Rutherglen Muscat) but less likely at this MW level
  - Vin Doux Naturel (France): possible but less iconic than the big three
  - The question practically dictates Spain + Portugal + Portugal
- Rule-outs:
  - Australian fortified: possible but Jerez/Douro/Madeira is the canonical MW trio
  - Marsala or Vin Santo: wouldn't make three fortified wines at this quality level
- Conclusion: Sherry (Palo Cortado, Cayetano del Pino), Tawny Port (Graham's 20yr), Sercial Madeira (Henriques & Henriques 10yr)
