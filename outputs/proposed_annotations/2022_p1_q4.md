# Proposed annotation for 2022 Paper 1 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 9-12 are from different countries and different single grape varieties. a) Identify the grape variety and origin of each wine. (4x15) b) Comment on the quality and market position of each wine. (4x10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W9: Torrontés, Colomé, 2021. Salta, Argentina. (13.0%)
- W10: Albariño "La Trucha", Notas Frutales, 2020. Rias Baixas, Spain. (13.0%)
- W11: Grüner Veltliner, Loimer, 2020. Kamptal, Austria. (12.0%)
- W12: Roussanne, Domaine de Tunnel, 2020. Saint-Péray, Rhône. (14.0%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of single-variety white wines that are strongly associated with a specific country or region. The stem confirms four different countries, four different varieties — so no guesswork on whether they might be blends. The universe of "distinctive single-variety whites with clear geographic signatures" is actually a manageable list: Riesling (Germany/Alsace/Austria/Australia), Grüner Veltliner (Austria), Albariño (Spain), Torrontés (Argentina), Viognier (northern Rhône), Roussanne/Marsanne (northern Rhône), Chenin (Loire/South Africa), Semillon (Hunter Valley), Gewurztraminer (Alsace/Alto Adige), Assyrtiko (Greece).

The easiest wine to ID here is probably the Torrontés — intensely floral, jasmine, orange blossom, grapey lift, and nothing else in the world smells quite like it. The Grüner Veltliner should also be legible: white pepper, citrus, crisp and linear with low alcohol. Albariño should give Atlantic saline freshness and peach, though distinguishing Rias Baixas from Torrontés takes palate experience. The curveball is the Roussanne from Saint-Péray — rich, textural, apricot blossom, a touch of almond and old oak. Getting "Roussanne" rather than misidentifying it as Viognier or Chardonnay requires knowing the northern Rhône's still white styles specifically.

The market position part is interesting because you have one major-volume international category wine (Albariño is now globally distributed), one extremely niche wine (Roussanne from Saint-Péray has very limited production and low consumer recognition), one quirky but growing category (Torrontés), and one Austria-dominant but internationally available variety (Grüner Veltliner).

- Conclusion: Argentina (Torrontés/Salta), Spain (Albariño/Rias Baixas), Austria (Grüner/Kamptal), France (Roussanne/Saint-Péray)
