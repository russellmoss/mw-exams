# Proposed annotation for 2022 Paper 3 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 10-12 are from three different countries. a) Identify the origin of each wine. (3x10) b) Comment on the key winemaking decisions for each wine. (3x5) c) Comment on the style, quality and commercial position of each wine. (3x10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W10: Recioto della Valpolicella, Corte Sant'Alda, 2018. Veneto, Italy. (15.0%)
- W11: 10 Year Tawny Port, Quinta do Noval, NV. Douro, Portugal. (19.5%)
- W12: Para 10 Year Grand Tawny, Seppeltsfield, NV. Barossa Valley, South Australia. (20.9%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of sweet red and fortified wines, and specifically your ability to distinguish appassimento sweet red from aged tawny fortified, and Portuguese tawny from Australian tawny. Three very different styles from three different countries: Italy, Portugal, Australia.

Wine 10 is the curveball — Recioto della Valpolicella is the sweet counterpart to Amarone, made from the same dried Corvina-based grapes but with fermentation arrested before dryness. It should look like a deep ruby-garnet sweet red, rich with dried cherry, dark chocolate and violet, and sweet on the palate without the bitterness that Amarone gets as it dries out. Many candidates will think "Amarone" and then be confused by the sweetness — but the key is that it's noticeably sweet, not dry. The winemaking question should get at appassimento: grapes dried 3-4 months on bamboo racks, then fermentation deliberately arrested.

Wines 11 and 12 are the tawny comparison. Both are 10-year average aged tawnies: Quinta do Noval (Portugal, 19.5%) vs Seppeltsfield Para Grand Tawny (Australia, 20.9%). Both should read amber-tawny with an orange-brick rim, oxidative nutty rancio character, dried fruit and walnut. The key differences are: the Portuguese is more precise and fresher, with the Douro's underlying tension; the Australian is richer, heavier, with more dried-fruit concentration from Barossa's heat. Getting both of those right — country AND style nuance — is the challenge.

The commercial question (c) is interesting for all three: Recioto is genuinely niche even within Italy and has limited export presence; Quinta do Noval Tawny is premium Port with global distribution; Seppeltsfield Grand Tawny is one of Australia's heritage fortified icons with a small but devoted following.

  - Very high ABVs (15%, 19.5%, 20.9%) — ranges from sweet red to fortified tawny
  - All NV or older vintages — oxidative/aged styles

  - Italy (Recioto, Vinsanto, Marsala) — sweet or fortified Italian
  - Portugal (Tawny Port, Colheita, Madeira) — always a contender in P3 fortified
  - Australia (Seppeltsfield Grand Tawny, Rutherglen Muscat) — increasingly included in MW fortified questions
- Rule-outs:
  - Recioto is a genuine curveball — most will know Amarone but not its sweet equivalent
  - Distinguishing 10yr Tawny Port from Australian Grand Tawny is the key discrimination test
  - ABV of 20.9% is fractionally higher than typical Port (19-20%) — slight signal toward Australian fortified
- Conclusion: Italy (Recioto della Valpolicella, appassimento sweet red), Portugal (Quinta do Noval 10yr Tawny), Australia (Seppeltsfield Para Grand Tawny)
