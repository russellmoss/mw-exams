# Proposed annotation for 2018 Paper 3 Question 2

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 7-9 all come from the same country. a) Identify the origin and grape variety of each wine (3x10) b) Comment on the quality of each wine in context (3x10) c) Comment on the maturity of each wine (3x5)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W7: Domaine de Chevalier Blanc, 2010. Pessac-Léognan, Bordeaux. (14%)
- W8: Châteauneuf-du-Pape Blanc, Domaine Bousquet des Papes, 2014. Rhône. (13.5%)
- W9: Saint-Romain, Sous le Château, Olivier Leflaive, 2015. Burgundy. (13%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of French white wines from three completely different appellations and grape varieties. The stem says same country — and with three whites in Paper 3, France is the dominant candidate (Italy or Spain could work but France offers the most variety of distinct white wine styles). You need to get origin and variety for 10 marks each, which means they need to be identifiable.

The three wines are Pessac-Léognan Blanc (Sauvignon Blanc/Sémillon blend, full-bodied, toasty, 14% ABV, deep gold at 8 years), CdP Blanc (Grenache Blanc-led southern Rhône blend, aromatic, opulent, low acid, 13.5%), and a village Burgundy Chardonnay (cooler, crisper, Saint-Romain being a lesser-known side-valley appellation). The Pessac-Léognan is probably the most distinctive if you know the style — the nutty, barrel-fermented, evolved white Bordeaux character is quite unlike Burgundy. The CdP Blanc is the curveball: aromatic, full, dry but seemingly rich — most candidates will know CdP as a red appellation, so the blanc style is a test of depth. The Saint-Romain is the most classically Burgundian of the three but at village level without any premier cru designation, the complexity won't be overwhelming. Maturity sub-question (3 marks each) favors the 2010 Chevalier (oldest, most evolved, 8 years) versus the 2015 Saint-Romain (youngest, should be at peak or just past it).
