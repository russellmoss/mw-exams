# Proposed annotation for 2016 Paper 3 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-5 are all made from the same single grape variety.

For each wine:

a) Discuss winemaking, with particular reference to oak. (5 x 10 marks)

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (5 x 8 marks)

c) Comment on quality and commercial appeal. (5 x 7 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W1: 'Initial' Blanc de Blancs, Jacques Selosse, NV. Champagne, France. (12%)
- W2: Blanc de Blancs, Pol Roger, 2006. Champagne, France. (12.5%)
- W3: Puligny Montrachet Vieilles Vignes, Vincent Girardin, 2013. Burgundy, France. (13%)
- W4: Chardonnay, Vidal Legacy, 2014. Hawkes Bay, New Zealand. (13.5%)
- W5: Mâcon Blanc Villages, M&B Talmard, 2014. Burgundy, France. (13%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Chardonnay across five radically different expressions and winemaking styles. Five wines, same variety, with oak as the primary winemaking focus — this is almost certainly Chardonnay, the only white grape that spans sparkling Champagne, Côte de Beaune grand/village Burgundy, and New World premium styles in a coherent lineup. Riesling could theoretically appear in five styles but the oak question would make no sense for Riesling. Pinot Gris at five styles would be a stretch. Chardonnay is the only sensible answer.

The oak sub-question at 50 marks total (10 per wine) is the dominant focus of this question. The examiner wants you to articulate: Selosse Initial (barrel-fermented in old oak, oxidative reserve-wine philosophy, essentially a still wine with bubbles — very unusual approach for Champagne), Pol Roger Blanc de Blancs 2006 (stainless steel fermented, traditional-method, autolytic complexity from extended lees, minimal oak), Puligny-Montrachet VV Girardin (barrel-fermented in French oak, 15% new, 14 months — classic white Burgundy oak regime), Vidal Legacy Hawkes Bay (French oak barrel fermented, full malo, New World richness), and Talmard Mâcon-Villages (minimal to no oak — unoaked or lightly handled Burgundy).

So you get the full oak spectrum: heavily oxidative-barrel-shaped (Selosse), sparkling autolysis without oak (Pol Roger), measured-oak village Burgundy (Girardin and Talmard at opposite ends), and New World barrel-fermented generous (Vidal). That's a beautifully structured question testing the full Chardonnay winemaking toolkit.
