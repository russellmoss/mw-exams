# Proposed annotation for 2016 Paper 1 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 7 and 8 come from the same country.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (2 x 10 marks)

b) Comment on the winemaking. (2 x 8 marks)

c) Discuss style and quality. (2 x 7 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W7: Acodo White Rioja, Basilio Izquierdo, 2010. Rioja, Spain. (13.5%)
- W8: Albariño, Bodegas de Fefiñanes, 2014. Rías Baixas, Spain. (12.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Spain's white wine traditions. The "same country" pairing of two whites on Paper 1 means you need a country with two distinct white wine regions at a recognisable quality level. Spain is the obvious answer here, as the two well-known Spanish white styles are traditional white Rioja (barrel-aged Viura) and Albariño from Rías Baixas — and they could not taste more different. The traditional white Rioja at 6 years old (2010 tasted in 2016) would be deep gold, nutty, oxidative, waxy — almost like a mature Burgundy or a Jura white. The Albariño would be pale, crisp, aromatic, stone fruit and citrus blossom, very fresh at 2 years old.

The risk for the candidate is the aged Rioja white. Many tasters won't have encountered traditional barrel-aged Viura and might call it aged white Burgundy, Jura, or even Hunter Valley Semillon. The Albariño is actually the easier one — it's distinctive enough that most candidates should get it. The examiner is really testing whether you know that Spain produces both a serious reductive aromatic white AND a complex oxidatively-aged white tradition.

Winemaking sub-question wants: Rioja = extended barrel aging in old American/French oak, possibly malo, no temperature control vs Rías Baixas = stainless steel, no oak, cold fermentation, no malo, all about aromatic preservation. Pretty unlikely this question comes from any other country — it really only works for Spain.
