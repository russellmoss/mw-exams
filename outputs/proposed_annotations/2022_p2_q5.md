# Proposed annotation for 2022 Paper 2 Question 5

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 11 and 12 are made from the same single grape variety or predominant grape variety and come from the same region. a) Identify the grape variety or predominant variety and region. (20) b) Compare the style and quality of the two wines. (20) c) Comment on the market potential of these wines, with reference to current trends. (20)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W11: Zinfandel Mother Clone, Pedroncelli, 2019. Dry Creek Valley, California. (15.5%)
- W12: Lytton Springs, Ridge Vineyards, 2019. Dry Creek Valley, California. (14.6%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of California Zinfandel and specifically Dry Creek Valley as the premium sub-region. The "single grape variety or predominant grape variety" phrasing in the stem is a big tell — when they add "or predominant" that almost always means one of the wines is a field blend or a legally defined blend where another variety leads but the dominant grape is still what you'd call it. Ridge Lytton Springs is exactly this: a Zinfandel-dominant field blend with Petite Sirah, Carignane and others. So the examiner is essentially flagging that you won't be confused when you notice wine 12 is not 100% Zinfandel.

Both wines being from Dry Creek Valley (same region) and both being Zinfandel-led makes the ID relatively concentrated: you need to know Dry Creek is the heartland of premium Zinfandel in Sonoma, and then distinguish two quality levels within it. Wine 11 (Pedroncelli) at 15.5% is the more extroverted, jammy, high-alcohol style — classic Mother Clone Dry Creek. Wine 12 (Ridge Lytton Springs) at 14.6% should be more structured and complex, with more savory earth and spice notes from the field blend, longer finish, and a quality level that sits clearly above the straight varietal bottling.

Market potential: Zinfandel is almost exclusively American in appeal; the variety never fully broke through in international markets the way Cabernet or Pinot Noir did. Ridge is one of the exceptions with genuine fine-wine credibility globally, while Pedroncelli is a solid domestic value brand. The contrast there is actually an interesting market story.

- Rule-outs:
  - 15.5% ABV narrows heavily to California or Southern Italy — California more likely for an exam with market discussion
  - Ridge Lytton Springs is an iconic enough wine to appear in a Paper 2 question
- Conclusion: California Zinfandel / Dry Creek Valley — Pedroncelli Mother Clone (varietal, high-alcohol, value tier) vs Ridge Lytton Springs (field blend, more structured, fine-wine tier)
