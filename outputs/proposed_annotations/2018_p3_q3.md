# Proposed annotation for 2018 Paper 3 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 10-12 come from different countries and different single grape varieties (minimum 90%) associated with the Rhône Valley. a) Identify the predominant variety of each wine (3x5) b) Identify the origin of each wine (3x8) c) Comment on the style of each wine and its commercial appeal (3x?)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Rhône-associated grape varieties when they're grown outside the Rhône Valley itself. The stem is unusually specific — it tells you the varieties are all associated with the Rhône, and that they're each from a different country. So you're looking at the main Rhône grapes: Syrah, Grenache, Mourvèdre, Cinsault, Viognier, Marsanne, Roussanne. But grown elsewhere in the world.

The three wines are Cinsault from Lodi California (12.5% ABV — lighter than you'd expect from California, pale, fragrant), Garnacha from high-altitude inland Spain (14% at 800m in Valencia — elegant rather than extracted, herbal), and Bandol Mourvèdre from Provence (14%, full body, garrigue, structured tannin). What's clever here is that only Bandol is actually in Provence adjacent to the Rhône — so the candidate has to understand the variety's global spread. Cinsault in Lodi is a genuine curveball: most people don't associate old-vine Cinsault with California. The Mustiguillo Garnacha is from a VdlT in Valencia-La Mancha, which is also not an obvious Rhône connection.

For the commercial appeal sub-question, these three wines sit at very different market positions: Birichino Bechthold is artisan/niche ($30-40 but small production, cult following), Mustiguillo is an internationally recognised artisan producer at similar pricing, and Bandol Château de Pibarnon is a serious appellation wine with aging potential and restaurant trade demand. Not exactly mass-market wines.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Rhône Valley-associated varieties" — narrows immediately to Syrah, Grenache, Mourvèdre, Cinsault, Viognier
  - Different countries, minimum 90% each variety — emphasis on variety purity
  - Style and commercial appeal — focuses on market positioning of less mainstream wines
- Universe I considered:
  - Syrah (New World), Grenache (Spain, Australia, California), Mourvèdre (Bandol, Australia), Cinsault (South Africa, California), Viognier (various)
- Rule-outs:
  - Syrah already in P2 Q3; probably wouldn't repeat
  - Viognier possible but the lightness of one wine (12.5%) doesn't fit Viognier's typical richness
- Conclusion: Cinsault from Lodi (USA), Garnacha/Grenache from inland Spain (VdlT), Mourvèdre from Bandol (France/Provence)
