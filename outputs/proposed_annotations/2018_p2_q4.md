# Proposed annotation for 2018 Paper 2 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 11 and 12 come from the Americas. a) Identify the grape variety of each wine (2x5) b) Identify the origin of each wine (2x10) c) Comment on the quality of each wine in context of its origin (2x10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W11: Old Vines Zinfandel, Ravenswood, 2014. Lodi, California. (14.5%)
- W12: Cabernet Sauvignon, Viñedo Chadwick, 2014. Puente Alto, Maipo, Chile. (13.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of red wine from the Americas — which covers North and South America. With only 5 marks per variety ID, the examiner is signalling that getting the exact grape is less important than getting the origin right (10 marks each). That suggests at least one wine is from an unusual variety or a less immediately obvious one.

The likely candidates for Americas reds are Cabernet Sauvignon (California, Chile, Argentina), Zinfandel (California), Malbec (Argentina), Carménère (Chile), Merlot (California/Washington), and Pinot Noir (already covered in Q2). The 14.5% ABV on wine 11 is a strong signal — that points directly at Lodi Zinfandel or perhaps warm-climate Grenache. Zinfandel at this ripeness level shows jammy blackberry, sweet spice, and warmth that is pretty distinctive. Wine 12 at 13.5% from a premium Chilean estate like Viñedo Chadwick reads very differently — graphite, blackcurrant, cassis, polished tannin, cool Andean freshness. That's a very serious Cabernet in the mold of a Napa icon but with Chilean precision and a more restrained style. Origin identification here is the key challenge: distinguishing Lodi (San Joaquin Valley, California) from premium Chile (Maipo/Puente Alto) requires knowing both regions exist as distinct entities.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Americas" = US, Chile, Argentina most likely; variety IDs only 5 marks each
  - Different varieties implied (no "same variety" qualifier)
  - Origin worth 10 each — most of the marks
- Universe I considered:
  - Zinfandel (California), Malbec (Argentina), Cab Sauv (Chile/California), Carménère (Chile)
- Rule-outs:
  - Pinot Noir already used in Q2
  - Malbec would likely come from Mendoza; wouldn't rule out but Cab more likely for a premium Chilean wine
  - Carménère possible as a curveball but Cab is the premium Chilean benchmark
- Conclusion: Zinfandel from Lodi California, Cabernet Sauvignon from Maipo Chile
