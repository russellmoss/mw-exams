# Proposed annotation for 2017 Paper 1 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 8-10 are all from Europe and each is made from a different grape variety. a) ID variety (3x8) b) ID origin (3x8) c) Sense of place (3x9)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W8: Muscat Réserve, Trimbach, 2014. Alsace. (12.5%)
- W9: Albariño, Pazo de Villarei, 2015. Rias Baixas. (12.5%)
- W10: Condrieu Coteau de Vernon, Vernay, 2014. Rhône. (14%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of distinctive European white varieties where the variety itself is a strong signal. Three different European whites, each from a different variety — the "sense of place" sub-question for 9 marks each tells you these wines should each read as expressing their terroir distinctly, not just as commercial varietal wines.

The three wines are actually very different aromatic profiles: Muscat is intensely grapey and floral but dry (Trimbach style); Albariño is saline, peachy, and Atlantic coastal; Viognier/Condrieu is the full-on oily, honeysuckle, apricot aromatic white from the Northern Rhône. All three are highly recognizable to someone who knows them well. The variety identification (8 marks each) is relatively accessible for these three — they're not obscure — but the origin specificity in sub-part b) is where it gets harder. You need to know that dry grapey white = Alsace Muscat rather than an Italian Moscato or Greek Muscat, that the Atlantic saline peachy white is Rias Baixas specifically, and that oily/apricot/honeysuckle at 14% is Condrieu not just generic Viognier from somewhere.

The "sense of place" question is testing whether you can connect each wine's aromatic/structural character to its specific terroir rather than just naming the variety.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - Europe confirmed — limits to Old World varieties
  - Three different varieties — each wine is a distinct variety ID challenge
  - "Sense of place" sub-question — implies terroir-expressive, not commodity wines
  - 8+8+9 mark structure = balanced emphasis on variety, origin, terroir
- Universe I considered:
  - Muscat (dry, Alsace) — STRONG SIGNAL once you taste it
  - Albariño (Rias Baixas) — one of Europe's most distinctive coastal whites
  - Viognier/Condrieu — classic aromatic Northern Rhône
  - Other options: Pinot Gris, Chenin, Grüner Veltliner, Verdejo — all possible
- Rule-outs:
  - Chardonnay and Sauvignon Blanc less likely as "sense of place" becomes more generic
  - Riesling very plausible too but three aromatics together is more interesting to examine
- Conclusion: Muscat (Alsace) + Albariño (Rias Baixas) + Viognier (Condrieu)
