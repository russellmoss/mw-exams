# Proposed annotation for 2017 Paper 3 Question 2

## Question (verbatim)
Consider wine 4 to be of unknown origin. a) Key winemaking (15) b) Style, quality, market positioning (10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W4: "Amber", Cullen, 2014. Margaret River, WA. (15%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of orange/skin-contact white wine as a style category, and specifically your ability to describe what's happening in the glass without relying on knowing the origin. The question explicitly removes origin as a variable — "unknown origin" — which is a huge signal that the wine is hard to place and the examiner doesn't expect you to get there. Instead, almost all the marks go to winemaking (15) and style/quality/positioning (10).

The Cullen Amber is a skin-contact white wine (sometimes called "orange wine") made from Sauvignon Blanc and Sémillon with extended maceration on skins. At 15% ABV it's unusually high for a white wine, and the amber colour, tannin structure, and oxidative complexity would be very distinctive in the glass. The examiner wants you to recognize that this is a white grape wine made with prolonged skin contact — distinguishing it from an orange wine made by some other technique, or from an oxidative white like a vin jaune or an aged Rioja blanco.

The winemaking (15 marks) question is therefore really asking: can you identify skin contact maceration as the defining technique? You should also be able to discuss the biodynamic/natural wine context, ambient yeast, neutral vessel ageing. The style/quality question (10 marks) wants you to assess where this wine sits commercially — it's a niche, avant-garde product, premium-priced, targeting the natural wine, restaurant, and fine wine collector market.
