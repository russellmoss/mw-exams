# Proposed annotation for 2018 Paper 3 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-6 are presented as pairs — wines 1 and 2 are one pair, wines 3 and 4 are another pair, wines 5 and 6 are a third pair. Each pair is made by a different single producer. a) Identify the origin of each pair (3x10) b) Comment on the key winemaking features of each pair (3x12) c) Identify the vintage of each pair with comments on maturity (3x3)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W1: Dom Pérignon 1999. Champagne. (12.5%)
- W2: Dom Pérignon 2009. Champagne. (12.5%)
- W3: Château Coutet 2002. Sauternes-Barsac. (14%)
- W4: Château Coutet 2013. Sauternes-Barsac. (13.5%)
- W5: Taylor's Vintage Port 1985. Douro. (20.5%)
- W6: Taylor's Vintage Port 2009. Douro. (20.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of three iconic producers across three completely different wine categories — vintage Champagne, classified Sauternes/Barsac, and Vintage Port — with a paired vintage comparison within each category. The structure is clever: same producer, same wine, two different vintages. So for each pair you have to identify the origin AND read the maturity signals to sequence the wines by age.

The Champagne pair is probably the most identifiable — Dom Pérignon's consistent bead, autolytic complexity, and Pinot Noir/Chardonnay balance should be recognizable. The 1999 (19 years at exam date) will show much deeper color, toast and truffle development versus the 2009 (9 years, still relatively fresh and primary). The Sauternes pair at 14% and 13.5% ABV are both full sweet wines — amber versus golden color is the immediate visual maturity signal. The 2002 will be marmalade-and-toffee evolved; the 2013 will be primary apricot and honey. The Port pair is simplest in some ways: 20.5% ABV on both is unambiguous — that's fortified. The 1985 (33 years) will be tawny-amber with dried fruit and leather complexity; the 2009 (9 years) will be opaque purple with fresh dark berry and grippy tannin still very much present.

The vintage identification sub-questions only carry 3 marks each — the examiner isn't expecting precision, just a reasonable decade assessment and maturity commentary.
