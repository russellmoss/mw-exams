# Proposed annotation for 2025 Paper 1 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1 and 2 are blends of the same two grape varieties.

For both wines:

a) Identify the grape varieties. (10 marks)

For each wine:

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (2 x 8 marks)

c) Comment on the style, winemaking and quality. (2 x 12 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference -- DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W1: Chateau de Fieuzal Blanc, 2021. Pessac-Leognan, France. (12.5%)
- W2: Sauvignon Semillon Circa 77, Xanadu, 2023. Margaret River, Australia. (13%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of white blends. They are both still whites, so we know it is highly likely one of two possible blends -- a white Bordeaux blend (Sauvignon Blanc and Semillon) or a Marsanne and Roussanne blend from the Rhone, California, or Australia. Those are really the only two classic two-grape white blends that show up globally at volume. You could maybe throw in Torrontes/Muscat blends or some oddball stuff but that would be pretty unlikely on the exam. Once you taste them, if they show citrus and gooseberry with some textural weight from the Semillon, you're in Sauvignon-Semillon territory. The question then wants you to distinguish Old World vs New World -- the Bordeaux version will likely show more oak influence, more restraint, maybe some waxy lees character, while the Margaret River or similar New World version will be more fruit-forward and aromatic. The mark allocation is heavy on style/winemaking/quality (12 marks each) vs origin (8 marks each), which tells you the examiner cares more about whether you understand HOW these wines are made differently than exactly WHERE they are from. In this case it was white Bordeaux (Pessac-Leognan) and Margaret River, Australia.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference -- DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "blends of the same two grape varieties" -- this immediately narrows the universe to well-known two-grape white blends
  - Paper 1 = white still wines, so no sparkling or fortified considerations
  - Only 2 wines, so a direct comparison question, likely Old World vs New World
  - Mark allocation: 10 for variety ID, 8+8 for origin, 12+12 for style/winemaking/quality -- style matters most
- Universe I considered:
  - Sauvignon Blanc + Semillon (white Bordeaux blend) -- Bordeaux, Margaret River, possibly South Africa or Hunter Valley
  - Marsanne + Roussanne -- Northern Rhone, some California and Australian producers (Tahbilk, Chapoutier)
  - Possibly Gruner Veltliner + Riesling or other Austrian/German blends but extremely unlikely
  - Possibly Viognier + Marsanne but those are less classic as two-grape blends
- Rule-outs:
  - Marsanne/Roussanne is plausible but less globally established as a two-variety blend pairing compared to SB/Semillon
  - SB/Semillon is THE classic white blend, made in Bordeaux, Margaret River, Hunter Valley, South Africa
  - With only 2 wines it is almost certainly going to be the most well-known option
- Conclusion: Sauvignon Blanc + Semillon blend, one Old World (Bordeaux) and one New World (Margaret River, Australia)
