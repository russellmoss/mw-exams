# Proposed annotation for 2019 Paper 3 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-2 are both from the same region.

For both wines:

a) Identify the region. (10 marks)

b) Compare and contrast the method of production. (15 marks)

c) Compare and contrast the style, quality and commercial appeal of the two wines. (25 marks)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W1: Grand Brut, Perrier-Jouët, NV. Champagne, France. (12%)
- W2: Egly-Ouriet Grand Cru Millésime, 2007. Champagne, France. (12.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of Champagne quality tiers and the ability to contrast large-house commercial NV against high-end grower-producer vintage Champagne. The "same region" clue narrows things down fast when both wines are sparkling — the major same-region sparkling pairs you'd consider are Champagne, Cava (DO Cava), Prosecco (Valdobbiadene vs DOC), or possibly Crémant. If both wines are autolytic, chalk-mineral and have fine persistent mousse, you're almost certainly in Champagne.

The really interesting part here is what part c) is asking — 25 marks for style, quality and commercial appeal. That's a lot. The examiner wants you to be able to say: NV house Champagne is designed for broad accessibility, consistent house style, immediate drinking, massive production, priced for restaurants and entry-level occasion; versus grower Champagne Millésime from a single vintage is smaller production, extended lees ageing (Egly-Ouriet is famous for 3–4 years on lees), deeper autolytic complexity, lower dosage, finewine-collector positioning and a very different price/distribution model.

The production contrast in part b) is also clear: multi-vintage assemblage with reserve wines vs single vintage wine; different lees ageing duration; possibly different dosage levels; grower vs négoce.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Same region" for two sparkling wines in Paper 3 — almost always Champagne
  - 25 marks for commercial appeal — very high allocation, examiner wants detailed commercial thinking
  - NV vs vintage, large house vs grower are the canonical Champagne contrasts
- Universe I considered:
  - Champagne (most likely for two wines of this complexity)
  - Cava (two Cava DO wines theoretically possible)
  - Prosecco DOCG vs DOC (see Q2 in this same paper — examiner split the two Prosecco questions)
- Rule-outs:
  - Given Q2 is the Prosecco pair, Q1 is almost certainly Champagne
  - If both wines show fine mousse, autolysis, chalk minerality, and 12% ABV — it's Champagne
- Conclusion: Both Champagne — NV large-house (Perrier-Jouët Grand Brut) vs vintage grower (Egly-Ouriet 2007 Grand Cru Millésime)
