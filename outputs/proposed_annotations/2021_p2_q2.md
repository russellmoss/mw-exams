# Proposed annotation for 2021 Paper 2 Question 2

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 4-6 are each made from a different single grape variety. Each wine is from a different continent. a) Identify the variety of each wine (3x5) b) Identify the country and region of each wine (3x10) c) Comment on the style and commercial appeal of each wine (3x10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W4: Pinot Noir, Flowers, 2018. Sonoma Coast. (14.1%)
- W5: Barbaresco, Produttori del Barbaresco, 2017. Piedmont. (14.5%)
- W6: Grenache The Custodian, d'Arenberg, 2016. McLaren Vale. (14.5%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of single-variety reds from three different continents and your ability to distinguish them by variety, origin, and commercial positioning. "Different variety, different continent" is a classic MW framing — it's essentially asking can you taste the difference between, say, Pinot Noir, Nebbiolo, and Grenache, and can you place each globally.

The three top-tier candidates for this format on Paper 2 are Cabernet Sauvignon, Pinot Noir, Syrah/Shiraz, Tempranillo, and Grenache — any of which could land in Europe, the Americas, and Oceania. The variety ID is only worth 15 marks total, which suggests these aren't obscure grapes; the examiner wants to know if you understand stylistic and commercial differences.

Flowers Sonoma Coast Pinot Noir is North America — cool climate, relatively restrained, elegant for California. Produttori del Barbaresco is the cooperative-driven benchmark for Nebbiolo in Piedmont — high acidity, firm tannin, tarry and floral. D'Arenberg Custodian is old-vine McLaren Vale Grenache — warm, spicy, plush, classic Australian style. The commercial contrast is stark: Barbaresco is fine wine with auction relevance; Custodian is accessible quality and value; Flowers sits in the premium-but-accessible US Pinot tier.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Different variety, different continent" = three distinct styles, probably recognizable varieties
  - Style and commercial appeal weighted at 30 marks = the commercial angle is key
- Universe I considered:
  - North America: Pinot Noir (Sonoma/Oregon), Cab (Napa/Sonoma), Zinfandel (CA)
  - Europe: Nebbiolo (Piedmont), Tempranillo (Rioja), Pinot Noir (Burgundy), Cabernet (Bordeaux)
  - Oceania/Australia: Shiraz (Barossa/McLaren Vale), Grenache (McLaren Vale), Cabernet (Coonawarra)
- Rule-outs:
  - Blends would violate "single grape variety" stem
  - South America continent would displace one of the above three continents — here it's N America, Europe, Australia
- Conclusion: Pinot/Nebbiolo/Grenache confirmed across three continents. Nebbiolo from Produttori is a great MW wine — cooperative benchmark, widely studied.
