# Proposed annotation for 2021 Paper 3 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 9-10 and 11-12 are two pairs. Each pair is from a different region of origin. a) Identify the origin of each pair (2x10) b) Compare the style, quality and commercial position of each pair (2x20) c) Describe the method of production of each pair (2x10)

## Wines actually used (for the user's reference — DO NOT include this in the eventual annotation)
- W9: Oloroso Pata de Gallina, Lustau, NV. Jerez. (20%)
- W10: Matusalem Oloroso Dulce 30yr, González Byass, NV. Jerez. (20.5%)
- W11: Full Rich 3yr, Henriques & Henriques. Madeira. (19%)
- W12: Single Harvest Boal, H&H, 2000. Madeira. (20%)

## Proposed annotation

This is testing your knowledge of the two great Iberian fortified wines — Sherry and Madeira — and your ability to distinguish them both between regions and within each pair's quality tiers.

The two-pair structure here is perfect for this comparison: both Jerez and Madeira produce dry and sweet styles from multiple grapes, and both involve oxidative aging. The key discriminations are: Sherry (W9/W10) vs Madeira (W11/W12), and within each region, basic/NV vs premium aged/vintage.

The Jerez pair is Lustau Oloroso Pata de Gallina (dry Oloroso, nutty, dried fig, very recognizable) vs González Byass Matusalem (a sweetened Oloroso using PX, 30-year average age, extremely concentrated, syrupy but with that characteristic acetaldehyde backbone). The Madeira pair is H&H Full Rich 3-year NV (cheaper, blended Madeira, made from Tinta Negra, the workhorse grape) vs H&H Single Harvest Boal 2000 (vintage Madeira, specific noble variety, 20+ years of estufagem and barrel aging).

The commercial contrast within each pair is sharp: Lustau Pata de Gallina is specialist retail with strong fine dining trade; Matusalem is a luxury collector item. H&H Full Rich is accessible export-market Madeira; the Single Harvest Boal is serious fine wine at auction prices.

Both regions use solera or oxidative aging, but the mechanism differs — Sherry solera vs Madeira's estufagem and canteiro. That's the key production point.

## Reasoning trace (for the user's reference — DO NOT include in final annotation)

- Stem signals I picked up on:
  - "Two pairs, different regions" + Paper 3 fortified context = two classic oxidative fortified wine regions
  - Production method asked (2x10) = the examiner wants you to distinguish solera from estufagem/canteiro
  - Style/quality/commercial (2x20) = within-pair quality tiers are central
- Universe I considered:
  - Jerez: Fino, Amontillado, Oloroso, PX — the range is broad
  - Madeira: Sercial, Verdelho, Boal, Malmsey — style range from dry to sweet
  - Port: possible but Port more often appears as a standalone or paired with Madeira
  - Marsala: unlikely at this level
- Rule-outs:
  - Vin Jaune: no fortification, would not pair with Madeira in a "fortified pairs" question
  - Rutherglen: already appeared in Q2 of this paper
- Conclusion: Sherry and Madeira confirmed. The within-pair quality gradient (simple NV vs aged/vintage) is the core teaching point.
