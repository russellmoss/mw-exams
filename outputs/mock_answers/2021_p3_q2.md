## a) Comment on the method of production with reference to residual sugar and alcohol levels.
Wine 4
Appassimento — grapes harvested early October, dried on bamboo racks or wooden crates for 3-4 months, losing 35-45% weight; fermentation then lasts up to 50 days  Minimum 2 years in large oak (Slavonian botti or French tonneaux) before release; often longer
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still dry, tannic structured, neutral old style.
Wine 5
Charmat (tank) method for spumante style, or natural refermentation for frizzante; arrested fermentation preserves sweetness and low alcohol  Tank; early bottling; consumed fresh
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the sparkling, sweet, dessert style.
Wine 6
Rapid extraction in granite lagares or modern robotic lagares; fortification with neutral grape spirit (aguardente) mid-fermentation when ~90 g/L RS remains, raising ABV to 20%  Typically 2 years in large old wood (lodge or at Douro estate) before bottling; thereafter all aging in bottle for 20+ years
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, sweet, full rich, neutral old style.
Wine 7
Partial fermentation arrested by mutage — addition of neutral spirit mid-fermentation (VDN = vin doux naturel method, developed by Arnaldus de Villa Nova in late 13th C)  Aged in large old oak or concrete; "Grande Reserve" NV suggests extended aging/blending — potentially oxidative aging component
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, sweet, full rich, neutral old style.
Wine 8
Harvested very late for maximum concentration and sugar; partial fermentation then fortification with neutral spirit  Fractional blending (solera-like) system in small old oak barrels exposed to temperature extremes; the "Classic" tier represents average 12 years of barrel aging
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, very sweet, dessert, neutral old style.
