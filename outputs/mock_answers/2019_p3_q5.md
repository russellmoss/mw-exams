# Mock answer - 2019 Paper 3 Question 5

## a) Compare and contrast the method of production.
Wine 11
Grapes partially dried (appassimento) on the island in the intense Mediterranean sun; then macerated with fresh Zibibbo grapes to extract concentrated juice; fermentation stops naturally when ABV and RS are in balance  Stainless steel; no oak; bottled to preserve freshness and aromatic intensity
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still sweet, sweet, dessert style.
Wine 12
Grapes are late-harvested or semi-raisined; fermentation stopped by addition of grape spirit (fortification) at approximately 17% ABV, leaving very high residual sugar  Solera-blended fractional blending system in old American and/or French oak barrels; average age of blend varies by tier (Rutherglen < Classic < Grand < Rare — classification system introduced 1998). "Classic Rutherglen Muscat" listed at $40/500ml (source: Campbells website)
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, very sweet, dessert, neutral old style.
