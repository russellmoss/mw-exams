## c) Discuss the method of production.
Wine 4
Manual harvesting; fermentation in temperature-controlled wooden vats; traditional pump-over and pump-down regime  24 months in Muga's own oak casks (80% French, 20% American origin), made in-house by the bodega's own cooper workshop; minimum 12 months in bottle before release (source: bodegasmuga.com/en/muga/)
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still dry, medium balanced, mixed style.
Wine 5
Machine or hand harvested; fermentation in stainless steel with extended maceration; pump-overs  Mix of American and French oak, predominantly older barrels for Portrait range (Portrait is the accessible commercial tier below Masters and Stonewell); 12-18 months typical
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still dry, full rich, mixed style.
Wine 6
Semi-carbonic maceration or whole-berry fermentation; short maceration to preserve freshness and aromatics  Stainless steel or older large wood; minimal or no new oak aging; released early
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still dry, light crisp style.
