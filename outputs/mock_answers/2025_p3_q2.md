## b) Comment on the winemaking techniques.
Wine 4
Each variety fermented separately in stainless steel vats. Malolactic fermentation completed. Blended, then second fermentation in bottle (methode traditionnelle).  Minimum 24 months on lees in bottle (Reserva classification)
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the sparkling, medium balanced style.
Wine 5
Hand-harvested, natural yeast fermentation, long maceration (adapted shorter in 2022), partly fermented with less skin contact  9 months in French oak vats (large format, neutral)
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still dry, bone dry, medium balanced, neutral old style.
Wine 6
Oxidative ageing under flor-free conditions in a solera system.
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, bone dry, full rich, neutral old style.
## c) Comment on the quality and commercial position.
Wine 4
outstanding quality in context: balance/freshness, length, complexity, typicity.
Wine 5
outstanding quality in context: balance/freshness, concentration, oak handling, ageing capacity. It is young but developing; it should improve in bottle in the medium term.
Wine 6
good quality at best, more commercial than profound in context: balance/freshness, length, complexity, oak handling. It is already mature in style rather than youthful; it has the structure to improve further and hold in the long term.
