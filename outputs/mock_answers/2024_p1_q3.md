## b) Comment on the method of production with reference to the use of oak.
Wine 10
manual harvest, long slow whole-bunch pressing, indigenous yeast  10 months on full lees in used demi-muids, barrels and sandstone eggs, then 2 months in stainless steel
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still dry, medium balanced, neutral old style.
Wine 11
white wine vinified 50% in stainless steel and 50% in new French oak barrels; no malolactic fermentation  12 months in French oak, with total ageing stated as 16-18 months depending on the vintage style
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still dry, full rich, new french style.
Wine 12
manual harvest and sorting; pressing and clarification before vinification 70% in wood and 30% in stainless steel  9 months, 70% in wood (barrels and demi-muids)
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still dry, full rich, mixed style.
