## d) From what you can detect in the glass, describe the method of production from vineyard to bottle.
Wine 9
fermented in Fuder (old large oak casks) or stainless steel; minimal intervention; no malo (not typical in Mosel); residual sugar left by stopping fermentation
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still sweet, off dry, light crisp style.
Wine 10
grapes naturally frozen on vine at ≤−8°C; harvested and pressed while frozen; the water remains frozen, yielding concentrated juice (typically ≤35° Brix minimum by VQA regulations); slow cold fermentation
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still sweet, very sweet, dessert style.
Wine 11
aszú berries individually hand-harvested; trampled into paste (göncölszekér historically); must or base wine macerated on the paste; transferred to göncii oak barrels for fermentation and aging  typically 2–3 years in old Hungarian oak casks before release (Source needed — exact for Royal Tokaji 2008)
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still sweet, very sweet, dessert, neutral old style.
Wine 12
grapes dried on racks for approximately 3 months ("Malvasia and Trebbiano grapes dry on racks for about three months; they are then soft-pressed and slowly fermented" — source: isoleeolena.it)  11 years in small sealed wooden vessels (caratelli) of various sizes — confirmed from producer website; wine maintains contact with madre (mother yeast) throughout aging
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still sweet, sweet, dessert, neutral old style.
