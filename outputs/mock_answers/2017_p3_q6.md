## b) Comment on the method of production with reference to the wine’s quality.
Wine 11
Base wine fermented dry; fortified to ~18% to kill flor; then aged as Palo Cortado. Note: True Palo Cortado arises when a wine that started ageing biologically (under flor, like Fino/Amontillado) spontaneously loses its flor veil and develops oxidatively — a rare and unpredictable event (this is the classic definition; some producers create PC deliberately by fortifying higher and then adding older wines)  Criadera y solera system (fractional blending between multiple barrels over multiple years); the "Viejo" (old) and "CP" designation signals an aged, complex Sherry from an established solera; likely average age >30 years (Source needed for exact average solera age)
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, full rich, neutral old style.
Wine 12
Mutage — arrested fermentation by addition of grape spirit (neutral alcohol) to approximately 95 g/L potential RS retained; fortified at ~6–8% ABV during fermentation  Extended oxidative ageing in old barrels, demi-muids, or bonbonnes (glass demijohns exposed to oxygen and temperature variation) — the "Grande Réserve" designation implies significant ageing, likely 10+ years average; NV solera-style blending
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, very sweet, full rich, neutral old style.
