## c) Comment on the method of production.
Wine 9
Fermented to dryness; fortified to 17-18% (flor cannot survive above ~15.5%), suppressing development of flor; placed into oxidative aging from the start  Criadera and solera (fractional blending system) in old American oak butts; no flor; full oxidative aging
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, full rich, neutral old style.
Wine 10
As per Oloroso (flor suppressed, oxidative aging); then blended with PX for sweetness  30+ year average age in criadera-solera in old American oak; VORS (Vinum Optimum Rara Signatum) category = "Very Old Rare Sherry" min average 30 years
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, sweet, dessert, neutral old style.
Wine 11
Partial fermentation then fortification; then heated via estufagem (artificial heating in tanks or pipes at ~45°C for 3 months) for younger commercial Madeiras  Minimum 3 years (hence "3 y.o.") in old wood (oak or chestnut)
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, sweet, full rich, neutral old style.
Wine 12
Partial fermentation; fortification with grape spirit; aged via canteiro (passive natural warming) rather than estufagem — the premium aging method  Single Harvest (Colheita) requires minimum 5 years aging; premium bottlings age much longer in old wood under canteiro; this 2000 would have been aged 20+ years
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, medium, full rich, neutral old style.
