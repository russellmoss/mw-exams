## b) Comment on the key winemaking techniques used.
Wine 1
Traditional méthode champenoise; 8-10 years minimum aging before release (Dom Pérignon was released c.2007-2009)  Extended time on lees in bottle creates autolytic complexity; disgorged to brut dosage
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the sparkling, full rich style.
Wine 2
Méthode champenoise; 8-10 years lees aging before first release c.2017  Extended bottle aging; low dosage brut
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the sparkling, full rich style.
Wine 3
Botrytis-affected harvested grapes; multiple selective passes (tries); fermented in French oak  New French oak barrels; extended aging; approximately 4,500 cases/year for the Grand Vin
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still sweet, very sweet, dessert, new french style.
Wine 4
Selective botrytised harvest (tries); French oak fermentation  New French oak; ~4,500 cases grand vin/year
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the still sweet, very sweet, dessert, new french style.
Wine 5
Fermentation halted by addition of grape spirit (aguardente) at approximately 77 g/L RS; colour and tannin extraction via treading (lagares) or automated vinification  2 years in large oak pipes in Vila Nova de Gaia before bottling; then decades of bottle aging (design for 20-50 year cellaring)
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, very sweet, tannic structured style.
Wine 6
Grape spirit addition arresting fermentation; extraction in lagares (traditional foot treading) or modern pneumatic methods  2 years maximum in large oak pipes (Vila Nova de Gaia) before bottling; designed for 30-50 year cellaring
These are the decisive production choices because they explain the fortified, very sweet, tannic structured style.
