a) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (2 x 10 marks)

## Paper context
Paper 3 paired questions usually compare two expressions of the same broad family within one region, then repeat the exercise in another region.

## Rule-outs
- Unlikely one pair of sparkling and one pair of rose; production would be too broad and less pair-comparable
- Unlikely both pairs from the same country but unrelated categories, because the question says each pair is from a different region, not merely different wines
- Unlikely fresh table wines; pairwise commercial-position discussion is more natural with fortified categories that span clear quality tiers

## IMW's likely intent
The examiner wants comparison within region, not just recognition of a category. A strong answer would explain why one wine is entry-level or sweeter/softer while the other is rarer, older, drier, or more prestigious. Production marks force the candidate to discuss solera, fortification timing, oxidative versus biological handling, estufagem or canteiro, and perhaps the role of age statement versus actual profile.

## What to focus on in the glass

- Madeira adds volatility, lifted acidity, and a cooked/cask-aged profile
- Commercial position should be contrasted inside each pair: by-the-glass versus collector, mixing versus sipping, entry versus age-statement prestige
