## IMW's likely intent
This question tests the candidate's ability to **identify a variety through two different stylistic lenses** and then articulate how winemaking choices (oak, lees, fermentation temperature, malo) create those different expressions. The "different countries" constraint adds a terroir/climate overlay. With winemaking worth 20 of 50 marks, the IMW is specifically testing production knowledge -- the candidate needs to go beyond "this wine has oak" to "this wine was likely barrel-fermented in 228L barriques with partial malo and batonnage."

## What to focus on in the glass
- Aromatic profile: primary fruit character vs. secondary winemaking aromas (butter, toast, yeast)
- Acidity structure: cool climate (higher acid, leaner) vs. warm climate (riper, broader)
- Oak markers: vanilla, toast, cedar -- if present, estimate type and duration
- Texture: lees influence (creaminess, weight), malo (softened acidity)
- Alcohol: can help place climate/country
- Finish: mineral vs. fruit-driven persistence
