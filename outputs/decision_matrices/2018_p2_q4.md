# Decision matrix — 2018 Paper 2 Question 4

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 11 and 12 come from the Americas.

For each wine:

a) Identify the grape variety(ies) used. (2 x 5 marks)

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (2 x 10 marks)

c) Discuss quality in relation to the region of origin. (2 x 10 marks)

## Paper context
Paper 2 = red wines. Two wines from the Americas. This is the closing question — typically the IMW places less familiar or more challenging wines at the end of a paper.

## Stem signals
- **"The Americas"** — North America (USA, Canada, Mexico) or South America (Argentina, Chile, Uruguay, Brazil). Broad geographic scope.
- **"Grape variety(ies)"** — the parenthetical plural suggests at least one may be a blend or a varietal associated with blending, though both could be single varietals
- **Only 5 marks per variety** — low weighting implies the IMW does not expect these to be easy variety calls, OR that getting the variety is less important than origin + quality context
- **10 marks per origin** — the IMW wants sub-regional specificity (not just "California" but "Napa" or "Russian River")
- **"Quality in relation to the region"** — testing knowledge of which American regions produce which quality levels

## IMW's likely intent
Testing knowledge of distinctly American varieties and regions. The low variety marks (5 each) and high origin marks (10 each) suggest the IMW expects candidates to know the regional context deeply — not just "this is Zinfandel" but "this is old-vine Zinfandel from Lodi, a warm inland region that produces ripe, high-alcohol wines at mid-range price points." The quality-in-context framing rewards candidates who can place a wine within its regional hierarchy.

## What to focus on in the glass
- **Alcohol**: Zinfandel often 14.5%+, Malbec 14-15%, Chilean Cab 13.5-14.5%
- **Fruit character**: jammy/brambly (Zinfandel), plum/violet (Malbec), cassis/eucalyptus (Chilean Cab), green pepper (Carmenere)
- **Oak**: American oak (coconut, dill) vs. French oak (toast, cedar)
- **Tannin**: moderate soft (Malbec), grippy (Zinfandel), structured (Cab Sauv)
- **Spice notes**: black pepper, cinnamon (Zinfandel), sweet spice (Malbec)
- **Colour**: deep purple-black (young Malbec) vs. brick-tinged (aged Zinfandel)
