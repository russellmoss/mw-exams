# Decision matrix -- 2017 Paper 3 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 5-6 are labelled as different single grape varieties.

For each wine:

a) Identify the grape variety. (2 x 8 marks)

b) State the level of residual sugar (g/l) and level of alcohol (%). (2 x 4 marks)

c) Discuss the quality and commercial potential. (2 x 13 marks)

## Paper context
Paper 3 = mixed bag. Two wines from different single varieties. The residual sugar and alcohol questions strongly indicate these are off-dry to sweet wines or wines with atypical sugar/alcohol profiles.

## Stem signals
- **Different single grape varieties** -- two distinct grapes
- **"Labelled as" different varieties** -- interesting phrasing; the label declares the variety
- **Variety ID at 8 marks each** -- moderate; these are identifiable grapes
- **Residual sugar (g/l) and alcohol (%) at 4 marks each** -- the candidate must quantify sweetness and alcohol precisely; this is a P3 hallmark for sweet, off-dry, or low-alcohol wines
- **Quality and commercial potential at 13 marks each** -- heavily weighted; commercial viability is the primary concern

## Universe
Two single-variety wines where residual sugar and alcohol level are explicitly tested. This points to wines that are NOT dry, or have unusual alcohol levels:
- Off-dry whites (Riesling, Gewurztraminer, Muscat, Chenin Blanc)
- Late harvest wines
- Low-alcohol wines (Moscato d'Asti style)
- Wines where the sugar/alcohol balance is distinctive and testable

## Candidate varieties (confidence tiers)
- **Riesling** (off-dry or sweet): **STRONG SIGNAL** -- Riesling Spatlese, Kabinett, or similar; residual sugar varies widely; alcohol can be 7-13%
- **Gewurztraminer** (late harvest or off-dry): **STRONG SIGNAL** -- often has residual sugar, distinctive aromatics, higher alcohol possible
- **Muscat/Moscato**: **PLAUSIBLE** -- Moscato d'Asti is low alcohol, sweet; Muscat de Beaumes-de-Venise is fortified sweet
- **Chenin Blanc** (demi-sec): **PLAUSIBLE** -- Vouvray demi-sec style, residual sugar present
- **Viognier**: **CURVEBALL** -- sometimes off-dry, high alcohol

## Candidate origins per slot
- Wine 5: Germany or NZ (Riesling, off-dry, moderate to low alcohol)
- Wine 6: Alsace or California (Gewurztraminer, late harvest, moderate alcohol)

## Rule-outs
- Unlikely two dry wines -- the RS/alcohol question would be pointless for fully dry wines
- Unlikely fortified wines -- those would be in a separate fortified question
- Unlikely mainstream Chardonnay or Sauvignon Blanc -- these are not associated with RS testing

## IMW's likely intent
Testing the candidate's ability to estimate residual sugar and alcohol -- practical tasting skills that are essential for MW candidates. The two varieties likely differ in their sugar/alcohol profiles, creating a contrast (e.g., one is a light, sweet Riesling at 10% and 40g/l RS, vs a richer, late-harvest Gewurztraminer at 11% and 80g/l RS). The commercial question tests whether the candidate understands the market for off-dry and sweet wines -- a category that is commercially significant but often dismissed.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Sweetness level: taste for residual sugar and try to quantify (30-60g/l for off-dry, 60-120g/l for sweet)
- Alcohol: gauge by heat on the finish and body
- Varietal aromatics: petrol/lime (Riesling), lychee/rose (Gewurztraminer), grapey/floral (Muscat)
- Acidity balance: high acid + RS = Riesling; lower acid + RS = Gewurztraminer
- Color: pale green-gold (Riesling) vs deep gold (late harvest Gewurz)
