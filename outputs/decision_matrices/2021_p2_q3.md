# Decision matrix -- 2021 Paper 2 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 7-8 are made from different single grape varieties. Each wine is from a different country in the Americas.

For both wines:

a) Compare and contrast the styles and consumer appeal. (30 marks)

For each wine:

b) Identify the variety and origin as closely as possible. (2 x 10 marks)

## Paper context
Paper 2 = red wines only. Two reds, different single varieties, different countries in the Americas.

## IMW's likely intent
The 30-mark style/consumer appeal comparison is the heart of this question. The IMW wants candidates to articulate how two different Americas wines appeal to different consumers. For example: Zinfandel's bold fruit and high alcohol appeals to domestic US consumers in casual dining; Carmenere's herbal complexity appeals to value-seeking international markets. Or: Petite Sirah's density and tannin vs. Malbec's plush approachability. The IMW is testing commercial wine awareness as much as palate. The low marks for identification (10 each) suggest the varieties might be less mainstream -- perhaps Petite Sirah or Tannat rather than the most obvious choices.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Pyrazine/green bell pepper: signature Carmenere marker
- Bramble fruit + spice + high alcohol: Zinfandel marker
- Plum + violet + plush tannin: Malbec marker
- Dense black fruit + massive tannin + ink: Petite Sirah marker
- Dried herb + dark cherry + firm tannin: Tannat marker
- Alcohol level: Zinfandel often 14.5-16%; Carmenere 14-14.5%; Malbec 14-15%
- Oak integration: American oak (coconut, dill) vs French oak (cedar, vanilla)
- Finish: hot alcohol = Zinfandel; green/herbal finish = Carmenere
