## IMW's likely intent
Testing within-region quality discrimination: given the same grape and the same region, can the candidate articulate what separates a basic producer from a premium one? This tests knowledge of sub-regional hierarchy, producer philosophy, and how winemaking choices at different quality ambitions manifest in the glass. The IMW wants to see that candidates can navigate quality tiers, not just variety and country.

## What to focus on in the glass
- **Concentration**: depth of color, weight on palate
- **Oak quality**: new French barrels (premium) vs. older/cheaper oak (basic)
- **Fruit purity vs. complexity**: simple varietal character vs. layered secondary flavors
- **Tannin quality**: fine-grained and integrated (premium) vs. coarser or simpler (basic)
- **Length and finish**: persistent finish = higher quality
- **Alcohol balance**: both should be similar ABV if from the same region; differences suggest different ambitions
