## Rule-outs

- Very obscure varieties: the question needs the candidate to assess commercial position, which requires recognizable categories

## IMW's likely intent
Testing the candidate's ability to taste and articulate oak influence. The IMW wants nuanced commentary: not just "this has oak" but what type, how much, new vs. used, how it interacts with fruit, whether it elevates or overwhelms the wine. The commercial position component tests market awareness -- where does each wine compete, at what price point, through what channels?

## What to focus on in the glass
- **Oak character**: vanilla/coconut/dill (American oak), cedar/smoke/spice (French oak), absence of oak (tank or old wood)
- **Oak integration**: well-integrated (the oak serves the wine) vs. dominant (oak overwhelms fruit)
- **Tannin type**: silky/fine (French oak) vs. firm/grainy (American oak) vs. fruity/soft (no new oak)
- **Fruit quality and concentration**: does the fruit quality match the oak level?
- **Price signal**: heavy new oak + concentrated fruit = premium aspirations; simple fruit + obvious oak = mid-range; no oak + simple = entry level
- **Age indicators**: developed oak character (tertiary cedar, cigar box) vs. primary oak sweetness (vanilla, toast)
