## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-3 are from different countries and are each made from a different, single grape variety. Wine 4 is a blend of all three of these varieties.

For each wine 1-3:

a) Identify the grape variety and the origin as closely as possible. (3 x 15 marks)

b) Comment on the style, considering possible reasons for not blending the variety used for this wine. (3 x 10 marks)

For wine 4:

c) Comment on the purpose of blending these varieties with reference to balance and quality. (15 marks)

d) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (10 marks)

## IMW's likely intent
This question is a masterclass in blending theory. The IMW tests: (1) Can you identify three Bordeaux varieties in their single-variety expressions from different countries? (2) Do you understand what each variety contributes — structure (Cab Sauv), flesh (Merlot), aromatics/freshness (Cab Franc)? (3) Can you articulate why these varieties work alone AND together? This is arguably the most pedagogically rich question structure in the red paper.

## What to focus on in the glass

- **Cab Sauvignon markers**: blackcurrant/cassis, cedar, firm tannins, full body, eucalyptus
- **Merlot markers**: plum, black cherry, softer tannins, rounder mouthfeel, chocolate
- **Bordeaux blend (W4)**: look for complexity > any single variety, integrated tannins, multiple fruit layers
- **Alcohol levels**: Cab Franc from Loire = lower (12.5-13%); Cab Sauv from Napa = higher (14-15%)
