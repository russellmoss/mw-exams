## IMW's likely intent
This is fundamentally a commercial analysis question dressed as a tasting question. The IMW wants candidates to understand the business pressures on regions that traditionally produced sweet wines but are now diversifying into dry styles. The 30-mark commercial question is the giveaway — the candidate must discuss declining global demand for sweet wines, the economics of botrytis production (risk, yield loss, high costs), climate change impacts on botrytis formation, and the strategic pivot to dry wines as a survival mechanism. The quality and maturity sub-questions anchor this commercial discussion in actual tasting evidence.

## What to focus on in the glass
- **Sweetness spectrum**: dry to lusciously sweet — residual sugar assessment is critical
- **Botrytis markers**: honey, dried apricot, marmalade, ginger, noble rot complexity
- **Colour progression**: pale gold (dry/young) to deep gold/amber (aged sweet)
- **Acidity**: essential in sweet wines for balance; higher acid = longer ageing potential
- **Alcohol**: dry white likely 12-13%, sweet wines can range from 13-15% (Sauternes) or lower (German)
- **Concentration**: viscosity, extract, length of finish — quality markers in sweet wine
- **Oak influence**: some Sauternes see new oak; dry whites may be barrel-fermented
