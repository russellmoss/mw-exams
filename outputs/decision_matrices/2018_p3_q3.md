For each wine:

a) Identify the predominant grape variety used. (3 x 5 marks)

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (3 x 8 marks)

c) Comment on the style, quality and commercial potential. (3 x 12 marks)

## IMW's likely intent
The examiner is testing whether the candidate understands grape identity separately from the grape's home region. It is also a commercial question: can these Rhone grapes succeed outside the Rhone, and in what market segment? The best answers will explain whether a wine feels varietally convincing, whether the region is a natural fit, and whether the result is niche sommelier interest or broader commercial opportunity.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Determine whether each wine is red or white, then build the likely Rhone-variety shortlist
- Use tannin, color depth, alcohol, and aromatic profile to split Grenache, Mourvedre, Cinsault, and Syrah

- Commercial potential should address whether the grape is already accepted in that country or still a specialist proposition
