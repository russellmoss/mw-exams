## IMW's likely intent

This is a winemaking knowledge question more than a geography question. The candidate who can name the technique and explain its impact on flavour, texture, and structure will score well.

## What to focus on in the glass
- **Colour**: rosato/pink (rose), golden (sweet/oxidative), pale/green (sparkling)
- **Bubbles**: presence, size, persistence indicate method (fine/persistent = traditional; frothy = Charmat)
- **Sweetness level**: dry (rose, still) vs. off-dry vs. sweet
- **Oxidative markers**: nutty, bruised apple, toffee = flor or oxidative ageing
- **Fruit freshness vs. secondary/tertiary character**: fresh fruit = young/tank/direct press; developed = ageing/oak/oxidation
- **Alcohol level**: fortified wines will be noticeably higher (15-20%+)
