## IMW's likely intent
This is a method-recognition question. The examiner wants the candidate to explain how sugar and alcohol levels arise, not just observe that the wines are sweet. The strongest answers will link sensory evidence to process: high alcohol plus sweetness suggests fortification; very low alcohol plus sweetness suggests arrested ferment; richness without spirit may suggest appassimento. Origin and grape matter because each classic style has a canonical home and variety set.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Estimate alcohol before origin
- Ask whether sweetness feels natural, concentrated, or spirit-supported
- Mousse or slight spritz would point toward lightly sparkling aromatic wines
- Raisined, figgy, or prune notes suggest dried-grape methods
- Grapey, floral, sticky, or rancio notes help split Muscat-family wines from Port- or Maury-like wines
- Quality should be judged within style, not against dry table-wine norms
