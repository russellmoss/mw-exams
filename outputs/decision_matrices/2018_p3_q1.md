## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-6 are presented as pairs, 1 and 2, 3 and 4, and 5 and 6. Each pair is made by a different single producer.

For each pair:

a) Identify the origin, as closely as possible. (3 x 10 marks)

b) Comment on the key winemaking techniques used. (3 x 12 marks)

c) Identify the vintage(s) and consider its/their key characteristics. (3 x 10 marks)

d) Discuss the quality and maturity of the wines. (3 x 18 marks)

## IMW's likely intent
This is the ultimate "identify the house, the vintage, and assess maturity" question. The IMW is testing: (1) recognition of prestige producers, (2) vintage knowledge across three different wine categories, (3) the ability to assess maturity and quality simultaneously, and (4) understanding of the winemaking techniques that define each category (riddling and dosage in Champagne, botrytis selection and barrel fermentation in Sauternes, fortification timing and barrel ageing in Port). The 18-mark quality/maturity weighting means the IMW values nuanced ageing assessments above all else.

## What to focus on in the glass
- **Champagne pair**: mousse quality, autolytic complexity (brioche, toast), oxidative development, fruit freshness vs. tertiary character, dosage level
- **Sweet wine pair**: botrytis markers (marmalade, honey, dried apricot), acidity balance, colour (deep gold vs. amber), viscosity, sugar integration
- **Port pair**: colour depth (ruby-purple vs. tawny rim), fruit vs. dried fruit/nuts, tannin resolution, spirit integration, oxidative vs. reductive ageing character
- **Across all pairs**: younger wine should be more primary/tight; older wine should show development/integration
