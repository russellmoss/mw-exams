# Decision matrix — 2018 Paper 2 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-5 all come from classic Western European origins.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin as closely as possible making reference to the grape variety(ies) used. (5 x 10 marks)

b) Discuss quality within the context of the region of origin. (5 x 8 marks)

c) What has the winemaker done to maximise quality and regional typicity during the winemaking process? (5 x 7 marks)

Five wines across these regions. The IMW likely wants diversity — not two from the same country if possible, or at most two from France (which has the most distinct red styles).

## IMW's likely intent
This is a foundational competency test: can the candidate identify five of Europe's greatest red wine regions, articulate what makes each region's winemaking philosophy distinctive (extended maceration in Barolo, American vs. French oak in Rioja, assemblage in Bordeaux, whole-cluster in Burgundy), and assess quality relative to the regional benchmark? The "regional typicity" emphasis means the IMW selected wines that are textbook examples of their origin.

## What to focus on in the glass
- **Tannin structure**: fine-grained (Burgundy), firm/angular (Barolo), ripe/integrated (Bordeaux), dusty (Rioja)
- **Acidity**: high (Nebbiolo, Sangiovese) vs. moderate (Bordeaux, Rioja)
- **Oak signature**: American oak vanilla/coconut (Rioja), French oak cedar/toast (Bordeaux, Barolo modern), neutral/no new oak (Burgundy traditional)
- **Fruit profile**: red fruit/earth (Pinot, Sangiovese, Tempranillo), dark fruit/cassis (Cabernet), dark fruit/pepper (Syrah), tar/roses (Nebbiolo)
- **Maturity markers**: brick rim (aged Nebbiolo, Rioja GR), still purple (young Syrah)
- **Alcohol**: moderate 12.5-13% (Burgundy, traditional Rioja) vs. higher 14-14.5% (modern Barolo, Southern French)
