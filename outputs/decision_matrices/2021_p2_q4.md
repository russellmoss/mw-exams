## IMW's likely intent
The pair structure tests whether the candidate can identify a region from two wines and then articulate what differentiates quality levels within that region. The "method of production" component (within the 20-mark comparison) means the wines may differ in oak treatment, maceration time, blend composition, or aging duration. The "drinking window" question (5 marks per wine) is specifically testing whether the candidate can assess tannic structure, acidity, and fruit concentration to predict aging trajectory. Rioja is ideal for this because the classification system (Crianza/Reserva/Gran Reserva) literally defines aging windows, and the style difference between a young Garnacha-forward Rioja and an old Tempranillo-dominated Gran Reserva is dramatic.

- Pair 2: Look for common grape/regional character and then assess oak/age differences

- Specifically for drinking window: high acid + high tannin + concentrated fruit = long window; soft tannin + forward fruit = drink now
