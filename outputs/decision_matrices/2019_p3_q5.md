# Decision matrix -- 2019 Paper 3 Question 5

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 11-12 come from two different countries.

For both wines:

a) Compare and contrast the method of production. (14 marks)

For each wine:

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (2 x 7 marks)

c) Comment on quality and maturity. (2 x 7 marks)

d) State the level of residual sugar (g/l). (2 x 2 marks)

e) State the level of alcohol (%). (2 x 2 marks)

## Rule-outs

## IMW's likely intent
This is a sweetness-method question disguised as a two-wine comparison. The examiner wants the candidate to distinguish concentrated naturally sweet wine from spirit-fortified sweetness, then tie that difference to alcohol level, texture, maturity trajectory, and commercial use. The best answer will compare vineyard concentration, drying, fermentation management, and fortification rather than just saying one is sweeter than the other.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Alcohol first: table-strength sweet wine versus spirit-supported sweet wine
- Aromatics: raisined apricot and dried citrus point one way; grapey, spirituous, and rancio Muscat notes another
- Texture and finish can help estimate RS
- Maturity cues in sweet wines include color deepening, caramel, coffee, fig, marmalade, and nutty development
- Commercial position should consider gifting, dessert pairing, independent retail, and after-dinner service
