Wines 4-6 are each made from a different single grape variety. Each wine is from a different continent.

For each wine:

a) Identify the grape variety. (3 x 5 marks)

b) Identify the country and region as closely as possible. (3 x 10 marks)

c) Comment on the style and commercial appeal. (3 x 10 marks)

Paper 2 = red wines only. Three reds, each a different single variety, each from a different continent.

## IMW's likely intent
Three different single varieties from three continents tests breadth of tasting experience. The 5-mark variety identification is intentionally low, suggesting either the varieties are identifiable or that the IMW wants candidates to focus more on commercial appeal (10 marks) than on nailing the grape. The commercial appeal question tests whether the candidate knows who buys these wines -- mass market, specialist, restaurant, export market. This is a "world tour" question designed to see if the candidate can taste globally.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Varietal markers: Pinot Noir (red fruit, transparency, lower tannin), Nebbiolo (tar, rose, high acid+tannin), Malbec (plum, violet, plush texture), Grenache (red fruit, spice, warmth), Syrah (pepper, dark fruit, savory)
- Climate signatures: warm = ripe fruit, higher alcohol; cool = herbaceous, lower alcohol, higher acid
- Oak treatment: new French oak (vanilla, toast) vs older/neutral (less oak influence)
- Tannin structure: fine-grained vs coarse; resolved vs grippy
- Color: transparency (Pinot, Nebbiolo) vs opacity (Malbec, Syrah)
- Alcohol: under 13% = cool climate; 14%+ = warm climate
