# Decision matrix — 2024 Paper 2 Question 3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 8-12 are from the same single variety and five different countries.

With reference to all five wines:

a) Identify the grape variety. (25 marks)

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (5 x 5 marks)

c) Discuss how climate and winemaking techniques have influenced the quality and style of the wine. (5 x 15 marks)

## Paper context
Paper 2 = red wines only. Five reds, same single variety, five different countries.

## Stem signals
- **Same single variety, five different countries** — this is the strongest possible signal. Very few red grapes are grown as single-variety wines in five or more countries at a level worthy of the MW exam.
- **Variety ID is worth 25 marks** — massive. They expect you to get this right, and they want detailed justification across all five wines.
- **Origin is only 5 marks per wine** — getting the country right is enough; sub-regional specificity matters less
- **Climate and winemaking** is the heavyweight (15 marks per wine, 75 total) — the question is fundamentally about how one grape expresses differently across climates and winemaking philosophies
- The user's annotation: "Five single variety wines from five different countries points directly at a well known international varietal"

## IMW's likely intent
This is the definitive "international variety across terroirs" question. The IMW wants candidates to demonstrate that they understand how climate (continental vs. maritime vs. Mediterranean) and winemaking (extraction, oak, whole-cluster, malo timing) shape the same grape into radically different wines. The 25-mark variety ID ensures candidates commit early and build their climate/winemaking discussion around that commitment. The 5-mark origin deliberately de-emphasizes pinpoint geography — it is the climate-winemaking story that matters.

## What to focus on in the glass
- **Colour depth**: Pinot = lighter/translucent, Syrah = deep/inky, Cab Sauv = deep but less inky
- **Aromatic markers**: Pinot (red fruit, earth, mushroom), Syrah (black pepper, violet, smoked meat), Cab Sauv (cassis, cedar, graphite)
- **Tannin quality**: Pinot = silky/fine, Syrah = medium-firm/savory, Cab Sauv = firm/angular
- **Whole-cluster indicators**: stem spice, herbal lift (common in Burgundy, Oregon, NZ Pinot and Northern Rhone Syrah)
- **Climate clues per wine**: acid level, alcohol, fruit ripeness (red vs. black fruit), phenolic ripeness
- **Oak regime**: new French oak (Burgundy, Napa), older/larger format (Germany, NZ), American oak (rare for these varieties except Australian Shiraz)
