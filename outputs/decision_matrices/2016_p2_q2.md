# Decision matrix -- 2016 Paper 2 Question 2

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 3-5 are not from France and are all made from the same single grape variety.

With reference to all three wines:

a) Identify the grape variety as closely as possible. (15 marks)

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible, with particular reference to the climate. (3 x 10 marks)

c) Comment on quality and consider key selling points. (3 x 10 marks)

## Paper context
Paper 2 = red wines only. Three wines, same single variety, NOT from France, three different origins with climate emphasis.

## Stem signals
- Same **single grape variety**, three wines, **not from France** -- explicit exclusion of France
- Variety ID worth **15 marks** -- substantial. The variety must be identifiable but the non-France constraint makes it trickier.
- Origin with **climate** reference -- 10 marks each. The IMW wants discussion of cool vs warm climate expressions of the same grape.
- Quality and **key selling points** -- commercial positioning, what makes each wine marketable
- Three different climatic expressions of the same grape outside France

## Universe
A red grape variety grown significantly outside France in at least three different countries/regions with distinct climates. Must be an international variety with enough global presence to have three distinct non-French expressions.

## Candidate varieties (confidence tiers)
- **Pinot Noir**: STRONG SIGNAL -- grown extensively outside France in New Zealand, Germany, USA, Australia, Chile, South Africa. Climate is THE defining factor for Pinot Noir expressions.
- **Syrah/Shiraz**: PLAUSIBLE -- Australia (warm), NZ Hawke's Bay (cool), South Africa, Chile. Climate hugely impacts style.
- **Cabernet Sauvignon**: PLAUSIBLE -- Chile, Napa, Australia, South Africa. But climate variation is less dramatic.
- **Merlot**: CURVEBALL -- Chile, USA, Italy. Less climate diversity discussion.
- **Tempranillo**: CURVEBALL -- almost exclusively Spanish, limited non-France relevance

## Candidate origins per wine

## Rule-outs
- France explicitly excluded
- Gamay already used in Q1 (Beaujolais)
- Nebbiolo -- almost exclusively Italian, limited three-country spread
- Malbec -- primarily Argentina, limited climate diversity
- Grenache -- limited non-French presence at this point (pre-2020 exam)

## IMW's likely intent
Testing knowledge of how climate shapes a single red variety across the New World and non-French Old World. Pinot Noir is the strongest candidate because it is the variety most sensitive to climate, and discussing cool vs warm climate Pinot is a core MW skill. The "key selling points" question asks the candidate to position each wine commercially -- cool-climate elegance vs warm-climate richness.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Color intensity: cool-climate = lighter ruby, warm = deeper
- Fruit profile: cool = red fruits (cherry, cranberry), warm = darker fruits (plum, black cherry)
- Alcohol: cool-climate Pinot typically 12.5-13.5%, warm can reach 14%+
- Tannin structure: cool = fine-grained, warm = more pronounced
- Acidity: cool = higher, more linear, warm = riper, rounder
- Oak: varies by producer philosophy
- Whole-cluster influence: spice, stem tannin, herbal notes
