## What to focus on in the glass
- **Bubble quality**: fine, persistent mousse (traditional method) vs. larger, frothier bubbles (Charmat). This is the first diagnostic.
- **Autolytic character**: brioche, toast, biscuit, almond = extended lees aging in traditional method. Absence = younger disgorgement or tank method.
- **Acidity profile**: high, linear, citric = cool climate (England, northern France). Rounder, softer = warmer origin (parts of Spain, California).
- **Fruit character**: green apple, citrus, chalk = cool-climate Chardonnay/Pinot; tropical, stone fruit = warmer climate or Charmat Glera.
- **Dosage level**: assess perceived sweetness. Brut Nature vs. Brut vs. Extra Dry -- this differentiates Cava (often Brut Nature) from others.
- **Oxidative vs. reductive style**: some Cava and Cremant show more oxidative base wine character; English sparkling tends reductive and precise.
- **Color**: pale with green tints = young, cool-climate traditional method. Deeper gold = either age, warmer climate, or oxidative base wines.
