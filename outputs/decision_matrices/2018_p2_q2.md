Paper 2 = red wines. Three wines from the same single red grape variety. High marks (15) for variety ID.

## Rule-outs

- **Sangiovese** — mostly Italian; limited convincing non-Italian expressions
- **Tempranillo** — mostly Iberian; three countries is a stretch
- **Gamay** — too regionally limited for three distinct origins
- **Malbec** — almost exclusively Argentine for single varietal; Cahors is the only other serious origin

## IMW's likely intent

The winemaking emphasis suggests wines where extraction, oak, and whole-cluster decisions are visible in the glass. This complements Q1's European focus by shifting to New World origins.

## What to focus on in the glass
- **Fruit purity vs. complexity**: clean red cherry/raspberry (NZ, Oregon) vs. darker, spiced fruit (California)
- **Alcohol**: 13-13.5% (Oregon, NZ) vs. 14%+ (California)
- **Oak influence**: more prominent new oak (California) vs. subtle/older oak (Oregon, NZ)
- **Tannin**: silky (fine Pinot) vs. slightly grippy (over-extracted or warmer climate)
- **Whole-cluster markers**: herbal/stemmy notes, structure without heaviness
- **Colour**: lighter ruby (cool-climate Pinot) vs. deeper purple-ruby (warm-climate)
