## IMW's likely intent
By giving away the variety, the IMW shifts the test to **origin discrimination within Pinot Noir**. The candidate must demonstrate they can taste the difference between cool-climate Old World (Burgundy's earthiness, structure), cool-climate New World (NZ's purity, acid), and potentially warmer New World (California's richness). The "ability to improve" question targets ageability knowledge -- which Pinot styles age? (Premier/Grand Cru Burgundy yes, entry-level NZ less so). The commercial appeal component tests whether candidates understand how Pinot is positioned in global markets.

## What to focus on in the glass

- Nose: earth/mushroom/sous-bois (Burgundy) vs. red cherry/floral purity (NZ) vs. ripe red fruit/spice (California/Oregon)
- Palate: acidity (high = cool climate), tannin structure (fine-grained = quality Burgundy)
- Oak: French oak subtlety (Burgundy) vs. more overt use (California) vs. minimal (NZ)
- Alcohol: <13% (Burgundy/Germany), 13-14% (NZ/Oregon), >14% (California)
- Finish: mineral/savory persistence (Old World) vs. fruit-driven length (New World)
