# Decision matrix -- 2017 Paper 3 Question 1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-3 are roses from three different countries.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin as closely as possible with reference to the style. (3 x 7 marks)

b) Highlight the key winemaking techniques used. (3 x 8 marks)

c) Discuss the commercial appeal and market positioning. (3 x 10 marks)

Paper 3 = mixed bag (sparkling, fortified, sweet, rose, oxidative, etc.). Three rose wines from three different countries. Rose is a classic P3 category.

- **Commercial appeal and market positioning at 10 marks** -- the heaviest sub-question; rose is a booming commercial category and the IMW wants trade-level insight

## Rule-outs
- Unlikely all three are premium dry roses -- the IMW typically includes a range of styles and quality levels in rose questions
- Unlikely all European -- "three different countries" often includes a New World producer

## IMW's likely intent
Testing the candidate's understanding of the global rose market, which has exploded commercially. White Zinfandel vs Provence represents the spectrum from mass-market sweet to premium dry. The winemaking question differentiates direct pressing (Provence), saignee, and blending/arrested fermentation (White Zinfandel). The commercial question is where most marks lie -- can the candidate articulate who drinks each wine, where it's sold, and what the growth trajectory looks like?

## What to focus on in the glass
- Color: deep pink/salmon (White Zin or saignee) vs barely-there pale pink (Provence)
- Sweetness: off-dry (White Zin) vs bone-dry (Provence, most European roses)
- Aromatic profile: candy/strawberry (White Zin) vs garrigue/citrus (Provence) vs red fruit/spice (Spanish rosado)
- Alcohol level: low (White Zin ~9-10%) vs moderate (Provence ~13%)
- Bubbles or perlant quality -- if any effervescence, consider sparkling rose
