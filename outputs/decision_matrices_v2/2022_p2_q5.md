Remaining STRONG SIGNAL candidates for a same-region pair at Q5:

- **Zinfandel / California (Dry Creek Valley): STRONG SIGNAL** — the "or predominant" language is the Zinfandel/Ridge field-blend signal. California Zinfandel is classically labelled as varietal even when it contains Petite Sirah, Carignane, or Mourvèdre in a field blend — making "predominant" the accurate legal and commercial description for these wines. Dry Creek Valley is the canonical same-region quality-comparison region for Zinfandel. F1c leaf in the corpus explicitly cites 2022 P2 Q5 / Zinfandel / Dry Creek.

## IMW's likely intent
Parallel to Q4 but with the added blending dimension. The "or predominant" language specifically tests whether candidates understand that some wines labelled as varietal Zinfandel may legally and commercially contain other grapes in field blends (Ridge's California appellation releases). The market potential question again tests commercial literacy — entry-level Dry Creek Zin vs Ridge premium is a wide price and prestige gap, and the candidates are expected to articulate different market channels (grocery/supermarket/direct vs fine wine retail and restaurant wine list).

## What to focus on in the glass
- Bramble/blackberry jam + sweet spice + high alcohol (14.5%+) → Zinfandel confirmed across both wines.
- Alcohol level: 15%+ is a very reliable Zinfandel tell; both wines should be at or above this threshold.
- Tannin: softer/simpler in pure entry-level Zin; firmer, darker, more structured in the Petite Sirah field-blend wine.
- Colour depth: field-blend premium likely shows more depth from Petite Sirah addition.
- Oak character: simpler/sweeter on entry-level; more integrated/complex with possible older barrel on premium.
- Market tells: entry-level — fruity, exuberant, accessible price point; premium — structured, terroir-driven, aged, fine wine positioning.
