# Decision matrix v2 — 2018 P1 Q1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-4 are all made from the same single grape variety.

With reference to all four wines:

a) Identify the grape variety. (20 marks)

For each wine:

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (4 x 10 marks)

c) Discuss quality with reference to winemaking techniques used. (4 x 10 marks)

## Anti-collapse note

Four wines, same variety, enormous stylistic range. The exam risk is variety doubt on W1 (commodity, no markers) or losing confidence on W2 (so different from W1). Do not second-guess the variety: the question stem locks it. The 20-mark variety ID demands the candidate explain what all four wines share (white-fruit family, Chardonnay's neutral baseline, the way winemaking sculpts rather than intrudes on the variety). The per-wine quality discussion must use classification / winemaking language, not just quality adjectives.
