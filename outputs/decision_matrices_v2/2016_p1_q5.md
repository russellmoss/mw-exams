## Anti-collapse note

**F4 anti-overlink discipline:** The four-different-countries constraint stated in the stem is both a useful pre-taste narrowing tool and a trap. It narrows the country universe (eliminating repeated countries from earlier questions), but this should not lead to assuming any shared style, vintage character, or aromatic family across wines. Each wine is an independent identification problem. The most common collapse error in four-variety breadth questions is confusing aromatic whites from different origins (Torrontés/Gewurztraminer/Muscat) because the candidate carries the aromatic expectation from one wine into the next. The hard rule — do not infer wine N from wine N−1 — applies here without exception.
