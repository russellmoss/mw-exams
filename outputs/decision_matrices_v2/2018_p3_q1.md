# Decision matrix v2 — 2018 P3 Q1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-6 are presented as pairs, 1 and 2, 3 and 4, and 5 and 6. Each pair is made by a different single producer.

For each pair:

a) Identify the origin, as closely as possible. (3 x 10 marks)

b) Comment on the key winemaking techniques used. (3 x 12 marks)

c) Identify the vintage(s) and consider its/their key characteristics. (3 x 10 marks)

d) Discuss the quality and maturity of the wines. (3 x 18 marks)

## What to focus on in the glass (F7 tasting branch order)
1. Category lock: sparkling (Champagne pair) / sweet still (Sauternes pair) / fortified sweet red (Port pair) — three immediately distinguishable families
2. Family lock: autolysis + mousse → Champagne; botrytis honey + acidity → Sauternes; spirit warmth + red fruit + tannin → Vintage Port
3. Quality calibration via concentration, length, complexity: each pair's older wine should show greater complexity and tertiary character; younger wine should show more primary freshness
4. Classification slot: Champagne vintage (minimum 6-year lees ageing mandated) vs NV; Sauternes premier cru vs basic; Vintage Port (declaration required) vs other Port styles
5. Commercial-position framing: Champagne prestige cuvée (fine dining/special occasion/collector); Sauternes premier cru (restaurant dessert/fine cheese/collector); Vintage Port historic declaration (collector/special occasion/decanted)
6. Vintage identification: colour and development stage narrow the vintage range; classic vintages (1999, 2009 Champagne; 2002 Sauternes; 1985 Port) should be confirmed or approached from developmental evidence

Key glass markers:
- Champagne pair: colour — pale gold (younger) vs deeper gold/amber (older); autolysis — fresh brioche (younger) vs complex nut/honey/toast (older); mousse persistence indicates both are vintage
- Sauternes pair: colour — deep gold with amber (younger) vs orange/amber (older); botrytis character — fresh honey/pineapple (younger) vs dried apricot/marmalade/saffron (older); viscosity and length
- Port pair: colour — ruby-purple/garnet (younger) vs garnet-brick with orange rim (older); tannin — grippy and forward (younger) vs fully resolved/silky (older); dried fruit/nuts vs primary black fruit
