### W6
- **Snapshot:** Amber-gold, walnut / hazelnut / dried-orange-peel oxidative complexity with residual flor-derived almond, dry to off-dry, fortified.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still fortified · med-high acid · medium body · dry → off-dry · ~19–20%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Long solera ageing; biologically aged first (under flor), then oxidatively aged after fortification or natural flor death; American oak butts.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Biologically-then-oxidatively aged Sherry — Palo Cortado / Amontillado pathway.
- **Key production lever:** Initial fortification to ~15% so flor can develop; subsequent fortification to ~17–20% to kill flor and continue oxidative ageing; solera and criadera fractional blending.
- **Style consequence:** Aromatic delicacy of an Amontillado plus the textural weight of an Oloroso; bone-dry to off-dry; oxidative depth without losing aromatic finesse.
- **Origin:** Spain — Jerez — Palomino-based.
- **Quality / maturity:** Aged solera tier; maturity is steady-state — the solera maintains the wine's profile rather than allowing it to deteriorate. VOS / VORS tier possible.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Aged Oloroso (more weight, less aromatic delicacy) or Amontillado (more flor character, less oxidative weight).

### W7
- **Snapshot:** Tawny-amber colour, nut / caramel / dried-apricot / mahogany / coffee, sweet, spirit-warm.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still fortified · med acid · medium-full body · sweet · ~20%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Long oxidative cask ageing in small pipes; deliberate oxidation; fractional blending.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Aged-Tawny Port — oxidative-barrel pathway.
- **Key production lever:** Fortification with aguardente at ~7% ABV during fermentation to retain residual sugar (mutage); long ageing in 550 L pipes; oxidative development.
- **Style consequence:** Caramelised dried-fruit / nut / sweet-spice character with brick-amber colour; the antithesis of reductive Vintage Port.
- **Origin:** Portugal — Douro.
- **Variety:** Touriga Nacional / Touriga Franca / Tinta Roriz / Tinta Cão field blend.
- **Quality / maturity:** Age statement is the key quality signal — 10 / 20 / 30 / 40 year tiers; maturity is *integrated* by the time of bottling (released ready to drink).
- **Branch-B alternative:** Colheita Port if vintage-dated single-harvest Tawny, or a Vintage Port if reductive primary fruit dominates.

### W8
- **Snapshot:** Pale-to-mid amber with green edge, electric acid, dried-apple / curry / candle-wax / preserved-citrus, dry to slightly off-dry, spirit-warm.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still fortified · **very high acid** · medium body · dry (Sercial) → off-dry (Verdelho) · ~19–20%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Heated maturation (estufagem in stainless steel/tanks for entry tiers; canteiro in cask in attic heat for premium tiers); deliberate aldehyde and ester formation.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Madeira — heated oxidative pathway.
- **Key production lever:** Fortification timing dictates sweetness (Sercial fortified later, drier; Bual / Malmsey fortified earlier, sweeter); maturation under heat creates the characteristic volatile lift and high acid; canteiro for premium tiers.
- **Style consequence:** Volatile lift + curry + crystalline acid + caramelised depth; nearly indestructible wine that survives months open.
- **Origin:** Portugal — Madeira.
- **Variety:** Sercial (driest) or Verdelho (off-dry) given the question's quality / maturity band rewards a discussable dry-end Madeira.
- **Quality / maturity:** Age statement (5 / 10 / 15 / 20 / 40 year, or Frasqueira / vintage). Madeira essentially does not deteriorate; the maturity discussion is about how heating develops aldehydes and esters.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Bual or Malmsey if the wine is markedly sweeter; less likely given the question rewards a contrasted dry style alongside the sweet Tawny.

## Anti-collapse note
The three production mechanisms are deliberately distinct: solera + flor / oxidative for Sherry; reductive- or oxidative-cask + mutage for Port; heated maturation for Madeira. The matrix must articulate each mechanism in its own technical language and not blur them under generic "fortified ageing" wording.
