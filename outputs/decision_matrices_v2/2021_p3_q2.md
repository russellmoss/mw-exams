# Decision matrix v2 — 2021 P3 Q2

## Question (verbatim)

For each wine:

a) Comment on the method of production with reference to residual sugar and alcohol levels. (5 x 10 marks)

c) Comment on the style and quality within the context of the origin. (5 x 5 marks)

- **Production-family slots (five mechanisms, one wine per mechanism):**
  - Slot W4: Appassimento / dried-grape fermented to dryness (Amarone della Valpolicella) → **STRONG SIGNAL**. ~16% ABV, technically dry palate, high-alcohol-from-dried-grape mechanism.
  - Slot W5: Arrested fermentation under tank pressure (Brachetto d'Acqui / Moscato d'Asti, Asti / partial-Charmat method) → **STRONG SIGNAL**. ~5.5–7% ABV, sweet, lightly sparkling.

  - Slot W7: Vin Doux Naturel (mutage with grape spirit, sweet red) — Maury / Banyuls / Rivesaltes → **STRONG SIGNAL**. ~15.5% ABV, sweet, French Roussillon VDN pathway.

- **Anti-collapse rule:** Five wines, five mechanisms. Do not put two slots in the same mechanism — that breaks the stem's "different methods" wording. The matrix should be one mechanism per slot, **named explicitly**.

- **Default rule-outs:** Dry table wines (the framing rules them out); wines made by the same method (the stem rules them out).

- **Main traps:** Defaulting to all sweet-white-mechanism (botrytis + Icewine + late-harvest + passito + Vouvray moelleux) and missing the red / fortified / sparkling spread; under-using the RS × alcohol matrix as the pre-taste ballot tool.

### W4
- **Snapshot:** Deep ruby-black, dried-cherry / fig / cocoa / chocolate, full body, dry palate despite very high alcohol.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still red · med acid · full body · dry · **16%**.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Long oak ageing in large oak botti or barriques; oxidative tendency moderated.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Appassimento + fermented to dryness — Amarone pathway.
- **Key production lever:** Grape drying on racks (fruttaio) for 3–4 months pre-fermentation concentrating sugar; long fermentation taken to dryness, generating ~16% ABV; *bitter / "amaro"* finish gives the wine its name.
- **Style consequence:** Dried-cherry concentration + chocolate-bitter depth + high alcohol + technically dry palate; the alcohol-from-sugar pathway *without* residual sweetness.
- **RS estimate:** Dry to off-dry (≤9 g/L).
- **Origin:** Italy — Veneto — Valpolicella.
- **Variety:** Corvina + Corvinone + Rondinella.
- **Quality / style:** Amarone della Valpolicella DOCG; quality justified by appassimento depth and integration.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Recioto della Valpolicella (same appassimento mechanism but fermentation stopped early — sweet palate).

### W5
- **Snapshot:** Pale pink, frothy / lightly sparkling, strawberry / rose petal / Muscat lift, very sweet.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Lightly sparkling / pétillant · med-high acid · light body · sweet · **5.5%**.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** No oak; reductive; fresh primary fruit dominates.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Arrested fermentation under tank pressure — Asti / Brachetto method.
- **Key production lever:** Single fermentation in pressurised tank (Charmat-like) interrupted before all sugar is fermented; cooling halts yeast; CO2 retained as a soft sparkle.
- **Style consequence:** Very low alcohol + high RS + light sparkle + intact varietal aromatics; sweet but not cloying due to acidity and bubbles.
- **RS estimate:** Sweet (~100+ g/L).
- **Origin:** Italy — Piedmont — Acqui DOCG.
- **Variety:** Brachetto.
- **Quality / style:** Brachetto d'Acqui — distinctive sweet aromatic-red sparkling; modest in serious-collector terms but high-quality within category.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Moscato d'Asti (white grape Muscat version of the same pathway) if the wine reads white-aromatic rather than red-aromatic.

### W6
- **Snapshot:** Deep purple-black, intense primary blackberry / blueberry / liquorice / pencil-shaving, sweet, very high alcohol with spirit warmth and grippy tannin.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still fortified red · med acid · full body · sweet · **20%**.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Reductive bottle ageing (Vintage Port) rather than oxidative cask ageing (Tawny); primary fruit preserved.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Spirit-stopped sweet red fortified — Vintage Port pathway.
- **Key production lever:** Fermentation stopped at ~7% ABV by addition of grape spirit (aguardente) to ~20%; mutage preserves grape sugar.
- **Style consequence:** Concentrated primary black fruit + sweetness + grippy young tannin + spirit warmth; reductive bottle development.
- **RS estimate:** Sweet (~90–110 g/L).
- **Origin:** Portugal — Douro.
- **Variety:** Touriga Nacional + Touriga Franca + Tinta Roriz field blend.
- **Quality / style:** Vintage Port from a declared year (e.g. 2000 / 2003 / 2007 / 2011 / 2016); top tier of Port hierarchy.
- **Branch-B alternative:** LBV Port if the wine is less concentrated and less ageworthy; Single-Quinta Vintage if it carries an estate signature.

### W7
- **Snapshot:** Deep brick-purple, sweet red fruit + cacao + fig + Mediterranean herb, moderate spirit warmth, lower than Port.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still fortified red · med acid · full body · sweet · **15.5%**.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Some oxidative ageing; "Grande Reserve" tier implies extended bottle / cask development.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Vin Doux Naturel — mutage with grape spirit on Grenache.
- **Key production lever:** Mutage = grape spirit added during fermentation; lower final ABV than Port (15.5% vs 20%); Roussillon climate concentrates Grenache sugar naturally.
- **Style consequence:** Sweet Grenache fruit + spirit integration + Mediterranean savoury edge; less grippy than Port and lower spirit warmth.
- **RS estimate:** Sweet (~90–110 g/L).
- **Origin:** France — Roussillon — Maury or Banyuls.
- **Variety:** Grenache Noir.
- **Quality / style:** Grande Reserve VDN; quality justified by mutage timing and élevage.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Banyuls Grand Cru (older oxidative VDN) if the wine reads more rancio.

### W8
- **Snapshot:** Mahogany-amber, sweet raisin / coffee / mocha / molasses / dried-fig / rancio, very sweet, spirit-warm.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still fortified white · med acid · medium-full body · very sweet · **18%**.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Long fractional cask ageing in solera-like blending (Classic / Grand / Rare classification ladder); deliberate oxidation.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Oxidative fortified Muscat with fractional blending — Rutherglen Muscat pathway.
- **Key production lever:** Mutage to ~18% then long oxidative cask ageing and fractional blending across multiple vintages; deliberate rancio development.
- **Style consequence:** Very high RS + rancio character + mahogany colour + extreme aromatic complexity; the antithesis of fresh primary fruit.
- **RS estimate:** Very sweet (200+ g/L).
- **Origin:** Australia — Victoria — Rutherglen.
- **Variety:** Muscat à Petits Grains Rouge ("Brown Muscat").
- **Quality / style:** Rutherglen Muscat Classic (10–12 years average age) → Grand (15–20) → Rare (20+) classification ladder.
- **Branch-B alternative:** PX Sherry if Spanish raisin / molasses character dominates (single-grape Pedro Ximenez very-sweet Sherry).

Lesson:
A five-wine "different methods of production" stem with RS and alcohol explicitly named is the corpus signature for the five-mechanism sweep. The ABV ladder (5.5 / 15.5 / 16 / 18.5 / 20) is the most reliable in-glass routing tool: estimate alcohol first, then mechanism, then origin and variety.
