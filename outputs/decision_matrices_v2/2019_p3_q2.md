## IMW's likely intent

Testing ability to distinguish quality tiers within one of the world's fastest-growing sparkling categories, and to discuss its market dynamics with authority. The 30-mark comparison rewards candidates who can articulate what creates quality in Prosecco (hillside vineyard sourcing, vintage focus, shorter and more controlled Charmat cycle, Glera selection from better clones) vs. what drives the commercial DOC tier (volume, consistency, price accessibility, brand recognition). The 10-mark commercial appeal section requires market segmentation knowledge: who buys Valdobbiadene Superiore vs. basic DOC, at what price point, through which channels, and how the category's explosive growth has both created opportunity and diluted the premium image.

## What to focus on in the glass

- Bubble quality: finer, more persistent bead on the premium DOCG; slightly coarser, shorter-lived on the DOC
- Fruit definition: cleaner, more precise pear/white peach/apple on DOCG; simpler, broader fruit on DOC
- Finish length: noticeably longer on DOCG; short and refreshing on DOC
- Sweetness level: many commercial Proseccos are Extra Dry (slightly sweeter) vs. Brut (drier) on DOCG — check dosage perception
- Aromatic complexity: slight floral (wisteria, acacia) and mineral notes on quality DOCG; simpler fruit on basic DOC
