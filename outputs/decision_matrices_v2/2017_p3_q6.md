### W11
- **Snapshot:** Amber-gold, walnut / dried-orange-peel / saline almond / aldehyde, bone-dry to off-dry, fortified.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still fortified · med-high acid · medium body · dry → off-dry · ~19–20%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Long solera ageing; oxidative or biologically-then-oxidatively aged; American oak butt.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Dry oxidative Sherry — Palo Cortado / Aged Oloroso pathway.
- **Key production lever:** Solera and criadera fractional blending; static or post-flor oxidative ageing; later fortification to ~17–20%.
- **Style consequence:** Walnut / saline / aldehyde / dried-citrus oxidative depth; bone-dry; long crystalline finish.
- **Origin:** Spain — Jerez — Palomino-based.
- **Quality tied to method:** Aged solera tier (VOS / VORS) where method drives length, complexity, and dryness — quality is justified through the production lever, not just hedonic palate.
- **"Who would buy this wine?":** Specialist sommelier (Sherry-by-the-glass programme), independent merchant clientele, wine-trade and Sherry-enthusiast collectors, savoury-food-pairing on-trade (umami / aged-cheese / consommé / nuts). Channel: independent retail and Michelin / Sherry-bar on-trade. Demographic: experienced wine drinkers; not impulse buyers.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Aged Oloroso (more weight, less aromatic finesse) if the wine reads heavier.

### W12
- **Snapshot:** Deep ruby-purple to brick-garnet, dark berry / cacao / fig / cinnamon, sweet, spirit-warm.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still fortified · med acid · full body · sweet · ~15–20%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Reductive or moderately oxidative cask ageing depending on tier; bottling young (Ruby / LBV / VDN) preserves primary fruit.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Sweet red fortified — VDN (mutage) or Port (mutage) pathway.
- **Key production lever:** Mutage = neutral grape spirit added during fermentation to halt yeast activity and retain residual sugar; warm climate yields concentrated grape sugar; spirit-fruit integration is the quality marker.
- **Style consequence:** Dark berry + cacao + dried-fig + sweet-spice fortified profile; spirit warmth integrated rather than burning.
- **Origin:** France (Roussillon — Maury / Banyuls — Grenache Noir) OR Portugal (Douro — Touriga Nacional blend Port). The French VDN reading is preferred when the wine reads slightly fresher and less Vintage-Port-grippy.
- **Variety:** Grenache Noir (VDN) or Douro field blend (Port).
- **Quality tied to method:** "Grande Reserve" / aged VDN tier, or LBV / Vintage / Aged-Tawny Port tier — production-method-driven complexity and integration of spirit.
- **"Who would buy this wine?":** After-dinner / dessert market; restaurant dessert lists (chocolate / cheese pairing); gift market (Father's Day, Christmas hampers); broader on-trade with mainstream appeal; older demographic but with growing on-trade dessert relevance. Channel: supermarket premium plus specialist retail plus on-trade dessert list.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Ruby / LBV / Vintage Port if the wine reads grippier and more tannic; Maury / Banyuls if it reads slightly less dense and more red-fruited.
