## Universe (F5e reasoning)
European white wines where the human-versus-natural debate is rich, distinct, and defensible in an exam answer. The spectrum needs:

- **Nature-dominant archetype**: A wine where vineyard factors (soil, slope, climate) do most of the work and winemaking is minimal. **German Riesling (Mosel)** is the canonical example: Devonian slate reflects heat to ripening grapes, cooling nights preserve acidity, steep south-facing slopes maximise sun exposure; winemaking is stainless, no oak, no malo, low intervention, often no additives.
- **Human-dominant archetype via extended oak ageing**: A wine where winemaker choices completely transform a relatively neutral grape. **White Rioja Gran Reserva (Spain)** is the canonical example: Viura / Macabeo is neutral until years of oak ageing (American or French) transform it into a nutty, oxidative, amber-coloured wine; the human decision to age is the defining act.
- **Extreme human intervention archetype**: A wine where pre-fermentation and ageing choices are both extreme. **Vin Santo (Tuscany, Italy)** is the canonical example: appassimento (multi-week/month drying on racks or in attic), pressing of concentrated must, fermentation in small sealed caratelli barrels, multi-year oxidative ageing without topping up.

### Candidate varieties (confidence tiers)
- Riesling, Germany (Mosel / Franken / Rheingau): **STRONG SIGNAL** — "natural factors" poster child; Mosel slate, steep slope aspect, cool climate, minimal winemaking
- White Rioja Gran Reserva (Viura / Malvasia / Garnacha Blanca), Spain: **STRONG SIGNAL** — "human inputs" dominant via extended oak ageing; transformative human decision
- Vin Santo (Trebbiano / Malvasia / Sangiovese Bianco), Tuscany, Italy: **STRONG SIGNAL** — extreme human intervention: appassimento + multi-year oxidative barrel ageing
- Grüner Veltliner, Austria: **PLAUSIBLE** — shows terroir clearly (Kamptal, Wachau) but human/natural contrast is less dramatic
- Tokaji Furmint, Hungary: **PLAUSIBLE** — botrytis (natural) vs winemaking decisions (human); interesting but not as extreme
