## Anti-collapse note

The two wines taste radically different despite being the same variety. W5 is warm, round, and barrel-influenced; W6 is cool, linear, and reductive. Do not let the richness of W5 cause doubt about variety — Chenin Blanc is the only white with this specific wax/lanolin/quince signature at both extremes. Alcohol differential (14% vs 12.5%) is a direct country signal before any aroma analysis.

*Prediction accuracy:*
- Variety: CORRECT — Chenin Blanc STRONG SIGNAL called correctly; the 20-mark winemaking emphasis was the decisive diagnostic.

- Lesson: when a two-wine same-variety question weights winemaking at 40% of the marks, the variety must be one whose winemaking choices are maximally divergent across its two homes. Chenin Blanc (Loire vs SA) is the premier example of this principle in P1.
