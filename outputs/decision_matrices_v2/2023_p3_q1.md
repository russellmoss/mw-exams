# Decision matrix v2 — 2023 P3 Q1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-4 are traditional method sparkling wines from four different countries. None is from Champagne.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin and grape variety/ies as closely as possible. (4 x 13 marks)

b) Comment on quality in the context of origin. (4 x 10 marks)

c) State the residual sugar. (4 x 2 marks)

## Pre-taste matrix

### Universal header
- **Paper:** P3
- **Family:** F5a Method / Production Dominant — sparkling-method comparison

- **Mark map:** origin + grape 13 each (52 total) · quality in context 10 each (40 total) · RS 2 each (8 total). Origin + variety dominate, but the production family is fixed by the stem so identification reduces to picking the correct country and its native traditional-method grape set.
- **Constraint:** method *and* origin spread are both bound — production family fixed to traditional method, country spread fixed to four different non-Champagne countries.

### F5 pre-taste matrix

- **Sub-form:** F5a sparkling-method comparison. Pure Branch 5.1 from the P3 family tree pack.

- **Production-family slots (four different countries):**
  - Slot W1: Cava (Spain, Penedès) — Macabeo / Xarel-lo / Parellada → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - Slot W2: Crémant d'Alsace or Crémant de Bourgogne (France) — Pinot Blanc / Auxerrois / Pinot Gris (Alsace) or Chardonnay / Pinot Noir (Bourgogne) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - Slot W3: English sparkling (West Sussex / Hampshire / Kent) — Chardonnay / Pinot Noir / Pinot Meunier → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - Slot W4: German Sekt (Rheingau / Mosel) — Riesling traditional method → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - PLAUSIBLE swap-ins: Franciacorta (Italy), Cap Classique (South Africa), Trento DOC, California prestige cuvée
  - CURVEBALL: Tasmania, Nova Scotia, Brazil, Argentine traditional method

- **Anti-collapse rule:** Four different countries — no two slots may share a country. Within each country, default to the *native* traditional-method category, not Champagne-look-alike.

- **Default rule-outs:** Champagne (stem); Prosecco / Asti (tank method excluded by stem); pet-nat (ancestral method, excluded); South American sparkling (out-competed by the canonical "big four" non-Champagne countries).

- **Main traps:** Predicting the same country twice (e.g. two Crémant appellations); over-defaulting to Chardonnay/Pinot Noir in all four slots and missing Riesling Sekt's aromatic signature; assuming Brut RS across the board when one wine may be Brut Nature.

## In-taste matrix

### W1
- **Snapshot:** Persistent fine mousse + apple / yeast / almond + slightly broader mid-palate + lower acid than Champagne.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Sparkling · med-high acid · medium body · likely Brut Nature → Brut · ~12%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Long lees autolysis (biscuit / almond) · no oak · reductive base wine.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Traditional-method sparkling, Cava-style.
- **Key production lever:** Extended lees ageing under cap; Brut Nature dosage; native Penedès grape set (Macabeo / Xarel-lo / Parellada).
- **Style consequence:** Mediterranean-warm fruit gives apple-yeast-almond rather than Champagne's chalky lemon; Brut Nature dosage gives bone-dry finish.
- **Origin:** Spain → Penedès (Cava DO).
- **Variety:** Macabeo + Xarel-lo + Parellada (classic Cava blend).
- **Quality in regional context:** Reserva or Gran Reserva tier — minimum 18 / 30 months on lees beyond Cava de Guarda baseline.
- **RS estimate:** Brut Nature (0–3 g/L).
- **Branch-B alternative:** Corpinnat (premium spin-off from Cava) if the wine over-delivers on lees and complexity.

### W2
- **Snapshot:** Persistent fine mousse + apple / lees + softer mid-palate than Champagne + integrated spice / blossom.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Sparkling · high acid · medium body · Brut · ~12.5%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Moderate lees autolysis · little to no oak · reductive.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Traditional-method sparkling, Crémant-style.
- **Key production lever:** Whole-cluster pressing per Crémant rules; ~9-month minimum lees; regional grape mix.
- **Style consequence:** Less chalk than Champagne, slightly riper orchard fruit, softer mousse.
- **Origin:** France — Crémant d'Alsace likeliest (Pinot Blanc / Auxerrois signature); Crémant de Bourgogne the strong alternative (Chardonnay-led).
- **Variety:** Pinot Blanc + Auxerrois (Alsace) OR Chardonnay + Pinot Noir (Bourgogne).
- **Quality in regional context:** Standard Brut; some Crémant houses now produce extended-lees prestige cuvées (Émotion, Eminent).
- **RS estimate:** Brut (6–12 g/L).
- **Branch-B alternative:** Crémant de Loire (Chenin-led) if quince / wax appears on the palate.

### W3
- **Snapshot:** Very high acid + lean green-apple fruit + autolytic biscuit + cool-climate signature.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Sparkling · very high acid · light-medium body · Brut · ~12%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Significant lees autolysis (extended ageing) · no oak · highly reductive.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Traditional-method sparkling, English-style cool-climate.
- **Key production lever:** Long lees ageing (3+ years), low malolactic conversion, cool-climate base wines from chalk-influenced sites.
- **Style consequence:** Champagne-like autolysis but with slightly less ripeness, more green-apple acidity, and a leaner palate.
- **Origin:** England — West Sussex / Hampshire / Kent (chalk belt). Likely Blanc de Blancs given clean Chardonnay-driven profile.
- **Variety:** Chardonnay (Blanc de Blancs) OR Chardonnay + Pinot Noir + Pinot Meunier classic blend.
- **Quality in regional context:** Vintage Blanc de Blancs is England's prestige expression. Likely a benchmark producer (Nyetimber, Gusbourne, Ridgeview).
- **RS estimate:** Brut (6–12 g/L) but at the lower end.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Cool-climate Tasmania or Nova Scotia if the wine is even more austere.

### W4
- **Snapshot:** Floral / lime / kerosene-adjacent aromatic Riesling signature riding on fine traditional-method mousse.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Sparkling · very high acid · light body · Extra Brut → Brut · ~13% (Riesling Sekt usually a touch higher than Champagne base).
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Some lees autolysis · no oak · reductive · varietal aromatics carry.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Traditional-method sparkling, German Riesling Sekt.
- **Key production lever:** Traditional-method bottle fermentation on Riesling base wine; longer lees if Winzersekt or vintage Sekt.
- **Style consequence:** Floral, limey, petrol-edged aromatic signature unmistakably Riesling. The aromatic intensity differentiates it instantly from the three Chardonnay/Pinot/Macabeo-dominant slots.
- **Origin:** Germany — Rheingau / Mosel / Pfalz. Premium Winzersekt category.
- **Variety:** Riesling, single-variety traditional method.
- **Quality in regional context:** Riesling Sekt b.A. or Winzersekt — quality estate-bottled tier above commodity tank-method Sekt.
- **RS estimate:** Extra Brut (3–6 g/L).
- **Branch-B alternative:** Pinot-based German Sekt (Pinot Noir / Pinot Blanc / Pinot Meunier) if the aromatic profile is more neutral; less likely given Riesling's signature is iconic.

## Anti-collapse note
Do not let the "four traditional method" framing pull all four wines into Chardonnay/Pinot Noir territory. Two of the four (Cava, German Sekt) use entirely different native grape sets, and that variety spread is half the test.

- Country spread: Cava + Crémant + English + Sekt — **exact match on the "big four" non-Champagne template**. Variety in each slot confirmed.

In-taste accuracy:
- W1 Brut Nature dosage confirmed (≤3 g/L), exactly as predicted by RS branch.
- W2 Crémant d'Alsace confirmed; the Bourgogne alternative branch was the secondary candidate and was correctly demoted by the apple-blossom / softer-mousse fingerprint.
- W3 Nyetimber Blanc de Blancs 2014 confirms the extended-lees English template; vintage 2014 gives ~8 years on lees by exam date.
- W4 Schloss Reinhartshausen Riesling Extra Brut from Rheingau confirms both varietal and RS branch.

Lesson:
The "big four" non-Champagne traditional-method template — Cava (Spain), Crémant (France), English sparkling (England), Riesling Sekt (Germany) — is the default for any P3 Q1 stem that excludes Champagne and specifies four different countries. The pre-taste matrix should reserve one slot per country up front. The discriminating in-taste markers are dosage level (Cava Brut Nature), regional grape signature (Alsace Pinot Blanc / Auxerrois vs Bourgogne Chardonnay), cool-climate austerity (England), and unambiguous Riesling aromatics (Germany).
