## IMW's likely intent

Testing whether the candidate understands that a single region can produce dramatically different wines through different production choices, and can articulate the specific technical process in detail. The 16-mark production method comparison is the center of gravity. The candidate must articulate:
- Dry Furmint: normal vinification, full fermentation to dryness, possibly some Hungarian oak aging
- Aszú: botrytized berries individually harvested (sometimes over weeks of multiple passes through the vineyard), made into paste, added to base wine at measured concentration, very slow and difficult fermentation, minimum 18 months in oak, extensive cellaring before release

The quality/maturity per-wine sub-question (10 marks each) tests whether the candidate can assess each wine on its own terms — the dry Furmint as a dry wine, the Aszú as a sweet wine — rather than applying a single quality metric.

## What to focus on in the glass

- Colour contrast: pale gold to light straw (dry Furmint) vs. deep amber-gold (Aszú, deepening with age)
- Sweetness: immediately apparent — bone dry vs. intensely sweet
- Acidity: both wines should show the characteristic Furmint piercing acidity — this is the regional anchor that confirms the bound variety across both wines; the Aszú's sweetness is always counterbalanced by very high acid
