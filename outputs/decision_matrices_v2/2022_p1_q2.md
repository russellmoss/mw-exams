## Anti-collapse note

The quality/maturity sub-question (10 marks each) is the key differentiator for this F2a question. All three wines have different aging trajectories: the Riesling (20+ years Clare potential), the Margaret River Chardonnay (8–10 years), and the Tahbilk Marsanne Museum Release (released after 8 years' winery aging, still developing). Collapsing the third wine into "aged Chardonnay" because the colour is gold misses the waxy/lanolin Marsanne signature. The Marsanne Museum Release is the quality/maturity curveball — and the quality/maturity discussion of a wine released after 8 years' cellaring is exactly the type of differentiated answer that earns full marks.
