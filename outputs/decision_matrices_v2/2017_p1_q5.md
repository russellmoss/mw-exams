## Anti-collapse note

The two wines are from the same region (Burgundy) but different sub-regions (Chablis vs Cote de Beaune). The key exam risk is conflating them as generically "Burgundy white" without sub-regional discrimination. Origin carries 10 marks per wine — sub-regional specificity is required. The oak question (7 marks per wine) must be answered at the level of technique: barrel fermentation vs ageing, new oak percentage, MLF extent, lees protocol — not just "it was/wasn't oaked."
