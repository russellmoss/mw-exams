## Anti-collapse note

The three production methods each merit their own 5-mark discussion:
- W7 (Muscadet cru communal): extended sur lie lees aging in large cask — the winemaking story is the time on lees and the autolytic character it creates
- W8 (Alsace Riesling): cold stainless fermentation with off-dry RS preservation — the story is how Gassmann's richer ripeness style differs from bone-dry Alsace Riesling
- W9 (Vouvray Demi-Sec): arrested fermentation preserving RS in Chenin Blanc — the story is the balance of natural RS against Chenin's high acidity

Do not collapse W7 into "just neutral white wine with no story." The cru communal Muscadet lees aging is a distinctive production philosophy (the l'Ancestrale is aged 20+ months on lees in cask before bottling, which is more extended than en primeur Muscadet). This is what the 5-mark question rewards.
