# Decision matrix v2 — 2015 P3 Q1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-4 are all from Europe.

For each wine:

a) Discuss the major factors involved in the method of production, with particular reference to the role of yeast. (4 x 15 marks)

b) Comment on quality and style. (4 x 10 marks)

## Pre-taste matrix

### Universal header
- **Paper:** P3
- **Family:** F5b Method / Production Dominant — yeast-role axis (autolysis vs flor/voile biological aging)
- **Stem summary:** Four European wines, production method with explicit yeast-role emphasis.
- **Mark map:** production (yeast-focused) 15 each (60 total) · quality and style 10 each (40 total). **No origin and no variety identification marks at all.**
- **Constraint:** the yeast lens is the binding clue. The flight is built so that yeast plays a markedly different role in each wine.

### F5 pre-taste matrix
- **Method family lock:** wines where yeast is a *structural* production lever — i.e. traditional-method sparkling (autolysis) plus flor / voile biological aging (Sherry / Jura). Other yeast topics (wild ferment, hands-off natural) can appear but are weaker for 60 marks of production discussion.
- **Sub-form:** F5b yeast-mediated production (with overlap into F5a sparkling and F5c fortified-oxidative).

- **Production-family slots (two-and-two yeast structure):**
  - Slot W1: Champagne (traditional-method sparkling, autolytic) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - Slot W2: English sparkling (traditional-method sparkling, autolytic, cool-climate) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - Slot W3: Fino / Manzanilla Sherry (biological aging under flor) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - Slot W4: Vin Jaune / Arbois Savagnin (voile aging, unfortified) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - PLAUSIBLE swap-ins: Cava (autolytic, Spanish), Crémant (autolytic, French alternative); Amontillado / Palo Cortado (flor → oxidative); Tokaji Szamorodni száraz (under-flor Hungarian variant)
  - CURVEBALL: orange / wild-ferment natural wine where ambient yeast is the discussion

- **Anti-collapse rule:** Do not put all four wines in the autolytic-sparkling lane. The richest answer reserves at least two slots for biological aging under a yeast film. A two-and-two split (sparkling autolysis + flor/voile aging) is the corpus default.

- **Default rule-outs:** non-European wines (stem); Port / Madeira (yeast is not the central production lever — fortification stops fermentation, oxidation continues without yeast); still dry wines where yeast is merely the fermentation agent rather than a structural style driver.

- **Main traps:** Treating "yeast" as just "fermentation" and burying autolysis / flor biology in vague language; forgetting that flor wines must remain dry because flor metabolises sugar and ethanol; predicting Port for the "yeast" slot.

## In-taste matrix

### W1
- **Snapshot:** Pale gold, fine persistent mousse, brioche / biscuit / toast autolysis on a citrus core.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Sparkling · high acid · light-medium body · Brut → Extra Brut · ~12%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Extended lees · little oak · reductive base wine with autolytic depth.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Traditional-method sparkling — autolysis archetype.
- **Key production lever:** Long lees ageing post-tirage; *Saccharomyces* dies, autolyses, and releases mannoproteins, polysaccharides, and nucleotides into the wine.
- **Style consequence:** Brioche / biscuit / toast aromatics + creamy mid-palate from autolytic mannoproteins.
- **Origin (not scored):** France — Champagne.
- **Variety:** Pinot Noir / Chardonnay / Pinot Meunier classic blend.
- **Quality / style:** Vintage Champagne benchmark of yeast autolysis; high quality, dry, structured.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Vintage English sparkling (same autolysis principle, leaner acid frame).

### W2
- **Snapshot:** Pale pink (rosé), fine persistent mousse, red-fruit core with biscuity autolysis, very high acidity.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Sparkling rosé · very high acid · light body · Brut · ~12%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Lees autolysis · no oak · reductive · red-fruit primary intact.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Traditional-method sparkling rosé — cool-climate English autolytic.
- **Key production lever:** Saignée or blending for rosé colour; second fermentation in bottle on lees.
- **Style consequence:** Champagne-like autolytic mechanism applied to a cooler base producing very high acid and pure red-fruit character.
- **Origin (not scored):** England — Cornwall or southern counties.
- **Variety:** Pinot Noir / Pinot Meunier / Chardonnay (Champagne varieties on English chalk-belt soils).
- **Quality / style:** Emerging-region premium quality, less developed than Champagne, fresher red-fruit signature.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Crémant de Limoux or Crémant de Bourgogne rosé if the autolytic complexity reads more polished.

### W3
- **Snapshot:** Pale straw-gold, bone-dry, saline / chamomile / almond / bruised-apple, no bubbles.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still fortified · med-high acid · light body · bone dry · 15%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Long biological ageing under flor in butt; some oxidation post-flor possible.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Biologically aged fortified wine — flor system.
- **Key production lever:** Solera and criadera fractional blending; live *Saccharomyces beticus / cerevisiae* film (flor) consuming glycerol and ethanol, producing acetaldehyde; static fortification to ~15% (the upper limit flor will tolerate).
- **Style consequence:** Aldehydic apple-bruise / saline / yeasty / chamomile signature; bone-dry because flor consumes residual sugar and glycerol.
- **Origin (not scored):** Spain — Jerez or Sanlúcar de Barrameda.
- **Variety:** Palomino.
- **Quality / style:** Quality Fino or Manzanilla; saline freshness preserved by ongoing flor, narrow style range, exam-classic.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Amontillado (post-flor oxidative) if walnut character dominates over saline freshness.

### W4
- **Snapshot:** Deep gold, walnut / curry / hazelnut / dried-apple oxidative complexity, dry, no spirit warmth.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still **unfortified** · high acid · medium body · bone dry · 13–14%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Long oxidative ageing in cask under a partial voile of yeast — barrels not topped up.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Voile-aged Jura oxidative white — unfortified flor-analogue.
- **Key production lever:** Aged ouillé-style at first, then sous voile (under a thin yeast film) for 60+ months without topping the barrel; minimum 6 years 3 months for Vin Jaune.
- **Style consequence:** Walnut / curry / dried-fruit oxidative complexity with no spirit heat (key contrast vs Sherry); high acid preserved.
- **Origin (not scored):** France — Jura — Arbois / L'Étoile / Château-Chalon.
- **Variety:** Savagnin.
- **Quality / style:** Benchmark voile-aged Jura; cult quality status; distinctive oxidative depth without fortification.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Oxidative Tokaji Szamorodni száraz if the curry character is muted and a more central-European-honey signature appears.

## Anti-collapse note
The yeast-axis question deliberately splits into autolysis (sparkling) vs biological aging under a yeast film (flor / voile). Reserve two slots per axis end. Do not let Champagne knowledge crowd out the Sherry / Jura half.

## Reality check (post-prediction)

Actual wines (from data/exams.json):
- W1: Billecart-Salmon Vintage Extra Brut, 2006. Champagne, France.
- W2: Knightor Vineyard Rosé NV. Cornwall, England.
- W3: Inocente Fino, Valdespino, NV. Jerez, Spain.
- W4: Arbois Savagnin, Domaine de la Pinte, 2006. Jura, France.

Pre-taste accuracy:
- Two-and-two yeast structure: confirmed exactly. Two autolytic sparklers + one flor Sherry + one voile-aged Jura.
- Country spread: France + England + Spain + France — all within the European stem.

In-taste accuracy:
- W1 Billecart-Salmon Vintage Extra Brut confirms the autolysis archetype.
- W2 Knightor Rosé adds the rosé twist but does not break the autolytic-sparkling family.
- W3 Inocente Fino confirms the flor branch.
- W4 Arbois Savagnin from Domaine de la Pinte confirms the voile branch.

Lesson:
A four-wine "yeast role" P3 stem reliably splits two-and-two between autolytic sparkling and biological-aging-under-yeast-film. Champagne + English sparkling + Sherry + Jura is the corpus-default template. The discriminating in-taste markers are: bubbles + brioche (autolysis), saline + flor-derived aldehyde + fortification (Sherry), and walnut + oxidation + no spirit heat + unfortified (Jura).
