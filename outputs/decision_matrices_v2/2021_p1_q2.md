# Decision matrix v2 — 2021 P1 Q2

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 5-7 are from the same single grape variety.

With reference to all three wines:

a) Compare and contrast quality and maturity. (36 marks)

b) Identify the variety. (15 marks)

For each wine:

c) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (3 x 8 marks)

## Pre-taste matrix

```
Paper:        P1 (white still wines)
Family:       F1b — same single grape variety, cross-country (Loire x2 + South Africa)
Stem summary: Three-wine same-variety set; quality and maturity comparison dominates at 36 marks
Mark map:     36 quality/maturity / 15 variety / 24 origin — quality/maturity is the exam's centre of gravity
Constraint:   same single grape variety; "compare and contrast" across all three requires a variety with extreme quality ceiling-to-floor range AND genuine ageing capacity across origins
```

**Variety lock:** single unnamed white grape. The 36-mark quality/maturity comparison is the routing signal: the variety must have (1) a dramatic quality differential between expressions, (2) genuine ageing capacity (so that maturity differences are meaningful, not cosmetic), (3) recognizable markers at different stages. Chenin Blanc is the standout match — Anjou entry to aged Savennières is one of wine's greatest quality spreads; Loire Chenin is one of the world's most ageable white wines.

**Comparative axis:** quality tier (entry commercial → aged premium), maturity stage (primary/simple → fully developed secondary/tertiary), origin (mass-production Anjou → appellation-specific Savennières → New World artisan)

**Sub-form:** F1b cross-country (France two origins + South Africa)

**Candidate map (by slot):**

- Slot W5: STRONG SIGNAL — Anjou Chenin Blanc, Loire Valley, France. Entry commercial; simple quince/apple; lighter body and acid, short finish.
- Slot W6: STRONG SIGNAL — Savennières, Loire Valley, France. Premium aged expression (vintage 2007 or similar); textural density, phenolic grip, honey/wax/lanolin, very high preserved acid, very long finish.
- Slot W7: PLAUSIBLE — South Africa Chenin Blanc, Swartland or Citrusdal Mountains; artisan old-vine; quality between entry and premium; more tropical fruit density than Anjou but less tertiary complexity than aged Savennières.

**F1b pre-taste decision logic (36-mark quality/maturity emphasis):**

F1b tree guidance: "Wax + lanolin + quince + dry phenolic → Chenin Blanc (Loire / South Africa)." When the quality/maturity block carries 36 marks, Chenin Blanc is promoted to STRONG SIGNAL ahead of Chardonnay and Riesling — its quality floor-to-ceiling differential is more dramatic than either (cheap Anjou vs. Savennières Grand Vin is arguably the widest quality spread of any single white grape). The 2007 Savennières, if that is the aged wine, would be 14 years old at the 2021 exam — genuinely mature and producing tertiary development rarely seen in blind tasting conditions.

**Anti-collapse rule:** do not predict the same appellation for two of the three wines. The question says "same single grape variety," not "same country." With three wines, at minimum one must be cross-country (South Africa Chenin is the canonical third member). Loire-Loire-Loire would not generate a 24-mark origin section.

**Single rule-out worth naming:** Sauvignon Blanc — eliminated by limited ageing potential; the 36-mark maturity question is not achievable with three Sauvignon Blancs.

## Anti-collapse note

All three wines share the Chenin Blanc grape, but quality, maturity, and origin diverge maximally. The 36-mark comparison requires the candidate to articulate the quality ladder in classification language, not just adjectives: "entry commercial Anjou versus flagship appellation Savennières is the widest quality differential available from a single white grape." The maturity answer requires naming tertiary markers (honey, wax, TDN, lanolin, beeswax) for the aged Savennières versus simple primary fruit on the Anjou, with the South Africa wine positioned between them. Do not collapse W6 into a generic "old white wine" description — Savennières' defining characteristic is that its extreme acidity means it ages without oxidative tiredness.
