## F7 tasting branch order

1. Confirm region — Bordeaux on both wines: cassis / plum / cedar / tobacco / graphite family
2. Place each wine on the classification ladder: Left Bank (cassis + graphite + firm tannin) vs. Right Bank (plum + chocolate + rounder tannin)
3. Translate tier into classification language: "consistent with a cru classé of the Médoc" / "consistent with a Grand Cru Classé of Saint-Émilion"
4. Relative quality: compare length, concentration, complexity, and integration — which wine shows the finer tannin grain, the longer finish, the more complex nose?
5. Maturity / vintage overlay: colour evolution (browning = older), aromatic development (leather, tobacco, dried fruit, cigar box = secondary/tertiary), structural integration — then name the vintage

## Quality calibration notes (F7 conservative-calibration rule)
The 18-mark relative quality question rewards candidates who use the region's own language:
- Left Bank: "consistent with a 2nd or 3rd growth given the concentration, length, and fine tannin structure — more precise classification would require vineyard-level knowledge"
- Right Bank: "the weight, complexity, and structured elegance suggest Grand Cru Classé rather than Premier Grand Cru Classé, but clearly above generic Grand Cru quality"

Do not over-claim. "This must be Leoville-Las Cases" is a bold assertion. "This is consistent with a premier 2nd growth of Saint-Julien" is a calibrated claim.

## What to focus on in the glass (F7-informed)

- Cabernet markers (Left Bank): cassis, blackcurrant, graphite, cedar, firm and linear tannins
- Merlot markers (Right Bank): plum, blueberry, chocolate, softer / rounder tannins; slightly higher alcohol than Left Bank in warm years
- Tannin quality across both wines: the great vintage shows polished, integrated tannins even at 10+ years of age
- Secondary development: cigar box, leather, tobacco, dried fruit — the older the vintage, the more evolved these secondary notes
- Vintage character tells: deep colour + concentrated fruit + fine integrated tannins = warm / great year (2005 / 2009); any greenness or lighter body = less ideal year
- Alcohol: Left Bank typically 13–13.5%; Right Bank slightly warmer at 13.5%
