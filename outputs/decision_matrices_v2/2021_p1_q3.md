### Anti-collapse rule
Do not predict both wines at the same tier. The entire point of a same-producer pair question is to identify the classification difference. If both wines taste similar, look harder — the Smaragd should be materially richer, fuller, and longer than the Federspiel from the same producer.

### Conservative-calibration rule
When the two wines taste very similar (same producer fingerprint), do not over-claim the tier difference. Federspiel and Smaragd from the same vintage and producer share a house style; the difference is one of degree (concentration, length, alcohol) rather than kind (variety, winemaking philosophy).
