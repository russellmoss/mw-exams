## IMW's likely intent
After the breadth test of Q1 and the sub-regional finesse of Q2/Q3, this question rewards intimate knowledge of Mendoza's quality hierarchy. The 20-mark style/quality sub-question tests articulation of what separates entry Malbec from premium (tannin grain, concentration, oak integration, finish length). The market potential question tests commercial literacy: mid-tier Malbec sold on high street vs fine wine retail and restaurant lists.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Both should show violet/purple hue, plum/blackberry, moderate chocolate — Malbec signature across tiers.
- Quality differentiator: tannin grain (coarser and sweeter vs fine-grained and structural), fruit depth, concentration, finish length.
- The premium wine: denser palate, more concentrated dark fruit, better-integrated oak, longer finish.
- Alcohol: Uco Valley typically 13.5–14.5%; note whether one wine reads cooler and fresher.
- Oak handling: entry = sweeter, more obvious; premium = more integrated, higher-quality vessel.
