### W5
- **Snapshot:** Pale-mid salmon-pink, fine persistent mousse, brioche autolysis + red-fruit underlay, dry.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Sparkling rosé · high acid · medium body · Brut · ~12%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Extended lees autolysis · little oak · reductive base with autolytic depth.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Traditional-method sparkling rosé — saignée or blending.
- **Key production lever:** Either short skin maceration of Pinot Noir to extract pink colour before pressing (saignée) or blending white base wine with finished still Pinot Noir / Meunier (blending — Champagne method); then traditional-method second fermentation in bottle on lees.
- **Style consequence:** Champagne fingerprint applied to a rosé — fine mousse, biscuity autolysis, red-fruit core, dry palate, premium structure.
- **Origin:** France — Champagne.
- **Variety:** Pinot Noir + Pinot Meunier + Chardonnay classic blend.
- **Quality / commercial potential:** Luxury gifting; Michelin / fine-dining on-trade; ~€60+ retail. Commercial opportunity = the sparkling-rosé category is growing rapidly; challenge = price-tier pressure and grower-Champagne competition.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Cap Classique rosé or English sparkling rosé if reads cool-climate / more austere with leaner red fruit.

### W6
- **Snapshot:** Pale salmon / onion-skin, dry, red-fruit + Mediterranean herb + sometimes oak frame, no bubbles, fuller body.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still rosé · med acid · medium-full body · dry · ~14%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Premium Provence rosés may show oak ageing (Les Clans, Whispering Angel premium tiers); otherwise reductive stainless steel.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Direct-press still rosé — Provence Cru Classé / premium Côtes de Provence pathway.
- **Key production lever:** Whole-bunch direct press of red grapes — very short juice-skin contact yields pale salmon colour; immediate pressing; cool fermentation; primary fruit + Provence herb signature.
- **Style consequence:** Pale colour + dry palate + red-currant / strawberry / herbal lift + medium-full body; premium expressions show oak texture.
- **Origin:** France — Provence — Côtes de Provence Cru Classé.
- **Variety:** Grenache + Cinsault + Syrah + Vermentino (Rolle) blend.
- **Quality / commercial potential:** Premium dry rosé market is buoyant; Provence Cru Classé sits at the top of the still-rosé hierarchy; gift, on-trade, and lifestyle-luxury positioning.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Tavel (darker, fuller, more red-fruit) or Whispering Angel mainstream Provence (lighter, less complex) depending on weight.

### W7
- **Snapshot:** Mid-pink, slight pétillance, off-dry / semi-sweet, sweet red fruit + slight CO2 prickle, low body.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Pétillant rosé · med acid · light body · off-dry → medium · ~11%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** No oak; tank-method retention of light CO2; primary fruit.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Commercial off-dry lightly sparkling rosé — Portuguese tank-method pathway (Mateus archetype).
- **Key production lever:** Tank fermentation; retention of natural or added CO2 to give light pétillance; some RS retained to give off-dry style; mass-volume bottling.
- **Style consequence:** Sweet-fruited light-bodied lightly fizzy rosé with broad commercial appeal.
- **Origin:** Portugal — multi-region commercial blend.
- **Variety:** Baga + Touriga Nacional + other Portuguese blend.
- **Quality / commercial potential:** Volume / supermarket / impulse / lifestyle; ~€5–8 retail; one of the world's best-selling rosé brands. Commercial opportunity = scale and brand recognition; challenge = perceived as "old-fashioned" by younger drinkers and squeezed by premium Provence on the upmarket end and commodity rosé on the value end.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Lancers (sister Portuguese brand) or Italian Lambrusco rosato if Italian character; less likely.

## Method discipline
The three production methods named explicitly in the matrix:
- Traditional-method sparkling (saignée or blending plus tirage + lees).
- Direct-press still (whole-bunch press, minimal skin contact, dry premium).
- Tank-fermented off-dry pétillant (commercial light pétillance, residual sugar retained).

The matrix must articulate one method per slot in production-technique language, not just style description.

## Anti-collapse note
Do not predict three from same category (e.g. three still rosés). The stem's "different methods" wording, paired with three wines, points to three pathways for producing rosé.
