### Variety lock
F1b sub-form: Branch 1.1 — Cross-country single variety.

Stem signals: Q1 position in the paper (typically a recognizable international variety); three wines with implied different origins; 30 marks on styles and consumer appeal. The F1b tree note is explicit: "when sub-question (b) asks for consumer appeal comparison across three wines, Syrah/Shiraz is the stronger fit because the cool/warm axis generates the most compelling consumer differentiation story."

- **Syrah/Shiraz: STRONG SIGNAL** — the cool (Northern Rhône, peppery, restrained, niche) to warm (Barossa, jammy, blockbuster, accessible) arc is the widest stylistic range of any single red variety and maps directly onto a consumer segmentation story: prestige French collector vs mainstream Australian supermarket shopper. The three-country structure (Old World cool, New World warm, mid-weight emerging) is tailor-made for this variety. 2024 P2 Q1 is explicitly listed in the tree's evidence base.
- **Pinot Noir: STRONG SIGNAL** — maximum terroir transparency; excellent style range; but three origins without a pairing structure is less typical for Pinot at Q1.
- **Cabernet Sauvignon: PLAUSIBLE** — globally grown but style discussion is less dramatic (warm everywhere except Bordeaux).
- **Merlot: PLAUSIBLE** — possible but fewer interesting country comparisons.
- **Cabernet Franc: CURVEBALL** — used in 2025 P2 Q1; unlikely to repeat in consecutive years.

## IMW's likely intent
Testing whether the candidate can: (1) identify a major red variety across three contrasting climate expressions, (2) articulate why the same grape tastes different in cool vs warm climates, (3) translate those stylistic differences into consumer appeal language (who buys Cornas vs who buys Barossa Shiraz, at what price, through what channel). Origin is secondary (10 marks each); the market analysis is the real test. This is the classic "Syrah vs Shiraz" question that MW candidates should approach as a set-piece.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Colour depth and hue: deeper/inky = warm-climate Shiraz; transparent garnet = cool-climate Syrah or Pinot Noir.
- Aromatics: black pepper + violet + olive = Northern Rhône; dark chocolate + licorice + overripe blackberry = Barossa; mid-weight fruit = South American Syrah.
- Tannin structure: grippy/firm with savour (cool-climate Syrah) vs plush/sweet (warm Barossa).
- Alcohol: 12–12.5% = cool Old World Northern Rhône; 14–14.5% = warm New World — key consumer appeal differentiator.
- Oak: new French oak (spice, toast) vs American oak (coconut, vanilla) vs minimal oak.
- Climate gradient: order the three wines from coolest to warmest by alcohol and fruit-ripeness to confirm the Syrah/Shiraz arc.
