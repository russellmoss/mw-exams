## F2 pre-taste analysis

**Origin lock:** Champagne. P3 Q1 sparkling-opening pattern (7 of 10 corpus years open P3 with a sparkling question). "Same region + compare production method" in a sparkling context with a 25-mark commercial appeal discussion maps directly to Champagne: the only sparkling region where NV vs. vintage or house vs. grower generates this depth of commercial discussion.

**Internal divergence axis:** Production method within Champagne. The two production paths that generate the most contrast within one region:
1. NV house Champagne: multi-vintage blend with reserve wines; assemblage philosophy; consistent house style; dosage typically 8–12 g/L Brut; short post-disgorgement shelf life; global commercial volume
2. Vintage/grower Champagne: single-year fruit; extended lees aging (minimum 3 years by regulation, often 5–12 years for quality houses); zero-to-low dosage (Brut Nature / Extra Brut); terroir-specific character; limited volume; premium price

**Sub-form: F2d (Branch 2.3 — same region, different elevage/production):** The family tree pack working priors for Champagne F2d: NV vs. vintage Champagne; vintage vs. prestige cuvée; commercial house style vs. grower-récoltant expression.

The IMW wants the candidate to articulate Champagne's two-tier commercial world: global NV market dominated by large houses vs. the premium grower and vintage market that is reshaping the category's fine-wine positioning.

## IMW's likely intent

Testing understanding of how production choices within Champagne generate different commercial propositions. The 15 marks on production test: reserve wine system, dosage policy, lees aging duration, vintage vs. non-vintage philosophy, and the legal framework (NV minimum 15 months; vintage minimum 36 months). The 25 marks on commercial appeal test: Champagne's brand-driven NV market (value at scale, consistent style, major house identity) vs. the emerging grower and vintage market (single-vineyard identity, terroir narrative, fine-wine consumer, higher price). These are live commercial debates in the MW community and in the trade press.

## What to focus on in the glass

- Mousse persistence and bead size: both should show fine, persistent bead if traditional method; the vintage/grower wine often shows tighter, more persistent mousse from longer lees contact
- Autolytic character: brioche/toast/biscuit more pronounced on vintage/grower; fresher and simpler fruit on NV commercial
- Dosage perception: slightly drier and more precise finish on lower-dosage vintage/grower wine; rounder and softer on commercial NV
- Complexity and length: vintage/grower shows greater finish length and vineyard-specific character; NV commercial shows accessible, balanced fruit-forward profile
- Colour depth: NV often pale straw; vintage may show slightly deeper gold from extended aging
