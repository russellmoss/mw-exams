### Origin lock
Same region — a single broad region whose two sub-zones have enough winemaking and stylistic divergence to generate 7 marks each on techniques alone. This is a key filter: the region must offer two wines with genuinely different winemaking stories within one regional boundary.

### Internal divergence axis
F2d: same broad origin, but sub-zone divergence in production technique and style. The wines share a regional label but differ in variety, sub-appellation, and winemaking philosophy.

### Sub-form
F2d: same origin, different sub-zone / production style.

### Candidate map

Countries used in this paper so far: France (Q1 Beaujolais), non-France (Q2 Pinot Noir cross-country), France (Q3 Bordeaux). Bordeaux and Beaujolais are spent as French regions. The natural remaining French option for a winemaking-heavy two-wine pair is the **Rhône Valley**.

The Rhône Valley uniquely supports F2d because: its Northern (Syrah, cool granitic slopes, whole-cluster vinification, possible co-fermentation with Viognier) and Southern (Grenache-dominant GSM blends, galets roulés, high alcohol, multi-variety blending) sub-zones are radically different in technique while sharing one broad regional name.
