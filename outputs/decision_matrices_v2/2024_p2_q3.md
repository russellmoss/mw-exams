## IMW's likely intent
The definitive "international variety across terroirs" question. Candidates must demonstrate: (1) confident variety ID across five expressions (25 marks — the largest single variety block in the corpus), (2) understanding of how cool continental vs maritime vs southern hemisphere climates shape the same grape, (3) knowledge of winemaking approaches (extraction level, whole-cluster inclusion, oak regime, malo) that differ across the five countries. The 5-mark origin de-emphasizes geography; the 15-mark climate/winemaking essay per wine is where the question is won or lost.

## What to focus on in the glass
- Colour transparency: Pinot = translucent; never opaque. If any wine shows deep colour, immediately reconsider.
- Aromatic profile: red fruit/earth/mushroom = Pinot; heavy black pepper or dark fruit = reconsider Syrah.
- Tannin fineness: silky/fine = Pinot hallmark; chunky tannin = eliminated.
- Whole-cluster indicators: stem spice, herbal lift — common in Burgundy, some NZ and Oregon examples.
- Acid/alcohol gradient: map the five wines coolest to warmest by alcohol and acidity. Pinot Noir from five cool-climate countries typically clusters in the 13–13.5% band, unlike Syrah/Shiraz.
- Oak treatment: subtle/invisible = Burgundy or German; more evident = some New World.
