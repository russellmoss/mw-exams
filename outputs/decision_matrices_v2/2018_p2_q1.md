### Branch: F4c — hidden organising theme
The theme ("classic Western European origins") is a filter, not a narrower. It rules out any wine from outside western Europe's established fine-wine canon. It does NOT mean all five wines are from France, or all from the same tier — it means the candidate should expect the Big Five European red regions.

**Theme as filter:** Rules out New World entirely. Rules out Languedoc basic regional. Rules out German reds (Spätburgunder). Rules out Greek, Austrian, Hungarian, Eastern European reds. Admits: Burgundy, Bordeaux, Northern Rhône, Piedmont, Tuscany, Rioja, Douro.

**Five-wine structure:** With five classic Western European reds, the IMW is effectively asking for a tour of Europe's greatest red appellations. France may claim two or three slots (Burgundy, Rhône, Bordeaux). Italy claims one (Piedmont or Tuscany — rarely both). Spain claims one (Rioja).
