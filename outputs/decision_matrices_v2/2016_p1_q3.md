## Anti-collapse note

The greatest exam risk here is failing to identify both wines as the same variety. The richness of W5 (Alsace) and the neutrality of W6 (Oregon/Italian) can read as different varieties to an unprepared palate. The connecting thread is the faint waxy / stone-fruit texture and the absence of diagnostic markers from other varieties (no petrol of Riesling, no pyrazine of SB, no lychee/rose of Gewürztraminer). Spending time in the glass to exclude other varieties will confirm the connection.
