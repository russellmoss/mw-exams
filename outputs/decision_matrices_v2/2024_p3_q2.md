**Internal divergence axis:** Oxidative vs. protective winemaking philosophy. The two paths in Jura:
1. Modern protective/ouillage: barrels filled completely (no headspace), oxygen excluded throughout aging; fresh fruit, mineral, citrus character preserved; this is the wine Jura is trying to market to the broader wine public
2. The candidate must explain the sous voile mechanism specifically: what the voile (flor/voile yeast film) is, what it does to wine chemistry (acetaldehyde production, sotolon formation, slow oxidation while protecting from gross oxidation), and why the traditional oxidative style generates walnut/curry/hazelnut character. The commercial potential sub-question (20 marks, the heaviest) asks the candidate to assess both styles on their commercial merits: protective Jura is growing but still small; oxidative Jura is shrinking commercially but has a passionate following and strong on-premise identity.

## What to focus on in the glass

- Colour contrast: pale straw/green (protective) vs. deep gold/amber (oxidative) — this is the immediate first diagnostic
- Nose: walnut/curry/dried mushroom/marmite (sous voile) vs. fresh citrus/mineral/stone fruit (protective) — usually decisive within seconds
- Palate weight: oxidative wines show more extract and textural power; protective wines are leaner and more delicate
- Acidity: both should retain good Jura acidity — if acid is absent, question the Jura origin
- Alcohol: oxidative styles often show higher alcohol (13–14.5%); protective styles typically 12–13%
- Finish: oxidative — distinctive long nutty/spiced finish; protective — clean citrus/mineral finish
