# Decision matrix v2 — 2021 P3 Q3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 9-10 and 11-12 are two pairs. Each pair is from a different region of origin.

For each pair:

a) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (2 x 10 marks)

b) Compare and contrast the style, quality and commercial position of the two wines. (2 x 20 marks)

For each wine:

c) Comment on the method of production. (4 x 10 marks)

## Pre-taste matrix

### Universal header

- **Family:** F4b Mixed Identification Breadth — *partly linked* (two pairs from two regions; within-pair link is sanctioned, cross-pair link is not)
- **Stem summary:** Four wines arranged as two same-region pairs (different regions); per-pair comparison + per-wine method = the heaviest production marks in any P3 question.
- **Mark map:** origin per pair 10 each (20 total) · within-pair style + quality + commercial comparison 20 each (40 total) · **method per wine 10 each (40 total — dominant)** = 100 total.
- **Constraint:** two same-region pairs; different regions; production knowledge is the deciding band.

### F4 pre-taste matrix
- **Pure breadth (F4a) / partly linked (F4b) / hidden theme (F4c):** F4b — within-pair link sanctioned and required (same region of origin); cross-pair link is not (two separate regions). The within-pair pair behaves like a mini-F2 same-origin.
- **Theme (if any):** Fortified / oxidative dual-region pair. The 10-mark-per-wine production weighting (40 marks for production) is the heaviest in any P3 question. Only two regions can sustain four 10-mark production discussions and within-pair commercial contrast: **Jerez** (Sherry styles) and **Madeira** (Madeira styles). Both regions are method-rich, multi-tier, and have clear within-region quality / sweetness segmentation.

- **Slot ballots (independent per pair, cross-pair independence enforced):**
  - **Pair A (W9-10) — Jerez:**
    - W9: dry Oloroso (or Palo Cortado) — concentrated, oxidative-aged, non-sweet → **STRONG SIGNAL**
    - W10: sweet Oloroso Dulce or Matusalem-style 30-year (sweetened with PX or arrope) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
    - PLAUSIBLE swap-ins: dry Amontillado vs PX; Fino vs Oloroso (less likely because Fino is more typical for opening flights).
  - **Pair B (W11-12) — Madeira:**
    - W11: entry-tier (3yo Full Rich, Tinta Negra-based, estufagem) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
    - W12: aged single-harvest Boal / Verdelho / Sercial Colheita or Frasqueira (canteiro, noble grape, age statement) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
    - PLAUSIBLE swap-ins: 10yo vs 20yo single-noble-grape Madeira (less dramatic but still pair-valid).
  - PLAUSIBLE alternative regional pairs (if Sherry/Madeira are not chosen): two Ports (Tawny vs Vintage); two Tokaji (Dry vs Aszú) — but the same-producer pattern (Henriques & Henriques twice in Madeira) is the corpus tell.
  - CURVEBALL: Marsala Vergine pair; Sicilian Passito di Pantelleria pair.

- **Hard rule:** Within-pair link = sanctioned (must articulate both wines as the same region). Cross-pair link = forbidden (Pair A and Pair B are independent regions). The within-pair "compare and contrast" 20-mark band rewards explicit articulation of *differences within the shared region*: sweetness (Sherry pair), age + grape variety + cask method (Madeira pair).

- **Default rule-outs:** Port (already used in Q2); Champagne (Q1 territory); dry table wines (the 10-mark method weighting forces fortified / oxidative).

- **Main traps:** Treating the four wines as four independent slots (the same-region pair link is sanctioned and must be used); writing four separate method answers without showing the within-region method ladder (estufagem → canteiro for Madeira; flor-then-oxidative → pure-oxidative-with-PX-blending for Sherry); under-weighting the 40 production marks.
