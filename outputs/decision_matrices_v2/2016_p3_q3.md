- **Hard rule:** "Do not infer wine N from wine N−1." Even with the botrytis filter, the two wines should be assessed on their own merit. The Tokaji-vs-Sauternes split is the *prior*, not the answer.

- **Main traps:** Treating both wines as a single composite (Sauternes-Tokaji blend in the head); under-using the Furmint vs Sémillon distinction (the 10-mark "variety" emphasis demands varietal discrimination); skipping the maturity band on the assumption that botrytis sweet wines are timeless (they have measurable optimum windows).
