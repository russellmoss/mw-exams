# Decision matrix v2 — 2021 P1 Q1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-4 all come from the same country.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin and variety/ies used. (4 x 10 marks)

b) Comment on quality and maturity. (4 x 10 marks)

c) Comment on the method of production. (4 x 5 marks)

**Origin lock:** Same country, unnamed. Four different white varieties or styles — requires the country with the deepest white wine diversity bench. Quality and maturity each worth 10 marks — wines must be at different development stages.

**Internal divergence axis:** Variety + production method across four slots; quality and maturity across different development stages within one country.

**Sub-form:** F2a — same country, different varieties.

**Country candidates within F2 universe:**

France is the opening question (Q1 of P1 2021) — this is the default slot for France in the corpus. France is the only country that can fill four distinctly different sub-regional variety slots (Jura, Burgundy, Rhône, Alsace) with wines that each merit their own 10-mark origin + variety discussion, 10-mark quality/maturity discussion, and 5-mark production method. The 5-mark method sub-question is the diagnostic: at least one wine will have a technically unusual or highly distinctive production method worth discussing (Vin Jaune's sous voile/voile aging under a film of yeast without topping is the canonical P1 curveball production method — it yields 5 marks of very specific discussion).

## In-taste matrix

### Wine 1

```
Wine slot:  W1
Snapshot:   Deep gold to amber; walnut, curry, dried apricot, toast, nutty oxidative — tertiary-dominated, powerful
Style:      still white
Acid:       medium (oxidative handling softens perception)
Body:       full
Sugar:      bone dry
Alcohol:    ~14–15%
Oak / lees / oxidation: sous voile (biological film) oxidative aging; no topping up; very specific nutty oxidative signature
```

**Confirm bound origin (France):** Oxidative, walnut/curry aromatics + high alcohol + bone dry palate = Vin Jaune, Jura. The walnut/curry combination is the single most distinctive aroma fingerprint in white wine globally. No other wine style replicates it.

**Place on divergence axis:** Most technically distinctive production method in the flight; curveball slot; maximum age-worthiness.

**Sub-region:** Arbois or Château-Chalon AOP, Jura, France. Variety: Savagnin (100%). Production: aging under voile (similar to sherry flor) without topping up, typically 6+ years.

**Classification / tier:** Vin Jaune (legal style designation); Château-Chalon is the prestige sub-appellation; Arbois Vin Jaune is the most common exam-level example. Sold in clavelin (62cl).

**Quality in regional context:** Benchmark quality for the appellation; demands age; develops complexity over decades. Maturity: a 2013 vintage (8 years old at exam time) is still in primary-to-secondary development for the style.

**Branch-B alternative:** Aged Jura Chardonnay under partial voile (Ouillé style with some oxidative character); Chablis-aged (less likely given the walnut/curry markers are absent in Chablis).

## Anti-collapse note

The four slots span Jura, Burgundy, Rhône, and Alsace — France's "four corners" white wine diversity. The Vin Jaune (W1) is the production-method curveball that earns its 5-mark production sub-question; any candidate who explains sous voile aging and the absence of topping-up has a decisive advantage. Do not flatten the flight by over-assigning Chardonnay to multiple slots: W3 (Rhône blend) and W4 (Pinot Gris) are not Chardonnay wines.
