## Anti-collapse note

The two wines taste similar in variety signature but radically different in quality register. The risk is under-ranking W3 (dismissing it as "bad wine") rather than explaining precisely why its winemaking choices produce a lower-quality outcome. Marks at 20 require a benchmarked regional argument, not just a quality adjective. The post-fermentation maturation sub-question (14 marks) is where the candidate must name specific techniques: barrel fermentation vs ageing, new vs used oak percentage, MLF extent, batonnage frequency — not just "it was oaked."
