a) Identify the origin and variety(ies) as closely as possible. (4 x 12 marks)

Italy is uniquely qualified for F2a four-wine format because its regional indigenous varieties are each unmistakable and exam-recognized: Nebbiolo (Piedmont), Sangiovese (Tuscany), Montepulciano (Abruzzo), Nerello Mascalese (Sicily). ### Anti-collapse rule
Do not predict the same variety for two different slots. Italy's F2a template requires each slot to occupy a different regional variety. Do not predict two Sangiovese or two Nebbiolo wines — the "different regions" constraint makes this redundant.

### Single rule-out worth naming
France and Australia are both effectively used in Q1 (Cabernet Sauvignon cross-country). Spain, while possible, has fewer dramatic regional variety contrasts for four wines. Italy is the only country where four genuinely distinct sensory fingerprints are available at exam-recognized quality level.
