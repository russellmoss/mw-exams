Wines 1 and 2 are sparkling wines, neither of the wines are from Champagne.

For each wine:

a) Discuss the quality, winemaking, and style. (2 x 15 marks)

For both wines:

b) Compare and contrast the commercial opportunities and challenges. (20 marks)

  - Slot W2: Cava (Reserva or Gran Reserva, Brut Nature) → **STRONG SIGNAL**. Quality-tier Cava pushing back against commodity perception, mid-price, traditional-method native grapes.

- **Anti-collapse rule:** A two-wine flight with a 20-mark commercial sub-question demands *contrasting* commercial narratives — do not predict two wines from the same commercial position. The prototype is "one prestige niche + one volume/value category fighting for premium recognition."

- **Default rule-outs:** Champagne (stem); Prosecco DOC basic (insufficient winemaking depth for 15 marks); pet-nat (too niche for a 20-mark commercial contrast); Asti / Moscato d'Asti (off-axis for the question).

- **Main traps:** Predicting two wines from the same commercial archetype (e.g. two prestige English sparklers); under-rating the commercial 20-mark band as an afterthought; spending 15 winemaking marks on origin guessing when origin is not asked.

## In-taste matrix

### W1
- **Snapshot:** Pale-mid gold with very fine persistent mousse; deep brioche / hazelnut / toasted-almond autolysis from very long lees ageing.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Sparkling · med-high acid · medium body · Brut → Extra Brut · ~12.3%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Extreme lees autolysis (multi-year tirage) · no oak · reductive base wine but tertiary autolytic depth.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Traditional-method prestige sparkling, heritage / extended-lees archetype.
- **Key production lever:** Vintage base wine plus 8–10+ years on lees before disgorgement; Chardonnay-dominant base; very low dosage.
- **Style consequence:** Champagne-comparable depth via lees time rather than chalk-driven mineral structure; warmer-climate orchard fruit replaces the cool-climate citrus core.
- **Origin (not scored):** California — North Coast / Carneros — prestige American sparkling house.
- **Variety:** Chardonnay-dominant; possibly Pinot Noir component.
- **Quality / commercial position:** **Prestige niche.** $90+ per bottle, US heritage brand, gifting and high-end on-trade, Champagne-comparator pricing. Commercial opportunity: a small but real US luxury demographic; challenge: limited international visibility and difficulty justifying the price away from US patriotism.
- **Branch-B alternative:** English vintage Blanc de Blancs if the wine reads cooler and more austere; demoted here because the autolytic complexity and fruit ripeness suggest a warmer base.

### W2
- **Snapshot:** Pale lemon-green with fine persistent mousse; bone-dry palate, apple / almond / herbal Penedès signature, lower acid than Champagne, broader mid-palate.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Sparkling · med acid · medium body · **Brut Nature** (0–3 g/L) · ~12%.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Moderate to long lees autolysis · no oak · slight oxidative tendency typical of Cava.
- **Method family in one phrase:** Traditional-method sparkling, Cava quality-tier (Reserva / Gran Reserva).
- **Key production lever:** Second fermentation under cap on Penedès native grapes (Xarel-lo / Macabeo / Parellada); Brut Nature dosage; Reserva 18-month minimum lees or Gran Reserva 30+ months.
- **Style consequence:** Mediterranean fruit replaces chalky minerality; Brut Nature removes the dosage-sweetness cushion and exposes the base wine.
- **Origin (not scored):** Spain — Penedès — Cava DO.
- **Variety:** Xarel-lo + Macabeo + Parellada classic Cava blend.
- **Quality / commercial position:** **Volume-with-aspiration.** Cava sits as the world's largest non-Champagne traditional-method category but is fighting commodity perception. Opportunities: Brut Nature, Reserva / Gran Reserva / Cava de Paraje, Corpinnat breakaway. Challenges: average price-per-bottle pulled down by the supermarket Cava segment; consumer confusion about quality ladder; weakening domestic Spanish demand.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Premium Crémant de Bourgogne or Crémant d'Alsace if the wine reads softer and less herbal, with a more orchard-fruit profile.

## Commercial contrast (20-mark band)

The two wines map onto opposite ends of the non-Champagne traditional-method commercial spectrum:

- **W1 prestige niche:** small volume, very high price, single-market visibility, narrative built on heritage and extended lees. Opportunity = upmarket US gifting and Michelin-listed on-trade; challenge = limited reach outside the US and dependence on patriotic brand loyalty.
- **W2 volume-with-aspiration:** very large category volume, low average price, global distribution, narrative built on value and Spanish authenticity. Opportunity = premium-Cava tier (Reserva, Gran Reserva, Brut Nature, Corpinnat) capturing trade-up consumers; challenge = the supermarket Cava tail dragging the category's perceived quality down.

The matrix should explicitly frame the contrast as **niche-prestige vs scale-with-premiumisation tension**, then attribute each wine's commercial opportunities and challenges to those archetypes.

## Anti-collapse note
The 20-mark commercial band is the lead, not a footnote. The matrix must give it as much space as the two 15-mark winemaking bands combined.
