# Decision matrix v2 — 2018 P3 Q3

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 10-12 come from different countries and different single grape varieties (minimum 90%) that are associated with the Rhône Valley.

For each wine:

a) Identify the predominant grape variety used. (3 x 5 marks)

b) Identify the origin as closely as possible. (3 x 8 marks)

c) Comment on the style, quality and commercial potential. (3 x 12 marks)

## Pre-taste matrix

### Universal header
- **Paper:** P3

- **Stem summary:** Three wines from three countries and three different Rhône-associated varieties (≥90% varietal); origin and commercial potential dominate the marks.
- **Mark map:** variety 5 each (15 total) · origin 8 each (24 total) · **style + quality + commercial potential 12 each (36 total — dominant)** = 75 total.
- **Constraint:** variety pool fixed (Grenache / Syrah / Mourvèdre / Cinsault / Viognier / Marsanne / Roussanne); three different countries; varieties grown *outside* the Rhône.

### F4 pre-taste matrix
- **Pure breadth (F4a) / partly linked (F4b) / hidden theme (F4c):** F4c — the hidden theme is the Rhône variety family transplanted elsewhere. The theme governs the variety pool but each origin slot is independent.
- **Theme (if any):** Rhône-variety diaspora. The IMW's evidence base for this question shows that the examiner reads "associated with the Rhône Valley" *broadly* — Cinsault counts even from Lodi, Mourvèdre counts from Bandol, Grenache counts from Spain. Theme used as *filter* and *flag*: this is a curveball-leaning question that prefers less-obvious choices.

- **Slot ballots (independent, no cross-inference):**
  - Slot W10: California or South Africa — **Cinsault** (Birichino-style Lodi old-vine Cinsault; the tree's elevated Cinsault call) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - Slot W11: Spain — **Garnacha** (old-vine, e.g. Terra Alta, La Mancha, El Terrerazo) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - Slot W12: France — **Mourvèdre / Bandol** (technically outside the Rhône proper; serious Mourvèdre expression) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
  - PLAUSIBLE swap-ins: Australian Shiraz (too obvious; the examiner likely avoids); South African Cinsault (Stellenbosch revival scene); Chilean Carignan (not a Rhône variety strictly but adjacent in casting); Viognier (only if one white slot is signalled, which the stem does not).
  - CURVEBALL: Greek Syrah from Drama; US Marsanne / Roussanne; Lebanese Cinsault / Grenache blend (single-variety constraint blocks blend-led Lebanese wines).

- **Hard rule:** "Do not infer wine N from wine N−1." The three Rhône varieties span Grenache / Cinsault / Mourvèdre / Syrah → each variety has its own sensory signature that must be diagnosed independently.

- **Default rule-outs:** Wines from the Rhône itself (stem); white Rhône varieties (stem signals red-dominant by mark structure and commercial framing); obvious-origin Syrah (Barossa Hawke's Bay) — the "associated with" softer language usually signals the IMW avoids the over-obvious.

- **Main traps:** Choosing Syrah-Shiraz from a major region as one of the three (too obvious; the corpus pattern punishes this); collapsing the three wines into a single Rhône-style narrative when the point of the question is to test grape identity *separated from* the grape's home region; missing Cinsault's distinctive low-ABV light profile and pattern-matching it to Pinot Noir.
