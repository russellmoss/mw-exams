### F4 pre-taste matrix

**Classification: Pure breadth (Branch 4.1) — method-led, inverted-marks variant**
Three wines from three different single varieties, no linking constraint. This is a critical departure from the standard F4a identification structure: variety ID is last and worth only 6 marks, method of production comes first. The tree explicitly flags this as a technique-priority outlier — "build from method inward to variety, not the other way."

The appropriate navigational approach: assess production signals first (wild ferment complexity, lees contact, reductive vs. oxidative markers, whole-cluster), then use those signals to confirm variety, not to discover it. The aromatic character will reveal the variety broadly; the method detail confirms the candidate's tasting depth.
