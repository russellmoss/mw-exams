- Alcohol: 13–13.5% (Oregon, NZ) vs. 14%+ (California) — a decisive sensory signal.
- New oak influence: more prominent in California (vanilla undertone) vs. subtle in Oregon/NZ.
- Colour depth: lighter ruby (cool-climate) vs. deeper purple-ruby (warm-climate California).
- Whole-cluster markers: herbal/floral/stemmy structural note; common in Oregon producers.
- Tannin texture: fine-grained and silky (all three) but integration level varies with extraction.
