# Decision matrix v2 — 2022 P3 Q1

## Question (verbatim)
Wines 1-4 are all sparkling rosé wines. Wines 1 and 2 are from one country, and wines 3 and 4 are from another country.

For each wine:

a) Identify the origin as closely as possible and the grape variety(ies) used. (4 x 10 marks)

b) Comment on the key winemaking techniques. (4 x 10 marks)

c) Comment on the quality within the context of the origin. (4 x 5 marks)

The two countries are almost certainly **Italy** (Prosecco Rosé DOC + Lambrusco rosato) and **France** (Champagne rosé + Crémant rosé) — the only countries that simultaneously support a two-tier sparkling-rosé production-spectrum within their borders.

    - W1: Prosecco Rosé DOC (Charmat method; Glera + Pinot Nero; very topical post-2020 category launch) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
    - W2: Lambrusco Rosato Spumante / Frizzante (Charmat or ancestral; lower-ABV; Lambrusco grape varieties) → **PLAUSIBLE / STRONG SIGNAL**
  - **Country B (W3-4) = France:**
    - W3: Champagne Rosé (traditional method; saignée or blending; prestige tier — Bollinger / Billecart-Salmon / Laurent-Perrier archetype) → **STRONG SIGNAL**
    - W4: Crémant rosé (Crémant de Bourgogne / Loire / Alsace / Limoux; traditional method at value tier) → **PLAUSIBLE / STRONG SIGNAL**

None as likely as Italy + France for two reasons: (1) Italy + France between them launched two of the largest commercial sparkling-rosé categories in living memory (Prosecco Rosé DOC 2020; Provence rosé halo lifting Champagne Rosé); (2) the 40 production marks demand within-country method diversity, which only Italy and France can sustain.

- **Hard rule:** "Do not infer wine N from wine N−1." Same-country pair-link is structural (assert two-country structure once) but within the pair each wine has its own method, variety, dosage, and colour-extraction technique.

- **Default rule-outs:** Sekt rosé (too niche for pairing); pet-nat (only the most aware corpus year would include); Australian sparkling rosé (paired structure unlikely); still rosé (stem excludes).

- **Main traps:** Missing Prosecco Rosé DOC's 2020 launch (very topical pre-2022 question); reading both Italian wines as Charmat / both French wines as traditional method without checking each glass; under-articulating rosé colour-extraction techniques (saignée / blending / direct-press) in the 40-mark winemaking band.
