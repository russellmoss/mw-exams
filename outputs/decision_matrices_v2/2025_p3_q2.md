## In-taste matrix guidance (per wine)

**W4 (Cava, Penedès):**
- Confirm bound origin (Spain): traditional-method sparkling; fine, persistent mousse; autolytic character (biscuit, almond, toasted brioche) at Reserva level; lower acid and slightly broader mid-palate than Champagne; apple, pear, citrus fruit; 12% alcohol
- Place on divergence axis: Cava Reserva or Gran Reserva tier — minimum 15 months lees for Reserva (9 months for basic); Gran Reserva minimum 30 months; the autolytic complexity level indicates tier
- Sub-region: Penedès (Sant Sadurní d'Anoia is the heartland); if the wine shows more complexity and precision, Corpinnat (higher-standards sub-group) or Cava de Paraje Calificado are sub-designations to consider
- Winemaking: traditional method (secondary fermentation in bottle, lees aging, riddling/gyropalette, disgorgement, dosage); yeast autolysis during extended lees contact generates the biscuit/brioche complexity
- Quality in regional context: Reserva Cava with genuine autolytic complexity represents the quality tier; Gran Reserva adds finesse and depth
- Commercial position: growing premium sub-category within Cava; competing with Champagne at a significantly lower price point; on-trade and retail both strong; juvé y Camps, Recaredo, Gramona are the quality anchors
- Branch-B alternative: Corpinnat or Penedès TM sparkling if the wine shows higher quality than standard Cava DO

**W5 (Fine still Spanish wine — Garnacha):**
- Confirm bound origin (Spain): dry red wine; red fruit (strawberry, raspberry, cherry), Mediterranean character but with fresh acidity from altitude; moderate tannin; no spirit heat; 14% alcohol typical for Garnacha
- Place on divergence axis: fine artisan still Spanish red — premium sub-region, small production, gastronomy positioning
- Sub-region: Sierra de Gredos / Valle del Tiétar (altitude-driven freshness in Garnacha, distinctive granitic mineral quality) is the most likely answer; Priorat (more concentrated, schist-influenced, higher alcohol) is the alternative; Rioja (oaked, classic structure) is PLAUSIBLE if the wine shows more obvious oak
- Winemaking: at Sierra de Gredos/Priorat premium tier, minimal intervention; whole-cluster fermentation possible; old-vine Garnacha from bush vines; minimal new oak or no oak (clay vessels/used oak); wild yeast fermentation; natural/minimal-addition winemaking increasingly common at this level
- Quality in regional context: premium artisan still wine; assessed against the growing Spanish fine wine ecosystem; Comando G (Sierra de Gredos), Clos Mogador (Priorat) are reference producers
- Commercial position: rising artisan fine wine category; gastronomy and specialist retail; limited production; cult following; natural wine adjacency; growing export market for premium Spanish wine
- Branch-B alternative: Rioja Reserva / Gran Reserva if the wine shows more obvious oak structure and traditional winemaking character

**W6 (Sherry, Jerez):**
- Confirm bound origin (Spain): fortified (20% alcohol from spirit addition); oxidative character — walnut, toffee, caramel, dried fruit; amber to dark brown colour; dry (Oloroso) or off-dry
- Place on divergence axis: aged Sherry — VORS (30-year average age minimum) or VOS (20-year minimum) classification provides the commercial classification anchor
- Sub-region: Jerez (the Sherry Triangle — Jerez de la Frontera, Sanlúcar de Barrameda, El Puerto de Santa María); no sub-commune precision typically needed
- Winemaking: Oloroso pathway (no biological aging, direct oxidative aging in incomplete solera); spirit addition (fortification) to ~17% kills flor; extended oxidative solera aging produces the walnut/toffee/rancio character; VORS = Vinum Optimum Rare Signatum = average solera age minimum 30 years (verified by DO Jerez regulatory body sampling)
- Quality in regional context: VORS is the highest age tier; extreme concentration, complexity, and length justify the classification; assessed against what a 30-year average age Oloroso should deliver — Bodegas Tradición, Lustau, González Byass VORS as reference producers
- Commercial position: hyper-specialist aged Sherry market; collector and on-premise fine dining; limited by production scale; globally misunderstood and commercially disadvantaged relative to quality level; growing sommelier community awareness; the "best value fine wine in the world" narrative is gaining traction
- Branch-B alternative: VOS (20-year) if the concentration and complexity are significant but fall short of VORS intensity; or Amontillado VORS if the wine shows less pure walnut and more hazelnut/dried orange peel character
