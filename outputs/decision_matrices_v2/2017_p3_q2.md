# Decision matrix v2 — 2017 P3 Q2

## Question (verbatim)
Consider wine 4 to be of unknown origin.

For this wine:

a) Highlight the key winemaking techniques used. (15 marks)

b) Discuss its style, quality and possible market positioning. (10 marks)

- **Constraint:** the stem **explicitly de-emphasises origin** — origin is not scored. The candidate must lead with production and commercial logic, not geography.

### F5 pre-taste matrix
- **Method family lock:** wines where the production technique is so distinctive that the wine identifies itself by method rather than by region or variety. The "unknown origin" framing maps directly to the master-tree curveball case for 2017 P3 Q2 ("explicit instruction to treat origin as secondary; style/technique dominates").
- **Sub-form:** F5f hybrid origin-de-emphasised stem (Branch 5.4 of the P3 family tree pack). The candidate should "switch off the variety-first mindset and lead with production logic."

- **Production-family slots (single-wine, candidate method families):**
  - Skin-contact / orange / qvevri-style white → **STRONG SIGNAL**. Amber colour + tannin on a white grape is unmistakable and instantly justifies the "unknown origin" framing.
  - Natural / minimal-intervention wine with overt VA or unusual texture → **PLAUSIBLE**. Often overlaps with skin-contact.
  - Oxidative unfortified white (Jura-style or oxidative Rioja Blanco) → **PLAUSIBLE**. Less likely because Jura and Rioja would normally be identifiable by region.
  - Pet-nat or other ancestral-method sparkling → **CURVEBALL**. Bubbles would be obvious; stem does not mention them.

- **Anti-collapse rule:** Single-wine, not applicable; the rule here is *do not chase origin*. Origin is excluded from the mark scheme. Spend the matrix on method, style, and market positioning.

- **Default rule-outs:** Conventional still wines (the "unknown origin" framing would not be needed for a classical Burgundy or Bordeaux); mainstream sparkling / fortified (separate category questions cover these in P3); botrytis sweet (RS would normally be asked).

- **Main traps:** Spending mental energy on origin guessing when origin is not scored; failing to name a specific production technique (skin maceration on a white, biodynamic / wild ferment, no SO2, extended ageing on lees / skins); writing the market-positioning band in vague terms instead of naming the natural-wine / progressive-on-trade channel.

## In-taste matrix

### W4
- **Snapshot:** Pale-mid amber / orange / copper, dry, phenolic grip / soft tannin on a white-grape base, dried-apple / bruised-apple / oxidative depth + Mediterranean / honeyed lift.
- **Style / acid / body / sugar / alcohol:** Still white-on-skins · med acid · medium-full body · dry · **~14–15%**.
- **Oak / lees / oxidation:** Extended skin maceration; possible oak / amphora ageing; oxidative handling; some natural / minimal-intervention markers (slight VA, mild cloudiness, lees character).
- **Method family in one phrase:** Skin-contact / orange wine — extended-maceration biodynamic white.
- **Key production lever:** Extended skin maceration (weeks to months) on white grapes — extracts phenolics, tannin, amber colour; biodynamic / wild-ferment / minimal-intervention handling; no fining / filtering / SO2 (or minimal).
- **Style consequence:** Amber colour + tannin grip on a structurally-white wine + oxidative-tinged primary fruit + textural complexity unlike any orthodox white.
- **Origin (not scored, speculative):** Australia (Margaret River biodynamic), Italy (Friuli orange tradition), Slovenia (Brda), Georgia (qvevri amber) all credible. The stem deliberately removes this.
- **Variety (not scored, speculative):** Sauvignon Blanc + Sémillon (Margaret River template), Friulano / Ribolla Gialla (Friuli template), Rkatsiteli / Mtsvane (Georgia template).
- **Quality:** Quality justified by integration of skin tannin, balance of phenolics against acid, length, and complexity — *not* by classical white-wine criteria.
- **Market positioning:** **Niche progressive / natural-wine channel.** Buyers: natural-wine sommeliers, progressive bistros, specialist retailers (Noble Fine Liquor, Newcomer, Tutto), wine bars (Sager + Wilde, Noble Rot). Demographic: younger urban drinker (25–45), often food-led, sometimes ideologically natural-wine aligned. Channel: independent on- and off-trade; very rarely supermarket. Opportunity: rapidly growing natural-wine category and the orange-wine moment is at peak commercial relevance. Challenge: polarising style not suited to mainstream consumers; tannic / oxidative profile reads as flawed to traditionalists.
- **Branch-B alternative:** Oxidative unfortified white (Jura sous voile, oxidative Rioja Blanco Gran Reserva) if oxidative depth is more dominant than the skin-tannin signature; the matrix should state this fallback if amber colour is less developed than expected.

## Method discipline

Name three production techniques specifically:

1. Extended skin maceration on a white grape — to extract phenolics, tannin, and amber colour.
2. Wild / ambient yeast fermentation — to allow natural microbial expression and authenticity narrative.
3. Minimal-intervention handling — limited or no SO2, no fining, no filtration; possible biodynamic farming certification.

Then translate these into the style consequences (amber colour, tannin grip, oxidative complexity, textural breadth) and the market consequences (niche progressive on-trade, premium independent retail).

## Anti-origin note
Origin is *not* in the mark scheme. The matrix should explicitly note this constraint at the top and not allocate marks-equivalent text to geographic guessing.

Lesson:
When a P3 stem explicitly says "consider this wine to be of unknown origin," the IMW is signalling F5f — production and market are the test, geography is *deliberately suppressed*.
