# Paper 1 Production-Method Contrast Audit

*Created 2026-05-26 after reviewing `mock_full_2026_05_26_v6` Paper 1 Question 3 against the historical corpus.*

## Finding

The v6 Paper 1 Question 3 flight uses Alsace Pinot Gris, Gewurztraminer, and Muscat, then asks candidates to comment on method of production for each wine. That is weaker than the historical pattern. The wines are useful for aromatic variety identification, but their method answers collapse into the same broad Alsace template: careful pressing, clean aromatic fermentation, stainless steel or large neutral foudre, little/no overt oak, limited or no MLF, and lees ageing.

The closest historical Alsace precedent is 2011 Paper 1 Question 1: Muscat d'Alsace, Riesling, and Pinot Gris Vendanges Tardives from the same Alsace producer/region. That question did **not** ask production method; it asked region, variety/quality, age/vintage, and development potential. This supports using such a flight for ID, maturity, and quality, not for a production-method sub-question.

## Historical Calibration

When Paper 1 explicitly asks method/winemaking, the corpus normally gives candidates at least one distinct production hook:

- 2011 P1 Q2: Chardonnay across Chablis, South Australia, and Carneros; oak and MLF are named in the stem.
- 2012 P1 Q1: Loire whites include Muscadet sur lie, Vouvray sec, Sauvignon Blanc, and Coteaux du Layon; method differences include lees ageing and sweet-wine production.
- 2014 P1 Q1: Australia includes Viognier, aged Hunter Semillon, Chardonnay, and Sauvignon-Semillon; method differences span aromatic handling, age-release, oak/lees, and blending.
- 2017 P1 Q1: Loire Muscadet, Savennieres, and Pouilly-Fume; method differences are not radical but still give sur lie, Chenin texture, and Sauvignon handling.
- 2019 P1 Q3: Georgia qvevri Chinuri and oxidative Vecchio Samperi; the whole question is method-led.
- 2021 P1 Q1: France includes Vin Jaune, Corton-Charlemagne, Rhone white, and Alsace Pinot Gris; Vin Jaune is the method curveball.
- 2022 P1 Q2: Australian Riesling, Chardonnay, and Marsanne; method differences include reductive aromatic handling, oak/lees/MLF, and mature release.
- 2024 P1 Q2: Moschofilero wild ferment, Viognier, and Alsace Gewurztraminer; method is a leading mark band, with different production hooks.
- 2024 P1 Q3: French white blends from Jurancon, Bordeaux, and Chateauneuf-du-Pape Blanc; the stem specifies oak, and the wines differ in oak/blend/texture handling.
- 2025 P1 Q3: Muscadet sur lie, Alsace Riesling, and Vouvray demi-sec; the same-country/different-variety structure includes real production hooks.

## Writer Rule

For Paper 1, a production-method or winemaking sub-question should pass a "method hook ledger" before the mock is finalized. Each wine needs a distinct discussable hook, or the flight needs at least one clear outlier that prevents the method answer from repeating.

Avoid assigning method marks to flights where all wines are clean, aromatic, neutral-vessel whites from the same region and similar quality tier. If the educational aim is variety recognition, ask origin/variety, style, quality, maturity, or commercial position instead.
