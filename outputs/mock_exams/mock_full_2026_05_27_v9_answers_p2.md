### a) Identify the grape variety

**Cabernet Sauvignon**

The structural architecture -- high tannin, moderate-to-high acidity, and medium-to-full body at 13-14% ABV -- is diagnostic of Cabernet Sauvignon across its classic global expressions.

The alternative considered was Merlot, but the tannin grip is too firm and the fruit profile too cassis-driven; Merlot would show softer, plummier fruit and rounder tannin. Syrah/Shiraz was eliminated by the absence of pepper, violets, and smoked-meat character. The consistent cedar-graphite-cassis triad across all four wines locks Cabernet Sauvignon.

### b) Identify the country and region of origin

**Wine 1: Margaux, Bordeaux, France**
Medium-deep garnet with a slight brick edge suggesting 5+ years of age. The nose is restrained and classically Left Bank: cassis, pencil lead, cedar, rose petal, and a mineral-gravel note. The palate is medium-bodied with fine-grained, drying tannins, moderate alcohol (13.5%), and a savoury, long finish showing tobacco and dried herbs. The restraint, moderate body, and gravelly character point to Margaux rather than the firmer Pauillac or Saint-Julien structure. The vintage warmth (2018) shows in the fruit ripeness, but the elegance is pure Margaux.

**Wine 2: Santa Cruz Mountains, California, USA**
Deep ruby, still youthful. The nose shows mountain blackberry, cassis, anise, lavender, and earthy mineral complexity. The palate is muscular but medium-bodied, with grippy iron-like tannins, firm acidity, and notable freshness for California. **Wine 3: Coonawarra, South Australia**
Deep crimson-purple. The nose immediately shows cassis, blackcurrant, eucalyptus, and mint -- the classic Coonawarra aromatic signature. The palate has cigar box, chocolate, and roasted chestnut notes over a structure of fine savoury tannins and chalky, minerally acidity. At 14% ABV with this particular eucalyptus-mint overlay on Cabernet fruit, the wine reads as classic terra rossa Coonawarra rather than Margaret River (which would show more restraint) or Barossa (which would show more richness and less mint).

**Wine 4: Puente Alto, Maipo Valley, Chile**

The Bordeaux-influenced structure (reflecting Rothschild involvement) is evident in the cedary, graphite-threaded character.

**Wine 8 -- Tempranillo; Rioja, Spain**
Deep cherry-red with violet hues. The nose shows dark fruit (blackberry, plum), liquorice, and sweet vanilla from American oak. The palate is gentle on entry, generously fruited with rounded tannins, moderate acidity, and a medium finish. The American oak profile -- coconut, vanilla, sweet spice -- is immediately diagnostic of Rioja. At 14% ABV with this forward, fruit-driven style and accessible tannins, this is a young, commercially positioned Rioja. The classic Tempranillo markers are present: cherry-dust character under the oak, medium body, and moderate acidity. This reads as Rioja Crianza: 12-18 months in oak (predominantly American), designed for early accessibility.

**Wine 9 -- Tempranillo-dominant blend (with Graciano, Mazuelo, Garnacha); Rioja, Spain**
Medium-deep garnet with some tawny-brick hints at the rim suggesting bottle age. The nose is complex: pencil shavings, star anise, ripe black cherry, currant, jasmine, and mocha, with resinous oak tones, rosemary, and cedar. The complexity and tertiary development (jasmine, mocha, resin) indicate Reserva level: minimum 36 months ageing including 12 in barrel.

**Wine 10 -- Tinto Fino (Tempranillo) with minor Merlot; Ribera del Duero, Spain**
Deep ruby-garnet. The nose is refined and complex: black cherry, cassis, plum, violet, graphite, cedar, sweet spice, and a balsamic note. Earl Grey and cocoa powder emerge with air. The five-year barrel-and-bottle ageing programme (hence "5 Ano") gives a distinctive integration of oak and fruit.

**Wine 11 -- Garnacha-dominant blend with Carinena (Garnacha 70%, Carinena 30%); Priorat, Spain**
Deep ruby-purple. The nose is bright and fresh: ripe raspberry, dark cherry, dried sage, white pepper, slate, and spiced blueberry. The slate-mineral signature, the Garnacha's raspberry and garrigue character, and the firm Carinena tannin backbone are diagnostic of Priorat. This is not Rioja Garnacha (which would be softer, rounder) or Southern Rhone (which would show more warmth and less mineral grip).

**Wine 12 -- Mencia (with minor white grapes and other reds); Bierzo, Spain**
Medium ruby, the lightest wine in the flight. The nose is perfumed and floral: red cherry, hibiscus, black olive, and aromatic herbs, with a watermelon freshness. The palate is juicy and round with velvety tannins, medium body, and a long finish with lingering red fruit and floral notes. At 13% ABV this is notably lighter and more aromatic than the other Spanish reds. The floral, Pinot-like elegance combined with the olive and herbal notes is diagnostic of Mencia from Bierzo. The slate soils give mineral lift. The lower tannin and lighter colour distinguish Mencia from Tempranillo, while the herbal complexity and olive notes separate it from Pinot Noir.

### b) Quality within the context of region, with reference to winemaking and style

**Wine 8** -- Good quality for Rioja Crianza. The winemaking reflects classic commercial Rioja: hand-harvested Tempranillo, optical sorting, fermented in oak vats with 20-day maceration, aged approximately 14 months in predominantly American oak. The American oak influence (vanilla, coconut) is well-judged for the category -- present but not overwhelming. Within Rioja's hierarchy this sits at the Crianza/entry-premium level: accessible, fruit-forward, intended for drinking within 3-5 years. Comparable to Marques de Caceres Crianza or La Rioja Alta Vina Alberdi. Retail EUR 10-15.

**Wine 9** -- Very good to outstanding quality for Rioja Reserva. The winemaking represents the modern-traditional Rioja model: 24 months in barrel (French and American oak), with the transition toward predominantly French oak adding finesse and spice complexity alongside the traditional American oak sweetness. The extended maceration (20 days) and gravity-flow handling preserve fruit purity. Within Rioja, this is a benchmark Reserva -- comparable to Lopez de Heredia Vina Bosconia, Muga Reserva. The 2018 vintage is excellent. Retail EUR 25-35.

**Wine 10** -- Outstanding quality, iconic within Ribera del Duero. Valbuena represents the pinnacle of Tinto Fino expression, second only to Unico within the Vega Sicilia hierarchy. Winemaking uses indigenous yeasts, 3-4 day cold soak, fermentation with extended maceration, then a long and complex ageing regime over five years in a mix of new and used French and American oak barrels (225L) and large vats (8,500L). The winemaking philosophy prioritises elegance over extraction -- the result is a wine of extraordinary finesse and complexity. Within Ribera del Duero, this represents the Gran Reserva tier in all but name. Retail EUR 130-180.

**Wine 11** -- Very good to outstanding quality for Priorat. Les Terrasses is Alvaro Palacios' entry-premium Priorat cuvee (above Camins del Priorat, below Finca Dofi and L'Ermita). The winemaking balances old-vine concentration (70+ year Garnacha) with freshness: moderate extraction, barrel ageing that integrates rather than dominates, and a focus on slate-terroir expression. Within Priorat, this is a regional benchmark at the serious-but-accessible tier. Competes with Clos Mogador, Clos Erasmus entry wines, and Torres Perpetual. The 2021 shows particular freshness.
