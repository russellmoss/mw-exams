# MW Write Pipeline Guidance

This note converts the Moss answer-rationale PDFs and Stubbs Toronto 2026 notes into rules for generated 8-minute MW practical answers.

## Global Answer Voice

- Lead with the answer, then justify it.
- Use evidence-linked argument sentences: observation -> inference -> conclusion.
- Prefer two or three diagnostic observations over a tasting-note dump.
- Separate killer evidence from merely consistent evidence. Medium colour, medium body, and 12.5-13.5% alcohol are usually only useful as exclusions or mop-up.
- Use calibrated language: "points to" for strong evidence, "suggests" for moderate evidence, "is consistent with" for supporting evidence, and "makes X less likely" for exclusions.
- Default to a mini-funnel when the answer is not unmistakable: state the category, eliminate one plausible alternative, then land the final call.
- Preserve the 8-minute constraint by using compact, mark-earning sentences rather than expansive prose.

## Identity Sections

- If the prompt asks for origin and variety, answer per wine with both.
- Give the most specific origin first where available: appellation/sub-region/region/country.
- Add one evidence sentence that combines sensory, structure, and known exam-context evidence.
- Where useful, include a brief comparator exclusion: alcohol/tannin/acid/fruit/oak/maturity should explain why the final call is better than a plausible alternative.

## Quality Sections

Use BLIC CT TOAD V selectively: balance/structure, length, intensity versus concentration, complexity, commercial position in peer group, texture, typicity/terroir, oak handling, ageability, development in glass, and final value judgement.

Quality is relative to region, variety, peer group, or appellation. The answer should say whether this is a sound, good, very good, excellent, or outstanding example of its type and why.

## Style And Commercial Sections

- Style means winemaker intention achieved in the glass, not fruit descriptors.
- Use category, sugar, body, acid, tannin, texture, general style, oak, and drink-now/age-worthy positioning.
- Commercial comments should include likely channel or buyer: mass retail, by-the-glass, independent retail, restaurant, fine dining, specialist, collector.

## Winemaking/MOP Sections

- Most MOP answers only deserve four or five decisive decisions.
- Do not walk through every possible production step.
- Prioritize the choices that explain the wine style: harvest ripeness/rot/timing, protective versus oxidative handling, yeast/temperature/vessel, carbonic/whole bunch/cap management/maceration, MLF/lees, oak and maturation, finishing/bottling.

## Paper Emphases

- Paper 1: climate, acid/alcohol, oak/lees/MLF, texture, and ageing logic.
- Paper 2: colour depth, fruit colour, tannin quantity/quality, alcohol, acidity, extraction, oak regime, and comparator elimination.
- Paper 3: method evidence. For sparkling use mousse, dosage, lees, autolysis. For fortified/sweet use alcohol, RS, fortification timing, oxidative/reductive ageing, cask/solera/canteiro/estufagem.
