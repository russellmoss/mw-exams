# Merlot, Beringer, 2011. Napa Valley, California

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple, opaque with youth. Nose shows Napa Merlot character: plum, black cherry, cassis, vanilla, mocha, chocolate from American/French oak, with a soft, plush quality distinct from Bordeaux Merlot. Full-bodied, generous and plush tannins, medium-plus acidity, long finish with oak-derived sweetness. 14.5% ABV signals warm-climate, commercial-tier California Merlot.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Predominantly Merlot; typically blended with small percentages of Cabernet Franc, Cabernet Sauvignon, and/or Malbec per Napa AVA regulations (minimum 75% Merlot to use varietal designation)
- Vineyard: Napa Valley AVA; Beringer sources from Knight's Valley, Howell Mountain, and various Napa appellations for the Napa Valley Merlot tier
- Soils: Variable: volcanic ash and rocky loam in Knight's Valley; clay-based soils in valley floor Napa
- Vinification: Fermentation in stainless steel, temperature-controlled; pump-overs; extended maceration for colour and structure
- Elevage: French and American oak barrels; for the Napa Valley tier (not Bancroft Ranch), typically 16-18 months; mix of new and older barrels

## Vintage character (2011 Napa Valley)
2011 was a cool, challenging vintage in Napa Valley: a cold, wet spring followed by a cool summer delayed ripening significantly. It produced lower-alcohol, more structured wines than typical Napa — an atypically cool year where ABV was often below normal. The 14.5% for Beringer's wine suggests either late-harvest picking to achieve ripeness or blending. The vintage is considered less commercially appealing than the warm 2012, 2013, or 2014, but can show fine structure. (Source needed for specific 2011 Napa vintage report URL.)

## Producer style
Beringer Vineyards is one of Napa's oldest continuously operating wineries, founded 1876 in St. Helena. Now owned by Treasury Wine Estates (TWE), a publicly traded Australian company. The Beringer portfolio spans multiple price tiers from Main & Vine (entry) through Founders' Estate (mid), Napa Valley Collection (premium), and Bancroft Ranch (prestige single-vineyard). The Napa Valley Merlot sits in the premium commercial tier (approximately USD 35-45). The style is consistent Napa: ripe, plush, oak-forward, generous. Market position: large-volume, widely distributed commercial premium.
