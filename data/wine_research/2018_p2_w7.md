# Pinot Noir, Felton Road, 2016

## Quick tasting profile
Bright ruby with red cherry, thyme, rose, and fine spice. The palate is dry and more tensile than the Californian example, with vivid acidity, fine tannin, and a savory mineral edge typical of Bannockburn.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: Bannockburn, Central Otago
- Soils: Schist-derived soils are a core regional cue

## Vintage character (2016 Central Otago)
2016 in New Zealand was generally clean and successful, with good ripeness and freshness. In Central Otago Pinot Noir, that usually translates into purity of fruit, aromatic lift, and structural clarity.

## Producer style
Felton Road is one of New Zealand's benchmark Pinot Noir producers, strongly associated with biodynamic farming and transparent single-site expression. The wines typically marry ripe fruit with tension rather than pursuing sheer ripeness.

## Why it's in this exam
This is the New Zealand point in the 2018 paper 2 Pinot Noir trio. It helps test Central Otago identification through ripe but vivid fruit, schist-derived savory notes, and balanced oak.
