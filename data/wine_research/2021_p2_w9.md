# Chateauneuf du Pape, Clos des Papes, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby with ripe red and black fruit, garrigue, spice, leather and warm stone notes. The palate is full-bodied, dry and broad, with high alcohol, generous fruit and firm but rounded tannins. This is classic high-quality southern Rhône, combining power with savoury complexity.

- Vineyard: Multiple parcels across Châteauneuf-du-Pape

- ABV / RS / TA: 15.0% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2015 Chateauneuf-du-Pape)
Decanter's 2015 southern Rhône guide describes a strong vintage with power and balance, though some wines leaned hot. Clos des Papes is well placed to convert that generosity into depth and complexity rather than simple alcoholic weight.

## Producer style
Clos des Papes is one of the benchmark traditional estates of Châteauneuf-du-Pape, known for a single serious red cuvée blended from many parcels. The style prizes completeness, complexity and ageing capacity over flashy extraction or sweet oak.

## Why it's in this exam
In 2021 Paper 2 Question 4, this is paired with Guigal Côtes du Rhône as the southern Rhône comparison. It is the appellation-prestige benchmark, testing hierarchy, texture and ageing potential within the region.
