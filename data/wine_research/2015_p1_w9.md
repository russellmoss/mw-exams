# Dürnsteiner Kellerberg, Grüner Veltliner Smaragd, F.X. Pichler, 2012. Wachau, Austria

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold; powerful, complex nose of white pepper, lemon oil, quince, ripe peach and subtle mineral/spice character unique to Grüner Veltliner. Palate is full-bodied, broad and rich — classic Smaragd weight (minimum 12.5% alcohol; this shows 14.5%). Texture is dense and glycerol-rich; the white pepper spice of Grüner is persistent. No new oak (Wachau never uses new oak). Dry finish of considerable length. One of Austria's benchmark single-vineyard wines.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Grüner Veltliner
- Vineyard: Dürnsteiner Kellerberg is a steep terraced vineyard site near Dürnstein village in the Wachau; one of the most celebrated single-vineyard sites in Austria
- Soils: Steep loess/loam terraces over primary rock (gneiss/granite); the combination of mineral-rich soils and extreme diurnal temperature variation creates wines of great complexity (source: Wikipedia Wachau — "temperature variation in the valley between day and cold nights")
- Vinification: Stainless steel or large old-format casks; no new oak; no malolactic fermentation (Wachau rarely uses MLF) — "rarely using new oak or malolactic fermentation" (source: Wikipedia Vinea Wachau)
- Elevage: Large old neutral casks or stainless steel; extended lees contact typical at this quality level; long cork (minimum 49mm) required for Smaragd (source: Wikipedia Vinea Wachau)
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.5% / dry (max 9 g/L RS for Smaragd) / high acidity (Source needed for exact figures)

## Vintage character (2012 Wachau)
2012 was a very good vintage in the Wachau. A warm, dry growing season produced ripe grapes with naturally high sugars — entirely consistent with the 14.5% ABV seen in this wine. The Wachau's Smaragd tier (minimum 12.5% alcohol, named for an emerald lizard that basks in the sun) is the region's richest, fullest expression, and 2012 provided the ripeness to make genuinely powerful Smaragd wines. ## Producer style
F.X. Pichler (Franz Xaver Pichler, succeeded by son Lucas Pichler) is widely regarded as one of Austria's greatest winemakers and a key figure in the international reputation of Wachau Grüner Veltliner and Riesling. The estate is based in Oberloiben and holds top-rated single-vineyard sites including Kellerberg, Loibner Berg, Unendlich, and M. The style is powerful, mineral-driven, and built for long aging — described as "very pure, mineral wine capable of long aging" comparable to world-class white Burgundy (source: Wikipedia Grüner Veltliner). Wines are famously age-worthy: top Smaragd wines develop for 10–20 years. The Vinea Wachau appellation, of which FX Pichler is a founding member, prohibits new oak, chaptalization, and concentration technologies. Commercial positioning: Austria's highest tier; prestige pricing, international collector market.

## Why it's in this exam
W9 is part of Q3's six single-variety flight. GV Smaragd from FX Pichler represents the pinnacle of Austrian white wine and is a classic MW identification challenge: the white pepper/spice signature of Grüner Veltliner is distinctive, but at 14.5% Smaragd weight, without new oak, with full body and complexity, it can challenge candidates who associate weight with oak or New World provenance. The Dürnsteiner Kellerberg single-vineyard designation and FX Pichler's status as Austria's leading producer make this a prestige benchmark wine. "Further ageing" answer: yes, highly age-worthy — 10+ years in prime condition.
