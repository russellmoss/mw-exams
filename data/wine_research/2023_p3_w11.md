# Fine Ruby Port, Cockburn's, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple, youthful and fruit-driven rather than oxidative. Blind, expect cherry and raspberry fruit, moderate sweetness, soft texture and a straightforward, accessible style that clearly separates it from older tawny or more serious declared-vintage Port categories (source: https://www.cockburns.com/files/wine/2_cockburn-s-fine-ruby-port_file1727101795.pdf).

## Vintage character (NV / category character)
This is non-vintage, so the category matters more than any single year. Ruby Port is blended to preserve primary red-fruit character, modest wood influence and immediate drinkability, which is exactly why it reads so differently from the oxidative 40-year tawny appearing later in the same question set.

## Producer style
Cockburn’s positions Fine Ruby as adaptable, approachable and widely drinkable rather than cerebral or collectible (source: https://www.cockburns.com/wine/cockburn-s-fine-ruby-port/2). Commercially it is an entry-level classic: high-recognition category, broad usage occasions and lower prestige than aged tawny or vintage Port.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 11 is a classification trap in 2023 Paper 3, Question 4. Candidates need to be precise about what “Fine Ruby” means commercially and stylistically, and distinguish youthful ruby maturation from the much more cask-aged, premium, age-indicated tawny model.
