# Semillon, Moss Wood, 2013. Margaret River, Western Australia

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw to light gold; fresh primary nose of lemon, green apple, fig, and fresh herbs — the characteristic "green" quality of Margaret River Semillon. Palate shows lively acidity, light-to-medium body with a subtle textural grip (Semillon's natural phenolic structure), citrus fruit and crisp finish. Stainless steel fermented — no oak, no MLF. Clean, bright, unoaked white. Very different from Hunter Valley Semillon (no secondary development at this young age), and from Bordeaux-style Semillon/Sauvignon blends. (Source: Moss Wood website: "Fresh primary fruit aromas of lemons, green apples & figs, lifted acidity, rich texture and a touch of tannin on the palate.")

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Semillon
- Vineyard: Wilyabrup sub-region of Margaret River; 926 Metricup Road; second-oldest estate in Margaret River, planted 1969 (source: Moss Wood website)
- Soils: Gravelly loam over clay typical of Wilyabrup; free-draining
- Vinification: Stainless steel fermentation; no oak; preserves primary fruit aromas (source: Moss Wood website — "utilising stainless steel fermentation to preserve primary fruit characteristics")
- Elevage: Stainless steel; early bottling
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / dry / medium-high acidity (Source needed: exact figures)

## Vintage character (2013 Margaret River)
Margaret River enjoys a stable maritime Mediterranean climate with "lowest mean annual temperature range, of only 7.6°C" (source: Wikipedia Margaret River wine region). Year-to-year variation is lower than in many wine regions. 2013 was a generally good vintage in Margaret River — warm, dry conditions through harvest with Mediterranean consistency. At 13.5% ABV, the 2013 Moss Wood Semillon shows normal maturity for this estate's style. (Source needed: specific 2013 Margaret River vintage data.)

## Producer style
Moss Wood is the second-oldest wine estate in Margaret River, planted 1969 in the Wilyabrup sub-region. Owned and operated by Clare and Keith Mugford since 1984 (Mugfords made their first vintage in 1973). The estate holds an "Exceptional" classification in Langton's Classification of Australian Wine — one of Australia's highest prestige rankings. Moss Wood produces Cabernet Sauvignon (the flagship), Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Semillon, and wines from their Ribbon Vale estate. Philosophy: "All decisions made in the vineyard, or at the winery, are based on the effect they will have on the quality of the wine." Moss Wood Semillon is a pure, unoaked expression that relies on fruit and structure rather than winemaking amplification. Internationally regarded; premium-to-prestige pricing. (Source: Moss Wood website, about page.)

## Why it's in this exam
W8 is part of Q3's six-variety single-grape flight. Semillon identification is a classic MW challenge: without oak (Hunter Valley-style) or secondary development, the variety is less easily fingerprinted than aromatic varieties. The Margaret River provenance at 13.5% ABV adds a New World clue. The examiner likely uses this to test whether candidates recognise Semillon's subtle textural phenolics, green herbal nose, and the distinct character of Australian unoaked Semillon versus, e.g., aged Hunter Valley Semillon or white Bordeaux. "Potential for further ageing" question is interesting here: Moss Wood Semillon can develop bottle complexity over 5–10 years, developing honey, wax and mineral complexity.
