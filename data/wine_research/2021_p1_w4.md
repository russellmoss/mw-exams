# Pinot Gris, Cave de Hunawihr, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Medium to deep gold with a slightly amber tinge; aromatic nose of ripe pear, quince, smoked bacon, gingerbread and subtle honey — the classic Alsace Pinot Gris aromatic register. Medium to full body (13% ABV), with Alsace Pinot Gris' characteristic viscous texture; the off-dry residual sugar balances the variety's naturally lower acidity. Warm 2018 vintage amplifies ripe stone-fruit and a slightly sweet mid-palate. Medium-long finish. Easy to confuse with Pinot Gris from other regions or Gewurztraminer at lower aromatic intensity.

## Vintage character (2018 Alsace)
2018 was one of the warmest, driest and earliest harvests in Alsace in decades, producing very ripe fruit with high natural sugars. Pinot Gris in 2018 tends toward richness and high alcohol, with broad, generous fruit and lower acidity than cooler years. Residual sugar levels may be higher than the label implies given the ripeness of the harvest.

## Producer style
Cave de Hunawihr is an Alsace cooperative founded in 1954, based in the wine village of Hunawihr (known for its medieval church). They produce varietal Alsace wines — Riesling, Pinot Gris, Gewurztraminer, Pinot Blanc — at accessible price points from member growers.
