# Château Berliquet, 2005

## Quick tasting profile
Deeper plum and black-fruit expression than the Léoville Barton, with sweeter oak, mocha, and a softer, rounder mid-palate. Tannins are present but less austere, and the overall shape is broader and more immediately generous, pointing toward Merlot-led right-bank Bordeaux. In blind tasting this reads as mature Saint-Émilion rather than left-bank Cabernet country.

## Vintage character (2005 Bordeaux)
2005 gave ripe tannin, concentration, and strong ageing potential across both banks of Bordeaux. On the right bank the vintage often produced fleshy, generous fruit wrapped around substantial but ripe tannins. That combination makes 2005 a useful exam vintage because it can look generous early but still carries serious structural authority.

## Producer style
Berliquet sits in Saint-Émilion and, even before its later ownership changes, was associated with a rounder, more accessible style than the sternest classed-growth left-bank estates. The grape mix and terroir naturally push the wine toward darker fruit, more mid-palate breadth, and a more polished texture. In practical terms it is a good foil for Saint-Julien because it highlights the sensory consequences of bank, blend, and élevage choices.

## Why it's in this exam
Paired with Léoville Barton in 2016 Paper 2 Question 3 as a same-vintage Bordeaux comparison. It tests whether the candidate can identify right-bank Merlot influence and discuss relative quality within the context of Saint-Émilion versus classed-growth Saint-Julien.
