# Tokaji Aszú 6 Puttonyos, Royal Tokaji Wine Company, 2011

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold with apricot, orange marmalade, saffron, acacia honey, and botrytis spice. The palate is very sweet but cut by piercing acidity, giving a vivid rather than cloying shape and a long, layered finish. Blind, the combination of botrytis richness, low alcohol, and acid tension is a classic Tokaji Aszú signal.

## Vintage character (2011 Tokaj)
2011 was a warm, dry year in much of Central Europe, but the decisive exam point here is that Tokaji's best sweet wines depend on noble rot, concentration, and acid retention rather than warmth alone. In a strong Aszú vintage the result is intense botrytis richness without loss of freshness. Source needed for a full regional 2011 Tokaj vintage report.

## Producer style
Royal Tokaji was one of the key estates in Tokaj's post-communist fine-wine revival and remains one of the most internationally visible names in the region. The wines aim for classical sweetness categories, precision, and polished international presentation while preserving traditional Aszú identity. A 6 Puttonyos bottling is a very useful benchmark for high-quality botrytised Tokaji.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 3 alongside Sauternes as a cross-country sweet wine comparison. It tests whether the candidate can explain botrytis, puttonyos classification, low alcohol, and the high-acid profile that differentiates Tokaji from richer oakier Sauternes.
