# Sauvignon Semillon Circa 77, Xanadu, 2023

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw with green tints. The nose is bright and lifted with passionfruit, lime, gooseberry, lemongrass, and fresh herb notes, plus a subtle hint of lemon sherbet from partial barrel ferment. The palate is medium-light bodied, bracingly dry and crisp, with freshly squeezed citrus and tropical fruit, a touch of honeysuckle and baker's spice, and a clean mineral-driven finish. The Semillon adds textural roundness without masking the Sauvignon Blanc's zippy acidity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 70% Sauvignon Blanc, 30% Semillon (source: Mise en Place / James Brock review; some retailers list 61/39 split -- source discrepancy)
- Vineyard: Estate-grown from Boodjidup and Lagan vineyards, Wallcliffe sub-region, Margaret River. Vines originally planted in 1977 (hence the cuvee name).
- Soils: Gravelly loam with maritime influence from the Indian Ocean (source: Bin Ends)
- Vinification: Fruit crushed, destemmed, and chilled before gentle pressing. Settled juice fermented at 15C in stainless steel using aromatic yeast strains (QA23 and X5 for SB; Mauri AWRI 796 for Semillon). 10% of fruit (all Sauvignon Blanc) whole-berry pressed and barrel fermented in French oak (8% new) for subtle texture and complexity. No malolactic fermentation.
- Elevage: Brief lees contact for added texture. Stainless steel dominant.
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% (per exam paper; some retail sources cite 12.5%) / dry / Source needed
- Closure: Screw cap
*(ABV discrepancy: retailer tech sheets cite 12.5%, but the MW exam paper states 13%.)*

## Vintage character (2023 Margaret River)
2023 was widely hailed as a "Goldilocks vintage" in Margaret River. A late budburst after a cold winter pushed vine development back 2-3 weeks. Spring was cooler and drier than usual, extending the flowering period. The absence of significant disease pressure and dry, moderate conditions through the growing season allowed gentle, consistent ripening across all varieties. The resulting whites show excellent natural acidity, aromatic intensity, and balance. Decanter praised 2023 as potentially the greatest Margaret River Chardonnay vintage ever; the same extended, even ripening benefited Sauvignon Blanc and Semillon blends. (source: margaretriver.wine vintage reports; Decanter; Jasper Morris)

## Producer style
Xanadu was founded in 1977 by Dr. John Lagan (Irish-born, arrived in Margaret River in 1968) and his wife Eithne. Named after the mythical paradise in Coleridge's "Kubla Khan." Acquired in 2005 by the Rathbone family (also owners of Mount Langi Ghiran and Yering Station), keeping it family-owned. Xanadu practices minimalist winemaking -- whole bunch pressing, wild fermentation on selected cuvees, French oak maturation, and lees stirring -- letting the terroir speak. Certified member of Sustainable Winegrowing Australia. Best known for Chardonnay and Cabernet Sauvignon, but the Sauvignon Blanc-Semillon blend is a Margaret River signature. The Circa 77 range sits at the entry-to-mid tier (~AU$22 / US$19), offering approachable, fruit-driven wines. The estate also produces premium tiers. Wine Enthusiast gave this wine 90 points.

## Why it's in this exam
Paired with Chateau de Fieuzal Blanc 2021 (Pessac-Leognan) in Question 1: "Wines 1 and 2 are blends of the same two grape varieties." This is the classic Old World vs. New World Sauvignon-Semillon comparison -- Bordeaux Blanc (barrel-fermented, structured, restrained) against Margaret River (fruit-forward, aromatic, lighter oak). The question asks candidates to identify the shared grape varieties (10 marks), distinguish origin (2 x 8 marks), and comment on style, winemaking, and quality (2 x 12 marks). The Xanadu represents the approachable, modern, New World expression of this blend at a commercial price point, contrasting the classified Pessac-Leognan.
