# Chablis, Domaine Henri (Defaix), 2012

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with green highlights; intense green apple, lemon curd, chalk dust and oyster shell on the nose. Palate is lean and linear, high acidity, steely minerality driven by Kimmeridgian soils, light body, clean stainless-steel fermented profile. No oak evident. Short-to-medium finish with saline lift. Textbook cool-climate unoaked Chardonnay.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay

## Vintage character (2012 Burgundy whites)
2012 was a challenging but ultimately rewarding vintage in Chablis and the Cote d'Or. A wet spring was followed by summer hail events that reduced yields significantly (20–40% below average in some parcels). Warm, dry September rescued quality, yielding wines of good concentration and lively acidity. The 2012 whites are racy, mineral, with relatively high natural acidity — well-suited to Chablis's steely archetype. Yields were low enough that concentrated village-level wines occasionally rival Premier Cru quality. (Source needed: specific vintage weather data; general character from MW study knowledge.)

## Producer style
Domaine Henri Defaix is a well-regarded Chablis estate based in Milly, near Chablis town. The domaine is known for a rigidly classical approach: no new oak, no malolactic fermentation on their village wines, stainless steel vinification throughout. Their village Chablis is considered a benchmark for the unoaked appellation style — lean, flinty, pure Chardonnay character framed by Kimmeridgian minerality.
