# Albariño, Santiago Ruiz, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with a distinctly Atlantic profile: citrus, white flowers, green herbs and a saline edge. The palate is dry, medium-bodied and polished, with ripe citrus fruit, brisk acidity and a rounded mid-palate from lees contact rather than oak. It reads as serious coastal Spain, not simple aromatic white wine.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: blend led by Albariño with Loureiro, Treixadura, Godello and Caiño Blanco
- Vineyard: Estate and grower fruit in O Rosal, Rías Baixas
- Soils: Mostly granitic and alluvial influences in O Rosal; Source needed on parcel breakdown
- Vinification: Fermented in stainless steel at controlled temperature
- Elevage: Fine lees ageing before bottling
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.0% / 1.8 g/L / 8.75 g/L

## Vintage character (2017 Rías Baixas)
The Burridge 2017 Spain report quotes the Rías Baixas technical director calling 2017 a "textbook vintage". The producer's own 2017 note also presents it as a successful year, supporting a style with ripe fruit, clean aromatics and the brisk salinity expected from O Rosal.

## Producer style
Santiago Ruiz is a benchmark Rías Baixas producer and an important historical name in the region. The house style is aromatic but controlled, built around Atlantic freshness and a regional field-blend mentality rather than a simple mono-varietal fruit bomb.

## Why it's in this exam
This is the Spanish counterpart to the Rioja Blanco in 2019 Paper 1 Question 2. It helps test whether the candidate can distinguish Atlantic, stainless-steel, saline white Spain from barrel-aged continental Rioja, while also spotting that the wine is not a simple single-variety Albariño in style terms.
