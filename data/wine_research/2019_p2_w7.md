# Carménère, Montes Alpha, Viña Montes, 2016. Colchagua, Chile

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple. Nose of dark plum, black cherry, dried herbs, tobacco, graphite and characteristic green bell pepper/pyrazine note of Carménère when not fully ripe — though Montes Alpha aims for full ripeness, reducing green notes. Palate is full-bodied with dense, ripe tannins, moderate-to-good acidity, and spiced dark fruit with a chocolatey, smoky finish. New oak adds structure. Powerful warm-climate Chilean signature.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Carménère
- Vineyard: Viña Montes estate vineyards in Colchagua Valley — Apalta and other high-quality sites; Colchagua is the heartland of Chilean Carménère production (source: Wikipedia Carménère)
- Soils: Alluvial valley and hillside soils typical of Colchagua
- Vinification: Modern winery; destemming, temperature-controlled fermentation, maceration for colour and tannin
- Elevage: French oak barriques (significant new oak proportion at Montes Alpha level); approximately 14–16 months (Source needed for exact 2016 protocol)

## Vintage character (2016 Colchagua/Chile)
2016 in Chile's Colchagua Valley was a warm, dry year favourable for red wine production. Carménère benefited from extended hang time, achieving full phenolic ripeness while reducing the green, under-ripe pyrazine character that historically plagued the variety. Wines show concentration and depth with improved varietal expression.

## Producer style
Viña Montes was founded in 1988 by Aurelio Montes Sr. and partners, becoming one of Chile's most internationally recognised premium producers. The Montes Alpha range sits at the premium tier ($20–35 range), with Montes Alpha M (ultra-premium) above it. Style is modern, internationally-oriented: generous fruit, new oak, ripe tannins. The Colchagua Valley site and the "Apalta" designation are their viticultural signature. Montes were pioneers in demonstrating Chile's potential for high-quality Carménère. ## Why it's in this exam
Montes Alpha Carménère represents the benchmark Chilean red for MW purposes: the definitive varietal example of Carménère from its spiritual home in Colchagua. Identifying Carménère (dark fruit + herbal/graphite notes, full body, warm climate) and distinguishing it from Merlot or Cabernet Franc is the exam's primary challenge. The 14.5% ABV signals warm-climate production.
