# Ben Ryé Passito de Pantelleria, Donnafugata, 2016. Sicily, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber-gold to orange-gold, viscous appearance. Nose is intensely aromatic and seductive: orange peel, dried apricot, candied citrus, dates, figs, rose petal, jasmine, honey and white almond paste. The Zibibbo (Muscat of Alexandria) character is unmistakable — floral, exotic and perfumed. Palate is sweet but never cloying: fresh, lively acidity for balance, with an unctuously textured mid-palate, complex dried fruit layering and a long, persistent, aromatic finish. One of the world's great sweet wines.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Zibibbo (Muscat of Alexandria) — the only variety permitted for Passito di Pantelleria DOC
- Vineyard: Pantelleria island, Sicily channel between Tunisia and Sicily; alberello (bush-trained) vines, mandatory for the DOC, grown close to volcanic/lava soils to protect from wind
- Soils: Volcanic basalt and lava rock on a windswept island; no phylloxera (ungrafted vines possible)
- Vinification: Grapes partially dried (appassimento) on the island in the intense Mediterranean sun; then macerated with fresh Zibibbo grapes to extract concentrated juice; fermentation stops naturally when ABV and RS are in balance
- Elevage: Stainless steel; no oak; bottled to preserve freshness and aromatic intensity
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.5% / very high RS (approximately 130–180 g/L, Source needed for exact 2016) / high natural acidity

## Producer style
Donnafugata is a major Sicilian producer founded by the Rallo family, with Pantelleria as a key sourcing location. Ben Ryé ("son of the wind" in Arabic) is their flagship Passito di Pantelleria and one of Italy's most internationally acclaimed sweet wines. The name reflects the Arabic heritage of Pantelleria (the island was historically Arab). Donnafugata is committed to sustainable viticulture and is a high-quality producer across multiple Sicilian DOCs. Market position: premium sweet wine ($40–60 per 500ml). Multiple international gold medals.

## Why it's in this exam
Ben Ryé is a Paper 3 essential: Italy's definitive sweet wine benchmark. It tests identification of: (1) the sweet dessert wine category, (2) the appassimento production method, (3) the Zibibbo/Muscat of Alexandria variety, (4) the Pantelleria island terroir. The intense floral-citrus-apricot character and volcanic island origin are highly distinctive in a blind tasting context.
