# Brunello di Montalcino Montosoli, Altesino, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
This is the more profound Tuscan wine in Question 2: sour cherry, dark berry, rose, balsam and spice with broader shoulders, deeper tannin and longer finish than Chianti Classico. Even at maturity it should still feel architecturally built, with site seriousness and bottle-age potential obvious.

## Vintage character (2015 Montalcino)
2015 is widely admired in Montalcino for ripeness, generosity and structure, producing Brunello with both immediate appeal and ageing capacity. In Montosoli that often translates into real concentration and warmth, but without losing Sangiovese's aromatic lift.

## Producer style
Altesino is one of Brunello's landmark estates and the Montosoli bottling is a prestige single-vineyard wine first produced in the 1970s. The estate style balances traditional longevity with relatively polished textures, making it a textbook fine-wine Brunello reference.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 6 is the Montalcino foil to Chianti Classico in 2022 Paper 2 Question 2. The examiner is asking candidates to separate not just sub-region but also style, quality and winemaking ambition within Tuscany's Sangiovese landscape.
