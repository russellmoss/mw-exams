# Vintage Extra Brut, Billecart-Salmon, 2006

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with persistent fine mousse; 9 years on lees at time of 2015 exam creates a pronounced autolytic nose of toasted brioche, fresh pastry, and hazelnut. The Extra Brut dosage (typically 3–4 g/L) leaves the palate lean and precise with green apple, lemon curd, and chalk minerality. High acidity; long, dry finish with little confected fruit — reads like a prestige cuvée but at mid-tier pricing.

## Vintage character (2006 Champagne)
2006 was a warm, precocious vintage in Champagne — an early harvest of ripe, generous fruit with relatively low natural acidity. Wines tend toward rounded, approachable profiles with good extract but less tension than 2004 or 2008. For Extra Brut expressions the low dosage exposes any acid shortfall, so the structural interplay is a key exam point. By 2015 these wines would have been drinking in their prime window. ## Producer style
Founded 1818, Mareuil-sur-Aÿ; seventh-generation family ownership (Mathieu Roland-Billecart as of 2019). Mid-sized house known for a restrained, precise, elegant style leaning toward Chardonnay characters even in multi-varietal cuvées. Brut Réserve aged minimum 36 months on lees. Ranked among the top 15 Champagne houses by Antoine Gerbelle. The Vintage Extra Brut positions itself as a purer, terroir-focused expression contrasting with the more commercial NV Brut range. (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billecart-Salmon)
