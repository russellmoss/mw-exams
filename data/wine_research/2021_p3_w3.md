# Brut Quartet, Roederer Estate, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw to pale gold with fine, persistent bubbles; a quality New World sparkling profile — brioche, green apple, citrus, white pear and a subtle toast/biscuit note from oak-aged reserve wines; floral and fresh, with good autolytic character. Medium body (12% ABV), refreshing acidity, balanced dosage; the mid-palate shows more richness and roundness than a similar-tier NV Champagne. Clean, medium-long finish. A high-quality California sparkling that approaches Champagne complexity but reads warmer in fruit expression.

## Vintage character (NV Anderson Valley)
Brut Quartet is a non-vintage blend; Anderson Valley's cool, fog-influenced climate (driven by Pacific proximity and Navarro River drainage) produces sparkling base wines with naturally high acidity that parallel the Champagne region. The estate's use of oak-aged reserve wines adds complexity unusual for New World NV sparkling.

## Producer style
Roederer Estate was established in 1982 by Jean-Claude Rouzaud (Louis Roederer president) in Philo, Anderson Valley — a deliberate extension of the Champagne house's expertise to California. The estate produces exclusively sparkling wine from 580 acres of estate fruit and is "considered one of the top sparkling wine producers in the United States." Anderson Valley Brut (their flagship) debuted in 1988; the Brut Quartet and L'Ermitage prestige cuvée complete the range. Winemaker Arnaud Weyrich; distributed by Maisons Marques & Domaines. (Source: Wikipedia)
