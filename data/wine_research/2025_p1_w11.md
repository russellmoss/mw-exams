# Rioja Blanco Gran Reserva, López de Haro, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Deep golden colour after a decade of age. The nose shows baked pear, quince, cinnamon, nutmeg, cigar box, and soft herbal aromas integrated with vanilla and toast from extended oak ageing. The palate is creamy and well-structured with vibrant acidity and refined salinity balancing the buttery, spice-laden character; the finish is long and sophisticated with polished texture. A classically styled, oxidatively aged Rioja Blanco that reads as mature, complex, and decisively Old World under blind conditions.

## Technical specs
- Grape varieties: 80% Viura, 15% Malvasia, 5% Garnacha Blanca (source: Marquis Wines, Club Oenologique; Decanter panel tasting lists 85/10/5 -- minor discrepancy across sources)
- Vineyard: Goblet-trained old vines, average age 65 years, from limestone soils in the Sonsierra and ferrous clay terraces of Alto Najerilla (high altitude, north-facing slopes of Sierra de la Demanda)
- Vinification: Co-fermentation of all varieties in stainless steel at 17-18C using indigenous yeasts from different vineyard sites; hand-harvested on a single day at different ripeness stages for complexity
- Elevage: 36 months in 225L Bordeaux-format barrels of European oak (Quercus petraea) from France and Eastern Europe; 1/3 new, 1/3 one use, 1/3 two uses; followed by extended bottle ageing before release
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2014 Rioja)
2014 was rated "good" by the Rioja Consejo Regulador. A cold spring and recurrent wet weather caused uneven ripening and a delayed harvest extending into November in some areas. Yields were slightly above 2012 levels. The control board found the 2014 whites and roses to be of exceptional quality specifically. The vintage is considered drinkable in the near-to-mid term, positioned between the difficult 2013 and the outstanding 2015. For a Gran Reserva Blanco with extended oak and bottle ageing, the lower-extraction, cooler-vintage character likely preserved freshness and acidity, contributing to the wine's balance at 12.5% ABV. (source: Harpers, BKWine, Decanter vintage spotlight)

## Producer style
Hacienda Lopez de Haro is the flagship brand of the Vintae Group (Arambarri family), founded in 2003 with a winery built in 2006 in San Vicente de la Sonsierra. Production is approximately one million bottles across two tiers: the main Lopez de Haro range (Crianza through Gran Reserva in red, plus white and rose) and the Classica line -- premium Gran Reserva wines released only in outstanding vintages from all three colours. The Classica Blanco Gran Reserva represents the traditional, extended-ageing white Rioja style reminiscent of Lopez de Heredia Vina Gravonia and CVNE Monopole Gran Reserva. The producer targets both traditional Rioja enthusiasts and a younger consumer base through wine tourism. Market positioning is quality-focused but accessible -- not at the prestige pricing of Lopez de Heredia or Muga. Scored 92 points by Vinous (Joaquin Hidalgo, 2025) and awarded IWSC Silver Medal (92 points).

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 11 in the 2025 Paper 1, Question 4: "Wines 10-12 are from three different European countries." The question asks candidates to comment on style with reference to human inputs versus natural factors (3 x 15 marks) and identify grape variety and origin (3 x 10 marks). This wine tests the candidate's ability to recognise the distinctive oxidative/oak-aged white Rioja style -- a classic MW curveball where the extended barrel ageing and Viura-based blend creates a profile that could be confused with aged white Burgundy, Jura, or even white Rhone. The question's emphasis on "human inputs versus natural factors" is directly relevant: the 36-month oak regime and traditional winemaking approach are the dominant stylistic signatures, far outweighing terroir expression. Paired alongside a Mosel Riesling (w10, Germany) and a Vin Santo (w12, Italy), representing three radically different European white wine philosophies.
