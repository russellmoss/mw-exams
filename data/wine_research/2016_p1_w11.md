# Vouvray, Domaine Marc Brédif, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold; the classic Chenin Blanc Vouvray profile. Nose: quince, green apple, honey, wool/lanolin, chamomile, light mineral. The key Chenin Blanc indicators at 3 years of age: honeyed but still fresh, with the variety's characteristic high acidity cutting through any sweetness. Style: sec or demi-sec (12.5% ABV suggests sec or very light demi-sec for 2013; cool vintage would promote this). Palate: medium body, high acid, off-dry to medium (RS likely 4–15 g/L range for Brédif sec/demi-sec style), long mineral finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc (mandatory for all Vouvray AOC)
- Vinification: stainless steel or old neutral oak; natural yeasts typical; no oak influence; residual sugar level depends on vintage and cuveé designation — Brédif produces both sec and demi-sec tiers; at 12.5% ABV this is likely sec or demi-sec
- ABV: 12.5% / RS: 4–25 g/L (sec to demi-sec range; exact designation — Source needed) / TA: high (Chenin Blanc typical)
- Vouvray is capable of extraordinary aging — Huet 1947 ranked 6th in Decanter's "100 Greatest Wines" (confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vouvray_(wine))
- Production: Marc Brédif is one of Vouvray's best-known négociants/estates, well-established in export markets

## Vintage character (2013 Vouvray/Loire)
2013 was a cool, late vintage in the Loire Valley — naturally the wines showed high acidity and lower sugar accumulation than warmer years. For Vouvray, the 2013s are lean, precise, and acidic — typical sec to demi-sec style. The Chenin Blanc's natural high acidity was well-expressed. At 3 years (2016) this wine was drinking in its early maturity window but built for longer aging. (Source needed — specific 2013 Vouvray vintage report)

## Producer style
Marc Brédif is a historic Vouvray producer founded in 1893; one of the most widely exported Vouvray labels internationally. Not a small artisan estate but a well-established commercial producer/négociant with consistent quality. The house is best known for its entry-level sec Vouvray and the medium-sweet Réserve. They also have a renowned library of old vintages (some dating back to the 1940s). (Source needed — marcobredif.com inaccessible during research)
