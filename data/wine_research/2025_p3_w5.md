# La Bruja, Comando G, 2022. Valle del Tietar, Sierra de Gredos, Spain

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with a translucent, almost Pinot-like appearance. Perfumed and lively nose of wild berries, subtle earthiness, herbs, and black pepper. The palate is medium-bodied with fine tannins, showing bright red cherry, pomegranate, raspberry and strawberry with a touch of wet-earth minerality. Refreshing acidity gives a Burgundian-like lift. Juicy, aromatic, fruit-driven — 94 points Wine Advocate.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Garnacha
- Vineyard: Old vines (50-80 years), high altitude (~900m), granitic/sandy soils with slate and quartz
- Vinification: Hand-harvested, natural yeast fermentation, long maceration (adapted shorter in 2022), partly fermented with less skin contact
- Elevage: 9 months in French oak vats (large format, neutral)
- ABV: 14% / RS: Source needed / TA: Source needed
- Biodynamic farming

## Vintage character (2022 Sierra de Gredos)
The 2022 was a warm vintage in central Spain. Comando G adapted by macerating less to maintain freshness and elegance. The resulting wine is more approachable and fruit-driven — "more in the style of the 2020" — open and expressive with good ripeness.

## Producer style
Comando G was founded in 2008 by Daniel Gomez Jimenez Landi and Fernando Garcia, two oenology graduates who met at the University of Madrid. They pioneered the rediscovery of old-vine Garnacha in the Sierra de Gredos mountains. Farmed biodynamically, they model a Burgundian hierarchy: La Bruja de Rozas is their village-level wine, with single-parcel wines positioned as "Grand Cru Garnacha." The project has inspired a new generation of Spanish winemakers and put Gredos on the fine wine map as a source of elegant, mineral, altitude-driven Garnacha.

## Why it's in this exam
Part of the Spanish trio (Q2, wines 4-6). La Bruja represents the "new Spain" — high-altitude, old-vine Garnacha with Burgundian elegance, a stark contrast to both the traditional-method Cava (w4) and the ancient-solera Oloroso VORS (w6). The candidate must identify the grape variety and origin, and assess commercial position within the context of Spain's evolving fine wine landscape. The wine's Burgundian parallels and light extraction may challenge candidates expecting fuller-bodied Spanish reds.
