# Puligny-Montrachet Vieilles Vignes, Vincent Girardin, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with lemon, white peach, almond, struck stone, and a fine oak sheen. The palate is dry and concentrated, with Puligny's typical mineral tension carrying through a richer, barrel-shaped texture and a long, chalky finish. In blind tasting it should read as serious white Burgundy: measured oak, ripe but not tropical fruit, and precise acidity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Vieilles vignes parcels in Puligny-Montrachet
- Soils: Brown limestone with marl-clay influence according to merchant technical detail
- Vinification: Hand-harvested
- Elevage: 14 months in 228L French oak barrels, about 15% new, without bâtonnage

## Vintage character (2013 Burgundy)
2013 in white Burgundy was a cooler, later, fresher vintage than many surrounding years, often giving good acidity and classic shape rather than broad opulence. In Puligny-Montrachet that usually means stronger mineral definition and tension, provided fruit ripeness was achieved. This is useful exam territory because the wine can be rich from élevage without losing its cool-vintage line.

## Producer style
Vincent Girardin is a well-known négociant-producer with a reputation for polished, terroir-conscious white Burgundy. The style generally aims at precision and texture, using oak to frame rather than dominate village and premier cru whites. This cuvée sits in the classic Puligny space where minerality, oak judgement, and fine balance matter more than sheer power.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 1 among Chardonnay-based wines, with explicit attention to oak. It allows the candidate to contrast still white Burgundy barrel work with both traditional-method Champagne and the New World Chardonnay later in the question.
