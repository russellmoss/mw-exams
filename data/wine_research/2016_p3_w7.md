# 20 Year Old Tawny Port, Graham's, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Medium tawny with amber rim, showing walnut, toffee, fig, orange peel, coffee, and polished wood. The palate is sweet but balanced by spirit and acidity, with creamy texture and a long, nutty, oxidative finish. Blind, it is clearly aged tawny Port rather than ruby Port because primary berry fruit has given way to cask-derived complexity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Traditional Douro red blend
- Vineyard: Graham's estate and partner fruit from the Douro Valley
- Soils: Schist-dominant Douro vineyards
- Vinification: Fortified during fermentation to retain sweetness
- Elevage: Aged for an average of 20 years in seasoned oak casks
- ABV / RS / TA: 20.0% / sweet / Source needed

## Vintage character (NV aged tawny Port)
As with all age-indicated tawnies, the style is defined by blending and prolonged cask maturation rather than a single harvest. The winemaker's job is to create a house expression that tastes approximately "20 years old" in aromatic complexity and texture. That is exactly the point an MW candidate needs to articulate in fortified-wine questions.

## Producer style
Graham's is one of the leading Port houses and is especially known for rich, generous styles. Even in the aged tawny range, the wines typically retain depth and texture alongside the expected nutty oxidative complexity. The 20 Year Old sits in the premium but still relatively accessible part of the Port market and is a classic benchmark bottling.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 2 with other fortified wines. It allows the examiner to test whether the candidate understands fortification, cask ageing, age-indication blending, and the sensory differences between tawny Port and Sherry or Madeira.
