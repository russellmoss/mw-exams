# Barbaresco, Produttori del Barbaresco, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium garnet-ruby with rose, red cherry, orange peel and tarry savour. The palate is dry, firmly structured and aromatic rather than heavy, with grippy but ripe tannin and a long, savoury finish. This is textbook Nebbiolo from Barbaresco with early openness but clear ageing potential.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Nebbiolo
- Vineyard: Blend of multiple cooperative member vineyards within Barbaresco
- Soils: Calcareous marl and limestone-rich Barbaresco hillside soils
- Vinification: Traditional Nebbiolo handling; Source needed on exact 2017 tank regime
- Elevage: Large old oak cask maturation in the house style

## Vintage character (2017 Barbaresco)
Produttori notes a hot, dry 2017 season, with frost and hail losses but enough ripe fruit and tannic structure to make balanced, pleasant Barbaresco. That points to a more open-knit, earlier approachable vintage than 2016, but still unmistakably structured Nebbiolo.

## Producer style
Produttori del Barbaresco is one of Piedmont's great cooperatives and one of the clearest reference points for classic Barbaresco. The blended village Barbaresco emphasizes perfume, transparency and ageworthy structure rather than heavy extraction or flashy oak.

## Why it's in this exam
This is the European wine in the mixed-variety, mixed-continent trio of 2021 Paper 2 Question 2. It tests whether the candidate can move from New World Pinot and McLaren Vale Grenache into a classical Nebbiolo frame of tannin, perfume and tar-and-rose complexity.
