# Chinon, Domaine de la Semellerie, 2014. Loire, France

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby, clear with a violet-blue tint (youth indicator). Nose of fresh red fruit (raspberry, redcurrant, strawberry), pencil shavings, green pepper, leafy herbs, and violet — Cabernet franc's unmistakable aromatic blueprint, more Loire-pure and less structured than Bordeaux. Light-to-medium body, fresh acidity, fine silky tannins (characteristic of Chinon limestone terroir), short-to-medium finish with herbaceous precision. 12.5% ABV signals cool-climate restraint and freshness over power.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Cabernet franc (the exclusive variety in Chinon AOC)
- Vineyard: Cravant-les-Côteaux, highest point of the appellation, 47 ha estate (source: domainedelasemellerie.fr); argilo-siliceous (argile-sableux) soils with stones, well-drained
- Soils: Argilo-siliceous (clay-sandy with silica) over tufa subsoil — gives freshness and mineral precision to the wines (source: domainedelasemellerie.fr)
- Vinification: Traditional methods with temperature control; family estate operation since 1964, managed by Fabrice Delalande since 1990 (source: domainedelasemellerie.fr)
- Elevage: Typically large neutral oak foudres or cement/stainless at entry Chinon level; Source needed for exact Semellerie protocol
- ABV: 12.5%

## Vintage character (2014 Loire / Chinon)
2014 was a very good vintage in the Loire Valley — an excellent growing season with good sunshine leading to ripe, balanced Cabernet franc. Better than the difficult 2013. Chinon 2014 produced wines with clean red fruit, fresh acidity, and characteristic herbaceous lift. At the 12.5% level, typical Loire freshness and approachability are evident.

## Producer style
Domaine de la Semellerie is a traditional family-owned Chinon estate established in 1964 by Jean-Claude Delalande and now run by his son Fabrice. The 47 hectares are planted exclusively to Cabernet franc at the highest point of Cravant-les-Côteaux — one of Chinon's better terroir locations. The domain sells to private buyers, restaurants, and distributors throughout France; this is a serious artisan producer at commercial-to-mid-range pricing. Source: domainedelasemellerie.fr.

## Why it's in this exam
Chinon Cabernet franc represents the cool-climate, Old World style of the variety — contrasting against warmer-climate or New World Cabernet franc or blended wines in P2. Key exam signals: herbaceous freshness, low ABV, Loire restraint, 100% Cabernet franc single-variety character, fine tannins on tufa/sand soils. The challenge under blind conditions is distinguishing Loire Cab Franc (Chinon/Bourgueil) from Saumur or from lighter Cabernet-franc-dominant Bordeaux.
