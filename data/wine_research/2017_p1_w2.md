# Savennieres, Chateau d'Epire, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Medium lemon with some development. The nose should show lanolin, yellow apple, quince, dried flowers, and a faint waxy, smoky edge; the palate is dry, firm, and textural, with Chenin's grip and a long mineral finish rather than easy fruit.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc
- Vineyard: Savennieres on the north bank of the Loire west of Angers

## Vintage character (2014 Loire)
2014 was a fresher, later-feeling Loire vintage than 2015, preserving acidity and definition. In dry Chenin from Savennieres, that often means tension, slow development, and a more severe mineral line in youth.

## Producer style
Chateau d'Epire is a classic Savennieres reference point: traditional, restrained, and not built around overt new oak. The style prioritizes longevity, texture, and schist-driven austerity over immediate charm.

## Why it's in this exam
This was part of the 2017 paper 1 Loire flight. It gives the exam a serious, ageworthy Chenin benchmark against the lighter Muscadet and the aromatic Sauvignon-based wine in the same opening question.
