# Shiraz The Bogan, Kaesler, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Deep crimson with purple hue. Dark plum, blueberry, berry compote, charcuterie, charred meat, and coffee aromas from integrated oak. Full-bodied, dense, and rich on the palate with layers of dark fruit. Firm yet fine tannins with a lick of salty licorice. Despite the power, the 2021 vintage brings tension and a savoury shape that tempers the pure Barossa horsepower. 95 points Halliday, 93 points The Real Review.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Shiraz
- Vineyard: Old vines in Marananga and Nuriootpa, some blocks ~120 years old, dry-grown
- Soils: Source needed (Barossa Valley typically red-brown earth over clay and limestone)
- Vinification: Handpicked and sorted, destemmed, fermented in tank for 8-9 days with pump-overs
- Elevage: 15 months in seasoned French oak (2021 vintage reportedly all seasoned; prior vintages used ~25% new French oak)

## Vintage character (2021 Barossa Valley)
Rated "Brilliant" — cool and dry conditions produced excellent quality. Lower yields and slower ripening delivered concentration with balance. Considered one of the great Barossa vintages, with winemakers noting exceptional purity and structure. The Bogan 2021 is described as arguably the best in its 20-year history. (source: Halliday Wine Companion)

## Producer style
Kaesler's origins trace to Silesian settlers who planted 96 acres in the Barossa in 1891-93. Some of those original Shiraz vines still produce fruit. Revitalized in 1999 under Reid Bosward's winemaking. 5-star Halliday-rated. Known for bold, old-vine Barossa wines — the "Old Bastard" Shiraz is their flagship (Langton's "Outstanding" classification). The Bogan sits in the premium-but-accessible tier, typically AUD 50-70. Sustainable, dry-grown viticulture with minimal intervention.

## Why it's in this exam
The full-throttle Barossa benchmark in the Syrah/Shiraz trio: big, rich, oaky, and high-alcohol (14.5%) against Montes' cool-climate restraint (14%) and Barret's ultra-low-alcohol natural Cornas (12.5%). The name "The Bogan" itself signals the unpretentious, generous Australian Shiraz archetype. The question on consumer appeal invites discussion of the approachable, fruit-forward commercial style versus the intellectual/terroir-driven positioning of the Cornas.
