# Riesling Beerenauslese, Dr. Loosen, 2018. Mosel, Germany

## Quick tasting profile
Deep golden colour, viscous. The nose is intensely concentrated with candied apricot, pineapple, orange marmalade, jasmine, and honey. On the palate, unctuous honey and peach flavours are dusted with slate minerality. Despite 155 g/L RS, the wine is effortlessly balanced by electric acidity (9.3 g/L TA), giving a high-toned lemon-lime finish. Full body, low alcohol (7.5%), immensely long. 20+ year ageing potential.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Estate vineyards, likely red slate soils (source: Wine Advocate notes reference "frisky character from red slate soils")
- Vinification: Late-harvested botrytis-affected berries, individually selected. Slow, cool fermentation arrested at low alcohol.
- RS: 155 g/L
- TA: 9.3 g/L
- ABV: 7.5%

## Vintage character (2018 Mosel)
2018 was a warm, dry vintage across Germany. The Mosel experienced an exceptionally long growing season with high must weights. Ideal conditions for botrytis development in late autumn produced outstanding Beerenauslese and Trockenbeerenauslese wines with remarkable concentration and balance.

## Producer style
See Wine 1 (2025_p3_w1) for full Dr. Loosen profile. The estate's sweet wines are benchmarks for the Mosel — produced in tiny quantities from the estate's top vineyards. The BA is sold primarily in 187ml bottles, indicating its rarity and positioning as a luxury dessert wine. Wine Advocate rated the 2018 BA at 93 points.

## Why it's in this exam
The sweet bookend of the Riesling trio (Q1, wines 1-3). The candidate must identify Riesling through the lens of extreme botrytised sweetness — a radically different expression from the sparkling (w1) and still (w2) versions. The low alcohol (7.5%) and very high RS (155 g/L) are strong signals of German Prädikatswein. Sharing the Dr. Loosen producer with w1 is an examiner's deliberate pairing — testing whether the candidate can discuss winemaking technique differences across the same producer.
