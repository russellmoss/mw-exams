# Portrait Shiraz, Peter Lehmann, 2013. Barossa Valley, Australia

## Quick tasting profile
Deep purple-ruby, youthful colour with strong extraction. Nose is archetypal Barossa Shiraz: blackberry, dark plum, cassis, black pepper, smoked meat, vanilla, and mocha from American/French oak. On the palate full-bodied, with generous ripe tannins, medium-plus acidity, and a long, warming finish. At 14.5% ABV this is a textbook warm-climate, entry-to-mid premium Barossa Shiraz — powerful but not overblown.

## Vintage character (2013 Barossa Valley)
2013 was a warm to hot vintage in the Barossa Valley with early harvest conditions producing ripe, generous fruit. Standard Barossa character: dark fruit concentration, firm tannins, warm alcohol. The Portrait tier is made for approachable, commercially positioned drinking rather than extended cellaring, so by 2015 the wine would be fully open and expressive. ## Producer style
Peter Lehmann Wines was founded by "The Baron of the Barossa" Peter Lehmann in 1979 as a champion of small growers. In 2003 the company was sold to a Swiss family (the Hess Group purchased it later in 2014, the year before this exam). The winery sources from long-term contracted growers across the Barossa, emphasising community relationships. Portrait is the entry-level commercial range within their portfolio hierarchy (below VSV, Masters, and Stonewell). The estate style is traditional Barossa: generous fruit, accessible tannins, American oak at the entry level. Priced at the accessible premium tier (approximately AUD 20-25 retail).
