# Meursault 1er Cru Les Charmes Dessus, Chateau de Meursault, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Medium lemon-gold with evident concentration and some early development. The nose should show ripe citrus, hazelnut, butter, cream, and toasted oak; the palate is full, dry, and layered, with Meursault breadth supported by enough acidity to keep the finish defined.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Les Charmes Dessus, Meursault Premier Cru
- Soils: Limestone and marl typical of the Cote de Beaune slope
- Vinification: Barrel fermentation and maturation are central to the style

## Vintage character (2013 Burgundy)
2013 in Burgundy was a later, cooler vintage marked by lower yields and fresher profiles than 2012. In white Burgundy, that often means tauter acids and less opulence, though top sites and richer villages like Meursault still carry notable depth.

## Producer style
Chateau de Meursault works in a polished, classically Burgundian register with visible but usually integrated oak. This cuvee aims for breadth and texture while still showing Premier Cru status and the generosity associated with Charmes.

## Why it's in this exam
This was paired with Chablis in 2017 paper 1 as the same grape variety from different Burgundian origins. It tests whether the candidate can distinguish oaked, richer, village-signature Meursault from the cooler, more linear Chablis style.
