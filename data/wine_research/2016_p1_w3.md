# Chardonnay, Clos du Bois, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Medium to deep gold; the commercial California Chardonnay profile — generous toasty vanilla and butterscotch from new American oak, tropical fruit (pineapple, mango, banana), with full malo completing the butter/cream register. Medium-to-full body; moderate acidity from a warm California vintage; medium-long finish. This is the benchmark for accessible, market-oriented California Chardonnay — designed for approachability over ageability.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- AVA: labelled simply "California" — sourced from multiple sub-appellations; no single vineyard
- Vinification: 100% malolactic fermentation; barrel fermented and/or aged in American oak with significant new oak; sur lie aging; commercial winemaking designed for consistency at scale
- Production volume: very large — selling over 200,000 cases/year by late 1980s (confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clos_du_Bois); now even larger under Constellation Brands ownership
- ABV: 13.5% / RS: dry to off-dry (some commercial CA Chardonnay has 2–4 g/L RS for approachability) / TA: relatively low for CA

## Vintage character (2014 California)
2014 was part of California's drought period — warm, dry year producing ripe, generous fruit with lower natural acidity. For Clos du Bois at a commercial California-appellation level, vintage variation matters less than house style consistency. 2014 wines showed very ripe fruit with warm-climate alcohol; acid additions likely to maintain freshness. ## Producer style
Clos du Bois founded by Thomas Reed and Frank Woods (both Cornell alumni) in Sonoma County in the 1970s. Reached 200,000 cases/year by late 1980s. Sold to Hiram Walker for ~$40 million in 1988; subsequently passed to Constellation Brands via multiple acquisitions. Now a mass-market brand with wide US supermarket distribution. Positioned as approachable, full-flavoured, commercially styled California Chardonnay. Direct contrast in this exam with Kistler McCrea (2016_p1_w4 — premium single-vineyard California Chardonnay). (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clos_du_Bois)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 1 white wines. Clos du Bois California Chardonnay is the commodity-tier benchmark — used to contrast with the premium Kistler McCrea Vineyard (also California Chardonnay, w4). The MW examiner is testing whether candidates can distinguish quality tiers within the same variety and region: both are California Chardonnay but one is commodity-large-production, the other is single-vineyard-premium. The Clos du Bois profile (heavy oak, full malo, tropical fruit, lower acidity) contrasts with Kistler's precision.
