# Acodo White Rioja, Basilio Izquierdo, 2010

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold to amber at 6 years of age; classic white Rioja profile — nutty, waxy, complex, with barrel-aging character. Nose: toasted hazelnut, quince paste, lanolin, yellow apple, subtle vanilla, slight oxidative note from extended oak aging. Palate: medium-to-full body, dry, richer texture than most white wines; long finish with bittersweet almond and honey. Viura is the dominant variety in traditional white Rioja — can age superbly. At 6 years old in 2016, this wine is in its mature drinking window.

2010 was an outstanding vintage in Rioja — one of the best of the decade, producing wines of great concentration, structure, and freshness. The classic growing season produced balanced ripeness with natural acidity. For a white wine intended for extended aging, 2010 provided excellent material. ## Producer style
Basilio Izquierdo is a respected winemaker who worked at CVNE before establishing his own project. Small artisan producer in Rioja Alta. Acodo (the vine-layering technique) is the signature wine. Positioned as a boutique, artisan, premium expression of traditional Rioja white — deliberately reclaiming the aged Viura barrel-fermented style that was dominant before the 1980s shift to fresh reductive whites. Limited production; sought after by collectors. (Source needed — producer website not accessible during research)

A traditional barrel-aged white Rioja at 6 years of age is a significant curveball for blind tasting — the aged, nutty profile could be mistaken for aged Burgundy, aged Alsace, Jura, or even mature Hunter Valley Semillon.
