# Taurasi Riserva Piano di Montevergine, Feudi di San Gregorio, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby and unmistakably southern Italian in power, but with volcanic-mineral tension rather than jammy sweetness. Expect black cherry, plum, balsamic and spice notes, with firm Aglianico tannin, high acidity, and a long savory finish that still needs or rewards bottle age.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Aglianico
- Vineyard: Piano di Montevergine, described by the producer as one of the estate's symbolic sites among hazelnut trees and olive groves
- Soils: Source needed
- Vinification: alcoholic fermentation and maceration in stainless steel for about 3-4 weeks
- Elevage: 18-24 months in French oak barriques and 50hl casks, then at least 24 months in bottle

## Vintage character (2015 Taurasi)
An authoritative bottle-specific 2015 Taurasi vintage report was not located, but Kobrand's 2015 Taurasi note for Campania describes the year as healthy and generous in both quality and quantity. That broad framing suggests a ripe, successful vintage rather than a dilute or hard one. For exam purposes, the key remains that Taurasi's structure comes as much from Aglianico's acidity and tannin as from heat or oak.

## Producer style
Feudi di San Gregorio has spent decades repositioning Campania's native grapes - especially Greco, Fiano and Aglianico - at the fine-wine level. The estate archive emphasizes a long commitment to studying Irpinia's terroir, while the Piano di Montevergine sheet shows a polished cellar approach using both barrique and large cask to civilize Aglianico without erasing it. This is modern benchmark Taurasi, not rustic old-school austerity for its own sake.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 3 in 2023 Paper 2 Question 1 broadens the Italian-red challenge beyond the north. It tests whether a candidate can identify Taurasi not just from grape and tannin, but from the combination of southern ripeness, volcanic seriousness, long maturation and capacity to age on a different curve from Barolo.
