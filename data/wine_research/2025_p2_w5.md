# Crozes-Hermitage "Papillon", Gilles Robin, 2023

## Quick tasting profile
Deep, vivid ruby. Nose of small dark fruits, cherries, white pepper, and licorice — clean and precise. Medium-bodied palate with a round, firm attack that gains amplitude, expressing ripe fruit with lively freshness. Velvety young tannins that will develop over 2–3 years. An elegant, fruit-forward Northern Rhône Syrah meant for relatively early drinking but with sufficient structure for short-term ageing.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Syrah
- Vineyard: Young Syrah plantings on pebble/gravel terroir with argilo-sandy subsoil
- Soils: Pebbles/gravel over clay-sand (les Chassis plateau)
- Vinification: Organic certified; whole-bunch fermentation (100% since 2019); optical sorting table (since 2013); gentle, slow extractions (30+ days)
- Élevage: 8 months in tank (no oak for Papillon cuvée)

## Producer style
The Robin family has grown grapes in Crozes-Hermitage for four generations, starting with a 1.27 ha plot acquired in 1920. Gilles took over in 1996, bottling his own wines instead of selling to the cooperative. The estate has expanded to 22 ha with holdings in Hermitage, Cornas, St-Joseph, and St-Péray. Certified organic since 2012. Average vine age over 40 years across the estate. Meticulous, understated winemaking focused on revealing Syrah's typicity. Since 2019, all reds use 100% whole bunches. Papillon is the entry-level cuvée — tank-aged, fruit-forward, expressive of young-vine Syrah on the flat les Chassis terroir.
