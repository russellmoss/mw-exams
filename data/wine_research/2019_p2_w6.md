# Malbec, Pulenta Estate, 2016. Mendoza, Argentina

## Quick tasting profile
Deep, inky purple-ruby with violet rim. Nose of ripe black plum, dark cherry, blueberry compote, violet flowers, dark chocolate and vanilla/mocha from new oak. Palate is full-bodied, plush, with soft but substantial tannins, moderate acidity, and a generous, round fruit-driven finish. Classic high-altitude Mendoza Malbec: lush yet structured.

## Vintage character (2016 Mendoza)
2016 was a challenging vintage in Mendoza due to late frosts in October and then a hot, dry summer. Yields were reduced but quality for surviving grapes was high — concentrated, well-structured wines with good natural depth. Considered a good-to-very-good vintage for premium Mendoza Malbec.

## Producer style
Pulenta Estate was established by the Pulenta family, who are one of Mendoza's historic wine dynasties (their broader family previously owned Trapiche before selling). Pulenta Estate makes high-quality, single-estate wines at multiple price points. Their Malbec is a commercially positioned premium-tier wine positioned at $20–40 range. Style is polished, modern and fruit-forward with good structure — exports significantly to the US and European markets. (Source needed for specific ownership and founding date confirmation.)

## Why it's in this exam
Pulenta Estate Malbec is a textbook premium Mendoza Malbec: the archetype the MW candidate should recognise. At 14% ABV with an inky colour and rich fruit profile, it signals New World warm climate immediately. Distinguishing from Chilean Malbec (cooler, lighter) or from a Bordeaux blend is the key exam task.
