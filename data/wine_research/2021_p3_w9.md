# Oloroso Pata de Gallina Almacenista, Lustau, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber-mahogany with olive-green rim — classic oxidative Oloroso colour; complex nose of walnut, dried figs, roasted hazelnuts, tobacco, dark toffee, leather and subtle sea salt; fully dry on the palate (technically ≤5 g/L RS) with a powerful, viscous texture; high alcohol (20% ABV) integrates well with the oxidative richness; very long, nutty, saline finish. Distinguished from sweet Oloroso/Cream Sherry by the complete absence of residual sugar.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Palomino Fino (the standard grape for dry Sherry)
- Vineyard: Albariza soils (chalk-limestone) of the Jerez triangle
- Vinification: Fermented to dryness; fortified to 17-18% (flor cannot survive above ~15.5%), suppressing development of flor; placed into oxidative aging from the start
- Elevage: Criadera and solera (fractional blending system) in old American oak butts; no flor; full oxidative aging
- Almacenista origin: "Pata de Gallina" refers to the specific almacenista (small stockholder/single-producer) source; Lustau bottles and markets wines from small almacenista bodegas under this sub-range (Source: Wikipedia/Lustau)
- ABV / RS / TA: 20% / dry (0-5 g/L RS) / medium acidity

## Vintage character (NV solera)
Non-vintage solera; age of the wine reflects the average time in the criadera-solera system. "Pata de Gallina" refers to the distinctive wrinkled "hen's foot" appearance of the butt wood grain. The Almacenista range features wines from individual small-scale stockholders — providing a single-source character contrasting with Lustau's blended commercial range.

## Producer style
Emilio Lustau, founded 1896 in Jerez de la Frontera, is one of the most internationally recognised quality Sherry producers. Their distinctive Almacenista range (since the 1980s) sources wines from small family bodegas, bottling them under the producer's name — a unique terroir/producer expression for Sherry. Acquired by Grupo Caballero in 1990. Known for exceptional quality across the Sherry range. (Source: Wikipedia)

## Why it's in this exam
Lustau Almacenista Oloroso is the dry Sherry benchmark in this fortified flight, paired with the sweet Oloroso (Matusalem Gonzales Byass, w10). Tests the candidate's ability to (a) identify dry vs sweet Oloroso, (b) distinguish Oloroso character (walnut/oxidative) from other fortified wines including Tawny Port and Madeira, and (c) understand the Almacenista production category.
