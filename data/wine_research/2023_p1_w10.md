# Chenin Blanc, David & Nadia, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
Expect a dry, layered Swartland Chenin with waxy citrus, yellow apple, preserved lemon and light herbal or salty notes rather than overt tropical sweetness. The palate should be medium-bodied and textural, with bright acidity, old-vine concentration, and understated oak influence, reading as soil-driven Cape Chenin rather than fruity commercial Chenin.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc
- Vineyard: multi-vineyard old-vine blend from Swartland sites; the producer note for the adjacent vintages emphasizes old dry-farmed bush vines from several vineyards
- Soils: a mix of Paardeberg granite with schist/shale, clay-rich northern sites, and iron-rich western Swartland soils
- Vinification: whole-bunch pressed and naturally fermented
- Elevage: 11 months in old neutral French oak; Wine.com also notes 18% of the wine in concrete

## Vintage character (2020 Swartland)
Justerini's 2020 Swartland report describes a cool winter, stable flowering and a steady growing season without excessive heat, producing healthy fruit and a larger, higher-quality crop than recent drought years. David Sadie is quoted there as reporting more time to plan harvest decisions and better volume without sacrificing quality. In blind terms, 2020 should therefore show ripe fruit but not heaviness, with freshness preserved by timing and dry-farmed old-vine balance.

## Producer style
David & Nadia describe themselves as a Swartland-driven family project focused on organic or biologically farmed old dryland bush vines, minimal cellar intervention, and pure, fresh wines that let site and soil speak. Their main Chenin is effectively a regional statement wine, built from multiple old-vine parcels to express Swartland rather than one single site. Commercially it sits in the serious fine-wine tier of modern Cape Chenin, not the easy-drinking mass market.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 10 sits in 2023 Paper 1 Question 3, the South Africa set where candidates must identify the country and varieties, then discuss winemaking plus quality and market potential. It is a strong foil to the more polished, broader Ken Forrester Chenin because it tests whether candidates can articulate the modern Swartland old-vine, low-intervention Chenin idiom.
