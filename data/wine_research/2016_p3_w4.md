# Legacy Chardonnay, Vidal, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Bright gold with ripe citrus, stone fruit, toasted hazelnut, meal, and sweet oak spice. The palate is full-bodied and creamy, with concentrated fruit, integrated acidity, and a distinctly New World generosity held in check by Hawke's Bay freshness. Blind, this sits in the premium barrel-fermented Chardonnay space rather than reductive, stainless-steel style.

## Vintage character (2014 Hawke's Bay)
2014 was a strong Hawke's Bay Chardonnay year, producing ripe fruit without sacrificing line and freshness. For top examples that meant concentration and texture, but not the tropical softness that would blur varietal shape. That combination makes the wine a strong New World counterpoint to white Burgundy in exam conditions.

## Producer style
Vidal is one of Hawke's Bay's long-established names and its Legacy range represents the top tier of the estate's still wines. The Chardonnay style aims for richness, oak integration, and ageworthy concentration rather than simple fruit-forward appeal. In an MW practical set it works as a premium, polished New World Chardonnay benchmark.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 1 with a strong emphasis on oak handling across all wines. It lets the candidate compare premium New World barrel-fermented Chardonnay with Puligny-Montrachet and Chardonnay-based sparkling wines from Champagne.
