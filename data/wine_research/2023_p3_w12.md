# 40 Year Tawny Port, Kopke, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber with greenish rim, intensely nutty and oxidative, with fig, raisin, walnut, vanilla and honey. On the palate it is dense yet fresh, with enormous concentration but enough acidity and spirit integration to keep the wine poised rather than cloying (source: https://kopke1638.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/KOPKE_40YOTawny-1.pdf).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Traditional Douro grape varieties (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/)
- Vineyard: Douro vineyards up to 600m (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/)
- Soils: Schist-sandstone (greywacke), with granite in some places (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/)
- Vinification: Careful maceration in vats/lagares with churning during fermentation, then fortification with grape brandy at the desired baumé (source: https://kopke1638.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/KOPKE_40YOTawny-1.pdf)
- Elevage: Blend of different harvests; the age on the label is the average age of the blend and the wine is shaped by long cask maturation in the tawny style (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/; https://kopke1638.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/KOPKE_40YOTawny-1.pdf)
- ABV / RS / TA: 20.0% / 147 g/L / 5.36 g/L (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/)

## Vintage character (NV / category character)
This is non-vintage age-indicated Port, so blending and cask age are the point. The exam takeaway is that 40-year tawny is a luxury oxidative category defined by average age and house style continuity, not by a single harvest declaration.

## Producer style
Kopke, founded in 1638, trades heavily on historical prestige and is especially associated with aged tawny and old-stock fortified wines (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/). Commercially this sits at the top end of the category: expensive, niche and strongly tied to heritage value.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 12 is the most overt classification test in 2023 Paper 3, Question 4. It asks for precise discussion of age-indicated tawny Port, extended oxidative ageing, blending logic and premium commercial positioning, all while distinguishing it sharply from the Fine Ruby in the previous glass.
