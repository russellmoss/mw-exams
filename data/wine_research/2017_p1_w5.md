# Pinot Gris, Neudorf, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Riper and cleaner-fruited than the Alsace comparator, with pear, white peach, blossom, and gentle spice. The palate is generous but controlled, with moderate acidity, rounded texture, and a dry to just-off-dry impression depending on serving and bottle evolution.

## Vintage character (2015 Nelson)
2015 in New Zealand generally delivered clean fruit, good concentration, and sound ripeness in the better sites. In Nelson Pinot Gris, that usually supports generous orchard fruit and texture without losing overall freshness.

## Producer style
Neudorf is one of Nelson's benchmark producers, known for thoughtful, site-led wines rather than overtly commercial styling. Even in Pinot Gris, the emphasis is on poise and texture rather than sweetness or simple aromatics.

## Why it's in this exam
This was the New World foil to the mature Alsace Pinot Gris in 2017 paper 1. The point of the pair is to test how the same variety can move from savory, developed, and weighty to more primary, polished, and modern-fruited.
