# Blaufränkisch, Weingut Heinrich, 2015. Leithaberg, Austria

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with violet-purple tint. Nose of sour red cherry, bilberry, dark berries, with characteristic Blaufränkisch spice: black pepper, dried herbs, and a distinct mineral earthiness. Palate is medium-to-full bodied with firm, fresh acidity (the variety's signature), medium-to-high tannins, and a savoury, peppery finish. Leithaberg DAC style is fruit-driven with mineral and spicy notes — less extracted than Mittelburgenland examples.

## Vintage character (2015 Burgenland/Austria)
2015 was a warm year in Austria, producing wines of natural richness and good phenolic development. In Burgenland, this meant Blaufränkisch with ripe dark fruit, good concentration, and healthy acidity. The Leithaberg's elevated limestone sites tempered the heat somewhat, maintaining freshness. Considered a very good vintage for Austrian Blaufränkisch.

## Producer style
Weingut Heinrich is a leading Leithaberg producer, based in Gols in Burgenland. The Heinrich family (Gernot Heinrich) is known for biodynamic viticulture and elegant, terroir-driven expressions of Blaufränkisch and white varieties. The Leithaberg-designated wines represent their middle tier, showcasing the minerality of the limestone hillsides. The winery is respected internationally as one of Austria's quality benchmarks for Blaufränkisch. (Source needed for specific founding date and production volumes.)
