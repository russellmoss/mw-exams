# Vin Santo del Chianti Classico, Badia a Coltibuono, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Amber-gold and clearly oxidative, with walnut, almond, caramelised citrus, dried apricot, honey, and spice. The palate should be dense and sweet, yet surprisingly fresh, with long nutty persistence. In blind tasting it should stand apart from botrytis wines because the sweetness comes with oxidative development and long cask ageing rather than noble-rot signatures.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 50% Malvasia, 50% Trebbiano
- Vineyard: Gaiole in Chianti / Monti area
- Soils: clay and limestone
- Vinification: organically farmed fruit harvested manually, dried for months in open lofts before pressing
- Elevage: fermented and aged undisturbed for about six years in small caratelli of roughly 50-100L
- ABV / RS / TA: 15.5% / 185 g/L / 7.4 g/L

## Vintage character (2014 Chianti Classico)
The Consorzio's 2014 report describes a difficult season overall, but one capable of positive surprises where growers worked carefully and waited for ripeness. For Vin Santo, the point is less broad red-wine character than raw-material integrity: lower yields and careful grape selection help preserve acidity and definition through the long drying and ageing process.

## Producer style
Badia a Coltibuono is a historic Chianti Classico estate with deep roots and an organic identity. Its Vin Santo is presented by the estate and trade sources as a traditional Tuscan hospitality wine made in very small quantities, with an emphasis on patience, long drying, and long ageing in tiny casks rather than a modern fruit-first style.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 10 appears in 2024 Paper 3 Question 4 as the oxidative sweet-wine foil. It tests whether the candidate can separate passito-and-caratello sweetness from Sauternes, Icewine, Muscat de Beaumes de Venise, and demi-sec Chenin.
