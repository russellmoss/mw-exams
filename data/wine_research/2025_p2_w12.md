# Xinomavro Hedgehog, Alpha Estate, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby to purple-red. In blind tasting this should show the savory, high-acid, structured side of Xinomavro, but in a polished modern register: small red berries, blackberry, leather, pepper, clove and clear oak spice, with rounded tannins and a long, quince-tinged finish (source: https://alpha-estate.com/product/xinomavro-sv/). The structure and aromatic shape invite comparisons to Nebbiolo, but the fruit is darker and the oak handling more overt.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Xinomavro (source: https://alpha-estate.com/product/xinomavro-sv/)
- Vineyard: Amyndeon plateau, "Hedgehog" sub-region, 620-710m, north-facing toward Lake Petron and Mount Voras (source: https://alpha-estate.com/product/xinomavro-sv/)
- Soils: Sandy to sandy-clay with excellent drainage (source: https://internationalcellars.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/alpha_estate_xinomavro.pdf)
- Vinification: Destemmed, not crushed, cold soak/skin contact, fermentation at gradually increasing temperatures, eight months sur lie with stirring (source: https://alpha-estate.com/product/xinomavro-sv/)
- Elevage: 12 months in French oak, then 12 months in bottle (source: https://alpha-estate.com/product/xinomavro-sv/)
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.0% on the exam list; cited technical sheets show 13.5% / Source needed / 6.6 g/L on an importer sheet, so keep the ABV tied to the exam corpus and treat RS as unverified (source: data/wines.json; https://internationalcellars.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/alpha_estate_xinomavro.pdf)

## Producer style
Alpha Estate is one of modern Greece's flagship quality producers, working from a high-altitude, technologically advanced gravity-flow winery in Amyndeon (source: https://internationalcellars.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/alpha_estate_xinomavro.pdf). This bottling is not trying to be rustic or natural; it is a deliberate premium PDO wine built from precise viticulture, controlled extraction and explicit French-oak élevage.

## Why it's in this exam
This appears in 2025 Paper 2, Question 3, the all-European origin-and-variety question. It is an effective examiner trap because candidates who clock the high acid, tannic frame and rose-leather-spice family may drift toward Nebbiolo unless they also spot the darker fruit, modern oak and Greek Xinomavro context.
