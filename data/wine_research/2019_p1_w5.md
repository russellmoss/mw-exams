# Rioja Blanco, Allende, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Deep lemon with a savoury, oak-framed profile rather than a simple fruity one. Ripe citrus, yellow apple, fennel and stone fruit sit alongside toast, spice and a waxy texture. The palate is dry, broad and gastronomic, with moderate acidity and a long, layered finish.

## Vintage character (2015 Rioja)
The Rioja Consejo Regulador rated 2015 as "Very Good" and described it as an early, healthy harvest with strong ripening conditions. In stylistic terms, 2015 supports concentration and ripeness, which fits the breadth and substance expected from a barrel-shaped white Rioja like this.

## Producer style
Finca Allende is associated with a modern, quality-driven Rioja style that still takes traditional oak élevage seriously. The white wines sit at the more ambitious end of Rioja Blanco, emphasizing texture, barrel integration and ageing capacity rather than simple freshness.

This appears in 2019 Paper 1 Question 2 as part of the Spanish pair with wine 6. It gives the examiner a more oxidative-textural, oak-influenced white from Spain, contrasting sharply with the fresher Atlantic profile of the Rías Baixas wine.
