# Château Brown Blanc, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with green tinge; aromatic nose shows Sauvignon Blanc lead — grass, gooseberry, grapefruit — with honeyed Sémillon body. Some barrel fermentation character: vanilla, toast, creamy texture. Palate: medium-weight, dry, with citrus-dominant fruit and waxy mid-palate from Sémillon. Good length. A 70/30 Sauvignon/Sémillon blend typical of Pessac-Léognan Blanc. At 3 years of age in the 2016 exam, relatively fresh but showing barrel integration.

## Technical specs
- Grape varieties: 70% Sauvignon Blanc, 30% Sémillon (confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Château_Brown)
- Vineyard: 4.5 ha white variety plantings in Gradignan, Villenave-d'Ornon, and Léognan
- Vinification: barrel fermented in French oak (proportion new oak — Source needed); malolactic fermentation likely suppressed or partially undertaken
- Production volume: ~20,000 bottles of the dry white Grand Vin annually (confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Château_Brown)
- ABV: 13.5% / RS: dry / TA: moderate-elevated

## Vintage character (2013 Pessac-Léognan)
2013 was a difficult vintage in Bordeaux — wet spring led to millerandage and coulure, reducing yields. Cool summer with late warmth; the whites performed better than the reds in many estates. Sauvignon Blanc harvested relatively late with good aromatic preservation. For dry whites of 2013 in Pessac-Léognan, the wines showed good acidity and freshness rather than opulence. By 2016 the wine had 3 years of bottle integration. (Source needed — specific 2013 Pessac-Léognan white vintage report)

## Producer style
Château Brown, south of Bordeaux city; the Scottish trader John Lewis Brown acquired the estate in the late 18th century. Production halted in the 1950s, making it ineligible for the 1953 and 1959 Graves Classification. Acquired by the Mau family in 2004 alongside Dirkzwager (Dutch). Investment since 2004 has lifted wine quality. Overall production is led by red Bordeaux (23.5 ha red varietals), with white as a smaller but distinct proposition. Mid-tier Pessac-Léognan. (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Château_Brown)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 1 is white wines. A Pessac-Léognan Blanc tests identification of the Bordeaux white Sauvignon/Sémillon blend — herbal, barrel-fermented, dry. The MW examiner sets this as a regional contrast against Loire (pure SB), NZ (reductive SB), and Burgundy (Chardonnay) styles. Candidates must identify: France, Bordeaux/Pessac-Léognan, Sauvignon Blanc dominant blend, oak fermented.
