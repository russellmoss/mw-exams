# Savigny-les-Beaune Les Goudelettes, Domaine Antonin Guyon, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Transparent ruby rather than saturated purple, with red cherry, raspberry, rose, and a little vanilla or cedar from measured oak. On the palate this should be medium-bodied, fresh, and finely tannic, with more lift and perfume than power. In blind tasting it reads as a cool, elegant Pinot Noir from a classic Old World origin rather than a plush New World example.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: Les Goudelettes, 2.13ha in Savigny-les-Beaune
- Soils: clay and limestone
- Vinification: hand-harvested fruit, sorted on arrival, 15-day fermentation in open wooden vats with punch-downs and temperature control
- Elevage: 15 months in oak barrels, about 15% new oak

## Vintage character (2021 Burgundy)
2021 in Burgundy was a small, late, disease-pressured vintage. Bancroft's report highlights severe frost losses, mildew pressure, and a return to fresher, more classical balances after the heat-driven years that preceded it. That matters here: this wine is more likely to show tension, red-fruited precision, and moderate alcohol than richness or sweetly solar fruit.

## Producer style
Domaine Antonin Guyon is a sizeable but quality-focused family estate based in Savigny-les-Beaune, farming broadly across the Cote d'Or. Frederick Wildman describes a 47-hectare domaine run by Dominique and Hombeline Guyon, with hand harvesting, traditional fermentations, and moderate barrel ageing aimed at terroir expression rather than heavy extraction.
