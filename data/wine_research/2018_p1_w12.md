# Eitelsbacher Karthäuserhofberg Riesling Auslese, Karthäuserhofberg, 2007. Mosel, Germany.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with a slight green tinge even at 11 years (2007 vintage, examined 2018). Nose: intensely concentrated lime, apricot, dried peach, honey, and Botrytis petrol/kerosene notes developing with age. Palate: sweet but not cloying — the tension of pure Ruwer/Mosel acidity cutting through the residual sugar. Very low alcohol (9%) is the key signal: this is a genuinely sweet wine, not just an off-dry. Long, honeyed, mineral finish. The Karthäuserhofberg vineyard character is of extreme finesse and penetrating mineral acidity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Eitelsbacher Karthäuserhofberg — historic Ruwer Valley vineyard (Ruwer is a tributary of the Mosel); the estate is described as the eighth-oldest winery in the world (founded 1335 by Carthusian monks, in family ownership since 1811)
- Soil: Steep schist/slate (Ruwer terroir is lighter and more delicate than Mittelmosel)
- Vinification: Selective harvest (Auslese = individual bunch/berry selection); fermentation arrested by cold stabilisation to retain RS; unoaked; sterile filtered for stability
- Elevage: Stainless steel; no oak; designed for 30-50 year cellaring
- ABV / RS / TA: 9% / sweet (approx 80-150 g/L RS for Auslese level) / very high

## Vintage character (2007 Mosel/Ruwer)
2007 was a good to excellent vintage in the Mosel, with warm conditions producing well-ripened fruit. The Ruwer sub-region, with its delicate terroir, gave elegant, perfumed wines with the classic Auslese concentration. By 2018, a 2007 Mosel Auslese is in its first mature phase — the petrol/kerosene note from Riesling TDN is beginning to emerge alongside the honeyed primary fruit. (Source needed: 2007 Mosel vintage report)

## Producer style
Karthäuserhof claims to be the eighth-oldest winery in the world (est. 1335). The estate has been in the same family since 1811 (currently seventh generation). Located in the Ruwer Valley — a quieter, lighter-bodied tributary of the Mosel — the estate emphasises terroir wines from the Karthäuserhofberg. Their range now focuses on Schieferkristall, Alte Reben, Kabinett, and the flagship GG. The Auslese style represents the historical sweet wine tradition of the estate. (source: karthaeuserhof.com)

## Why it's in this exam
Paired with the Robert Weil Kiedrich GG (w11) to test the full range of German Riesling styles — dry vs. sweet, Rheingau vs. Ruwer (Mosel). The 9% ABV is the instant identification signal: no still dry wine with this profile sits at 9%; this must be a süss/Prädikat wine. The Karthäuserhofberg is a named GC/VDP Grosse Lage site with strong historical reputation. The 2007 vintage adds a maturity discussion element.
