# Kiedrich Gräfenberg, Riesling Trocken GG, Robert Weil, 2016. Rheingau, Germany.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium gold with a green tinge. Nose: distinctive slate-mineral purity, lime zest, white peach, apricot, and a subtle floral note. The GG designation means bone dry — this is NOT the off-dry style associated with older Riesling labelling. Palate: powerful but precise, full-bodied by German Riesling standards, crystalline acidity, very long mineral finish. At 13% ABV (high for a German Riesling) this signals exceptional ripeness from a warm year or south-facing site. Long aging potential.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Gräfenberg vineyard, Kiedrich — "the Rheingau's top wines" according to regional sources; elevated site with excellent sun exposure; thin mineral soils
- Vinification: Typical German GG protocol — whole-cluster pressing; long cool fermentation; bone dry (under 9 g/L RS for trocken designation); stainless or large neutral wood
- Elevage: Extended lees aging; designed for 10-20+ year cellaring
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% / bone dry / high

## Vintage character (2016 Rheingau)
2016 was a fine vintage in the Rheingau — good balance, healthy fruit, and typical Riesling aromatic intensity. Not extreme ripeness like 2018, but elegant and structured. GG-level wines from 2016 Kiedrich are drinking well from 2020 onwards but will improve further. (Source needed: 2016 Rheingau vintage report)

## Producer style
Robert Weil is the most celebrated estate in Kiedrich, and arguably in the Rheingau. The winery occupies the former Villa of John Sutton. They produce the full Prädikat range from Kabinett through TBA, but the dry GG range (launched as part of VDP's GG classification) has become their critical star. The Gräfenberg vineyard is their premier site. Wines are priced from $60 (GG) to several hundred dollars (TBA). Robert Weil is a VDP member. (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kiedrich)

## Why it's in this exam
A premium German dry Riesling GG is the technical antidote to off-dry/sweet German Riesling stereotypes. The Kiedrich Gräfenberg GG tests whether candidates know that German Riesling can be bone dry, full-bodied, and powerful. At 13%, it is one of the most powerful dry Rieslings produced in Germany. Placed against the Mosel Auslese (w12), this pair tests the full quality and style spectrum of German Riesling — dry Rheingau GG vs. sweet Mosel Auslese.
