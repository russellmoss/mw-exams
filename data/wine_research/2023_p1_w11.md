# Chenin Blanc Petit, Ken Forrester, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
This should present as a fresh, fruit-forward but properly dry Cape Chenin: quince, pear, apple and grapefruit, with a crunchy, tangy finish rather than oak-derived richness. The palate is likely medium-bodied and accessible, with enough acidity to keep it lively, but aimed more at immediate drinkability than layered old-vine profundity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc
- Vineyard: Western Cape fruit; the Petit technical sheet identifies south-south-west-facing sites
- Soils: duplex soils with decomposed granite and fine sandy loam over clay
- Vinification: hand harvested, cool fermented, bottled early to preserve freshness
- Elevage: no oak regime is mentioned on the producer sheet; the style suggests stainless steel handling
- ABV / RS / TA: exam corpus lists 13.5% / Source needed / Source needed

## Vintage character (2021 South Africa)
WoSA reports that 2021 in South Africa was cooler and later than usual, with slower ripening, low pH and high natural acidity in well-managed vineyards. That broad picture fits the Petit range well: even in a warmer country context, 2021 should emphasize freshness, clarity and line. The result is more likely crisp and balanced than broad or blowsy.

## Producer style
Ken Forrester's own philosophy page divides the range into Petit, Reserve and Icon tiers; Petit is explicitly the elegant, affordable, everyday-drinking line. The estate history page also underlines Forrester's long commitment to Chenin Blanc and his role in building its prestige in South Africa. This wine therefore matters commercially because it shows how a strong Chenin specialist translates house identity into a widely marketable, accessible bottling.

## Why it's in this exam
