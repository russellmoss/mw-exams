# Spätburgunder Sasbacher Limburg Erste Lage, Bercher, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Think ripe Baden Pinot rather than fragile northern Spätburgunder: red and darker cherry fruit, some plum, subtle vanilla and a warm, long palate, but still with Pinot's line and savory nuance. The volcanic site and sunny 2018 season should give more body and generosity than Niagara or cooler Sonoma examples, while remaining more structured and less overtly sweet than many New World Pinots.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Spätburgunder (Pinot Noir)
- Vineyard: Sasbacher Limburg, a single VDP Erste Lage site in the Kaiserstuhl
- Soils: volcanic ash and the distinctive rock known as Limburgite

## Vintage character (2018 Baden)
VDP describes 2018 as a warm, sunny year with an early harvest, highly concentrated fruit and striking aromatic purity. The top Erste Lage and Grosse Lage parcels were said to produce expressive wines with freshness preserved by timely picking. In blind tasting, that should translate into a riper, fuller, more generous German Pinot than in a cooler year, but still with clean acidity and site detail.

## Producer style
Weingut Bercher is a long-established VDP estate in Burkheim, Kaiserstuhl, focused heavily on Burgunder varieties. Its cellar philosophy emphasizes elegance, structure and length rather than sheer power, with wood used to support rather than dominate. The Limburg site is singled out by the estate as geologically distinct within Kaiserstuhl, producing wines with more body and earlier accessibility.
