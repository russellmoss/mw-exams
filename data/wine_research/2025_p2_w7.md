# Côtes du Rhône Réserve, Famille Perrin, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Intense, deep ruby-red with purple undertones. Powerful nose of ripe black fruit, kirsch, undergrowth, truffle, and delicate spice notes. Medium to full-bodied palate with velvety texture from fine, well-integrated tannins. Black fruit flavours balanced by good acidity bringing energy and freshness. Long, harmonious finish with subtle garrigue notes typical of Syrah. An elegant, overperforming Côtes du Rhône — the "Baby Beaucastel."

## Vintage character (2022 Rhône Valley)
The hottest and driest growing season since 1950. In the Southern Rhône, Grenache ripened slowly but vines did not suffer. Reds show concentration with powerful but sweet tannic structure and fresh but lusty fruit flavours. The brightness of aromas is this vintage's hallmark.

## Producer style
Famille Perrin is the family behind Château de Beaucastel in Châteauneuf-du-Pape — one of the most iconic estates in the Southern Rhône. Pioneers of organic farming (1950) and biodynamics (1974). The Côtes du Rhône Réserve is their entry-level négociant wine, often called "Baby Beaucastel," offering Beaucastel's quality philosophy at an accessible price. Vinification in large old foudres is the house style. The art of blending is central to their approach, just as at Beaucastel where all 13 permitted Châteauneuf grapes are used. The Perrin family has been in winemaking since acquiring Beaucastel in the early 1900s.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in Question 2 (Wines 4–7, same country and region — Rhône Valley). The Côtes du Rhône Réserve represents the broadest, most accessible appellation tier in the Rhône flight — the regional-level wine against Gigondas (cru) and Cornas (cru). At 14% ABV with a GSM blend, it's the most commercial wine in the set.
