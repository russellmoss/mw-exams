# Riesling Ried Kellerberg Smaragd, F.X. Pichler, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium gold (Smaragd's deeper concentration shows in colour); intense and complex nose of ripe yellow peach, citrus, flint, herbs, white pepper and signature reductive sulphur/matchstick on opening that dissipates quickly. Full-bodied (13.5% ABV), bone dry, with extraordinary concentration and extract; very high, ripe acidity that drives a long, multilayered finish. Kellerberg is FX Pichler's most celebrated Riesling site — wines built for 10–20+ years of aging. Markedly richer and more powerful than the Federspiel (w8) from the same producer and vintage.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Ried Kellerberg — steep gneiss and mica-schist terraced slopes above Dürnstein; one of Wachau's most prestigious Riesling Lagen
- Vinification: Temperature-controlled stainless steel fermentation; no new oak; late-harvested at Smaragd ripeness levels (minimum 12.5% ABV, typically higher)
- Elevage: Stainless steel with extended lees contact

- Smaragd = Vinea Wachau's top quality tier, named after the emerald lizard; minimum 12.5% ABV (Source: Wikipedia)

## Vintage character (2018 Wachau)
2018 was a landmark warm vintage in the Wachau — early harvest, high ripeness across all tiers. Kellerberg Smaragd in 2018 achieved exceptional concentration with naturally high sugars driving the 13.5% ABV, while the site's steep terraced positions preserved acidity. The vintage produces powerful, rich Smaragds with more immediate appeal than the austere 2017s, yet with the structural backbone for long aging.

## Producer style
F.X. Pichler is Austria's most revered white wine estate, synonymous with the Wachau's finest Riesling and Grüner Veltliner expressions. Kellerberg is the estate's prestige Riesling cuvée and among Austria's most collectable whites, achieving prices comparable to premier Burgundy.
