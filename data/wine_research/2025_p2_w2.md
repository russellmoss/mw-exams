# Cabernet Franc, El Enemigo, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Ruby red with soft violet tones. Intense, concentrated nose of cassis, blue plums, ripe currants, sweet spices (black pepper, cloves), cedar, and vanilla from integrated oak. Medium-bodied palate with fine-grained tannins, fresh acidity, and flavours of red cherries, blackberry, eucalyptus, and mulberry. A linear, juicy, expressive Cabernet Franc with excellent balance and a long, chalky finish. Still young and energetic.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Cabernet Franc (predominant, with small percentage of Malbec)
- Vineyard: Primarily from chalky vineyards in Gualtallary at 1,430m altitude, with parcels in Luján de Cuyo
- Soils: Chalky/calcareous, alluvial
- Vinification: Spontaneous fermentation with native yeasts; no cultivated yeasts used

## Vintage character (2021 Mendoza)
2021 was a cooler vintage marked by spring frost (yield losses up to 25% in eastern Mendoza) and above-average precipitation. Red varieties benefited from a drier autumn, yielding wines with better natural freshness, crunchiness, more detail, and lower alcohol than warmer years. Widely considered one of the best vintages of the past decade, with Cabernet Franc bringing added tension and energy. The cooler conditions favoured elegance over power.

## Producer style
El Enemigo was founded in 2008 by legendary winemaker Alejandro Vigil (Chief Winemaker at Catena Zapata) and Adrianna Catena, motivated by a shared love of Cabernet Franc. The project aims to produce high-altitude wines rivalling Bordeaux. They work approximately 60 hectares of Cabernet Franc across Uco Valley and Luján de Cuyo, producing five Cabernet Franc cuvées. Vigil's Gran Enemigo Gualtallary 2013 received the highest score ever given to a pure Cabernet Franc by Wine Advocate. Native yeast fermentation is a signature, allowing terroir expression.
