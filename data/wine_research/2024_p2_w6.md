# Chateau Beau-Sejour Becot, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Deep purple-ruby. Perfumed nose of cassis, mulberry, spring flowers, chalky minerality, violets, and graphite. Bright, ripe red and blue fruit with undertones of mint, dark chocolate, creme brulee, toasted cinnamon, and star anise. Medium-to-full body, nicely concentrated with terrific mid-palate depth, flawless balance, polished yet significant tannins. Linear, deep, and earthy finish. Scores 93-96+ from major critics. Needs 5-7 years of cellaring; 30+ year potential.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 80% Merlot, 15% Cabernet Franc, 5% Cabernet Sauvignon
- Vineyard: 22 ha on Saint-Emilion's limestone plateau, some clay
- Soils: Limestone plateau with clay
- Vinification: Manual harvest, stainless steel tank fermentation
- Elevage: 16 months in French oak barrels, 65% new

## Vintage character (2018 Saint-Emilion)
Record-wet start: 550-600 mm rain January-July. Heavy downy mildew and rot pressure required vigilant vineyard management. Dramatic turnaround mid-summer: late July through harvest was the hottest, driest, sunniest since 2003. Wines rich, sensuous, high in alcohol (14.5-15.5% typical), but cool nights preserved acidity and fresh fruit. Limestone plateau sites (like Beau-Sejour Becot) performed exceptionally well. One of Bordeaux's great modern vintages for the Right Bank. (source: The Drinks Business, Wine Cellar Insider, James Suckling)

## Producer style
Premier Grand Cru Classe B since the 1955 classification (temporarily declassified 1986-1996, restored). Named "beau sejour" by General Jacques de Carles in 1787. Acquired by Michel Becot in 1969. Prime limestone plateau terroir adjacent to Canon and Beauséjour-Duffau. The 2018 and recent vintages show a modern but balanced approach: significant new oak (65%) but calibrated extraction. Merlot-dominant blend typical of Right Bank Saint-Emilion.

## Why it's in this exam
Wines 4-7 are from "classic European origins." This represents Merlot-dominant Right Bank Bordeaux — the ultimate European classic alongside Barolo, Brunello, and Rioja. The question asks for origin + grape variety identification, quality in context, vintage + drinking window. At 14.5% ABV like wines 4, 5, and 7, candidates must distinguish by variety and terroir. The Merlot-dominant profile (ripe, plush, cassis) versus Nebbiolo (rose/tar, high acid), Sangiovese (cherry, earthy), and Tempranillo (tertiary, leather) is the core challenge.
