# Semillon, Tyrrell's, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon, likely youthful in colour despite bottle age. Expect a restrained but high-definition nose of citrus, white flowers and lemon pith rather than obvious oak or tropical fruit, with a light-bodied palate, very high acidity, low alcohol and a long, tightly coiled finish. This is classic Hunter Valley Semillon territory: neutral winemaking, severe youth, and strong capacity to evolve into toast and honey later (source: Wine.com winemaker notes; Tyrrell's estate materials).

## Vintage character (2019 Hunter Valley)
Tyrrell's describes 2019 in the Hunter as another high-quality year, following 2018 with continued dry conditions, slightly lower cropping levels, and fruit arriving in perfect condition with intense concentration. That profile makes sense for a Semillon with more mid-palate intensity than dilute citrus simplicity while still preserving the region's hallmark acid line (source: Tyrrell's 2019 Semillon materials).

## Producer style
Tyrrell's is one of the defining names of the Hunter Valley, with estate history back to the mid-19th century and a deep bench of old-vine vineyards. The house identity is strongly tied to Semillon and to transparent, site-led winemaking with little cellar embellishment, which is exactly why Tyrrell's is such a frequent reference point for classic Australian Semillon (source: Tyrrell's family profile).

## Why it's in this exam
This is wine 3 in 2023 Paper 1 Question 1, paired with Julio Bouchon's Semillon Granito from Chile. It gives candidates the lean, low-alcohol, stainless-steel benchmark against a richer, barrel-shaped Semillon from a different country, testing both variety recognition and analysis of major winemaking influences.
