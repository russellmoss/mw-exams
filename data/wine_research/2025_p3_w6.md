# Oloroso VORS 30 Years, Bodegas Tradicion, NV. Jerez, Spain

## Quick tasting profile
Deep mahogany-amber with burnt orange tones. The nose is complex and intense — hazelnut, old leather, roasted wood, cocoa, cedar, bacon, spices, vanilla, mocha, and truffles with a balsamic quality. The palate is rich, heavy, and substantial with pronounced caramel and raisin notes alongside walnut, orange peel, and leather. Despite the complexity and weight, it is completely dry. Pleasantly oily, smooth and round, with a strong full-bodied character. Elegant finish with medicinal notes and dark chocolate. Said to be closer to 45 years of actual average age.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Palomino Fino
- Vinification: Oxidative ageing under flor-free conditions in a solera system. VORS certification guarantees minimum 30 years average age; this bottling is reportedly closer to 45 years.
- Vessel: American oak butts (solera system)
- Fortification: Fortified to 20% ABV
- RS: Dry (Oloroso style — no sweetening)
- ABV: 20%

## Vintage character (NV — solera aged)
Non-vintage solera blend. The VORS certification requires carbon-14 dating or equivalent verification by the Consejo Regulador to confirm the minimum average age of 30 years. Everything at Bodegas Tradicion is done by hand, from refreshing the soleras to labeling and lacquering bottles.

## Producer style
Bodegas Tradicion was founded in 1998 by Joaquin Rivero, a descendant of ancient Jerez winemaking dynasties. Despite the recent founding, the bodega's roots stretch to the 1650s. Rivero's vision was to dedicate exclusively to very old sherry — only VOS (20+ years) and VORS (30+ years) categories. The bodega also houses the Joaquin Rivero Collection, one of Andalucia's largest private art collections with 300+ works by Goya, Velazquez, Murillo, Zurburan, and El Greco. The wines have appeared on the lists of El Bulli and The Fat Duck. A prestige producer at the pinnacle of the sherry hierarchy.

## Why it's in this exam
The crown jewel of the Spanish trio (Q2, wines 4-6). This is the most complex and aged wine in the flight — a VORS Oloroso representing the absolute pinnacle of oxidative winemaking. The candidate must identify it as dry Oloroso (not sweetened Oloroso Dulce), distinguish it from Amontillado or Palo Cortado, and discuss the solera system, VORS classification, and commercial position as a luxury niche product. Paired against entry-level Cava and cutting-edge Garnacha, it tests the full breadth of Spanish wine knowledge.
