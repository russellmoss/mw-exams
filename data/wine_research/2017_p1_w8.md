# Muscat Reserve, Trimbach, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Highly aromatic and unmistakably grapey, but dry. Expect fresh grape, elderflower, citrus blossom, and a crunchy, almost fennel-like herbal note; the palate is light to medium-bodied, brisk, and finishes without sweetness.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Muscat d'Alsace, typically Muscat Ottonel and Muscat d'Alexandrie
- Vineyard: Alsace

- Vinification: Trimbach style is reductive, stainless-steel-led, and focused on dryness
- Elevage: Neutral vessel; no oak signature

## Vintage character (2014 Alsace)
2014 in Alsace was cooler and more delicate than the riper surrounding vintages, preserving aromatics and acidity. In Muscat, that is an advantage: it keeps the perfume lifted and the palate taut rather than blowsy.

## Producer style
Trimbach is one of Alsace's benchmark dry white producers, strongly associated with precision, dryness, and classical restraint. Even in aromatic varieties, the house style resists sweetness and exaggeration.

## Why it's in this exam
This wine belonged in the 2017 paper 1 European mixed-variety set. It is there to test whether the taster can call Muscat correctly while also recognizing that not all intensely grapey wines are sweet.
