# Chablis Grand Cru Les Preuses, Domaine William Fèvre, 2012

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold with hints of green. Nose: mineral flint, lime zest, white peach, and subtle oyster-shell iodine character typical of Kimmeridgean clay-limestone. By 2018 (six years on), shows emerging secondary notes of beeswax, toast, and brioche. Palate: full-bodied for Chablis, crystalline acidity underpinning ripe fruit, long saline finish. 12.5% ABV — classically lean for the level.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Les Preuses Grand Cru — one of seven GC climats on the southwest-facing hill above Chablis; Kimmeridgean soil (limestone, clay, fossilised oyster shells)
- Vinification: Whole-cluster pressing; William Fèvre ferments GC wines in a mix of oak barrels (partly new) and stainless, depending on cuvée
- Elevage: 12+ months in barrel and stainless; extended lees contact for GC
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / bone dry / high

## Vintage character (2012 Chablis/Burgundy)
2012 in Burgundy was a challenging year with late frost, hail, and reduced yields, but the wines that survived showed concentrated mineral character and good acidity. Chablis GC 2012s are tight and linear when young, with excellent aging potential. The small crop from Les Preuses that year produced wines of high extract relative to volume. ## Producer style
William Fèvre is the largest Grand Cru owner in Chablis, holding parcels in all seven GC sites including Les Preuses. Since 1998 it has been owned by Domaines Barons de Rothschild (Lafite), which brought investment in winery technology while preserving the Chablis character. The estate is widely cited as a reference point producer in the appellation — precise, terroir-transparent, age-worthy. (source: lafite.com/fr/domaines/william-fevre; wikipedia.org/wiki/Chablis_Grand_Cru)

## Why it's in this exam
Used as the high-quality Old World Chardonnay benchmark, contrasting with w1 (commercial Yellow Tail) and w3 (Napa). Tests candidate ability to identify Grand Cru Chablis by its distinctive Kimmeridgean minerality, razor-sharp acidity at low ABV, and absence of new-oak sweetness. The 2012 vintage adds a maturity dimension — showing secondary complexity by exam date (2018).
