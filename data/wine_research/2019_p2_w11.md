# Douro, Quinta da Leda, 2015. Douro, Portugal

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple with garnet edge. Nose of ripe black cherry, dark plum, slate minerality, tobacco, leather and dried herbs — classic Douro indigenous variety complexity. Palate is full-bodied with robust but well-integrated tannins, firm acidity, and a concentrated, savoury-spiced finish with good ageing potential. The eastern Douro Superior site gives these wines a powerful, sun-soaked intensity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Field blend of traditional Douro indigenous varieties — Touriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz (Tempranillo), Tinta Barroca, Tinto Cão and others; exact percentages Source needed
- Vineyard: Quinta da Leda estate in the Douro Superior (upper Douro), close to the Spanish border — one of SOGRAPE's premium single-quinta properties
- Soils: Schist (xisto) — the defining terroir of the Douro Valley

## Vintage character (2015 Douro)
2015 was an excellent vintage in the Douro — a warm, dry year with well-concentrated grapes achieving full phenolic ripeness. The wines show impressive depth, rich tannins and good freshness despite the heat. 2015 Douro reds have exceptional ageing potential and are still relatively young at the time of the 2019 exam.

## Producer style
Quinta da Leda is owned by SOGRAPE Vinhos (Portugal's largest wine group, also owner of Ferreira and Sandeman). The quinta sits in the Douro Superior — historically the most challenging zone due to extreme continental climate, but capable of the most concentrated fruit when conditions align. Quinta da Leda is SOGRAPE's flagship unfortified Douro red, positioned at premium tier. Production is modern and technically precise. (Source: SOGRAPE website redirect confirmed — sogrape.com.)

## Why it's in this exam
Quinta da Leda is a premium Douro red and tests recognition of Douro indigenous varieties: the schist minerality, the dark fruit concentration, the robust tannin structure. Distinguishing Douro reds from Rhône blends or Rioja is the key skill — the combination of dark fruit, firm acidity, schist-driven minerality and complexity of the indigenous blend is distinctly Douro.
