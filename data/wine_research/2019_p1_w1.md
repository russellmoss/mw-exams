# Sancerre Les Boucauds, Claude Riffault, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with a restrained, stony nose rather than overt tropicality. Citrus peel, white peach, herbs and chalk dominate, with the palate driven by brisk acidity, a firm mineral line and a long, dry finish. This reads as serious, terroir-led Sancerre rather than a simple aromatic Sauvignon Blanc.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Sauvignon Blanc
- Vineyard: Les Boucauds, from Kimmeridgian marl parcels in Sury-en-Vaux
- Soils: Kimmeridgian marl with a high limestone component
- Vinification: Indigenous yeast fermentation; European Cellars notes low-intervention cellar work and parcel-specific élevage

## Vintage character (2017 Sancerre)
Decanter described 2017 in Sancerre as a reduced-yield year shaped by spring frost, but one that could still deliver concentrated, expressive Sauvignon Blanc in careful hands. The style profile suits a compact, mineral, classically proportioned wine rather than a broad or lush one.

## Producer style
Claude Riffault is one of Sancerre's leading small domaines, working organically and biodynamically with a clear site-by-site philosophy. The estate is known for precise, ageworthy bottlings from specific lieux-dits, aiming for texture and mineral detail over simple varietal pungency.

## Why it's in this exam
This sits in 2019 Paper 1 Question 1, where wines 1-4 all share a common grape variety. It is the Old World benchmark in the set: a restrained, terroir-led Loire Sauvignon Blanc against more overt New World expressions and a Bordeaux blend carrying Sauvignon Blanc as a key component.
