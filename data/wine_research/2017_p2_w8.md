# Etna Rosso, Archineri, Pietradolce, 2014. Sicily, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-light ruby with a translucent, garnet-pink rim — deceptively pale colour for the ABV (15%). Nose of sour red cherry, dried cranberry, pomegranate, volcanic mineral (characteristic lava-ash salinity), wild herbs, and earthy notes; high aromatic intensity and complexity for a lighter-coloured wine. Full-bodied despite appearance, firm, angular tannins, searingly high acidity, very long finish with volcanic mineral persistence. The paradox of light colour + full body + high acidity + firm tannin is a defining Nerello Mascalese/Etna Rosso fingerprint.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Nerello Mascalese (the indigenous Etna variety; Source needed for Archineri exact spec — may include a small percentage Nerello Cappuccio)
- Vineyard: Archineri — a specific contrada (named single vineyard) on the north face of Mount Etna at altitude; old-vine Nerello Mascalese on volcanic basalt soils
- Soils: Volcanic basalt and lava; highly mineral, low fertility, excellent drainage; altitude 650–700m asl (Source needed for exact altitude)
- Vinification: Traditional; gravity-fed; minimal intervention; skin contact maceration for tannin and colour (though colour remains light due to Nerello genetics)
- Elevage: Large neutral oak casks (botti grandi) — characteristic of Pietradolce and traditional Etna winemaking; no new oak (Source needed for exact protocol)
- ABV: 15.0% (very high — volcanic heat and harvest timing)

## Vintage character (2014 Etna / Sicily)
2014 was a cooler, later vintage on Etna compared to the warm 2012/2013. The north face of Etna, where Archineri is located, is already the coolest exposure; 2014's fresher conditions produced wines with higher acidity and more restrained fruit than warmer years. The 15% ABV is notably high for a cool vintage year, suggesting full physiological ripeness achieved slowly. Etna wines are known for their ability to age; 2014 Archineri would be approachable but benefiting from further bottle age.

## Producer style
Pietradolce is a small artisan producer founded by Venerando Faro on Etna's north face. The estate focuses exclusively on the Nerello Mascalese variety and the volcanic terroir of Etna. Pietradolce is part of the modern Etna wine renaissance (since early 2000s) that has brought international attention to the appellation. The Archineri is the estate's flagship single-contrada wine, representing the top of their range. Winemaking is traditional and non-interventionist. Source: pietradolce.com.
