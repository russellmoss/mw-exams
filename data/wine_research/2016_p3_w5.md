# Mâcon Blanc Villages, M. & B. Talmard, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with fresh apple, citrus, white blossom, and light cream rather than overt toast or reduction. The palate is dry, medium-bodied, and straightforward, with clean Chardonnay fruit and moderate acidity in a crisp, early-drinking frame. Blind, this looks like modestly scaled white Burgundy from the Mâconnais rather than Côte de Beaune complexity.

## Vintage character (2014 Mâconnais)
2014 was a very successful white Burgundy vintage, often combining ripe fruit with freshness and clarity. In Mâcon-Villages that typically translates into expressive, accessible Chardonnay with enough balance to overdeliver at village level, even if it lacks the mineral depth of Puligny. ## Producer style
Domaine Talmard is a reliable Mâconnais specialist better known for value and typicity than for ambitious oak work. The house style favours clean fruit, freshness, and straightforward drinkability, which makes this a useful "unoaked or lightly handled Chardonnay" reference in an exam flight. It also shows how Burgundy can mean very different price and quality positions even within one grape.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 1 with other Chardonnay-based wines and explicit reference to oak. It acts as the simpler, less-oaked Burgundian counterpoint to Puligny-Montrachet and premium New World examples.
