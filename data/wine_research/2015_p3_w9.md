# Brauneberger Juffer-Sonnenuhr Riesling Spätlese, Max Ferd. Richter, 2008

## Quick tasting profile
Pale green-gold; pristine, reductive Riesling aromatics — white peach, lime zest, green apple, delicate petrol (kerosene) development at 7 years of age. The Mosel Spätlese profile: light-bodied, 8.5% ABV, pronounced acidity, off-dry residual sugar that counterbalances the high acidity without appearing sweet. Palate: elegant, precise, slate-driven minerality, long finish with citrus and white stone fruit. Classic Mittelmosel character — more flooral and delicate than Saar/Ruwer.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Brauneberger Juffer-Sonnenuhr (Sundial) — one of Mosel's top GG-equivalent sites; south-facing, steep, blue-grey Devonian slate; significantly sun-exposed due to river bend at Brauneberg; produces richer, fuller wines than the Mosel average (confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mosel_(wine_region))
- Vinification: fermented in Fuder (old large oak casks) or stainless steel; minimal intervention; no malo (not typical in Mosel); residual sugar left by stopping fermentation
- ABV: 8.5% / RS: ~50–80 g/L typical for Spätlese (exact — Source needed)
- Classification: Spätlese = late-harvest grapes with higher must weight than Kabinett; fully natural (no chaptalisation at QmP level)

## Vintage character (2008 Mosel)
2008 was considered a very good to great vintage in the Mosel — cool growing season with late Indian summer ripening. The wines showed pronounced acidity and very good natural residual sugar balance, with more nervosity than the broader 2007 vintage. Slate minerality particularly well-expressed. The Rieslings from 2008 were built for long aging: at 7 years (2015 exam) these wines are just entering their best drinking window. (Source needed — specific 2008 Mosel vintage report)

## Producer style
Max Ferd. Richter, Mülheim an der Mosel; family estate established 1680s; currently run by Dirk Richter. Owns parcels in the top Mittelmosel sites including Brauneberger Juffer-Sonnenuhr, Wehlener Sonnenuhr, Graacher Himmelreich. Traditional Mosel estate style — elegant, long-lived Rieslings across all quality levels. Medium size; exports globally; premium to luxury tier.
