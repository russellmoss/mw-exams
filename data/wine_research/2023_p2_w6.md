# Imperial Gran Reserva, CVNE, 2016

## Quick tasting profile
Deep cherry red. Complex mature-classic Rioja nose with liquorice, black forest fruit, clove, roasted coffee, cigar leaf, chocolate and balsamic lift. The palate is velvety yet structured, with polished tannin, savoury oak complexity and long, composed finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 85% Tempranillo, 10% Graciano, 5% Mazuelo
- Vineyard: Remedio and Encinas in Villalba, plus Barbarroja and Antonana in Torremontalbo, Rioja Alta
- Soils: Clay with areas of river pebbles; poor in organic matter
- Vinification: Hand harvest in 20kg boxes; double selection; whole bunches cooled; gravity-fed to small oak vats; indigenous yeast fermentation at 28-29C with punch-downs and delestage
- Elevage: Over 24 months in French and American oak, new and one-year-old barrels; minimum 36 months bottle ageing before release
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.5% / Source needed / 5.31 g/L

## Vintage character (2016 Rioja)
CVNE describes 2016 as excellent, with a dry mild winter, spring rain, hot dry August and welcome September rain that completed ripening. The result was a later-than-usual but very healthy harvest, which fits the wine's combination of ripe fruit, freshness and classical structural balance.

## Producer style
CVNE is one of Rioja's historic houses, founded in 1879 in Haro. Imperial is only made in vintages the house classifies as excellent and is positioned as a classical long-ageing Rioja, built from top Rioja Alta parcels and extended cask plus bottle maturation.

## Why it's in this exam
In 2023 Paper 2 Question 2, this gave candidates a textbook example of traditional fine-wine oak handling: overt but integrated maturation, a prestigious classification, and clear premium commercial position compared with more immediate styles in the same flight.
