# Châteauneuf-du-Pape, Domaine du Vieux Lazaret, 2013. Rhône Valley, France

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-garnet with some garnet-orange evolution. Nose of ripe dark plum, garrigue (thyme, rosemary, lavender — quintessential Southern Rhône signature), leather, iron, dried fruit, and a hint of black olive. Full-bodied, substantial but rounded tannins (Grenache backbone softened by fruit), moderate-to-high acidity, long finish with herbal/savoury spice persistence. Classic Châteauneuf-du-Pape profile with the garrigue freshness distinguishing it from warmer, jammier New World counterparts.

## Vintage character (2013 Châteauneuf-du-Pape / Southern Rhône)
2013 was a challenging, cooler vintage in the Southern Rhône — late flowering, some hail events, and a later harvest than typical. Wines from 2013 CdP are generally lighter and more aromatic than the hedonistic 2012 or 2010, with better freshness and more defined garrigue character. For a traditional CdP producer, 2013 would produce elegant rather than powerful wines, at 14% ABV (relatively moderate for CdP).

## Producer style
Domaine du Vieux Lazaret is a traditional Châteauneuf-du-Pape producer; it is one of the largest estates in the appellation. The Quiot family (who also own Domaine Saint-Prefert and other estates) are behind the brand. Source needed for confirmed current ownership and detailed producer background. Vieux Lazaret is known as a reliable, traditional, commercially distributed CdP at the quality tier below the prestige estates (Rayas, Château Rayas, Beaucastel) — widely available in French restaurants and export markets.

## Why it's in this exam
Domaine du Vieux Lazaret CdP represents the classic Southern Rhône Grenache blend benchmark in P3 — paired against the Torbreck Barossa GSM (w7) to test identification of Old World vs. New World Grenache-based blends. Key discriminators: garrigue herbal character (Old World), moderate tannin, the CdP galets-roulés mineral signature, and the more structured, savoury finish of AOC CdP vs. riper, jammier Barossa GSM.
