# Inocente Fino, Valdespino, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold, green-tinged; characteristically oxidative-reductive tension with pungent flor notes of fresh dough, almonds, camomile, and saline brine. Palate: bone-dry, razor-sharp acidity, low glycerol, bitter almond finish. The defining fino markers are unmistakable. Inocente is single-vineyard (Macharnudo Alto) and typically aged longer in solera than most finos — greater complexity and slightly deeper colour than house finos like Tío Pepe.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Palomino Fino from the Macharnudo Alto vineyard, Jerez Superior
- Vinification: free-run juice only (as typical for fino quality tier); fortified to 15% before solera entry
- Aging: criadera y solera system under active flor yeast; single-vineyard solera aged longer than average fino
- Solera age: believed to average 7–12 years (Source needed — exact solera entry age not publicly confirmed)
- ABV: 15% / RS: <1 g/L (bone dry) / TA: elevated apparent acidity from flor activity
- Production: Valdespino is part of Grupo Estévez

## Vintage character (NV)
As an NV solera wine there is no single vintage. The flor yeast culture in the criadera system maintains consistency across harvests. The Macharnudo Alto vineyard (albariza soil — high chalk content near Jerez de la Frontera) contributes the limestone minerality and terroir finesse that distinguish Inocente from generic fino. The longer average solera age delivers more nutty, developed character than younger finos.

## Producer style
Valdespino, established 1430, is one of Spain's oldest sherry producers and held royal warrant from 1883. Now part of Grupo Estévez. The house is positioned at the premium end of Sherry production with a focus on single-vineyard expressions (Inocente) and aged Palo Cortado (Cardenal). Inocente is the benchmark single-vineyard fino — widely cited as one of the finest expressions of the style. Competitors at a similar quality/price tier: Hidalgo La Gitana, El Maestro Sierra. (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valdespino; en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fino)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 3 typically includes Sherry to test fortified wine identification. Inocente Fino sits at the benchmark quality tier — not the entry-level (Tío Pepe equivalent) nor the obscure premium. Candidates need to identify: fortified (15% ABV), biologically aged (flor), bone-dry, Jerez, Palomino. The single-vineyard Macharnudo provenance is a bonus identification point.
