# Jurançon Sec Tauzy, Domaine Castera, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Dry but not austere: this should show a Pyrenean combination of ripe citrus, white flowers, orchard fruit and a slightly waxy, saline, lightly smoky edge rather than overt tropicality. In the mouth expect medium body, brisk acidity and a textural, lees-shaped palate with discreet wood influence, reading as serious southwest French white rather than simple fruity aromatics.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 50% Petit Manseng, 25% Petit Courbu, 25% Gros Manseng
- Vineyard: east-facing 2ha lieu-dit Tauzy, around 300-350m altitude
- Soils: silty-clay and red clay with iron-rich gravel over poudingue bedrock
- Vinification: manual harvest, long slow whole-bunch pressing, indigenous yeast
- Elevage: 10 months on full lees in used demi-muids, barrels and sandstone eggs, then 2 months in stainless steel

## Vintage character (2021 Jurançon)
2021 in Jurançon was shaped by the region's warm, humid oceanic climate and persistent disease pressure, which makes organic farming demanding in wetter years. Tauzy's slower-ripening east-facing site is presented by the estate as one that preserves freshness and digestibility, so in blind tasting this vintage should read more tension-driven and lifted than a riper, broader southwest white. Dry Jurançon is still a relatively recent qualitative success story, built on lower yields, denser planting and more exact parcel work rather than volume production.

## Producer style
Domaine Castera is an 11.5-hectare organic estate in Monein led by Franck Lihour. The domaine works parcel by parcel, farms organically with some biodynamic practices, harvests by hand, presses whole bunches slowly by gravity, ferments with indigenous yeasts and uses discreet maturation in old wood, eggs and steel to preserve freshness and site expression. Tauzy is one of the estate's more textural dry cuvées, built from Petit Manseng, Gros Manseng and Petit Courbu with full-lees ageing.
