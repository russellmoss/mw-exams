# Soave Classico, Inama, 2016

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon, subtly aromatic, and more about texture than overt fruit. Think pear skin, white flowers, almond, and a saline volcanic edge; the palate is dry, medium-bodied, and gently phenolic, finishing with a bitter-almond note typical of Garganega.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Predominantly Garganega
- Vineyard: Soave Classico hills
- Soils: Volcanic basalt is a key Inama and Soave Classico marker
- Vinification: Producer materials indicate temperature-controlled fermentation in stainless steel
- Elevage: Neutral vessel, preserving varietal purity
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.0% / Source needed / Source needed

## Vintage character (2016 Veneto)
2016 was broadly balanced in much of northern Italy, combining ripe fruit with fresh acids. For Soave Classico, that tends to support purity, definition, and moderate concentration rather than heat or excess phenolic weight.

## Producer style
Inama is one of Soave's strongest quality references, pushing the region's volcanic identity and serious Garganega credentials. Even the entry-level Soave Classico is usually precise, mineral, and more terroir-conscious than commodity Soave.

## Why it's in this exam
This sat in the 2017 paper 1 set of different European varieties. It is a useful Garganega benchmark, especially because the almond, volcanic, dry profile can be mistaken for other restrained Italian whites if the taster misses Soave's signature.
