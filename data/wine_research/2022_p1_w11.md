# Gruner Veltliner, Loimer, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
This should be dry, peppery and linear rather than aromatic in a floral sense: yellow apple, citrus, herbs, white pepper and a salty finish. The palate should feel taut and fresh, with modest alcohol but strong drive, making it a classic modern Kamptal DAC reference.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Gruner Veltliner
- Vineyard: Kamptal estate parcels around Langenlois
- Soils: mainly loess
- Vinification: selective hand harvest, short skin contact on part of the fruit, mainly whole-cluster pressing, spontaneous fermentation with 95% in stainless steel and 5% in used oak
- Elevage: 4 months on fine lees in stainless steel
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.0% / 1.5 g/L / 5.5 g/L

## Vintage character (2020 Kamptal)
Loimer's own 2020 note describes a pleasant summer without extreme heat after a very dry start, then regular rain that kept vines healthy into harvest. The resulting style is fresh, medium-bodied and fruit-forward but still with ripe, present acidity and strong overall balance.

## Producer style
Fred Loimer is one of Austria's benchmark organic and biodynamic producers, known for precision, spontaneous fermentations and transparent Kamptal expression. Even the regional Gruner Veltliner is made seriously, with hand harvesting and lees work that lift it above simple category bottlings.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 11 is the Austrian single-variety white in 2022 Paper 1 Question 4. It gives the set a textbook Central European dry white whose signature lies in pepper, acidity and soil expression rather than overt aromatic intensity or oak.
