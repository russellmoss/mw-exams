# Chablis 1er Cru Les Vaillons, Billaud-Simon, 2007

## Quick tasting profile
Classic cool-climate Chardonnay profile: pale color, citrus, green apple, oyster-shell and chalk notes, with restrained fruit and a taut mineral finish. At this age it should begin to show a little honeyed development while remaining clearly Chablisien rather than overtly oaky.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Premier Cru Les Vaillons, Chablis

## Vintage character (2007 Chablis)
2007 was widely regarded as a strong white-wine vintage in Burgundy, favoring freshness, definition and classicism. In Chablis that usually means bright acidity, citrus-led fruit and transparent site expression rather than heavyweight ripeness.

## Producer style
Billaud-Simon is a long-established Chablis house associated with classical, precise expressions of the appellation. Les Vaillons sits in the generous but still mineral Premier Cru band and is a useful exam benchmark for high-quality unoaked-or-subtly-oaked Chardonnay from cool northern Burgundy.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 4 opens the Chardonnay trio in Question 2. It is the Old World reference point against which the more generous South Australian and California examples can be calibrated, especially on acid line, oak impact and malolactic handling.
