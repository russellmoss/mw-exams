# Riesling Spätlese, Erbacher Hohenrain, Schloss Reinhartshausen, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw to pale gold with green tints; crystalline and pure on the nose — ripe peach, apricot, lime zest, slate minerality and the characteristic petrol/kerosene note beginning to develop at 7 years age (Riesling TDN maturation marker); medium-light body (7% ABV — the low alcohol is diagnostic of German Spätlese), off-dry with noticeable but balanced residual sugar; racy, precise acidity that structures the wine beautifully; medium-long finish. The low alcohol and sweet-acid tension are unmistakably German Riesling Spätlese.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Erbacher Hohenrain — an Einzellage (individual vineyard) in Erbach, Rheingau; clay-marl and phyllite soils on lower slopes of the south-facing Rheingau ridge
- Vinification: Fermented in stainless steel (common for Rheingau); arrested fermentation preserving RS at Spätlese level; minimal SO2
- Elevage: Tank; bottled with some protective SO2 to preserve freshness and enable aging
- ABV / RS / TA: 7% / medium (approx 50-80 g/L RS for Spätlese) / very high acidity
- Spätlese = "late harvest" — a QmP (Prädikat) level between Kabinett and Auslese

## Vintage character (2015 Rheingau)
2015 was a warm, excellent vintage in the Rheingau — abundant sunshine and low disease pressure produced very ripe, healthy Riesling. The Spätlese-level wines of 2015 show generous fruit (peach, apricot, melon) with good natural acidity; a forward vintage that's drinking well already at 7 years but with excellent aging potential. The extreme contrast (7% ABV, obvious off-dry sweetness, pristine acidity) makes it immediately diagnostic for German Riesling QmP identification.
