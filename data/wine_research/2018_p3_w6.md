# Taylor's Vintage Port, 2009. Douro, Portugal.

## Quick tasting profile
By 2018 (9 years old), this is deep purple-ruby with only the faintest rim variation. Nose: concentrated fresh blackberry, blackcurrant, dark plum, violets, dark chocolate, and grippy tannin character. Fortified spirit still somewhat present. Palate: very sweet, full body, high tannin — still quite closed and primary. At 20.5% ABV the spirit is integrated but firm. This needs 10-20 more years. Profound raw material but requires patience.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Traditional Douro field blend (Touriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinta Barroca, Tinta Cão)
- Vineyard: Taylor Fladgate estate vineyards, multi-quinta blend, Douro Valley
- Vinification: Grape spirit addition arresting fermentation; extraction in lagares (traditional foot treading) or modern pneumatic methods
- Elevage: 2 years maximum in large oak pipes (Vila Nova de Gaia) before bottling; designed for 30-50 year cellaring
- ABV / RS / TA: 20.5% / very sweet / medium

## Vintage character (2009 Douro)
2009 was a warm, concentrated vintage across Douro — hot dry conditions yielded intensely ripe, structured fruit. Taylor's declared 2009 as a full Vintage Port. At 9 years old in 2018, the wine is at the very beginning of its development arc — typical for Vintage Port, which can take 15-30 years to open. By all accounts 2009 is a well-regarded declaration year. (Source needed: 2009 Douro Vintage Port declaration report)
