# Botanica Chenin Blanc, Mary Delany Collection, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium gold; precise and expressive nose with layers of pure pear, lemon curd, citrus, white stone fruit and a tangy mineral/saline quality; some brioche and subtle oak from partial barrel fermentation. Medium-full body (13% ABV), mouth-watering acidity, and a concentrated, mineral core with a long, focused finish. South African old-vine Chenin at altitude — closer in style to Savennières than to Anjou AOC, and a potential curveball against Loire Chenin in a blind tasting.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc (old vines planted 1957)
- Vineyard: Skurfberg farm, Citrusdal Mountains; 500 metres above sea level, ~30 km from the coast
- Vinification: Hand-harvested and sorted; half spontaneous fermentation in stainless steel tanks, half in 400-litre French oak barrels
- Elevage: Fine lees aging 6–9 months depending on vintage; no malolactic fermentation
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% / dry / pH 3.22–3.44 (Source: botanicawines.com)

## Vintage character (2018 Citrusdal Mountain)
2018 in the Western Cape was warm with early harvest conditions, producing concentrated, full-flavoured fruit. Elevated Citrusdal Mountain sites are cooler and better-retain acidity. For old-vine Chenin at 500m, 2018 produced richly fruited wines with the characteristic pear-and-citrus precision that the altitude and coastal influence preserve. ## Producer style
Botanica Wines, based in Stellenbosch, is a boutique producer known for artisan winemaking from premium South African sites. The Mary Delany Collection takes its name from the 18th-century British botanical artist, with each label featuring a botanical paper collage. The Chenin Blanc has received up to 5-star ratings in Platter's guide and 92-97 points from Tim Atkin MW and Jancis Robinson. The wine is a showcase of old-vine Chenin from high-altitude terroir. (Source: botanicawines.com)
