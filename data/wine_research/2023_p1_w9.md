# Chardonnay, Hamilton Russell, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with a restrained but intense cool-climate profile: pear, lime, citrus peel, struck flint and a saline, mineral edge rather than overt tropical fruit. The palate should be dry, tight and textural, with bright natural acidity, discreet creamy breadth from barrel work, and a long chalky finish shaped more by site than by obvious oak.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: single-estate fruit from a 52ha monopole in Hemel-en-Aarde Valley
- Soils: low-vigour, stony, clay-rich, shale-derived soils
- Vinification: 100% barrel fermented
- Elevage: 9 months; 94% in French oak barrels and 6% in foudre; 26% first-fill oak
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.4% on the estate sheet; exam corpus lists 13.5% / 1.9 g/L / 6.8 g/L

## Vintage character (2021 Hemel-en-Aarde / South Africa)
Hamilton Russell describes 2021 as later and cooler than usual, with wetter winter conditions, delayed budbreak and harvest, and exceptional fruit quality with high acidity. WoSA's 2021 national harvest report also stresses a cool, slow season with low pH and strong natural acidity. In blind terms, that points to a poised rather than broad vintage, with mineral liveliness and precision more than sheer richness.

## Producer style
Hamilton Russell was the pioneering estate in Hemel-en-Aarde and has long specialized only in Pinot Noir and Chardonnay. The estate background note emphasizes tiny yields, estate-only production, and a classic, origin-expressive style from iron- and clay-rich shale soils close to the sea. This Chardonnay sits in the premium New World but with clear Old World aspirations: tension, salinity and measured oak rather than opulence.
