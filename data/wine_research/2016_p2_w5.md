# Old Winery Pinot Noir, Tyrrell's Wines, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Light to medium ruby, with straightforward red cherry, strawberry, and soft spice rather than deep savoury complexity. The palate is dry, moderate in body, and relatively easy-going, with light tannin and a simple, fruit-led finish. In blind tasting this reads as commercial Pinot Noir from a broad warm-to-moderate Australian GI rather than a site-specific premium bottling.

## Producer style
Tyrrell's is one of Australia's historic family wineries and is best known for classic Hunter Valley Semillon and Shiraz rather than Pinot Noir. The Old Winery range is positioned more accessibly than the estate's flagship bottlings, making commercial drinkability and broad appeal more important than terroir precision. That matters in an MW context because it frames the wine as a value/commercial benchmark rather than a prestige example.

## Why it's in this exam
Included in 2016 Paper 2 Question 2 with Dog Point and Meyer-Näkel as a three-country Pinot Noir set. It functions as the broader, more commercial Australian foil to the more site-expressive Marlborough and Ahr wines.
