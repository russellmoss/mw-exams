# Chateau Ducru-Beaucaillou, 2005

## Quick tasting profile
Deep garnet-ruby with evolved cedar, cigar box, cassis, graphite and truffle complexity. The palate is dry, structured and still firm, but the fruit has moved from primary to savoury secondary notes. This should feel complete, classed-growth and long rather than overtly flashy.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Cabernet Sauvignon-led Bordeaux blend

## Vintage character (2005 Saint-Julien)
Decanter's Bordeaux 2005 guide describes one of the modern benchmark vintages, with outstanding structure and longevity across the Médoc. In Saint-Julien that generally means ripe tannins, strong cassis fruit and the capacity to age for decades, all of which fit Ducru-Beaucaillou well.

## Producer style
At Ducru-Beaucaillou, even mature vintages tend to retain perfume and polish rather than collapsing into rusticity. The estate's Cabernet-heavy style and serious barrel regime support long bottle development and make older bottles especially useful in maturity comparisons.
