# La Rocca, Soave Classico, Pieropan, 2015. Veneto, Italy.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw-gold. Nose: white blossom, almond, citrus, green apple, and a distinctive volcanic mineral edge — basaltic tufa soils of the Classico hillsides. Palate: medium-bodied, generous texture for Soave (reflecting the 2015 warm vintage and late-harvest Garganega), fresh acidity, with a characteristic bitter almond finish. La Rocca's weight sets it above basic Soave; some oxidative nutty development possible after 3 years.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Garganega (Pieropan avoids Trebbiano additions for their top wines)
- Vineyard: La Rocca — single-vineyard site on volcanic/basaltic hillside soils in the Classico zone
- Vinification: Harvest of fully ripe Garganega; fermentation in large Slavonian oak casks or stainless; low intervention
- Elevage: Aged in large old Slavonian oak or stainless; no new oak
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% / dry / medium
*(Pieropan deliberately uses Soave Classico DOC rather than Superiore DOCG because their wines are "more refined and long-lived" — source: wikipedia.org/wiki/Soave_wine)*

## Vintage character (2015 Veneto/Soave)
2015 was a warm, dry summer in the Veneto. For Soave Classico, the heat produced ripe, generous fruit with higher-than-usual alcohol. La Rocca's Garganega from the hillside sites retained more freshness than valley-floor wines. 2015 is considered a good to excellent vintage for Soave's premium single-vineyard expressions. (Source needed: 2015 Veneto/Soave vintage report)

## Producer style
Pieropan is the defining reference producer of Soave Classico — the estate that almost single-handedly kept the appellation's premium reputation alive during the 1970s-1990s Soave commodity era. Based in the Classico zone, they produce Soave di Garganega (their basic wine) and two single-vineyard GCs: La Rocca and Calvarino. They deliberately avoid the Superiore DOCG in favour of Classico DOC to signal refinement over power. Their market position is as premium Italian white at mid-tier pricing ($25-45), widely stocked by top restaurants. (source: pieropan.it; wikipedia.org/wiki/Soave_wine)

## Why it's in this exam
La Rocca is a classic MW examiner choice for Paper 1 when the question group covers lesser-known Italian whites. Tests recognition of Soave Classico's hillside Garganega character — bitter almond finish, volcanic mineral note, Slavonian oak texture. Not an easy identification without prior knowledge of the style; distinguishes itself from other Italian whites (Greco, Verdicchio, Gavi) by its specific almond-and-stone-mineral profile.
