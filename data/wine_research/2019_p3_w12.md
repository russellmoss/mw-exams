# Muscat, Campbells, NV. Rutherglen, Australia

## Quick tasting profile
Deep tawny amber-brown with olive-green rim (rancio character in older examples). Nose is extraordinarily rich and complex: Christmas cake, raisins, dried figs, orange peel, cold tea, burnt toffee, roasted coffee, butterscotch, rancio and molasses. Palate is very sweet, viscous and luscious, with exceptional concentration, but preserved by high natural acidity and spirit; the finish is endless, complex and warming. Unlike any other sweet wine — a uniquely Australian fortified style.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Muscat Blanc à Petits Grains (Brown Muscat / Muscat à Petits Grains Rouges) — the grape responsible for Rutherglen Muscat; may also include Muscadelle component (Source needed for Campbells specific blend proportions)
- Vineyard: Campbells estate vineyards in Rutherglen, Victoria; Brown Muscat grown in heavy clay soils typical of Rutherglen
- Soils: Red clay loam over ironstone — classic Rutherglen terroir
- Vinification: Grapes are late-harvested or semi-raisined; fermentation stopped by addition of grape spirit (fortification) at approximately 17% ABV, leaving very high residual sugar
- Elevage: Solera-blended fractional blending system in old American and/or French oak barrels; average age of blend varies by tier (Rutherglen < Classic < Grand < Rare — classification system introduced 1998). "Classic Rutherglen Muscat" listed at $40/500ml (source: Campbells website)
- ABV / RS / TA: 17% / very high RS (likely 200–350 g/L depending on tier) / high natural acidity preserving balance

## Vintage character (NV — solera blend)
Non-vintage; no single vintage character applicable. Style reflects the accumulated complexity of solera blending. At the "Rutherglen" (base) or "Classic" tier, average age is younger and wines are more vibrant; Grand and Rare tiers offer profound aged complexity.

## Producer style
Campbells is a fifth-generation family winery established in 1870 in Rutherglen, northeastern Victoria (source: Campbells website). One of the region's benchmark Muscat producers alongside Morris, Chambers Rosewood, and Stanton & Killeen. Campbells occupies the quality-value mid-tier with their Rutherglen and Classic Muscat; their Merchant Prince Rare and Bobbie Burns are apex products. The family is committed to the Rutherglen appellation's unique heritage fortified wine tradition.
