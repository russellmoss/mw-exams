# Palo Cortado, Viejo CP, Valdespino, NV. Jerez, Spain

## Quick tasting profile
Amber-mahogany colour, clear with good depth. Nose of walnut, hazelnut, dried orange peel, toffee, leather, dried figs, and the distinctive rancio character of extended oxidative ageing under flor and then as an oloroso — uniquely complex, combining Amontillado's roasted-nuts with Oloroso's richness. Dry on the palate (Palo Cortado is always dry), medium-full body, high alcohol warmth (20%), long persistent finish. The rarity and complexity of Palo Cortado makes it one of the most elegant and intellectually fascinating of all Sherry styles.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Palomino Fino (the dominant Jerez variety; Palo Cortado can also include Pedro Ximénez but is typically Palomino; Source needed for Valdespino CP specific blend)
- Vineyard: Macharnudo Alto pago — Valdespino's exclusive access to this highly regarded Grand Cru equivalent site (source: bodegavaldespino.com)
- Soils: Albariza (white chalk-limestone soils — classic Jerez, highly moisture-retentive, essential for Palomino quality)
- Vinification: Base wine fermented dry; fortified to ~18% to kill flor; then aged as Palo Cortado. Note: True Palo Cortado arises when a wine that started ageing biologically (under flor, like Fino/Amontillado) spontaneously loses its flor veil and develops oxidatively — a rare and unpredictable event (this is the classic definition; some producers create PC deliberately by fortifying higher and then adding older wines)
- Elevage: Criadera y solera system (fractional blending between multiple barrels over multiple years); the "Viejo" (old) and "CP" designation signals an aged, complex Sherry from an established solera; likely average age >30 years (Source needed for exact average solera age)
- ABV: 20.0%

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage; solera blending makes vintage character inapplicable. The character reflects the age and complexity of the specific solera rather than a harvest year.

## Producer style
Valdespino is one of Jerez's most historically significant bodegas, with origins tracing back to the 13th century. The bodega holds unique access to Pago de Macharnudo Alto, considered Jerez's greatest single vineyard site. Valdespino is now owned by Grupo Estevez but retains its artisan, quality-focused identity. They are ranked among the world's top 100 wineries by Wine and Spirits Magazine (source: bodegavaldespino.com). The "CP" designation in Viejo CP likely refers to a specific category or solera designation within the Palo Cortado range; Source needed for definitive interpretation.

## Why it's in this exam
Valdespino Palo Cortado Viejo CP represents the benchmark dry fortified oxidative Sherry in P3. Palo Cortado is the rarest Sherry style and the most intellectually complex — its profile straddles Amontillado (lighter, almond/hazelnut) and Oloroso (full, dark fruit/walnut/rancio).
