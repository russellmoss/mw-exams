# Château de Fieuzal Blanc, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
This should read as serious barrel-worked Bordeaux Blanc rather than simple Sauvignon: pale gold, pure citrus and white flower notes, cut herbs, smoke and a dense flinty core. The palate should be tensile and layered, with Sauvignon-led drive, Semillon breadth, no malo softness, and a long, cool, mineral finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 65% Sauvignon Blanc, 35% Semillon
- Vineyard: 10ha of white vines within the Fieuzal estate in Leognan
- Soils: clay-limestone for the white plots; the wider estate is defined by Graves sand and gravel
- Vinification: white wine vinified 50% in stainless steel and 50% in new French oak barrels; no malolactic fermentation
- Elevage: 12 months in French oak, with total ageing stated as 16-18 months depending on the vintage style

## Vintage character (2021 Pessac-Leognan)
2021 was a late, stressful Bordeaux vintage. Fieuzal reports nine frost nights between late March and mid-April, a very wet spring, a long flowering, strong vegetative pressure and a late harvest, with the whites picked from 1-13 September. In blind tasting, that usually points toward a fresher, more linear and more Sauvignon-defined expression than in a solar year, with tension and precision more important than sheer volume.

## Producer style
Château de Fieuzal is a historic Leognan estate owned by Brenda and Lochlann Quinn since 2001. The property's stated vision places freshness at the center of every blend, and the white wine shows that clearly: hand-harvested fruit, plot-by-plot vinification, precise pressing, no malo, and a strong but controlled oak program designed to support ageability rather than obscure terroir. It is one of the reference dry whites of Pessac-Leognan.
