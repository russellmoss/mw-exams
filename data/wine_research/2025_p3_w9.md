# Tokaji Edes Szamorodni, Istvan Szepsy, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold. In blind tasting this should announce botrytis and oxidative-sweet Tokaj territory quickly: dried yellow fruit, apricot, marmalade, honey, spice and mineral lift, but with enough acidity to prevent heaviness (source: https://topselection.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Szepsy-Tokaji-Szamorodni-2017.pdf). The palate should feel dense, sweet and textural rather than airy, with botrytised richness but fresher lines than many cheaper sweet wines.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 60% botrytised Furmint, 20% overripened Harslevelu, 20% Yellow Muscat (source: https://topselection.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Szepsy-Tokaji-Szamorodni-2017.pdf; https://www.decanter.com/wine-reviews/hungary/tokaj/szepsy-edes-szamorodni-tokaj-hungary-2017-90312/)
- Vineyard: Estate vineyards in Tokaj on steep high-altitude slopes (source: https://topselection.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Szepsy-Tokaji-Szamorodni-2017.pdf)
- Soils: Zeolite, quartz and clay-rich weathered volcanic rhyolite (source: https://topselection.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Szepsy-Tokaji-Szamorodni-2017.pdf)
- Vinification: Hand selection of bunches with both healthy and botrytised berries; 24-36 hours maceration before pressing (source: https://topselection.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Szepsy-Tokaji-Szamorodni-2017.pdf)
- Elevage: Two and a half years in 300L Hungarian oak, bottled April 2020 (source: https://topselection.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Szepsy-Tokaji-Szamorodni-2017.pdf)
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.0% / 190 g/L / Source needed

## Vintage character (2017 Tokaj)
Tokaj-Hetszolo described 2017 as one of the best recent Tokaj vintages for both dry and sweet wines, with sunshine and rain arriving in good proportions and a beautiful aszu berry harvest in September and October (source: https://tokajhetszolo.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/SWEET-SZAMORODNI_2017-bio-Tokaj-Hetszolo-Tasting-Sheet.pdf). That fits the profile here: very high sweetness, full botrytis character and enough acidity to keep the wine energetic.

## Producer style
Szepsy is one of Tokaj's benchmark names, with a family history in the region dating back to the 1500s and a direct historical link to Tokaj Aszu production (source: https://topselection.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Szepsy-Tokaji-Szamorodni-2017.pdf). The estate style is uncompromising and site-driven, and even in Szamorodni the wine is handled as a serious fine-wine bottling rather than an inexpensive sweet category.

## Why it's in this exam
This sits in 2025 Paper 3, Question 3, the "mixed bag" set focused on quality, commercial position, classification and winemaking. It is ideal for testing whether a candidate can distinguish historic Tokaji sweet categories beyond Aszu and explain how Szamorodni is made from mixed healthy and botrytised bunches rather than separately selected berries.
