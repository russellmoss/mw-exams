# Chablis Vieilles Vignes de Sainte Claire, Jean-Marc Brocard, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon, taut, and distinctly cool-climate in feel. Citrus, green apple, oyster shell, and chalk dominate the nose; the palate is dry, linear, and saline, with little obvious oak and a focused, high-acid finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Old-vine parcels in Chablis
- Soils: Kimmeridgian limestone and clay
- Vinification: Producer materials indicate stainless steel or neutral handling to preserve purity
- Elevage: Limited or no obvious oak influence

## Vintage character (2014 Chablis)
2014 was a strong white Burgundy vintage and particularly successful in Chablis, where the wines often combined ripe citrus fruit with excellent acid definition. The style is classic rather than broad, with clarity and tension as the main cues.

## Producer style
Jean-Marc Brocard is a major Chablis reference for clean, terroir-led Chardonnay, often emphasizing organic and biodynamic farming. Vieilles Vignes de Sainte Claire is positioned as a more concentrated, old-vine Chablis while staying within the appellation's non-oaky idiom.

## Why it's in this exam
This was the foil to Meursault in the 2017 paper 1 Chardonnay pair. It helps the examiner test origin recognition inside Burgundy: cooler, chalkier, and more linear Chablis against broader, oak-shaped Cote de Beaune Chardonnay.
