# Rosé of Pinot Noir, Rodney Strong, 2018. Russian River Valley, Sonoma County, USA

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium salmon-pink with good brilliance. Nose of fresh strawberry, watermelon, raspberry, subtle floral notes and clean fruity freshness — Pinot Noir shows its delicate aromatic character even as rosé. Palate is light-bodied, dry-to-barely off-dry, with crisp acidity (characteristic of cool-climate RRV), clean texture and a refreshing, medium-length finish. More delicate than a Côtes de Provence rosé in body; American in its clean, polished fruit-forward style.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: Russian River Valley, Sonoma County — Rodney Strong was "the first to plant Pinot Noir in Russian River Valley" in 1968 (source: Wikipedia Rodney Strong). RRV is the definitive cool-climate California Pinot zone.
- Soils: Goldridge sandy loam over clay (RRV benchmark soil); cool, fog-influenced maritime climate
- Vinification: Short skin contact or direct press method typical for rosé; cold fermentation in stainless steel; no oak
- Elevage: Stainless steel only; early bottling for freshness

## Vintage character (2018 Russian River Valley)
2018 was generally a warm year across California, but Russian River Valley's marine influence provided significant cooling through fog and wind. Pinot Noir rosé from 2018 shows good fruit ripeness balanced by RRV's characteristic high natural acidity. An accessible, well-balanced vintage for RRV rosé.

## Producer style
Rodney Strong Vineyards was founded by Rod Strong in 1959 (one of Sonoma's early fine wine pioneers). Based in Healdsburg, Sonoma County. They pioneered Pinot Noir planting in the Russian River Valley (1968 — source: Wikipedia), establishing RRV as a benchmark Pinot Noir zone. Today a well-regarded mid-to-premium Sonoma brand ($15–35 range), making a broad range of varietals. The Rosé of Pinot Noir is positioned at the accessible quality tier.

## Why it's in this exam
This wine tests whether a candidate can identify American-style Pinot Noir rosé: cool-climate (Russian River Valley), single variety (Pinot Noir), clean and precise versus rustic. The 12.5% ABV is notably lower than the Spanish Rosado (13.5%), pointing to cooler climate production. Distinguishing from Provence (similar style but different variety blend) is the precise identification challenge.
