# Sercial 10 Years Old, Henriques & Henriques, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Medium amber, oxidative and dry, with nuts, dried apricot, vanilla and smoky old-wood notes. On the palate it should be firm, lifted and unmistakably Madeira, with high acidity and a long finish making the wine feel dry and incisive despite the oxidative richness (source: https://henriquesehenriques.pt/en/wines/sercial-10-years-old/).

## Vintage character (NV / category character)
This is non-vintage age-indicated Madeira, so category and élevage dominate over a single harvest narrative. The essential exam point is that Sercial is the dry end of the traditional noble-grape Madeira spectrum, with oxidative development and piercing acidity rather than youthful fruit.

## Producer style
Henriques & Henriques is one of Madeira’s benchmark houses and works across age-indicated and vintage bottlings. The producer’s own educational material emphasizes the classic noble-grape ladder and makes clear that the 10-year bottlings are blends defined by age and grape identity, which is exactly what candidates need to explain in an exam setting.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 10 sits in the same sweet/RS flight as Tokaj, Port and Alsace Pinot Gris, but it is structurally very different. It tests whether a candidate can recognize that residual sugar alone is not the story: Madeira combines fortification, oxidative ageing and searing acidity in a way unlike still sweet wine.
