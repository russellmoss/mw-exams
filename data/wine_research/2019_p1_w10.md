# M3 Chardonnay, Shaw and Smith, 2016

## Quick tasting profile
Medium lemon with ripe citrus, stone fruit, struck flint and finely integrated oak. The palate is dry and concentrated, carrying creamy texture, lees weight and a savoury nutty note, but held in shape by cool-climate acidity. This is a modern premium Australian Chardonnay with real ageing potential.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Principally from the M3 block and Lenswood estate fruit in Adelaide Hills
- Soils: Source needed
- Vinification: Whole-bunch pressing, barrel fermentation and full malo according to the 2016 technical sheet
- Elevage: Nine months in French oak, with 30% new barrels
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.0% / 1.0 g/L / 7.03 g/L

## Vintage character (2016 Adelaide Hills)
The Adelaide Hills regional report card describes 2016 as a favourable year with increases in Chardonnay tonnage and strong fruit condition. Shaw and Smith's own 2016 sheet points to warm but even ripening, which explains the wine's combination of generosity and precision.

## Producer style
Shaw and Smith is one of Adelaide Hills' benchmark quality estates, especially for Sauvignon Blanc and Chardonnay. The M3 is their established flagship Chardonnay style: cool-climate fruit, reductive handling, measured new French oak and enough texture to age.

## Why it's in this exam
This is the second Australian wine in 2019 Paper 1 Question 2. It provides a premium, oak-shaped Chardonnay foil to the taut Watervale Riesling, testing whether the candidate can separate grape-derived citrus and acid markers from winemaking-derived texture and reduction.
