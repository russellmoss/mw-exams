# Initial Blanc de Blancs, Jacques Selosse, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold for Champagne, with bruised apple, hazelnut, brioche, smoke, citrus oil, and oxidative complexity rather than simple autolytic freshness. The palate is dry but broad and vinous, with creamy mousse, high intensity, and a long savoury finish that feels more like serious white wine with bubbles than a light aperitif style. In blind tasting this is a strong grower-Champagne outlier and a clear quality marker.

## Vintage character (NV Champagne base wines)
For Selosse, the more important exam point is not a single declared vintage but the house's oxidative, reserve-wine-driven philosophy. Initial is built from several vintages to create depth, texture, and a mature, vinous profile that stands apart from cleaner, more reductive grandes marques. ## Producer style
Jacques Selosse is one of Champagne's cult growers, historically led by Anselme Selosse and associated with radical low-yield viticulture, barrel work, and a deliberately oxidative, terroir-first approach. The wines are intensely individual and often challenge expectations of what Champagne should taste like. Initial is one of the key reference cuvées for candidates who need to recognise ambitious grower Champagne in the glass.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 1 among Chardonnay-based wines where oak handling mattered. It gives the examiner a profound, oxidative, barrel-shaped Champagne benchmark against which more classical blanc de blancs and still Chardonnay expressions can be compared.
