# Vouvray Clos du Bourg Demi-Sec, Domaine Huet, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-gold in colour for its youth. Intensely aromatic nose of ripe quince, pear, honeycomb, orange zest, white flowers and chalky minerality with a subtle smokiness. The palate is full and rounded with lush yellow fruit, ~18 g/L residual sugar seamlessly integrated into a framework of zesty acidity and saline minerality. Silky-creamy texture, great depth, and a very long, focused finish with bitter almond and crushed flint notes. Outstanding quality with decades of ageing potential.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc
- Vineyard: Clos du Bourg — 6 ha walled vineyard on the Premiere Cote, one of Vouvray's oldest documented sites (7th century). Shallowest, stoniest soils on the estate.
- Soils: Clay-limestone (argilo-calcaire) over Turonian tuffeau limestone; clay topsoil only ~1 metre deep, with flint and chalk inclusions. Vines root directly into the tuffeau.
- Vinification: Hand-harvested in 2-3 successive passes (tries). Whole-bunch pressed. Fermented in a 50/50 mix of stainless steel tanks and old demi-muids with indigenous yeasts. No malolactic fermentation. Bottled relatively early to preserve acidity.
- Elevage: Old demi-muids (neutral oak); duration Source needed.
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% / ~18 g/L / Source needed
- Biodynamic: Certified since 1993 (one of France's earliest adopters).

## Vintage character (2022 Loire / Vouvray)
2022 was a "solar" vintage across the Loire — one of the hottest European summers on record. Cool winter, uneventful spring (frost risk avoided as budburst was late), then sustained summer heat from July onward kept fruit disease-free and pushed phenolic ripeness. Most vignerons acknowledge lower-than-average acidity, yet Chenin Blanc's natural tartness and Vouvray's tuffeau-derived freshness provided a counterbalance. The result: richer, rounder wines than typical, but well-balanced examples retain classic Vouvray tension. All styles from sparkling through to sweet were produced. (source: Wine Spectator vintage chart; Wine Industry Advisor; Winedoctor Loire 2022 first taste)

## Producer style
Domaine Huet is the benchmark estate for Vouvray and one of the Loire's most revered domaines. Founded in 1928 by Victor Huet, the estate farms three single-vineyard clos on the Premiere Cote: Le Haut-Lieu, Le Mont, and Clos du Bourg. Fully biodynamic since 1990 (certified 1993). Run by Benjamin Joliveau (winemaker since 2009), owned by the Hwang family (American). The house style favours purity and transparency of terroir over winemaking intervention — indigenous yeasts, no MLF, neutral vessels. Each vineyard is released in Sec, Demi-Sec, or Moelleux depending on what the vintage dictates. Clos du Bourg is widely considered the most powerful and age-worthy of the three sites, with the shallowest soils producing the most mineral and structured wines. Mid-to-upper tier pricing (~$35-50 USD retail for Demi-Sec); iconic status among Chenin Blanc collectors. (source: Rare Wine Co.; Polaner Selections; Hic Wine Merchants)

## Why it's in this exam
Part of 2025 Paper 1, Question 3: "Wines 7-9 are from the same country and different single grape varieties." The three wines are all from France — a Muscadet (Melon de Bourgogne), an Alsace Riesling, and this Vouvray (Chenin Blanc). The question tests the candidate's ability to distinguish three different French white varieties from three distinct Loire/Alsace terroirs, comment on production methods (including the significance of demi-sec residual sugar and biodynamic viticulture), and assess style/quality/maturity. Huet Clos du Bourg Demi-Sec serves as the high-quality, off-dry Chenin Blanc benchmark — a classic MW examiner choice for testing whether candidates can identify Chenin's honeyed quince character and Loire minerality under blind conditions.

**Note:** This is the second consecutive year the IMW has used Domaine Huet Clos du Bourg Demi-Sec — the 2020 vintage appeared as 2024_p3_w12 in Paper 3 Q4 (residual sugar identification flight). Candidates should expect Huet as a recurring reference point for top-tier Vouvray.
