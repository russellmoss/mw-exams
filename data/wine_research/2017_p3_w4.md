# "Amber", Cullen, 2014. Margaret River, WA, Australia

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber-orange colour (the wine's name describes its appearance). Nose of dried apricot, quince, orange peel, honey, walnut, chamomile, and oxidative notes from skin contact; complex, unusual, and aromatic in a non-typical-white-wine way. Full-bodied for a white wine, grippy tannins from prolonged skin contact, broad textured palate, low-to-medium acidity, long rich finish. The high ABV (15%) combined with the tannin structure and amber colour make this the unmistakable "orange wine" of the flight.

## Vintage character (2014 Margaret River)
2014 was a very good vintage in Margaret River — a warm, even growing season that produced ripe, concentrated wines. For a skin-contact white at 15% ABV, 2014's warmth would have delivered maximum phenolic extraction and aromatic complexity.

## Producer style
Cullen Wines is one of Margaret River's founding and most prestigious estates, established in 1971 by Dr Kevin and Diana Cullen. Now run by their daughter Vanya Cullen, the estate is certified biodynamic (since 2004) and uses minimal intervention winemaking throughout. Cullen is best known for the Cullen Diana Madeline (Cabernet Sauvignon/Merlot) and Kevin John Chardonnay, but the Amber represents their exploration of skin-contact winemaking at the avant-garde end of their range. The estate is a Carbon Neutral certified producer. Source: knowledge-based; Cullen website returned errors in this session.

Key exam signals: amber/orange colour (dramatically different from expected white or rosé), tannin structure on a "white grape" wine, oxidative aromas, high ABV.
