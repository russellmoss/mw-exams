# Pinotage, Kaapzicht, 2013. Stellenbosch, South Africa

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple, full opacity. Nose of ripe dark plum, blackberry, rubber/tyre (signature Pinotage descriptor — an acetone-like volatile compound), vanilla, coffee, and mocha from oak; some earthy barnyard notes possible depending on winemaking approach. Full-bodied, dense grippy tannins (bush vine concentration), high ripe acidity, long inky finish. Classic Stellenbosch Pinotage: powerful, extracted, and oak-forward. The rubber/acetone note is a highly diagnostic Pinotage signal under blind conditions.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinotage (South African cross: Pinot Noir x Cinsaut, created by Perold at Stellenbosch University, first planted 1925)
- Vineyard: Kaapzicht estate, Stellenbosch; bush vines planted 1995 (confirmed for flagship Steytler Pinotage on current vintage; likely similar for 2013)
- Soils: Stellenbosch granite-derived soils (Source needed for Kaapzicht specific soil profile)
- Vinification: Whole-berry fermentation (confirmed for Steytler Pinotage; Source: kaapzicht.co.za)
- Elevage: 20 months in 80% new oak (confirmed for current vintage Steytler Pinotage; Source: kaapzicht.co.za); the 2013 is likely from the flagship or standard Pinotage tier — elevage may vary
- ABV: 14.5%

## Vintage character (2013 Stellenbosch)
2013 was a warm, dry vintage in the Western Cape — producing concentrated, ripe Stellenbosch reds with generous fruit, high alcohol, and firm tannins. Pinotage in warm vintages develops maximum dark fruit concentration and plush texture. The 14.5% ABV reflects the vintage's generosity.

## Producer style
Kaapzicht is a family-owned estate in Stellenbosch, part of the Steytler family operation. They are significant specialists in Pinotage, producing multiple tiers including the premium Steytler Pinotage (flagship, limited release) and entry estate Pinotage. Kaapzicht is a leading advocate for serious, age-worthy Cape Pinotage at the opposite end of the style spectrum from commercial, confected Pinotage. The estate uses bush vines and high new oak aging for the flagship tier. Source: kaapzicht.co.za.

## Why it's in this exam
Kaapzicht Pinotage represents the flagship Cape variety — an exclusively South African cross used to test identification of Pinotage's distinctive profile. It sits in P2 alongside Meerlust Rubicon to provide two contrasting South African reds: the international Bordeaux-blend style (Rubicon) vs. the distinctly South African variety (Pinotage). Key exam signals: rubber/acetone aroma, deep inky colour, full body, Stellenbosch origin.
