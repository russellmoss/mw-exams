# Cinsault, Bechthold Vineyard, Birichino, 2016. Lodi, California, USA.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale garnet-ruby, light in colour. Nose: fresh cherry, raspberry, dried rose petal, a hint of spice, and earthy mineral complexity from 130-year-old own-rooted vines. Palate: light-bodied, low tannin, fresh acidity — a style completely unlike Californian Cabernet or Zinfandel norms. At 12.5% ABV this is deliberately restrained and ethereal. The palate resembles a cool-climate light red (Cru Beaujolais, light Pinot Noir) but with a distinct Californian sun-drenched fruit quality.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Cinsault (Cinsaut)
- Vineyard: Bechthold Vineyard, Lodi — planted 1886 by Joseph Spenker; described as "the oldest continuous Cinsault vineyard" in existence (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinsaut); own-rooted vines (pre-phylloxera survivors)
- Soils: Sandy loam (Lodi's Tokay sandy loam characteristic)
- Vinification: Minimal intervention; native fermentation; stainless or neutral; no oak per Birichino's stated philosophy
- Elevage: No new oak; stainless or old neutral; early bottling for freshness
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / dry / medium

## Vintage character (2016 Lodi)
2016 was a moderate vintage in Lodi — better balance than the drought years (2014-2015). Cinsault from old vines in Lodi tends to produce consistent aromatic, light-bodied wines regardless of year variation, given the vineyard's age and deep root systems. (Source needed: 2016 Lodi vintage report)

## Producer style
Birichino was founded in 2008 by Alex Krause and John Locke in Santa Cruz, California, bringing "combined four decades" of winemaking experience (California, France, Italy). Their philosophy: minimal intervention, native fermentations, stainless or neutral barrels, no filtration when possible. They source from own-rooted 19th and early 20th century family-owned vineyards in marine-influenced climates. The Bechthold Cinsault is their most celebrated wine — a historically important vineyard presented with Burgundian delicacy. Market positioning: artisan/natural wine adjacent, $30-40. (source: birichino.com)

## Why it's in this exam
The Bechthold Vineyard Cinsault is a genuine curveball in Paper 3: a light-bodied, low-alcohol, pale-coloured Californian red that looks and tastes nothing like Napa Cabernet or Zinfandel. The oldest Cinsault vineyard in the world adds historical interest. Tests candidate knowledge of: Lodi as a region, Cinsault as a variety (normally Southern France/South Africa), old-vine character, and the lighter end of Californian red wine production. The 12.5% ABV alongside Bandol (14%) and Mustiguillo (14%) in the same paper creates an immediate structural contrast.
