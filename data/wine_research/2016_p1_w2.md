# Châteauneuf-du-Pape Blanc, Domaine du Vieux Télégraphe, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold; full-bodied, aromatic white from the southern Rhône. Nose: Grenache Blanc-driven ripe stone fruit (white peach, pear), floral notes from Clairette, honeyed waxy Roussanne, and southern herb. Palate: broad, rich, medium-to-full weight, dry with a slightly oily texture from Grenache Blanc/Clairette; moderate acidity; warm finish from ripe grapes in southern Rhône. At 2 years of age in 2016, still fresh but beginning to develop.

## Technical specs
- Grape varieties: Grenache Blanc, Clairette, Roussanne, and Bourboulenc (confirmed from en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vieux_Télégraphe); this reflects the full palette of permitted white CDP varieties
- Vineyard: Domaine du Vieux Télégraphe's 70 ha estate, concentrated on the La Crau plateau with galets roulés (large rounded stones) soils
- Vinification: typically fermented in old demi-muids or foudres; no new oak; long lees contact likely (Source needed — exact 2014 vinification protocol)

## Vintage character (2014 Châteauneuf-du-Pape)
2014 was a cool, elegant vintage in the southern Rhône — unusually good for whites, which showed freshness and preserved acidity compared to the warmer 2013 and 2015 vintages. White CDPs from 2014 tend to be more precise and longer-lived than the broad 2013s. At 2 years of age in 2016, these wines would be in their primary freshness window. ## Producer style
Domaine du Vieux Télégraphe, Bédarrides; owned by the Brunier family for several generations. Named after the Chappe optical telegraph station once located on the La Crau plateau. Red CDP is the flagship (Grenache-dominant, 65% Grenache / 15% Syrah / 15% Mourvèdre). White CDP from Grenache Blanc, Clairette, Roussanne, and Bourboulenc is secondary production but high quality. Second wine: Vieux Mas des Papes. (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vieux_Télégraphe)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 1 white wines. Châteauneuf-du-Pape Blanc is the southern Rhône white benchmark — rarely seen in this paper but useful for testing candidates' ability to identify non-classic white blends. The multi-variety Grenache Blanc/Clairette/Roussanne/Bourboulenc profile is distinct from Burgundy Chardonnay, Loire Chenin, or Bordeaux Sauvignon. The rich, full-bodied, aromatic but dry profile and warm-climate origin are the key signals.
