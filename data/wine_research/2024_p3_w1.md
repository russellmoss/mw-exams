# Brut J Schram Blancs, Schramsberg, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold colour from extended lees aging. Complex nose of baked peach, caramel apple, marzipan, brioche, and Meyer lemon. Palate shows broad richness — tropical and citrus fruits layered with sponge cake, graham cracker, and vanilla custard — underpinned by generous, lively acidity. Long finish with lemon curd brightness. A prestige-cuvée sparkling wine with serious autolytic complexity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Chardonnay-dominant blend (Chardonnay with some Pinot Noir)
- Vineyard sources: Multi-regional North Coast — Sonoma (58%), Napa (30%), Marin (8%), Mendocino (4%). Named vineyards include Wilkinson, Schwarze, Ramsey (Napa), Stevens (Marin), Piedra Libre (Sonoma) (source: Wilson Daniels)
- Vinification: Méthode traditionnelle. ~25% of base wine fermented in oak barrels. Hand-harvested, whole-cluster pressed.
- Élevage: 8 years on lees in Schramsberg's historic Diamond Mountain caves. Hand-riddled. Brut dosage.

## Vintage character (2014 North Coast California)
2014 was an excellent vintage in North Coast California. Abundant sun with cool evening temperatures produced grapes with an agile balance of ripe fruit and lively acidity — ideal for sparkling wine base. The vintage yielded wines of generous fruit expression without sacrificing freshness (source: Schramsberg).

## Producer style
Schramsberg Vineyards, founded in 1965 by Jack and Jamie Davies in Napa's Diamond Mountain District, is California's pioneering traditional-method sparkling house. First to produce a California Blanc de Blancs (1965). Historic prominence: served at Nixon's 1972 "Toast to Peace" dinner with Zhou Enlai. Now led by Hugh Davies. Sources from 78+ vineyard sites across 4 North Coast counties. J Schram is their prestige cuvée, representing only 2–3% of annual production. Wine Enthusiast: 98 points (2016 vintage); James Suckling: 95 points.
