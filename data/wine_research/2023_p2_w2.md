# Valpolicella, Allegrini, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Bright ruby and much lighter in feel than Amarone-derived stereotypes. Expect sour cherry, plum, herbs and pepper, with crisp acidity, moderate tannin and a juicy, refreshing finish. This should read as a polished, high-quality everyday Valpolicella rather than a dense or appassimento-shaped wine.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 70% Corvina, 30% Rondinella
- Vineyard: mostly Monte dei Galli and other south-east-facing hillside sites in Valpolicella Classica, 180-280m
- Soils: volcanic with clay and calcareous components
- Vinification: destemmed after hand harvest; fermented in temperature-controlled stainless steel for about 9 days
- Elevage: stainless steel until mid-February, then 2 months in bottle
- ABV / RS / TA: official and importer sheets show about 13% / 1.6 g/L / 5.65 g/L

## Vintage character (2022 Valpolicella)
Allegrini's importer sheet describes 2022 as a dry, very warm year with lack of rain, relieved by August rainfall and later day-night temperature swings that helped final ripening. It also notes hail damage in part of the zone, mitigated by the estate's spread across multiple municipalities. In blind terms, that suggests ripe fruit but with enough acidity and freshness preserved to keep the wine energetic rather than baked.

## Producer style
Allegrini is one of the reference names of Valpolicella Classica, with deep family roots in Fumane and a major role in the region's quality revolution. Even this basic Valpolicella is handled carefully from hillside fruit and framed as a refined introduction to Corvina rather than a cheap by-product. The market position is therefore premium-for-category but still everyday in intent.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 2 in 2023 Paper 2 Question 1 gives the examiner a contrast to Barolo: still Italian, still ageworthy to a point, but much less tannic and far less oak-driven. The candidate has to recognize that quality in Italian red does not always mean concentration; freshness, typicity and drinkability are the point here.
