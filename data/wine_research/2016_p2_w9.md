# Châteauneuf-du-Pape, Clos des Papes, 2011

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby with ripe black cherry, kirsch, garrigue, lavender, warm stone, and sweet spice. The palate is full-bodied and generous, with high alcohol, broad texture, and ripe tannins, but the best examples retain freshness and savoury complexity rather than tipping into jam. Blind, this points clearly to serious southern Rhône Grenache-led blending rather than monovarietal Syrah.

## Vintage character (2011 Southern Rhône)
2011 produced earlier-drinking, aromatic southern Rhône reds compared with more massive benchmark years. The best Châteauneufs show ripe fruit, warmth, and garrigue complexity, but usually with softer structure and less monumental depth than 2010. That makes typicity easy to read in an exam without requiring the candidate to overstate scale.

## Producer style
Clos des Papes is one of Châteauneuf-du-Pape's most respected traditional estates and is valued for balance, finesse, and transparency rather than exaggerated extraction. Even in warm years the wine tends to stay savoury and composed, showing why top Châteauneuf can be serious fine wine rather than merely high-alcohol richness. It is a natural benchmark for the appellation in MW tasting sets.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 2 Question 4 with Domaine Gangloff La Barbarine as a Rhône comparison. It helps test whether the candidate can distinguish Grenache-led southern Rhône breadth, alcohol, and blend complexity from the more perfumed, Syrah-led northern Rhône profile of Côte-Rôtie.
