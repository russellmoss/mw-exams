# Muscadet Sevre et Maine, Vincent & Sebastien Chereau, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon, light-bodied, and quietly saline rather than overtly fruity. Expect citrus peel, green apple, wet stone, and a brisk, oyster-shell finish driven by high acidity and low phenolic weight.

## Vintage character (2015 Loire)
2015 in the Loire was generally warm and dry, giving riper fruit and a little more mid-palate breadth than cooler years. In Muscadet, that tends to mean slightly softer citrus and orchard fruit over the classic marine, saline line, while still keeping the region's essential snap.

## Producer style
Vincent & Sebastien Chereau sit in the traditional Nantais register: straightforward, dry Muscadet with freshness and seafood affinity ahead of oak or elaboration. The wine reads as regionally typical rather than ambitious or heavily worked.

## Why it's in this exam
This sits in the 2017 paper 1 Loire set. It works as the lightest, most neutral, Atlantic white in the bracket, testing whether the candidate can separate Muscadet from richer Loire Chenin or Sauvignon-based wines.
