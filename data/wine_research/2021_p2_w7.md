# Petite Sirah, Bogle Vineyards, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Deep opaque purple-black with boysenberry, blueberry, mocha, vanilla and sweet spice. The palate is full-bodied, dry and plushly concentrated, with thick fruit, moderate acidity and soft but substantial tannin. Blind, it reads as a dark, warm-climate, oak-influenced commercial red rather than a site-transparent fine-wine bottling.

## Vintage character (2018 California)
The Bogle fact sheet presents 2018 as fully successful for the variety, with ripe, jammy flavours and soft texture. In exam terms, the wine is driven more by warm-climate ripeness and brand style than by a finely delineated subregional signal.

## Producer style
Bogle is a large, reliable California producer with strong commercial reach and a reputation for delivering concentration at accessible prices. Petite Sirah is one of the house's heritage grapes, and the style aims squarely at richness, softness and broad consumer appeal.

## Why it's in this exam
This is one of the Americas pair in 2021 Paper 2 Question 3, matched against Chilean Carmenère. It tests whether the candidate can sort between two dark-fruited, generous New World reds by tannin, oak profile and varietal markers.
