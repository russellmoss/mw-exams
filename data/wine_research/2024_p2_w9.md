# Pinot Noir Wismer-Parke, Bachelder, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
This should show cool-climate Pinot with more mineral gravity than overt sweetness: red cherry, raspberry, rose, herbs and an iron-saline, slightly ferrous edge rather than plush New World ripeness. The palate should be fine-boned but not thin, with fresh acidity, savory complexity and a long mineral finish that reads more Bench than broad or jammy.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir

## Vintage character (2020 Niagara)
Bachelder calls 2020 a rare "artistic vintage": slow sugar accumulation, good acid retention, no rot pressure, normal picking dates and pristine fruit despite the warm season. The producer argues the cool nights of September saved the wines from becoming fruit bombs, preserving both terroir definition and longevity. In blind tasting, that suggests ripe but not overblown Pinot Noir, with precision and site clarity rather than simple generosity.

## Producer style
Thomas Bachelder's Niagara project is explicitly terroir-driven and parcel-specific. The estate's own material frames Wismer-Parke as one of the Bench's most original crus, marked by red fruit, saline minerality and a wild iron-tinged character. The house voice is Burgundian in ambition but Niagara-specific in language: site transparency, mineral tension and the avoidance of blowsy fruit are treated as the central goals.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 9 sits in 2024 Paper 2 Question 3, where wines 8-12 are the same single variety across five countries and candidates must identify origin and grape, then discuss how climate and winemaking shape style and quality. Wismer-Parke is the Canadian Pinot Noir in that sequence: a cool-climate, Bench-grown expression that tests whether the candidate can recognize Pinot Noir outside Burgundy and distinguish mineral Niagara from warmer or sweeter-fruited examples.
