# Crios de Susana Balbo Torrontés, Dominio del Plata, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw to light gold; very aromatic — Torrontés is one of the world's most perfumed white grapes, and a new-release 2015 tasted in the 2016 exam is maximally expressive. Nose: intense rose petal, lychee, peach blossom, jasmine, mandarin orange, and grapey Muscat-like aromatics. Palate: dry (despite the Muscat-like nose) to off-dry; medium body; high alcohol for the style (13.9%) from warm Cafayate Valley conditions; moderate acidity. Clean, fresh, extremely aromatic finish. Very easy to identify as Torrontés if you know the variety.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Torrontés Riojano (most aromatic subvariety; Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torrontés)
- Vinification: stainless steel; low temperature fermentation to preserve aromatics; no oak; bottled early for freshness
- ABV: 13.9% / RS: dry (likely <4 g/L despite aromatic intensity) / TA: moderate
- Production: Dominio del Plata, based in Luján de Cuyo, Mendoza; founded by Susana Balbo (former Catena winemaker, one of Argentina's most influential white wine producers)
- Crios (meaning "offspring" in Spanish) is the second label of Dominio del Plata; accessible, everyday tier
- Torrontés is "most widely planted white variety" in Argentina as of 2008 (~20% of white wine; confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torrontés)
- "Tend not to age very well and is often consumed within a year of its vintage date" (confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torrontés) — 2015 at 2016 exam is appropriate window

## Vintage character (2015 Salta/Cafayate)
2015 was a normal harvest year in Cafayate — the high-altitude (~1,700m) Salta region delivers extreme temperature variation between day and night, preserving acidity in Torrontés while enabling full aromatic development. As a fresh, early-drinking wine, the Crios Torrontés 2015 would be exactly at its ideal freshness window in 2016. (Source needed — specific 2015 Salta vintage report)

## Producer style
Susana Balbo founded Dominio del Plata with her husband Pedro Marchevsky in 1999. One of Argentina's most acclaimed winemakers; earned international recognition for premium Malbec and for championing Torrontés. Crios is the accessible, commercial line. The Torrontés from Cafayate is the signature white. Positioned at supermarket/restaurant-by-the-glass tier, not premium. (Source needed — producer website inaccessible during research)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 1 white wines. Argentine Torrontés is a signature exam variety for MW — intensely aromatic, distinctive, and specific to Argentina. If candidates know the Torrontés variety, it's an easy identification. If not, the Muscat-like nose might mislead toward Gewürztraminer (similar aromatics) or Moscato. The MW examiner tests whether candidates can distinguish Torrontés from Gewürztraminer: similar rose/lychee profile but different texture, acid structure, and origin.
