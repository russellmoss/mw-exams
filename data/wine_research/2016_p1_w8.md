# Albariño, Bodegas de Fefiñanes, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon-gold; aromatic, fresh, precise. Nose: white peach, apricot, citrus blossom (jasmine), crisp lemon-lime zest, subtle saline minerality from the Atlantic influence. Palate: light-to-medium body, bone dry, high acidity, clean and persistent. Albariño's signature high natural acidity and stone fruit character are unmistakable. Moderate alcohol (12.5%) confirms the cool, wet coastal Galician climate. At 2 years in 2016, drinking at peak freshness.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Albariño (Rías Baixas DO requires minimum 70% Albariño; Fefiñanes typically 100%)
- Vinification: stainless steel fermentation; no malo; cold maceration pre-fermentation for aromatic extraction; no oak contact; minimal sulfur addition (Source needed — specific Fefiñanes protocol)

## Vintage character (2014 Rías Baixas)
2014 was an excellent vintage in Rías Baixas — cooler, drier summer conditions than the rainy 2013. The Atlantic climate that normally brings heavy rainfall moderated in 2014, giving excellent ripeness with natural acidity preserved. Albariño from 2014 showed good aromatic complexity and fresh structure. (Source needed — specific 2014 Rías Baixas vintage report)

## Producer style
Bodegas de Fefiñanes is one of Rías Baixas' most prestigious and historic estates, operating from the Pazo de Fefiñanes — a 16th-century Galician manor house in Cambados. Family-owned; the Marqués de Figueroa family has vinified Albariño here for over a century. Three expression tiers: basic Albariño de Fefiñanes (young, fresh), 1583 (oak-aged), and III Año (extended barrel). The entry-level Albariño de Fefiñanes (this wine) is the benchmark for clean, pure Rías Baixas style. (Source needed — producer website inaccessible; no Wikipedia page found)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 1 white wines. Albariño from Rías Baixas is an important variety for MW candidates to identify — distinctive aromatic profile (white peach, apricot, citrus blossom), high acidity, low-medium alcohol, cool maritime origin. Fefiñanes is the prestige producer benchmark. Candidates must distinguish from: Vinho Verde (similar climate but different variety), Galician whites generally, and lighter Loire whites.
