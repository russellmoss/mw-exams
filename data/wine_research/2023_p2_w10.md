# Probus, Clos Triguedina, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Opaque purple-ruby. Dense black plum, cassis, violet, liquorice and dark spice with clear oak framing. The palate is full, muscular and firmly tannic, with Cahors' dark Malbec signature and enough freshness to avoid heaviness despite the rich scale.

## Vintage character (2018 Cahors)
2018 in southwest France was generally warm and generous, producing ripe, ample reds. In blind terms that aligns with this wine's elevated alcohol, dark-fruited density and broad scale, while Cahors still supplies firm tannic architecture.

## Producer style
Clos Triguedina is one of Cahors' reference estates, associated with the Baldès family and with elevating Malbec from regional rusticity toward ambitious fine-wine expressions. Probus is the estate's more powerful, cellarworthy side rather than an easy-drinking example.

## Why it's in this exam
In 2023 Paper 2 Question 3, this was paired conceptually against a Pinot Noir flight from Argentina. It functions as the darker, more tannic Old World counterpart, testing whether candidates can separate Pinot from Malbec while also identifying a classic Malbec home outside Argentina.
