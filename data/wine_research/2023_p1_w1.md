# Sancerre Les Baronnes, Henri Bourgeois, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon. The nose should show lime, apple, white flowers and a flinty edge rather than overt tropicality, with a crisp, linear palate and bright acidity. Under blind conditions this reads as classic cool-climate Sauvignon Blanc with chalky tension and moderate body rather than a barrel-shaped or plush expression (source: producer tech sheet).

## Vintage character (2021 Sancerre)
2021 in Sancerre was a difficult, low-yielding year marked by frost, mildew pressure and storms, with many growers describing the season as complicated or even catastrophic. Decanter reported yields around half of a normal crop, but growers still expected good quality from the fruit that survived, which fits a more taut, classically fresh profile than a broad or opulent one (source: Decanter).

## Producer style
Henri Bourgeois is a benchmark Chavignol family domaine whose house style aims at typicity and precision rather than winemaking artefact. The Les Baronnes sheet presents the cuvee as a regional signature wine: subtle but powerful, persistent, fruity, and mineral, built on chalky-clay terroir and intended to express textbook Sancerre character (source: producer tech sheet).
