# Muscat de Beaumes de Venise, Domaine de Durban, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with intensely grapey, floral aromatics: orange blossom, fresh grape, peach, apricot, and lychee. The palate should be sweet and viscous, but not heavy, with fortification locking in primary perfume rather than developing oxidative complexity. In blind tasting it is the most overtly aromatic and grapey sweet wine in the set.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Muscat a Petits Grains Blancs
- Vineyard: about 25ha for the Muscat at Domaine de Durban
- Soils: clay and limestone
- Vinification: manual harvest, no destemming noted by retail tech source; vin doux naturel production implies arrested fermentation by spirit addition

## Vintage character (2020 Beaumes-de-Venise)
The cited sources do not provide a clean vintage report for the 2020 Muscat bottling, so the safer exam point is stylistic rather than meteorological: this wine is built around preserving Muscat's fresh floral aromatics and sweetness rather than chasing botrytis, oak, or oxidative depth. That makes it especially easy to separate from the other sweet wines in the question.

## Producer style
Domaine de Durban is an established family estate in Beaumes-de-Venise represented by Kermit Lynch. The Kermit Lynch producer sheet emphasizes old vines, high-elevation Ventoux foothill sites, and classic southern Rhone farming, while the Muscat bottling is presented as a pure-fruited, appellation-typical vin doux naturel.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 11 sits near the end of the 2024 Paper 3 Question 4 sugar flight. It gives the candidate a fortified sweet wine that is neither Port nor Madeira: lowish color depth, explosive Muscat aromatics, and spirit-fortified sweetness rather than botrytis or drying.
