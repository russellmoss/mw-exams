# Toscana IGT "Aranum" (Arcanum), Tenuta di Arceno, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple. Opulent, seductive nose of violets, roses, dark cherries, sweet mint, vanilla bean, white pepper, and sauvage notes of rosemary and oregano. Full-bodied palate with soft, fine tannins, firm acidity, and gorgeous balance. Flavours of dark chocolate-covered cherries, black licorice, cedar, and balsamic notes. Built to age yet drinkable young — both opulent and elegant. Long, spicy finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Cabernet Franc (since 2016 vintage)
- Vineyard: L'Apparita and Belvedere blocks, selected via 'micro-cru' approach from 60 individually farmed blocks across 220 acres
- Soils: Sandy clay at 300–500m elevation; variable composition of rock, sand, clay, sandstone, basalt, schist
- Vinification: Specifically chosen yeasts; no other additives; micro-cru block-by-block vinification

## Vintage character (2018 Tuscany)
An exceptional vintage for Tuscany — five-star status for Chianti Classico. Very regular growing season with no extreme weather events, normal picking times. Warm but not excessively hot. The watchwords are silkiness, elegance, and surprising freshness. Excellent bright red fruit cores balanced with good acidity and structure. A charming, elegant vintage with many wines to enjoy both now and later.

## Producer style
Tenuta di Arceno is a Jackson Family Wines estate in the Chianti Classico zone near San Gusmé. Under winemaker Lawrence Cronin (and previously consultant Pierre Seillan), the estate employs a 'micro-cru' approach, dividing 220 acres into 60 individual blocks, each farmed and harvested separately. Cabernet Franc plantings expanded from 1.2 ha to 17 ha since 2002. Arcanum has been 100% Cabernet Franc since 2016 — a pure expression of the variety in Tuscan terroir. The estate also produces Chianti Classico from Sangiovese and Valadorna from Merlot.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in Question 1 (Wines 1–3, same grape variety — Cabernet Franc) as a Tuscan Super Tuscan expression. At 14.5% ABV with new French oak and full body, it represents the warmest, richest interpretation in the Cab Franc trio. The IGT designation signals non-traditional variety for Tuscany. Candidates must recognize this as Cabernet Franc despite the Italian origin — a potential curveball if they default to Sangiovese for anything Tuscan.
