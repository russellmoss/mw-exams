# Gigondas, Château de Saint Cosme, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple with purple undertones. Complex nose of wild berries, blackberries, fig, dried thyme, bay leaves, baking spices, violets, and charcoal. Full-bodied yet impressively fresh, with fine chalky tannins, a dense, velvety mouthfeel, and wonderfully ripe black cherry fruit. Peppery, powdery finish with garrigue-driven aromatics and lingering spice. Remarkably finessed for the warmth of the Southern Rhône.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 70% Grenache, 15% Mourvèdre, 14% Syrah, 1% Cinsaut
- Vineyard: Estate vineyards on the Dentelles de Montmirail slopes, Gigondas
- Soils: Limestone, clay-limestone
- Vinification: Spontaneous fermentation; some whole-cluster inclusion (stems absorb alcohol, retain acidity)
- Élevage: Cement tanks and mostly used 228L Burgundian pièces; bottled unfined and unfiltered
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2022 Rhône Valley)
The hottest and driest growing season since 1950 in the Rhône — even more extreme than 2003. Despite this, growers produced surprisingly elegant wines. In the Southern Rhône, Grenache ripened slowly but vines did not suffer. The reds show loads of concentration with powerful but sweet tannic structure and fresh but lusty fruit flavours. Blockages in ripening were more common further north (Cornas/Hermitage), but the Southern Rhône fared well overall.

## Producer style
Château de Saint Cosme is the leading estate of Gigondas, in the Barruol family since 1490. Louis Barruol took over in 1992, making a dramatic shift to quality. Converted to biodynamics in 2010. Added a négociant arm in 1997 producing single-vineyard Côtes du Rhône and Northern Rhône cuvées. His style combines Southern Rhône ripeness with freshness, using whole-cluster fermentation (stems absorb alcohol), spontaneous fermentation, mostly used Burgundian pièces for élevage, and bottling unfined/unfiltered. Named #1 Wine of the Year by Wine Spectator in 2017.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in Question 2 (Wines 4–7, same country and region — Rhône Valley). As the Grenache-dominant Southern Rhône cru, it contrasts with the Syrah-based Northern Rhône wines (Crozes-Hermitage, Cornas) and the blended Côtes du Rhône.
