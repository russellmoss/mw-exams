# Sauvignon Blanc, Errázuriz, 2010

## Quick tasting profile
Bright, pungent and coastal-leaning Sauvignon Blanc with citrus, gooseberry and green-herbal lift rather than tropical softness. The palate should be fresh and dry, giving a recognizably Chilean take on the variety with more polish than a mass-market bottling.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Sauvignon Blanc
- Vineyard: Aconcagua sourcing; by this period Errázuriz was actively developing coastal Aconcagua for Sauvignon Blanc
- Soils: Source needed
- Vinification: Cool, reductive stainless-steel style; no obvious oak
- Élevage: Source needed
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2010 Aconcagua)
Aconcagua can be warm inland, but the coastal sectors provide the freshness needed for Sauvignon Blanc. The exam point is a wine that combines New World aromatic clarity with enough acidity and restraint to stay distinct from softer, broader examples.

## Producer style
Errázuriz is one of Chile’s blue-chip names, and its Sauvignon Blanc program helped define serious coastal white production in Aconcagua. The style is clean, modern and internationally legible, but with enough site signature to reward a strong candidate.
