# Rosé, Kim Crawford, 2018. New Zealand

## Quick tasting profile
Pale salmon-pink, clean and bright. Nose is fresh, fruit-forward and aromatic: strawberry, watermelon, red cherry, tropical hints — a clean, commercial NZ rosé with vibrant aromatics. Palate is light, refreshing, dry to off-dry, with good Marlborough acidity, clean texture and a short-to-medium finish. Straightforward, commercial and crowd-pleasing — designed for broad market appeal. Similar in style to Provence rosé in terms of paleness and freshness, but with a New World fruit-forward character.

## Vintage character (2018 New Zealand)
2018 was generally a good year for New Zealand wine, with warm conditions in Marlborough producing ripe fruit with good natural acidity. Rosé from 2018 NZ would show generous fruit with the clean, high-acid freshness that is Marlborough's hallmark.

## Producer style
Kim Crawford Wines was founded by Kim Crawford in 1996, sold to Constellation Brands via Vincor in 2003. It is New Zealand's bestselling wine brand globally, with its Sauvignon Blanc as the flagship product. The brand is positioned firmly at the commercial mid-tier ($12–18 range), focusing on accessibility, consistent quality, and New Zealand's clean, fresh identity. The Rosé is a commercial extension product; quality is sound but not the brand's primary focus.
