# Gewürztraminer, Late Harvest, Husch Estate, 2014. Anderson Valley, California, USA

## Quick tasting profile
Deep golden-yellow with amber tints, viscous appearance. Intense nose of lychee, rose water, Turkish delight, ginger, orange blossom, and honeyed dried apricot — Gewürztraminer's signature exotic aromatics amplified by botrytis or late-harvest concentration. Rich, sweet palate, low acid relative to residual sugar, full body, long honeyed finish with floral persistence. The varietal character (lychee/rose) combined with late-harvest sweetness makes this highly identifiable.

## Vintage character (2014 Anderson Valley)
2014 was a warm California vintage overall, but Anderson Valley's Pacific Ocean proximity moderates the heat significantly. Late harvest wines depend more on botrytis conditions or hang time than on vintage warmth; 2014 in Anderson Valley would have produced ripe, concentrated Gewürztraminer with full varietal aromatic expression.

## Producer style
Husch Vineyards is the oldest bonded winery in Anderson Valley (established 1968), operated by the Hugo family (third generation). A small, family-owned producer with estate vineyards, producing 22 different wines total with six distributed nationally. The winery is known for Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Gewürztraminer, and late harvest wines from Anderson Valley's cool climate. Source: huschvineyards.com.
