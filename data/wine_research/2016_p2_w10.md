# Malbec, Bodega Colomé, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Opaque purple-black with blackberry, blueberry, violet, dark chocolate, and a high-altitude graphite edge. The palate is full-bodied and concentrated, carrying ripe tannin, strong alcohol, and dense fruit, but altitude keeps the finish fresher and more linear than softer Mendoza examples. In blind tasting it reads as serious Argentine Malbec with mountain intensity rather than plush, simple fruit.

## Vintage character (2013 Salta)
The key qualitative point for Colomé is altitude more than easy regional vintage shorthand: the fruit comes from very high vineyards that deliver strong UV exposure, thick skins, and significant diurnal shift. That combination tends to yield dark, intense Malbec with preserved acidity and floral lift. Source needed for a directly citable 2013 Salta vintage report.

## Producer style
Colomé is one of Argentina's emblematic high-altitude estates and has built its identity around concentration, purity, and dramatic mountain-grown fruit. The wines are often powerful, dark-fruited, and cellar-worthy, with oak used to frame rather than hide the natural intensity of the fruit. In MW terms it is a useful benchmark for premium Salta Malbec and the stylistic impact of altitude.
