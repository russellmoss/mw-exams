# Châteauneuf-du-Pape Blanc, Domaine Bousquet des Papes, 2014. Rhône, France.

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold. Nose: rich and aromatic — Grenache Blanc richness, white peach, honey, apricot, with herbal garrigue notes; possible Roussanne adds floral complexity; Clairette contributes freshness and zest. Palate: full-bodied, creamy, low acidity, weighty texture, warm southern-Rhône character. At 13.5% ABV the Châteauneuf Blanc character is recognisable: opulent, aromatic, textured, with minimal oak. Dry, but giving the impression of richness. A challenging blind tasting identification.

## Vintage character (2014 Châteauneuf-du-Pape)
2014 was a solid to very good vintage in the Southern Rhône — more balanced than the extreme heat years (2003, 2007). Good fruit concentration with manageable alcohol levels. White CdP in 2014 shows freshness relative to hot years. By 2018 the wine is four years old — a good drinking window for CdP Blanc, which typically peaks 3-6 years from vintage. ## Why it's in this exam
CdP Blanc is a classic Paper 3 challenge: a full-bodied, aromatic, dry white from the Southern Rhône that is neither Burgundy nor Alsace nor Bordeaux nor German. The candidate must identify: the Grenache Blanc-led blend, the southern Rhône origin, the distinctive aromatic-but-dry profile. Low acidity and richness combined with herbal garrigue set it apart from Northern Rhône whites (Viognier/Marsanne/Roussanne). Only about 5% of CdP production is blanc — rare enough to be curveball territory.
