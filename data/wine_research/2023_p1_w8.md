# Muscat, Albert Boxler, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon. The wine should be unmistakably Muscat on the nose, with floral and grapey perfume, but in a dry, weightless Alsace register rather than a sweet or fortified style. The palate is expected to be dry, precise and citrus-driven, with enough acidity and mineral tension to keep the aromatics disciplined and savoury at the finish (source: IPO / Kermit Lynch tech sheet).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Muscat (source: tech sheet)
- Vineyard: sourced from the lieu-dit Boland with south-southeast exposure (source: tech sheet)
- Soils: limestone (source: tech sheet)
- Vinification: hand harvested, no fining, cold settling before natural fermentation begins (source: tech sheet)
- Elevage: vinified in old foudre (source: tech sheet)

## Producer style
Albert Boxler is one of Alsace's elite small domaines, centred on Niedermorschwihr and led by Jean Boxler. Kermit Lynch's profile emphasises hand harvesting, natural fermentations, old foudres, organic farming, and a fierce commitment to terroir expression, especially from the family's holdings around Sommerberg and Brand. Even the ostensibly simple Muscat is presented as serious dry white wine, not a merely pretty aromatic curiosity (source: IPO / Kermit Lynch).
