# Chinon Les Barnabés, Olga Raffault, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with a youthful hue. Nose of ripe red cherries, dark strawberry, violets, and subtle earthy/herbaceous notes typical of Loire Cabernet Franc. Medium-bodied palate with fine tannins, medium acidity, flavours of raspberry, baking spice, and blood orange, finishing with charming licorice sweetness and moderate length. A lighter, fruit-forward style of Chinon designed for earlier drinking.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Cabernet Franc
- Vineyard: Les Barnabés, Savigny-en-Véron — alluvial plain near the Loire River
- Soils: Sandy, gravelly alluvial deposits
- Vinification: Hand-harvested, destemmed but not crushed, fermented in stainless steel with native yeasts at cooler temperatures
- Élevage: Aged in tank only (no oak) to preserve freshness and fruit purity
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2021 Loire Valley)
2021 was marked by savage April frosts that drastically cut yields, followed by a cool, wet summer with mildew pressure. September–October sunshine saved the vintage, yielding a small crop of classically proportioned wines with high acidity and modest alcohol. The reds show fresh red fruit character (raspberry, redcurrant) with fine, precise tannic structure. A lighter, more leafy vintage — good for early drinking compared to riper years on either side.

## Producer style
Domaine Olga Raffault is a benchmark Chinon estate farming 25 hectares of Cabernet Franc. Certified organic since 2014, now led by Olga's granddaughter Sylvie Raffault with her husband Eric de la Vigerie and son Arnaud. Winemaking is traditional: hand-picking, stainless-steel fermentation, ageing in larger neutral oak or chestnut casks (though Les Barnabés sees tank only). The flagship is Les Picasses — a more robust, age-worthy cuvée. Les Barnabés, from lighter alluvial soils, is the estate's most approachable wine.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in Question 1 (Wines 1–3, same single or predominant grape variety — Cabernet Franc) as the classic Loire benchmark. Paired against an Argentine Cab Franc (El Enemigo) and a Tuscan IGT Cab Franc (Arcanum), it tests climate/winemaking influence on the same variety. The low ABV, cool-climate leafy character, and tank-only élevage contrast sharply with the warmer-climate, oak-aged expressions.
