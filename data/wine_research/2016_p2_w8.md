# La Barbarine, Domaine Gangloff, 2011

## Quick tasting profile
Opaque ruby with violets, black olive, smoked meat, raspberry, and white pepper over a granite-driven mineral core. The palate is medium- to full-bodied, concentrated but not heavy, with ripe tannin, vivid acidity, and a long, savoury finish. Blind, this is a strong Côte-Rôtie signal: Syrah with perfume and lift rather than the mass and sheer density of hotter-climate Shiraz.

## Vintage character (2011 Northern Rhône)
2011 in the northern Rhône was more approachable and earlier-drinking than monumental vintages such as 2010. The best Côte-Rôties still show perfume, freshness, and fine detail, but generally with softer structure and less monumental density. That suits an exam setting because typicity is clear even if the vintage is not maximal.

## Producer style
Gangloff is a cult northern Rhône address, admired for intensely expressive Syrah that retains perfume and texture rather than brute force. The wines often combine glossy fruit concentration with real site transparency and carefully judged oak. La Barbarine sits comfortably in the high-quality traditional-modern Côte-Rôtie space and carries strong commercial prestige among Rhône specialists.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 2 Question 4 with Clos des Papes as a Rhône comparison. It tests whether the candidate can recognise Syrah from Côte-Rôtie and contrast northern Rhône perfume, granite, and oak handling with the broader, blended style of Châteauneuf-du-Pape.
