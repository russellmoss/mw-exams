# Riesling Iphöfer Julius-Echter-Berg Grosses Gewächs, Hans Wirsching, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Medium gold. Deep, intense, still reductive bouquet with herbal and stony-mineral character, bergamot, mirabelle fruit, and ripe peach. The palate is silky, lush, and very elegant — rich and round yet dense and fine, with concentrated energy and remarkable intensity. Very long finish with lemon aromas and mineral/herbal freshness. A dry, full-bodied Riesling at 13% ABV.

## Vintage character (2019 Franken/Germany)
2019 is considered one of the greatest German vintages of modern times. Harvest was rapid and in favorable weather, with grapes achieving optimal aromatics and phenolic ripeness alongside well over 10 g/L acidity. The combination of excellent ripeness and high acidity produced astonishing wines across the quality spectrum. Franken yields were slightly above the 10-year average, with very good quality.

## Producer style
Weingut Hans Wirsching is one of Germany's largest privately owned family estates, now in its 14th generation, based in the medieval town of Iphofen in Franconia. 225 acres of vineyards produce 37,000 cases annually. Silvaner (40%) is the lead variety, followed by Riesling (20%) and Scheurebe (7%). The Julius-Echter-Berg is the estate's Grand Cru vineyard, always yielding one of the most complex wines from Franken.

## Why it's in this exam
