# Campi Magri Valpolicella Ripasso Superiore, Corte Sant'Alda, 2012

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby with sour cherry, plum, dried herbs, cocoa, and a faint raisined note from the ripasso process. The palate is dry but broad and glycerol, with moderate-plus alcohol, supple tannins, and a darker, richer feel than straight Valpolicella. Blind, it points toward the Valpolicella family because freshness remains, but the texture and sweetness of fruit suggest dried-grape influence.

## Vintage character (2012 Veneto)
2012 was a ripe, warm Italian vintage in many areas, favouring concentration and generosity. In Valpolicella Ripasso that can amplify the category's natural richness, producing a broad, commercially attractive style that remains less dense and sweet than Amarone or Recioto. Source needed for a citable Veneto-specific 2012 report.

## Producer style
Corte Sant'Alda is a respected artisanal Valpolicella producer with an organic/biodynamic reputation and a serious, terroir-aware approach to the region's traditional styles. The wines often show more precision and integrity than heavily commercial ripasso bottlings. Campi Magri therefore makes a strong exam example of quality-minded Ripasso rather than an industrial interpretation.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 4 with Recioto from the same producer and region. It allows the candidate to explain the ripasso technique and contrast a dry, richer red table wine with the sweet dried-grape style of Recioto.
