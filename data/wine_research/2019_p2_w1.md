# Château de la Grenière, 2012. Lussac-Saint-Émilion

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby, garnet rim indicating some development at age 7 at tasting. Nose of ripe red plum, dried cherry, cedar, tobacco and earthy undergrowth; characteristic Merlot-dominant right-bank Bordeaux mid-weight profile. Palate shows soft, approachable tannins with moderate acidity, ripe fruit and a gentle savoury finish. Not a blockbuster; an accessible, value-level Right Bank Bordeaux.

## Vintage character (2012 Bordeaux Right Bank)
2012 was a mixed Bordeaux vintage. The right bank (Merlot) fared better than the left; spring frosts and uneven summer were partially offset by a fine September. Wines from the satellites showed honest, fruity character rather than great depth or concentration. The vintage produced sound, commercially accessible reds rather than iconic bottles.

## Producer style
Château de la Grenière is a family-domaine-scale property in Lussac-Saint-Émilion, one of four Saint-Émilion satellite AOCs north of Saint-Émilion proper. The appellation is dominated by small family estates (avg ~10 ha) and a cooperative; wines are approachable, fruit-driven and priced well below classified Saint-Émilion. Château de la Grenière is not a well-known export brand; it sits solidly in the quality-value mid-tier of Bordeaux.
