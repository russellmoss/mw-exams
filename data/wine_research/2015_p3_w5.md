# Quinta da Cavadinha Vintage Port, Warre's, 1998

## Quick tasting profile
Deep garnet, already showing some tawny-brick at rim after 17 years in bottle. Nose: mature dried fruit (prune, fig, dark cherry), spice, tobacco, leather, cedar, with background iodine and bitter chocolate. Palate: rich and sweet but with structural tannins softening after bottle age; long, port-typical spirit warmth on the finish. A single-quinta declaration — not an LBV or Warre's general vintage — indicates this is from the best Douro estate in a year not declared as a general vintage.

## Technical specs
- Grape varieties: traditional Douro field blend — primarily Touriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz (Tempranillo), Tinta Cão, Tinta Barroca (Source needed — specific Cavadinha blend confirmation)
- Vinification: lagares (foot-treading) or autovinification for extraction; fortified with grape spirit (aguardente) to arrest fermentation at ~20% ABV
- Elevage: maximum 2.5 years in wood before bottling per regulations; then long bottle aging
- ABV: 20% / RS: ~90–100 g/L (typical VP range; specific Cavadinha 1998 — Source needed)
- Production: Warre's owned by Symington Family Estates; Quinta da Cavadinha is in the Pinhão Valley, Cima Corgo

## Vintage character (1998 Douro/Port)
1998 was not a general vintage declaration year for most major port houses (they declared 1997, and next general declarations were 2000 for some, 2003/2007/2011 for others). Single quinta releases occur in "off" years from individual estates. The 1998 Douro growing season was warm with a dry August–September; wines showed good concentration and ripe tannins, giving single-quinta declarations of real quality. By 2015 the wine would be in full maturity with significant bottle bouquet. (Source needed — specific 1998 vintage Port report)

## Producer style
Warre's, established 1670, is one of the oldest British Port houses. Now owned by Symington Family Estates (alongside Dow's, Graham's, Smith Woodhouse). Quinta da Cavadinha in the Pinhão Valley is Warre's flagship estate for single-quinta releases; it is also used as a component in the general vintage Port blend when declared. The house style leans toward elegance and finesse vs. the more extracted Dow's. Positioned at the upper-tier of Port with a long heritage. (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symington_Family_Estates)
