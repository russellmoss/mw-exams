# Blaufränkisch, Moric, 2011

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with sour cherry, blackberry skin, pepper, dried herbs, and an iron-like mineral note. The palate is dry and tensile, with high acidity, fine but grippy tannin, and a cool, savoury finish that avoids the plushness of many international styles. Blind, this is a strong Central European red signal: a variety-driven wine where acid, spice, and mineral line matter as much as fruit.

## Vintage character (2011 Burgenland)
2011 was widely regarded as a warm, successful Austrian red vintage, giving ripe fruit while retaining enough structure for serious Blaufränkisch. In Burgenland the best wines combined dark fruit concentration with the variety's naturally vivid acidity and peppery savour. That balance is exactly what makes Moric a useful exam reference for the grape.

## Producer style
Moric, led by Roland Velich, is one of the benchmark producers for fine Blaufränkisch in Austria and one of the estates most responsible for elevating the grape's international reputation. The wines aim for precision, low cosmetic gloss, and a Burgundian sense of site expression rather than simple fruit weight. Even the regional Blaufränkisch is valued for tension, savoury complexity, and ageworthy shape.

## Why it's in this exam
Included in 2016 Paper 2 Question 5 as one of three variety-origin benchmarks. It tests whether the candidate can identify Blaufränkisch from its acid-tannin profile and place it convincingly in Burgenland rather than misreading it as a cooler-climate international variety.
