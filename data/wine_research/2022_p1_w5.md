# Marsanne Museum Release, Tahbilk, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
This is an age-marked Australian Marsanne, so the key blind cue is tertiary development without much obvious oak: beeswax, toast, lanolin, preserved lemon and nutty complexity over moderate body and soft but still useful acidity. It should feel mature and savory rather than youthful and aromatic.

## Vintage character (2014 Nagambie Lakes)
2014 in southeastern Australia was generally warm and capable of yielding ripe, early-accessible whites. In Tahbilk Marsanne, however, the exam interest lies less in primary vintage fruit and more in how bottle age transforms a neutral white into something waxy, toasty and distinctively mature.

## Producer style
Tahbilk is one of Australia's historic family estates and is particularly associated with old-vine Marsanne and deliberate museum-release programs. The house style favors varietal transparency and age development, which is why mature Tahbilk Marsanne can be a classic blind-tasting curveball.
