# Semillon, Vat 1, Tyrrell's, 2002

## Quick tasting profile
Classic aged Hunter Semillon: low alcohol, dry, initially understated aromatics that broaden into toast, lanolin, lemon curd and wax with bottle age. The palate should be light in body but intense in flavor, driven by acidity and tertiary complexity rather than oak or weight.

## Vintage character (2002 Hunter Valley)
Hunter Semillon’s style is shaped as much by method and bottle development as by the growing season. By 2011 the 2002 would already have entered its classic mature phase, with tertiary toast and wax emerging while the wine remained bone-dry and low in alcohol.

## Producer style
Tyrrell’s Vat 1 is one of Australia’s benchmark dry white wines and one of the clearest reference points for mature Hunter Valley Semillon. The wine is famous for being almost neutral in youth and gaining complexity through extended bottle age without oak influence.

## Why it's in this exam
In the mixed-bag Question 3 this is a high-value trap wine. Candidates who understand mature Hunter Semillon can score heavily on variety, country, region and quality even though the wine’s tertiary profile can initially seem atypical for a light-bodied dry white.
