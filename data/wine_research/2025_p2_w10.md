# Barbera d'Alba Trevie, Vietti, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby with a youthful purple edge. In blind tasting this should read as ripe, polished Barbera: red and black cherry, plum, violet, vanilla and a little leather, driven by bright acidity but with more body and oak sweetness than a simple trattoria style (source: https://www.vietti.com/en/wines/barbera-dalba-trevie; https://www.dallaterra.com/pdf/2022-tv-d-alba-vin-92.pdf). The palate should feel succulent and fruit-forward, with sweet tannin and enough freshness to stop the 14.5% ABV from becoming heavy.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Barbera (source: https://www.vietti.com/en/wines/barbera-dalba-trevie)
- Vineyard: Fruit from Castiglione Falletto, Monforte d'Alba and La Morra (source: https://www.vietti.com/en/wines/barbera-dalba-trevie)
- Soils: Limestone clay-rich soils (source: https://www.vietti.com/en/wines/barbera-dalba-trevie)
- Vinification: About two weeks alcoholic fermentation in stainless steel with daily pump-overs; malolactic in steel (source: https://www.vietti.com/en/wines/barbera-dalba-trevie)
- Elevage: About one year in a mix of stainless steel, barriques and casks; Dalla Terra specifies Slavonian casks, French barriques and stainless steel, plus four months in bottle (source: https://www.vietti.com/en/wines/barbera-dalba-trevie; https://dallaterra.com/tech-sheet/vietti-trevie-barbera-d-alba-doc)
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.5% / 6 g/L / 29 g/L extract; total acidity value not fully legible in the cited sheet, so TA remains Source needed (source: https://dallaterra.com/tech-sheet/vietti-trevie-barbera-d-alba-doc)

## Vintage character (2022 Piedmont)
Decanter's 2022 Piedmont reporting describes a drought-stricken, relentlessly hot season that nevertheless produced some surprisingly successful wines with round shapes and managed freshness in the better examples (source: https://www.decanter.com/wine/piedmont-wine-region/barolo-2022-our-guide-to-the-against-all-odds-vintage). That lines up with this wine's opulent fruit and generous texture, but also explains why acidity remains crucial to balance.

## Producer style
Vietti is one of Piedmont's reference names, founded in 1873 and now owned by the Krause family, with Eugenio Palumbo as winemaker (source: https://dallaterra.com/tech-sheet/vietti-trevie-barbera-d-alba-doc). Even in an entry-level DOC wine, the style is precise and layered rather than rustic: organic farming, multiple village sources, and mixed-elevage complexity built for global premium retail rather than simple local consumption (source: https://www.vietti.com/en/wines/barbera-dalba-trevie).

## Why it's in this exam
This sits in 2025 Paper 2, Question 3, where each wine is a different European variety strongly tied to origin. It is a useful examiner pick because it gives a classic Barbera d'Alba signature: high acidity, juicy cherry-plum fruit, moderate tannin, polished oak and commercial appeal at a high-quality but not icon-wine level.
