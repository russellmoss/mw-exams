# Riesling, Kappelweg de Rorschwihr, Rolly Gassmann, 2002

## Quick tasting profile
This should show mature Alsace Riesling rather than youthful Germanic austerity: wax, citrus oil, smoke and dried herb complexity over a broad, dry palate. Expect tertiary development and texture, with acidity still present but integrated into a richer frame.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Kappelweg lieu-dit in Rorschwihr
- Soils: Calcareous marl according to merchant and regional references
- Vinification: Traditional still white vinification; no strong evidence of new-oak influence

## Vintage character (2002 Alsace)
2002 was not a simple blockbuster year, so a mature bottle from a serious producer is likely to show bottle development more than raw power. In exam terms, that makes the wine useful for testing mature Riesling recognition outside the Mosel or Wachau frame.

## Producer style
Rolly Gassmann is known for powerful, site-specific Alsace wines with substantial extract and aging capacity. The domaine farms a wide spread of named terroirs around Rorschwihr, and Kappelweg is one of its established Riesling sites, associated with calcareous marl and a broad, layered expression.

## Why it's in this exam
This is the structural center of the first-question Alsace trio. It asks whether the candidate can identify Alsace through mature dry Riesling and then calibrate quality and age correctly against a much more overt Muscat and a sweet late-harvest Pinot Gris.
