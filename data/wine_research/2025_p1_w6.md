# Chardonnay, Moss Wood, 2023

## Quick tasting profile
Pale golden straw. Complex nose of ripe white peach, nectarine, lemon cheesecake, and fresh vanilla bean, with secondary notes of nougat, toasted oats, cashew, and shortbread. The palate is intense yet fresh, showing nashi pear, lime, white peach, and rock melon fruit, with creamy shortbread and roasted nut undertones from oak. Firm but integrated acidity; full-bodied with a long, textural finish. Under blind conditions, this reads as premium, warm-climate Chardonnay with generous new French oak and full MLF -- classic Margaret River markers.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay (predominantly Gingin clone, ~90% of plantings; supplemented by Dijon clones)
- Vineyard: Moss Wood estate, Wilyabrup; oldest Chardonnay vines planted 1976 (48 years old at harvest); dry-farmed
- Soils: Gravelly loam over clay (characteristic Wilyabrup terroir)
- Harvest: 7 March 2023, at 12.8 Baume; 120 days from flowering to harvest
- Yield: 5.89 t/ha (11% below estate average)
- Vinification: Hand-picked, hand-sorted on vibrating table; whole-bunch pneumatic membrane press; flotation clarification in stainless steel; primary fermentation initiated in tank with multiple yeast strains, then transferred to 228L French oak barriques mid-ferment; co-inoculated for MLF
- Elevage: 18 months in French oak barriques (49% new, 51% second/third use); medium-toasted Troncais and Allier oak; on lees without stirring
- ABV: 14.0% / RS: Source needed / TA: Source needed
- Bottled: 4 July 2024; released 29 August 2024

## Vintage character (2023 Margaret River)
2023 is being called a potential "Goldilocks vintage" for Margaret River Chardonnay -- Decanter asked whether it might be the greatest Margaret River Chardonnay vintage yet. A cold winter delayed budburst by 2-3 weeks; spring was drier and cooler than average, extending flowering for Chardonnay. The growing season was moderate throughout (season avg 19.39C at Moss Wood), with no significant disease pressure or heat events. Extended, gentle ripening produced wines of exceptional balance: intense, full-flavoured Chardonnays with weight, length, and complexity but retaining freshness and acidity. (source: Decanter, Margaret River Wine Association vintage reports)

## Producer style
Moss Wood is the second-oldest estate in Margaret River (planted 1969, first vintage 1973). Keith Mugford has been winemaker since 1979 and owner since 1985. The estate is classified "Exceptional" in Langton's Classification -- one of only 22 Australian producers at that tier. Moss Wood's Chardonnay has won major accolades including the James Halliday Best Chardonnay Award (2017 vintage, 99/100). The house style for Chardonnay is richly textured and generous, with full MLF, significant new French oak, and extended lees ageing, yet balanced by the natural acidity of the Wilyabrup site. Average retail ~AUD $58, placing it firmly in the premium/ultra-premium tier alongside Leeuwin Art Series and Pierro. The estate also produces benchmark Cabernet Sauvignon (flagship), Semillon, and Pinot Noir. (source: mosswood.com.au, wine-searcher.com)

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 6 is the fourth of four wines in Question 2 (2025, Paper 1), which states: "Wines 3-6 are from the same single grape variety." The question asks candidates to identify Chardonnay (16 marks), then for each wine comment on style, quality, and maturity (4 x 13 marks), and identify origin as closely as possible (4 x 8 marks). The flight spans Chablis Premier Cru 2010, Monterey California 2019, Elgin South Africa 2022, and this Margaret River 2023 -- a classic IMW spread testing candidates' ability to distinguish warm-climate, oak-generous New World Chardonnay (Moss Wood) from cool-climate, unoaked or lightly oaked styles. Moss Wood represents the premium Australian benchmark: full-bodied, 14% ABV, full MLF, generous new French oak, ripe fruit profile. Its pairing with 2022_p1_w4 (Pierro Vineyards, also Margaret River Chardonnay) in the 2022 exam corpus shows the IMW's recurring interest in Margaret River Chardonnay as a variety+region identification test.
