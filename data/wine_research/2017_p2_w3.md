# Cabernet Sauvignon, Silver Oak, 2012. Alexander Valley, California, USA

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-garnet. Nose of ripe blackcurrant, cassis, black cherry, vanilla, dill, and sweet coconut — the unmistakable signature of extended American oak elevage. Full-bodied with plush, velvety tannins and ripe acidity; mouth-filling, lush texture. Long finish with sweet oak spice and dark fruit. The American oak style is highly distinctive under blind conditions, separating this from French-oak Napa or Bordeaux comparators.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Cabernet Sauvignon (Source needed for 2012 exact spec; small addition of Cabernet Franc possible)
- Vineyard: Estate and purchased fruit from Alexander Valley; warmer, more generous climate than Napa
- Soils: Alexander Valley alluvial and benchland soils; volcanic and sandy loam components
- Vinification: Traditional fermentation; extended maceration for tannin extraction; Silver Oak's signature extended elevage
- Elevage: 24 months in American oak (Silver Oak uses primarily American oak, a distinctive house decision); significant percentage new; further 15 months in bottle before release (Source: Silver Oak house style, knowledge-based)
- ABV: 14.2%

## Vintage character (2012 Alexander Valley / California)
2012 was an excellent California vintage — dry, warm, and even growing season. Harvest was early and orderly, producing concentrated, well-structured Cabernet with ripe tannins and bright acidity. Considered a benchmark modern California vintage with broad critical acclaim. Alexander Valley delivered generous, fruit-forward wines with depth.

## Producer style
Silver Oak was founded in 1972 by Justin Meyer and Raymond Duncan specifically to produce American oak-aged Cabernet Sauvignon; this focus on American oak is the house's single most defining characteristic. The estate produces two tiers: Alexander Valley (AV, more accessible; around USD 100) and Napa Valley (more structured, around USD 165). The American oak treatment — using Barrel Select American oak — gives wines their signature vanilla/dill/coconut character, a deliberate stylistic choice that invites comparison to premium Rioja. Silver Oak is a Napa/Sonoma heritage brand with strong direct-to-consumer and US restaurant presence.
