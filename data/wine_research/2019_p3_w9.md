# Manzanilla Papirusa, Lustau, NV. Sanlúcar de Barrameda, Spain

## Quick tasting profile
Brilliant pale straw/lemon-gold with greenish highlights. Nose is distinctive and unmistakable: camomile, sea breeze, green apple, fresh hay, fennel and saline minerality — the hallmark of manzanilla's flor-aged character from Sanlúcar (source: Lustau). Palate is completely dry, light-bodied, fresh, crisp and piercingly clean with a saline, slightly bitter finish. The Atlantic humidity of Sanlúcar creates thicker flor and a fresher, more delicate style than Jerez fino.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Palomino Fino (source: Lustau tech sheet and Wikipedia Manzanilla)
- Vineyard: Palomino sourced from the chalky albariza soils around Sanlúcar de Barrameda
- Soils: Albariza (high-chalk marine sediment) — the definitive Sherry/Manzanilla terroir
- Vinification: Fermented to dry; fortified to approximately 15% ABV; aged entirely under flor yeast in American oak butts
- Elevage: Solera system, average 5 years ageing (source: Lustau tech sheet); Sanlúcar's cool, humid Atlantic microclimate (proximity to Guadalquivir estuary) maintains thick flor year-round, producing lighter, fresher profile than Jerez
- ABV / RS / TA: 15% / bone dry (no residual sugar) / Source needed

## Vintage character (NV — Solera)
Non-vintage solera blend. No single vintage character; style reflects consistent Sanlúcar flor-ageing character. Papirusa name reportedly from a doll founder Emilio Lustau gifted, meaning "beautiful and elegant" (source: Lustau producer page).

## Producer style
Emilio Lustau was founded in 1896 (source: Wikipedia Lustau), now based in Jerez with operations in Sanlúcar de Barrameda. Famous for their "Almacenista" range — single-bodega specialist sherries. Grupo Caballero acquired Lustau in 1990. Lustau is considered one of the top quality sherry producers, respected for breadth of styles (Manzanilla, Fino, Amontillado, Oloroso, PX, Almacenista). Market position: premium quality at accessible price ($15–25).

## Why it's in this exam
Manzanilla Papirusa is the definitive blind tasting test for bone-dry fortified wine under flor. The exam challenge: identifying it as Manzanilla (vs Fino) from the sea-breeze, saline, camomile character; locating it in Sanlúcar vs Jerez; and distinguishing it from other pale dry styles. The 15% ABV, pale colour, and bio-ageing character are non-negotiable identification markers.
