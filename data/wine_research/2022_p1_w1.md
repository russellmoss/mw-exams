# Meursault Les Chevalieres, Domaine Xavier Monnot, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Expect a serious Meursault rather than a broad caricature: ripe citrus, orchard fruit, hazelnut and light buttered-oak notes over a stony, saline core. The palate should read medium-plus to full, with creamy texture from barrel work but enough tension to point clearly to higher-quality white Burgundy.

## Vintage character (2019 Burgundy)
2019 Burgundy produced ripe but not excessively heavy whites, with small berries, concentration and better freshness than the hottest recent vintages. In blind tasting that often means generous fruit and early charm, but with enough acid line and mineral cut to preserve typicity.

## Producer style
Xavier Monnot is a quality-focused Meursault producer whose whites are usually described in terms of purity, controlled oak and site definition rather than maximal richness. The estate works across village, premier cru and grand cru holdings, so a wine like Les Chevalieres functions as a high-class village benchmark with obvious Meursault texture but less scale than the Corton-Charlemagne from the same producer.
