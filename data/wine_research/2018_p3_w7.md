# Domaine de Chevalier Blanc, 2010. Pessac-Léognan, Bordeaux, France.

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold with amber tints at eight years. Nose: textbook Pessac-Léognan blanc — toasted hazelnut, lanolin, ripe grapefruit, beeswax, and smoke; by 2018, the secondary complexity of honey, figs, and custard is emerging. Palate: full-bodied (14% ABV), creamy textured from barrel fermentation and lees aging, balancing acidity, very long nutty-savoury finish. 2010 is a landmark vintage — the wine should be at or approaching peak.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 70% Sauvignon Blanc, 30% Sémillon (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domaine_de_Chevalier)
- Vineyard: 4.5 hectares of white vines in Léognan (within Pessac-Léognan); gravelly sand soils
- Vinification: Barrel fermented in French oak; bâtonnage
- Elevage: 18 months in French oak (high proportion new); extended lees contact
- ABV / RS / TA: 14% / dry / medium-high
*(~1,200 cases grand vin per year; ~800 cases L'Esprit de Chevalier second wine)*

## Vintage character (2010 Pessac-Léognan)
2010 was one of the greatest Bordeaux vintages in memory — particularly for whites. Cool, late harvest with exceptional balance of ripeness and acidity. Pessac-Léognan whites from 2010 are widely considered some of the finest produced in decades, combining concentration, freshness, and extraordinary aging potential. This wine at 14% ABV reflects the ripe vintage. (Source needed: 2010 Bordeaux vintage report specifically for whites)

## Producer style
Domaine de Chevalier is one of very few Bordeaux estates named "domaine" rather than "château" — a deliberate statement. Located in Léognan, classified in both 1953 and 1959 Classifications of Graves, it produces both red (approximately 15,000 cases) and a small production white (1,200 cases). The white is considered one of the greatest Bordeaux blancs, often compared favourably to Premier Cru Burgundy. Second white wine: L'Esprit de Chevalier. (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domaine_de_Chevalier)

## Why it's in this exam
Domaine de Chevalier Blanc 2010 tests whether the candidate can identify a top-quality, barrel-fermented, aged Pessac-Léognan white — a very different profile from Burgundy Chardonnay or Alsace. The high ABV (14%) is a key structural signal. The 70% Sauvignon/30% Sémillon blend creates a richer texture than straight Sauvignon. Paper 3's mixed format means this could be placed between sparkling and sweet wines — requiring the candidate to reset their palate and recognise a mature, complex dry white.
