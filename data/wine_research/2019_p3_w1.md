# Grand Brut, Perrier-Jouët, NV. Champagne, France

## Quick tasting profile
Fine, persistent mousse; silvery gold colour. Nose of floral aromas (white blossom), fresh yellow apple, pear, citrus zest, hints of brioche and vanilla. Palate is crisp, light and fresh with a generous, lightly rich finish; medium autolytic depth. A commercially polished, accessible Champagne — soft and approachable rather than intensely complex. Classic large-house NV style.

## Vintage character (NV — multi-vintage blend)
Non-vintage Champagne is blended to maintain house style consistency across harvests. Perrier-Jouët Grand Brut is designed for immediate approachability and consistent fresh fruit expression. No single vintage character applies.

## Producer style
Perrier-Jouët was founded in 1811, currently owned by Pernod Ricard (source: Wikipedia). Known for its Chardonnay-forward elegance and the iconic Belle Époque art nouveau bottle design (Emile Gallé anemone motif, 1902). Grand Brut is the entry point; Belle Époque Blanc de Blancs is the prestige cuvée. Style philosophy: fresh, floral, light — "the world's first lightly dosed brut" lineage (Cuvée K, 1846 — source: producer website). Production approximately 3 million bottles annually. Premier and Grand Cru vineyard focus.

Grand Brut NV represents the large-house commercial Champagne benchmark in Paper 3 — used in contrast to grower Champagne (Egly-Ouriet, wine 2) to illustrate industrial vs artisan production philosophy.
