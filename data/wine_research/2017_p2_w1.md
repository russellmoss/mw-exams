# Château Montrose, 1996. St Estèphe, Bordeaux, France

## Quick tasting profile
Deep garnet with mature brick-orange rim at 21 years old. Nose of cedar, cigar box, graphite, dried blackcurrant, iron-mineral, and earthy undergrowth with secondary evolution. Full-bodied palate, high but increasingly resolved tannins (St Estèphe's characteristic grip), firm acidity (1996 vintage hallmark), long savory finish with tobacco and dried fruit. A wine of austerity rather than opulence — classic Médoc structure in a cool-climate, Cabernet-dominant style.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Cabernet Sauvignon dominant (~65%), Merlot (~25%), Cabernet Franc and Petit Verdot (~10%) (Source needed for 1996 exact blend)
- Vineyard: St Estèphe plateau, clay-limestone soils over bedrock; estate 95 ha planted
- Soils: Clay-limestone with significant clay content (differentiates from Pauillac's gravel); contributes to firmer tannins
- Vinification: Traditional vat fermentation, pump-overs and maceration; classic Médoc protocol
- Elevage: 18–24 months in French oak barriques, significant proportion new oak (Source needed for 1996 exact % new oak)
- ABV: 12.5% (stated on label — notably low for modern Montrose; reflects 1996 cool season)

## Vintage character (1996 Bordeaux)
1996 is a great Médoc vintage with exceptional results on the Left Bank. A warm August followed by a cool, wet September (saving grace of Cabernet-dominant estates) produced wines with very high acidity and firm tannins, particularly in St Estèphe, Pauillac, and St Julien. The Right Bank fared less well due to Merlot struggling in September rain. The wines are structured for very long ageing; at 21 years (as presented in 2017) they were at peak maturity for top estates but still showing austere backbone. Cabernet-dominant châteaux were the clear winners.

## Producer style
Château Montrose is a 2ème Grand Cru Classé estate in St Estèphe, purchased in 2006 by brothers Martin and Olivier Bouygues (of the Bouygues construction/telecom group) who invested significantly in cellar renovation. The estate's historic reputation rests on powerful, long-lived wines that demand extended cellaring — sometimes described as "the Latour of St Estèphe." The estate produces three tiers: the Grand Vin, La Dame de Montrose (second wine), and Tertio de Montrose (third wine). Under Bouygues ownership, the wines have become more polished without sacrificing the estate's structural signature. The 1996 predates the Bouygues era (owned by Jean-Louis Charmolüe until 2006).

## Why it's in this exam
Presented as the aged, classic Left Bank Bordeaux benchmark in P2 — a heavily-aged (21 years), structured red demanding vintage knowledge and the ability to identify mature Cabernet-based Bordeaux from a specific appellation. Its low ABV (12.5%) and austere 1996 structure would be a key identification signal versus modern, riper examples.
