# Amarone della Valpolicella, Torre del Falasco, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Deep garnet-ruby with slight browning rim; intensely concentrated nose of dried cherry, raisin, fig, dark chocolate, tobacco, leather and dried herbs — unmistakably appassimento-derived. Full-bodied (16% ABV), technically dry but with the perception of weight from the glycerol and concentration; velvety, grippy tannins; very long, warming finish with slight bitter-almond note (amarone = "great bitter"). One of Italy's most distinctive and powerful red wines.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Corvina Veronese (45-95%), Rondinella (5-30%), may include Molinara or Oseleta (DOCG rules)
- Vineyard: Valpolicella DOC zone, Verona province; limestone, clay and volcanic soils; hillside vine plots
- Vinification: Appassimento — grapes harvested early October, dried on bamboo racks or wooden crates for 3-4 months, losing 35-45% weight; fermentation then lasts up to 50 days
- Elevage: Minimum 2 years in large oak (Slavonian botti or French tonneaux) before release; often longer
- ABV / RS / TA: 16% / dry (legally, RS must be <12 g/L for Amarone) / medium acidity
- DOCG granted December 2009 (Source: Wikipedia)

## Vintage character (2015 Valpolicella)
2015 was an excellent vintage in Veneto — warm, dry summer with ideal harvest conditions for full ripening. The appassimento grapes of 2015 showed exceptional concentration and aromatic intensity, producing Amarones of great richness and deep colour. Lower disease pressure meant excellent fruit quality going into the drying lofts. 2015 is regarded as a very good year for Amarone.
