# Clos Windsbuhl Pinot Gris, Domaine Zind-Humbrecht, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold, almost viscous in appearance; one of Alsace's most complex Pinot Gris expressions. Nose: white peach, quince, smoked bacon fat (characteristic Pinot Gris), honey, ginger, and the distinctive waxy-unctuous register of biodynamic Zind-Humbrecht. Palate: rich, full-bodied, broad, with moderate residual sugar (off-dry to medium) despite the 14.5% ABV — the residual sugar/alcohol interplay creates complexity. Long, complex finish. The Clos Windsbuhl monopole is 5.2 ha in Hunawihr.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Gris
- Vineyard: Clos Windsbuhl, Hunawihr, Alsace — 5.2 ha (13 acres) (confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zind-Humbrecht); a walled monopole (clos) site, not Grand Cru but considered Grand Cru equivalent by producers; calcareous and clay-limestone soils
- Vinification: biodynamic farming; fermented in large old oak foudres; no temperature control in the traditional Alsatian manner; natural yeasts; variable residual sugar reflecting vintage conditions — a deliberate Zind-Humbrecht philosophy
- ABV: 14.5% / RS: variable per vintage — likely 15–30 g/L for 2013 (off-dry; exact — Source needed) / TA: moderate
- Low yields: 30–40 hL/ha typical (confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zind-Humbrecht)
- Olivier Humbrecht MW is one of only a few French MWs; biodynamic pioneer in Alsace

## Vintage character (2013 Alsace)
2013 was a cool, late vintage in Alsace — small crop but good quality. Pinot Gris from 2013 showed good acidity and structure without the high residual sugar levels of warmer years. The Clos Windsbuhl from 2013 is likely slightly leaner and more mineral than the 2012 or 2010 (hot years). At 3 years in 2016, it would be integrating well. ## Producer style
Domaine Zind-Humbrecht established 1959 by Léonard Humbrecht marrying Geneviève Zind; Humbrecht family winemaking since 1620. Olivier Humbrecht (son) is the first French MW and manages the domaine. Biodynamic certified; low yields; no temperature control; natural yeasts; variable RS by vintage. Top Alsace producer; international recognition. Multiple Grand Cru and lieu-dit wines (Grand Cru: Hengst, Brand, Rangen de Thann). Clos Windsbuhl is a non-GC monopole of equivalent prestige. (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zind-Humbrecht)

## Why it's in this exam

The Alsace version should read as: rich, full-bodied, off-dry, high ABV (14.5%), complex — clearly superior quality and weight vs the Willamette Valley Ponzi at 13.1%.
