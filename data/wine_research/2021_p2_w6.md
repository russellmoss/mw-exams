# Grenache The Custodian, d'Arenberg, 2016

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with bright red plum, raspberry, dried herb and spice aromas. The palate is dry, medium-bodied and supple, with savoury red fruit, moderate tannin and a distinctly earthy McLaren Vale Grenache personality. This is not blockbuster Shiraz; it is more fragrant and relaxed in structure.

## Vintage character (2016 McLaren Vale)
The 2016 wine notes describe the vintage as strong for Grenache, giving both bright fruit and the darker, earthier complexity that good McLaren Vale can achieve. That supports a wine with generosity but not excessive heaviness.

## Producer style
d'Arenberg under Chester Osborn is known for distinctive, traditionally handled McLaren Vale wines that often favour old oak, texture and personality over glossy international polish. The Custodian sits as a regional Grenache expression with clear commercial appeal but enough savoury detail for blind tasting interest.
