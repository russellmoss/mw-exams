# Semillon, Lovedale, Mount Pleasant, 2011. Hunter Valley, Australia.

## Quick tasting profile
By 2018 (seven years old), this wine is deep gold with amber hints. Nose: honeyed toast, beeswax, lanolin, and lime curd — classic aged Hunter Semillon development. No oak interference. Palate: lean but textured, laser-sharp acidity (notably high), savory and complex secondary characters, very long toasty finish. The 10% ABV is a key blind tasting signal — unusually low for a dry white, suggesting either residual sugar (ruled out by bone-dry character) or a cool-climate or particular terroir/style choice. Hunter Valley Semillon in this style.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Semillon
- Vineyard: Lovedale Vineyard, Hunter Valley — one of Mount Pleasant's four estate sites; sandy loam soils
- Vinification: Unoaked; stainless steel fermented; early harvest (to preserve acidity and low alcohol); no malolactic fermentation
- Elevage: No oak; bottle aging only — designed for long cellaring
- ABV / RS / TA: 10% / bone dry / high (6.8-7.4 g/L typical for Hunter Semillon)
*(Mount Pleasant describes Lovedale as "the finest single vineyard semillon in the world" per critic Matthew Jukes)*

## Vintage character (2011 Hunter Valley)
2011 was a wet, cool La Niña year in Hunter Valley — significant rainfall during growing season. This produced lower alcohol naturally (consistent with the 10% ABV), high acidity, and leaner fruit profiles. Wines were slow to develop but are now (2018) in a compelling middle phase of evolution: honeyed complexity without loss of freshness. (Source needed: independent 2011 Hunter Valley vintage report)

## Producer style
Mount Pleasant was established in 1921 by legendary winemaker Maurice O'Shea, a pivotal figure in championing Australian dry table wines. The estate manages four vineyard sites: Lovedale, Rosehill, Old Hill, and Old Paddock. Lovedale Semillon is their most acclaimed wine — a benchmark of the Hunter Valley style. The estate is now part of the McWilliams Wines group but maintains its identity and single-vineyard approach. The wines are priced in the $30-60 AUD range and are designed for 20+ years of cellaring. (source: mountpleasantwines.com.au; en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hunter_Valley_wine)

## Why it's in this exam
The Lovedale Semillon is a classic MW curveball: a bone-dry, unoaked, single-variety Australian white at an unusually low ABV (10%) with developed secondary characters by exam date. Requires the candidate to identify Hunter Valley Semillon's distinctive low-alcohol, high-acid, aged profile. The 2011 vintage (La Niña/wet year) reinforces the low ABV. Challenges any candidate relying on alcohol as a warm-climate Australia proxy.
