# Vin Jaune, Domaine Daniel Dugois, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold to amber colour with a greeny-gold rim; characteristic voile-derived nose of walnut, curry (fenugreek), dried apricot, beeswax and toasted hazelnuts with a saline, nutty intensity similar to dry fino but without fortification. Full-bodied on the palate with high alcohol (14.5%), racy acidity, pronounced oxidative nutty character and a very long, dry, umami-laden finish. Structurally and aromatically distinctive — no other French appellation produces anything quite like this.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Savagnin
- Vineyard: Arbois sub-appellation of the Jura; limestone and blue marl soils
- Vinification: Fermented dry in old oak barrels, then transferred to small 228 L unsealed ouillé casks where the voile (flor-like film yeast) develops
- Elevage: Minimum 6 years and 3 months under voile before bottling; no topping up; wine reduces to ~62% of original volume through evaporation
- Bottled in the distinctive 62 cl clavelin
- ABV: 14.5% / RS: dry / TA: Source needed
*(The 6-year-3-month aging requirement is legally mandated for all Vin Jaune; bottling therefore no earlier than early 2020 for this 2013 vintage.)*

## Vintage character (2013 Jura)
2013 was a cool, challenging vintage across much of France with late ripening and disease pressure; yields were reduced. In the Jura, Savagnin's naturally high acidity was even more pronounced in 2013, likely yielding a wine with exceptional freshness and longevity under the voile. The resulting Vin Jaune would typically show more austere, saline qualities than warmer years. Source needed for specific Arbois 2013 harvest data.

## Producer style
Domaine Daniel Dugois is a small family domaine in Les Arsures, Arbois, operated by the Dugois family for several generations. The estate is known for traditional-method Jura winemaking including Vin Jaune and Trousseau. Their Vin Jaune represents the pinnacle of Jura oxidative white winemaking — aged under voile with minimal intervention, producing wines of striking individuality. ## Why it's in this exam
Vin Jaune from Arbois is the defining example of oxidative white wine in Paper 1 (white still wines) — its distinctive voile-driven character, mandatory clavelin bottling, and Savagnin grape make it unmistakable blind and serve as the archetype of intentionally oxidative dry white. Used to test candidate knowledge of Jura's unique wine laws and the distinction between flor-aged Jura wines and fino Sherry.
