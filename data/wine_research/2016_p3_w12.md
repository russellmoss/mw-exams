# Recioto della Valpolicella, Corte Sant'Alda, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Opaque ruby-garnet with black cherry compote, dried fig, cocoa, spice, and a lifted dried-flower note. The palate is sweet and full-bodied, with dense fruit, significant alcohol, and enough acidity to keep the finish from becoming heavy. Blind, the sweet red profile, dried-grape concentration, and bitter-cherry edge point clearly to Recioto della Valpolicella rather than Port or Banyuls.

## Vintage character (2013 Veneto)
2013 was generally cooler and more classical than the warmest recent northern Italian vintages, which can help preserve freshness in dried-grape wines. In Recioto that matters because acid and bitterness are essential to balancing the sweetness and dried-fruit concentration. Source needed for a citable regional 2013 vintage report.

## Producer style
Corte Sant'Alda brings artisanal discipline and a strong sustainable reputation to Valpolicella's traditional categories. The wines are typically more precise and less caricatured than many commercial sweet reds, with attention to freshness as well as concentration. That makes the Recioto especially useful in an exam context: it demonstrates the style at a serious quality level.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 4 with the producer's Ripasso. It tests whether the candidate can explain appassimento and contrast two different outcomes from the same regional tradition: dry, enriched Ripasso versus sweet, unfortified Recioto.
