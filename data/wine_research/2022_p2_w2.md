# Cabernet Sauvignon, Double Diamond, Schrader Cellars, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
This is the richest and most overtly Napa wine in Question 1: cassis, plum, sweet bay, mocha and polished oak over dense tannins and full body. It should feel broader, darker and more plush than the Chinon, with riper alcohol and more obvious new-oak sweetness.

## Vintage character (2018 Napa Valley)
2018 is widely regarded as an excellent, more even Napa vintage, with long hang time and ripe tannins without the extremes of hotter years. That usually yields Cabernet with layered fruit, structure and comparatively classical balance by modern Napa standards.

## Producer style
Double Diamond is Schrader's second label, positioned below the flagship Schrader wines but made with serious Oakville sourcing and luxury-Napa polish. The brand is explicitly framed around quality-to-price value within high-end Cabernet, which matters for discussing commercial position in an exam setting.
