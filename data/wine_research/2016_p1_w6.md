# Pinot Gris, Ponzi Vineyards, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium gold; a delicate, dry expression of Pinot Gris in the Alsatian-inspired Willamette Valley style. Nose: fresh pear, white stone fruit, light melon, subtle smoky/spicy notes. Palate: medium-bodied, dry, refreshing acidity from the cool Willamette Valley climate; moderate weight — markedly lighter and less complex than the Zind-Humbrecht Clos Windsbuhl (w5) tasted alongside it. Clean, easy-drinking profile. The 13.1% ABV signals coolness and restraint relative to 14.5% Alsace.

2014 was an exceptional vintage in Willamette Valley — warm, dry summer and autumn producing excellent ripeness while retaining the region's signature acidity. For Pinot Gris at the estate level, 2014 delivered good body with clean fruit and sound structure. At 2 years in 2016, drinking in its primary freshness window. ## Producer style
Ponzi Vineyards established 1970 by Dick and Nancy Ponzi; one of Oregon's founding Pinot Noir producers alongside Eyrie Vineyards and Adelsheim. Luisa Ponzi (daughter) has been head winemaker for decades. Chehalem Mountains estate. Pinot Noir is the flagship; Pinot Gris is the lead white variety. Mid-to-premium tier; well-established Oregon name. The Pinot Gris style leans toward fresh, dry, light-to-medium body — different from the Alsatian interpretation of the same grape.
