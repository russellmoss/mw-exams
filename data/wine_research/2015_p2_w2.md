# Pinot Noir, Greywacke, 2012. Marlborough, New Zealand

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby, relatively clear. Nose is expressive New World Pinot: dark cherry, plum, raspberry, floral violet notes, and a hint of forest floor. The palate shows ripe fruit, medium-plus body for the variety and region, supple tannins, and medium acidity. The 14% ABV signals a warmer-year ripeness profile; less austere than Burgundy but with genuine varietal and regional character.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: Sourced from Southern Valleys and/or Wairau Valley sub-regions of Marlborough; Greywacke farms multiple sites
- Soils: Stony alluvial soils in Wairau; clay-rich in Southern Valleys
- Vinification: Hand-harvested; cold soak; open fermenters with pigeage; yeast selection varies by Kevin Judd's approach (partial wild/ambient ferments in some years)

## Vintage character (2012 Marlborough)
2012 was a warm, dry vintage in Marlborough following a relatively cool and wet 2011. The season produced ripe, concentrated Pinot Noir with good colour depth and bold fruit character. Higher alcohol levels (reflected in the 14% ABV) were common. Wines tend to be more approachable on release than the cooler-vintage style. (Source needed for specific vintage report URL.)

## Producer style
Greywacke is the personal project of Kevin Judd, who was the founding winemaker at Cloudy Bay from 1985 to 2009. Established in 2009, Greywacke quickly established itself as one of Marlborough's premium quality producers. The project sources fruit from premium sites across Marlborough and focuses on Sauvignon Blanc (which made Judd's reputation) plus Pinot Noir, Pinot Gris, and Riesling. The Pinot Noir sits at mid-to-premium price tier for New Zealand. Judd's winemaking background is traditional, using minimal intervention approaches adapted to New Zealand conditions.

## Why it's in this exam
Placed in the reds paper to test identification of New World Pinot Noir from a premium New Zealand producer against Old World Burgundy Pinot Noir (wines 1 and 3). At 14% versus the Burgundy examples at 13-13.5%, the ABV differential is a diagnostic signal. Marlborough Pinot Noir at this quality tier is a standard MW benchmark for New World style comparison.
