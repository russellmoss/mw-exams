# Château de Fieuzal Blanc, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with green tints. Expressive nose of orange rind, honeysuckle, yellow citrus, lemon peel, and grapefruit with white peach undertones. Medium-bodied palate showing candied orange, white peach, and chalky mineral notes; bright, vibrant acidity with a tensile, saline finish. No malolactic fermentation gives the wine purity and cut. Drinking well young but built to age.

## Technical specs
- Grape varieties: 65% Sauvignon Blanc, 35% Sémillon (source: The Wine Cellar Insider)
- Vineyard: 10 hectares of white varieties on gravelly plateau in Pessac-Léognan
- Soils: Gravel, sand, clay, and small white stones; vine density ~8,300 vines/ha
- Vinification: Fermentation in 50% new French oak barrels; no malolactic fermentation
- Élevage: 16–18 months in French oak barrels, on lees
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / dry / Source needed
- Closure: Diam 30 (source: Robert Parker)

## Vintage character (2021 Pessac-Léognan white)
A challenging growing season with devastating April–May frosts (down to -5C) and summer mildew outbreaks reduced yields significantly. However, an Indian summer from late August saved the vintage, and white grapes were harvested in excellent condition from late August to early September. The cooler overall season proved ideal for whites — producing wines with vivid natural acidity, purity, and tension. Critics called 2021 the "best whites since 2017" in Pessac-Léognan, with several estates producing exceptional results. (source: The Wine Cellar Insider; The Drinks Business)

## Producer style
Château de Fieuzal is a historic Pessac-Léognan estate dating to the 16th century, classified as a Cru Classé de Graves in 1959 — but only for its red wine. The blanc, despite being unclassified, is widely regarded as the estate's "real draw" and one of the finest dry whites in the appellation. Acquired in 2001 by Irish financier Lochlann Quinn (former chairman of Allied Irish Banks), the property underwent a multi-million euro renovation completed in 2012, modernising the winery with 63 vats in wood and stainless steel. Consultant Hubert de Bouard (since 2006) and winemaker Stephen Carrier (since 2007) have driven measurable quality improvements. The white vineyard is planted to roughly 50/50 Sauvignon Blanc and Sémillon, though recent vintages lean Sauvignon-dominant. Average retail price ~$50–70, positioning it in the serious-but-accessible tier of classified Bordeaux. (source: The Wine Cellar Insider; Wine-Searcher)
