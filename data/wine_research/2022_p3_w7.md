# T Noble Late Harvest Chenin Blanc, Ken Forrester, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold to amber with botrytis richness; complex, concentrated nose of honey, apricot jam, orange marmalade, vanilla, beeswax, ginger and dried citrus peel — classic botrytis Chenin Blanc showing Sauternes-like complexity. Full-bodied (11.5% ABV — low for the sweetness level, indicating arrested fermentation and high RS), very sweet, luscious, but Chenin's natural acidity provides essential balance; rich, long, honeyed finish. The "T" stands for "The One" — Ken Forrester's top botrytised sweet wine expression.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc (noble rot/botrytis-affected)
- Vineyard: Stellenbosch; Ken Forrester's own vineyards on schist and granite soils
- Vinification: Hand-selected botrytis-affected grapes; gentle pressing; slow, cold fermentation arrested to preserve RS
- Elevage: Aged in new French oak barrels — the "T" range is Ken Forrester's premium tier with significant new oak influence
- ABV / RS / TA: 11.5% / very sweet (approx 150-250 g/L RS) / high acidity (Chenin's natural acidity balances sweetness)
- Production: Very small volumes typical for late harvest botrytis wines

## Vintage character (2019 Stellenbosch)
2019 in Stellenbosch was generally a good vintage, with warm, dry conditions supporting late harvesting and botrytis development in selected parcels. For Noble Late Harvest production, optimal conditions require a combination of morning mist (for botrytis development) and warm afternoon sun (for concentration). ## Producer style
Ken Forrester, established 1993 in Stellenbosch, is one of South Africa's most important Chenin Blanc champions and a founder of the Old Vine Project. The flagship "T" (also called "The Gypsy" in some releases) Noble Late Harvest is among South Africa's finest sweet wines, made in tiny quantities from botrytis-selected Chenin from old vines.
