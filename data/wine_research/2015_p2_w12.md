# "Magnificat", Franciscan, 2011. Napa Valley, California

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple, concentrated colour. Nose shows Napa Meritage character: blackcurrant, blackberry, cedar, dark chocolate, tobacco, coffee, graphite — classic Cabernet-dominant Bordeaux blend. Full-bodied, structured tannins, medium-plus acidity, long and complex finish. The Meritage style prioritises Cabernet Sauvignon-driven structure over Merlot's plush generosity (contrast with w11). French oak dominates rather than American.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Bordeaux varieties blend (Meritage — Cabernet Sauvignon dominant, plus Merlot, Cabernet Franc, Malbec, Petit Verdot in various proportions; typical Magnificat composition is approximately 60-65% Cabernet Sauvignon, 20-25% Merlot, and balance of Franc/Malbec/Petit Verdot)
- Vineyard: Napa Valley AVA; Franciscan Estate is located in Rutherford AVA (Oakville-Rutherford border area, Highway 29 corridor)
- Soils: Deep, well-drained Bale clay loam and alluvial benchland soils typical of Rutherford/Oakville
- Vinification: Individual lot fermentation; temperature-controlled stainless steel; pump-overs; grape-by-grape selection

## Producer style
Franciscan Estate is a historic Napa Valley producer, founded 1973 in Rutherford, and now owned by Constellation Brands. Magnificat has been produced since 1985 as the estate's flagship red — one of the early Napa Meritage-style wines. The estate focuses on Napa Valley Cabernet Sauvignon and Meritage blends. Market position: premium commercial Napa ($50-70 retail for Magnificat); Franciscan is widely distributed in the US market. The winery also produces Magnificat in a style that has won consistent critical approval (Robert Parker scores consistently in the 88-92 range for the wine across vintages). The brand represents accessible premium Napa rather than cult-tier pricing.
