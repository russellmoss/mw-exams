# Albarino, Pazo de Villarei, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with bright peach, citrus zest, blossom, and sea-spray character. The palate is dry, zesty, and medium-light, with saline freshness and a slightly phenolic citrus-peel finish typical of Atlantic Albarino.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Albarino
- Vineyard: Val do Salnes, Rias Baixas
- Soils: Granitic, sandy coastal soils are a key regional cue
- Vinification: Stainless steel fermentation to preserve aromatics

## Vintage character (2015 Rias Baixas)
2015 was generally warm and successful in north-west Spain, giving ripe fruit without stripping the Atlantic edge from Albarino. The better wines balance fuller stone-fruit notes with the saline lift that keeps the variety distinctive.

## Producer style
Pazo de Villarei sits in the polished, varietally clear Rias Baixas camp: stainless-steel-led, fresh, and commercially accessible. The emphasis is on purity and maritime brightness rather than oak or oxidative texture.

## Why it's in this exam
This was part of the 2017 paper 1 set of European single varieties. It serves as a classic Albarino marker, particularly useful for testing Atlantic salinity, peachy fruit, and moderate weight against Italian and French aromatic whites.
