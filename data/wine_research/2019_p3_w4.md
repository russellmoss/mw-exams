# Prosecco, La Marca, NV. Veneto, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Very pale straw, light perlage. Nose of fresh pear, apple, light citrus and white flowers — clean, simple and fruit-forward, lacking the complexity of a premium millesimato. Palate is light, slightly sweet (Extra Dry or Dry range), with low acidity, a creamy sparkle and a short, clean finish. A straightforward, commercial NV Prosecco — designed for immediate consumption, aperitivo style.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Minimum 85% Glera; remainder other permitted varieties (source: Wikipedia Prosecco)
- Vineyard: Sourced across the broader Prosecco DOC zone (plains and lower hills of the Veneto/Friuli)
- Soils: Variable DOC zone, generally lower altitude than DOCG; alluvial plains and gentle slopes
- Vinification: Charmat/Martinotti method — secondary fermentation in stainless steel autoclave tanks; large-volume production; no oak
- Elevage: Minimal lees contact (faster production cycle than DOCG); no oak
- ABV / RS / TA: 11% / likely 15–20 g/L RS (Extra Dry style, Source needed for exact La Marca spec) / Source needed

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage blend; designed for consistency and immediate accessibility. No specific vintage character applicable.

## Producer style
La Marca is a large-volume commercial Prosecco brand owned by E&J Gallo (US), specifically targeting the American on-premise and off-trade market. It is one of the bestselling Prosecco brands in the United States. Style is deliberately accessible and crowd-pleasing: low ABV, slight sweetness, easy fruit. Market positioning: commodity/value tier versus premium DOCG producers. La Marca's quality positioning is deliberately below DOCG Valdobbiadene examples like Ruggeri.

## Why it's in this exam
La Marca NV DOC provides the quality contrast to Ruggeri Giustino B. DOCG in a Prosecco quality comparison. The key distinguishing factors: DOC vs DOCG, NV vs millesimato, lower complexity and simpler fruit profile, lower ABV (11% vs 11.5%), commercial brand versus estate producer.
