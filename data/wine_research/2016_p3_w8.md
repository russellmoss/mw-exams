# 10 Year Old Sercial, Henriques & Henriques, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Amber-gold with lifted volatile notes, citrus peel, roasted nuts, dried herbs, sea spray, and a smoky edge. The palate is dry and racy despite the oxidative concentration, with very high acidity carrying the wine through a long, tangy, savoury finish. Blind, the acid/salt/oxidative combination strongly signals Sercial Madeira rather than other fortified styles.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Sercial
- Vineyard: Madeira Island fruit
- Soils: Volcanic island soils are typical
- Vinification: Fermentation arrested by fortification
- Elevage: Aged using the traditional canteiro method in old oak casks
- ABV / RS / TA: 20.0% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (NV Madeira)
Vintage is subordinate to oxidative ageing regime and house blending. For a 10 Year Old Sercial, the key exam point is how canteiro ageing, fortification, and the naturally high acidity of the grape produce an apparently weightless but intensely persistent fortified wine. That structural profile is much more important than harvest-year discussion.

## Producer style
Henriques & Henriques is one of Madeira's best-known specialist houses and is especially respected for varietal, age-indicated bottlings. The style tends to show clarity, acidity, and typicity rather than sweet heaviness. This makes the 10 Year Old Sercial a very useful benchmark for dry Madeira in an MW tasting context.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 2 with two other fortified wines. It gives the candidate a clear chance to distinguish Madeira from Port and Sherry by its acid profile, maderisation, and canteiro ageing character.
