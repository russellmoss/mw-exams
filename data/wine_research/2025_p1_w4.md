# Chardonnay, Old Stage, 2019. Monterey, California, USA. (14%)

## Quick tasting profile
Medium gold in colour. Nose of citrus, green apple, white peach, and pear with subtle vanilla and butter notes from oak contact. Palate is medium- to full-bodied with a creamy texture, bright acidity balancing the ripe fruit, and a moderately long finish showing light minerality and oak spice. A solid, well-made New World Chardonnay that sits between lean cool-climate and opulent warm-climate expressions.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: 'Grava' estate vineyard, one of the most northerly plantings in Monterey County, on the eastern side of the Salinas Valley near the Gabilan Mountain Range

## Vintage character (2019 Monterey)
The 2019 California vintage was broadly successful. A wet spring raised mildew concerns but a long, temperate summer with few heat spikes followed, allowing extended hang-time. In Monterey, the marine influence of Monterey Bay kept temperatures moderate, and morning fog preserved acidity. Yields may have been slightly lower from spring rains. The resulting Chardonnays show good balance between ripe fruit and natural freshness — a classic cool-to-moderate California profile without the overripe character of hotter years. (source: Wine-Searcher 2019 California vintage report; Paso Robles Daily News harvest report)

## Producer style
Old Stage is the consumer label of Gabilan Wine Company, the sister company of Franscioni Farms — an 800-acre (290-hectare) vineyard operation owned and run by Tom and Rob Franscioni, fourth-generation farmers in the Salinas Valley. The family is one of the most significant vineyard owners in northern Monterey; they sell grapes to major producers but retain select parcels for their own wines. Consultant winemaker Dan Karlsen (40+ harvests; former winemaker at Talbott and Chalone) crafts the wines in a clean, fruit-forward, cool-climate style. The wines are positioned as premium value — UK retail around GBP 18-22, suggesting a mid-market California Chardonnay with above-average quality for the price. Distributed in the UK by Hallgarten & Novum Wines. (source: gabilanwine.com; aitkenwines.com; ratcliffe-co.com)

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 4 in 2025 Paper 1, Question 2: "Wines 3-6 are from the same single grape variety" — a four-wine Chardonnay flight. The question asks candidates to identify the variety (16 marks), comment on style/quality/maturity (4 x 13 marks), and identify origin as closely as possible (4 x 8 marks). Old Stage sits as the mid-tier New World representative alongside Chablis 1er Cru Defaix 2010 (aged Old World), Iona 2022 from Elgin (cool-climate New World), and Moss Wood 2023 from Margaret River (premium New World). It tests the candidate's ability to distinguish a cool-climate California Chardonnay (Monterey maritime influence, moderate oak, malolactic) from both the unoaked Burgundy and the more opulent Margaret River style. The relatively obscure producer name forces reliance on palate analysis rather than label recognition.
