# Sercial 10 Years Old, Henriques & Henriques, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Amber-gold. This should read as fortified, oxidative and dry rather than sweet: citrus peel, walnut, spice, smoke, saline notes and volatile lift, underpinned by sharp acidity and a long, savory finish (source: https://easterlywine.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/HHSERCIAL10YR-WEB.pdf; https://mentzendorff.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/henriques-and-henriques-10-year-old-sercial-madeira.pdf). In the glass it should feel tauter and more umami-driven than many candidates expect from Madeira.

## Vintage character (NV / category character)
This is non-vintage, so the key exam point is not a single harvest but the age-category and style. H&H describes the wine as a 10-year-old Sercial defined by extended canteiro ageing and the driest of the noble Madeira grapes, producing mouth-watering acidity and oxidative complexity rather than youthful fruit (source: https://easterlywine.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/HHSERCIAL10YR-WEB.pdf).

## Producer style
Henriques & Henriques is one of Madeira's most established houses, founded in 1850, notable for controlling much of its own vineyard supply and holding substantial old stocks (source: https://easterlywine.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/HHSERCIAL10YR-WEB.pdf). The style is traditional but quality-conscious: varietally pure bottlings, long cask ageing, and a house profile built on concentration and precision rather than sweetness alone.

## Why it's in this exam
This appears in 2025 Paper 3, Question 3, where classification and winemaking matter as much as origin. It is a strong examiner wine because candidates must identify Madeira, then distinguish a dry Sercial 10-year-old from richer Bual/Malmsey styles and explain canteiro ageing plus oxidative development.
