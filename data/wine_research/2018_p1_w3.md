# Red Shoulder Ranch Chardonnay, Shafer Vineyards, 2015. Napa Valley, California.

## Quick tasting profile
Deep golden yellow. Nose: generous pineapple, roasted hazelnut, crème brûlée, vanilla oak, and ripe apple — unmistakably New World Chardonnay with significant barrel influence. Palate: full-bodied, creamy texture from full malolactic fermentation, integrated toasty oak, low-medium acidity, long warm finish. At 14.9% ABV, this is firmly in the rich, opulent Californian style.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Red Shoulder Ranch, Carneros AVA — a cooler corner of Napa Valley influenced by San Pablo Bay fog and breezes; clay-loam soils
- Vinification: Barrel fermented in French oak; 100% malolactic fermentation; long lees aging with bâtonnage
- Elevage: Approximately 10-12 months in French oak (high proportion new)
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.9% / dry / moderate

## Vintage character (2015 Napa Valley)
2015 was a warm, drought-stressed vintage in Napa — one of the driest on record in California. Early harvest with concentrated, rich, high-alcohol fruit. Chardonnays show ripe tropical characters, low natural acidity, and full body. For Carneros, proximity to the Bay provided some moderating effect but yields were reduced. 2015 Napa Chardonnays required careful acidification to retain freshness. ## Producer style
Shafer Vineyards, founded 1972 by John Shafer on Stags Leap hillside vineyards, is best known for Hillside Select Cabernet Sauvignon. Red Shoulder Ranch Chardonnay is their white flagship, sourced from their estate in Carneros — a cool-climate site acquired specifically for Chardonnay and Pinot Noir. The wine is positioned as a premium, full-flavored Napa Chardonnay in the classic California butter-and-oak tradition, typically priced $45-55 at release. (source: shafervineyards.com)

## Why it's in this exam
Positioned as the premium New World Chardonnay against the GC Chablis benchmark (w2) and the commercial Yellow Tail (w1). At 14.9% ABV, the contrast with the 12.5% Chablis and 13% Yellow Tail is a deliberate structural signal. Tests recognition of Carneros/Napa Chardonnay hallmarks: oak integration, full malo, tropical-to-stone fruit, low acid relative to Old World.
