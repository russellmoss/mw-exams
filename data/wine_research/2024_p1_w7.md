# Moschofilero Wild Ferment, Gaia, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw to light gold. Highly aromatic, complex nose of rose, jasmine, orange blossom, pink grapefruit, lavender, and herb notes, with delicious buttery undertones from partial barrel fermentation. Richly flavoured palate with nerve and tension balancing the aromatic intensity. Characteristic sweet lemon and floral character with textural complexity from lees contact and wild fermentation. A fascinating Greek aromatic variety.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Moschofilero
- Vineyard: 25-year-old vines in Agiorgitika village, Mantineia plateau, 600-800m altitude
- Soils: Sandy-clay, south-westerly facing slopes
- Vinification: 48-hour pre-fermentation cold soak on skins; spontaneous wild yeast fermentation; 25% fermented at 28°C in barrels (1/3 French oak, 1/3 American oak, 1/3 French Acacia); 75% cold-fermented in stainless steel
- Élevage: 4 months on lees in stainless steel and wooden casks with frequent bâtonnage

## Producer style
Gaia Wines was founded in 1994 by Leon Karatsalos and winemaker Yiannis Paraskevopoulos (PhD, University of Bordeaux II). The estate operates two wineries — Santorini and Nemea (Peloponnese). Philosophy: exclusively Greek varieties, merging traditional methods with innovation. Paraskevopoulos pioneered wild yeast fermentation and alternative aging materials in Greek winemaking. The "Wild Ferment" designation signals the spontaneous fermentation approach. Gaia is one of Greece's most respected producers, responsible for elevating the international profile of varieties like Assyrtiko, Agiorgitiko, and Moschofilero.
