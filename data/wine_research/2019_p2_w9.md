# Pinot Noir, Maximin Grünhaus, 2015. Mosel, Germany

## Quick tasting profile
Pale-to-medium ruby, brilliant clarity with minimal tannin at rim. Nose of fresh red cherry, raspberry, cranberry, delicate floral notes and a distinctive mineral, slate-tinged quality from the Ruwer terroir. Palate is light-to-medium bodied with high, bright acidity, fine silky tannins and a pure, crystalline fruit expression. Minimal oak influence. Elegant, Burgundy-adjacent but distinctly German in its mineral tension and high acidity.

## Vintage character (2015 Germany/Mosel)
2015 was a warm, sunny year in Germany — one of the hottest on record — producing wines with unusually high natural ripeness. For Pinot Noir at Maximin Grünhaus, this means fuller fruit, slightly more body than typical, yet with the estate's characteristic mineral backbone from slate soils. The Ruwer's cool valley microclimate provided some relief from 2015's heat.

## Producer style
Maximin Grünhaus (Von Schubert estate) is one of Germany's most ancient and prestigious wine estates, with roots going back to the 9th century and continuous production under the Von Schubert family since 1882. Located on the Ruwer river, they are above all famous for their Riesling (Abtsberg, Herrenberg, Bruderberg). Their Pinot Noir is a secondary but serious offering from the same slate-terroir that produces their iconic Rieslings. The estate is considered a top-tier German producer at premium price points. ## Why it's in this exam
Maximin Grünhaus Pinot Noir is a genuine curveball: German Pinot Noir from the Mosel/Ruwer is extremely unusual context for a Paper 2 red. A candidate identifying this correctly must recognise Germany's Pinot Noir style — light body, high acidity, slate minerality — versus Burgundy's more structured expression. The 13.5% ABV from a warm year (2015) is a signal that this is a relatively full German Pinot.
