# Chateau Ducru-Beaucaillou, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby with cassis, cedar, graphite and cool herbal edges rather than plush opulence. The palate is dry, medium-bodied and classically Médocain, with fine but present tannin, fresh acidity and oak polish. In this vintage the wine should read restrained and earlier-drinking by Ducru standards.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Cabernet Sauvignon-dominant Bordeaux blend
- Vineyard: Saint-Julien estate parcels on Gunzian gravel
- Soils: Gravel, marl and large rounded stones over well-drained subsoils

## Vintage character (2013 Saint-Julien)
Decanter describes 2013 as a difficult Bordeaux vintage marked by wet conditions, uneven flowering and rot pressure, though parts of the Médoc performed better than expected. Saint-Julien was one of the steadier communes, which suits a wine showing freshness, definition and moderate scale rather than density.

## Producer style
Ducru-Beaucaillou is one of the emblematic "super seconds" of the Médoc, known for polished Cabernet-led Saint-Julien with strong oak framing and long ageing potential. The estate's gravel soils and exacting selection typically produce wines of perfume, structure and silk rather than brute force.

## Why it's in this exam
This is the lightest and least solar wine in the 2021 Paper 2 Question 1 Ducru vertical. It tests whether the candidate can identify Saint-Julien and discuss vintage effect, not just producer style in the abstract.
