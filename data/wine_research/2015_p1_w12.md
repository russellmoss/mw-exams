# Riesling, Cuvée des Comtes d'Eguisheim, Léon Beyer, 2007. Alsace, France

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold with amber hints at 8 years of age; complex, evolved nose of petrol/kerosene (classic Riesling TDN development), preserved lemon, beeswax, ginger and mineral slate. Palate is medium-full bodied with high natural acidity cutting through the mature, complex fruit — aged Alsace Riesling at its finest. Very dry (Léon Beyer is famously dry-style). Long, mineral finish with notes of apricot preserve and petroleum. Textbook aged, Grand Cru-sourced Alsace Riesling with excellent aging demonstration.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Cuvée des Comtes d'Eguisheim is Léon Beyer's flagship Riesling, sourced from Grand Cru parcels in Eguisheim (Eichberg and/or Pfersigberg Grand Crus — Léon Beyer is notable for not labelling wines as Grand Cru, preferring own cuvée names; Source needed: confirmed Grand Cru sourcing)
- Soils: Marl, clay and limestone (Eguisheim Grand Crus have heavy marl-clay over limestone — richer soils than northern Alsace granites, contributing to broader, more vinous Riesling style)
- Vinification: Fermentation in large old oak foudres or stainless steel; fully dry fermentation — Léon Beyer is one of Alsace's most committed dry-style producers; no new oak (Source needed: confirmed production method)
- Elevage: Extended aging in large old foudres; this is Léon Beyer's top Riesling and receives extended barrel aging — possibly 18–24 months (Source needed: confirmed elevage)
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.0% / dry (<4 g/L RS typical for Beyer style) / high acidity (Riesling's hallmark; Source needed for exact TA/RS)

## Vintage character (2007 Alsace)
2007 was a very good to excellent vintage in Alsace — warm and sunny through September with good natural ripeness. Riesling showed good aromatics and natural acidity, making the wines age-worthy. In 2015 (when tasted for the MW exam), 2007 Alsace Riesling would be 8 years old — showing classic petrol/mineral development (TDN development is one of Riesling's most reliable ageing markers). For Léon Beyer's Cuvée des Comtes, which is built for long aging, 2007 at 8 years would be entering its secondary complexity phase: petrol, preserved citrus, mineral depth. ## Producer style
Léon Beyer has roots in Eguisheim dating to 1580 — one of Alsace's oldest wine families (source: Léon Beyer website). Like Trimbach, Beyer is firmly in the dry-style camp: all wines, including Riesling and Gewurztraminer, are fermented to genuine dryness — this is a deliberate stylistic decision and a key differentiator from much of Alsace's more RS-variable production. The Cuvée des Comtes d'Eguisheim is the estate's prestige Riesling, sourced from the best parcels in Eguisheim, and is a wine built for extended aging. Beyer bottles under AOC Alsace rather than Grand Cru — a philosophical position about the quality of the wine speaking for itself. The house is internationally respected in fine wine circles, particularly in France and the UK. (Source: Léon Beyer website — general producer philosophy confirmed.)

## Why it's in this exam
W12 is the final wine in Q3's six single-variety flight — and likely the hardest to precisely identify. The 2007 vintage gives 8 years of bottle age by 2015, pushing this into secondary aromatic territory (petrol, preserved lemon, wax) that requires recognising aged Riesling's TDN development. The Alsace provenance at 13% ABV (relatively high for Riesling) and fully dry style may confuse candidates expecting residual sugar. The exam relevance is multifaceted: (1) identifies whether candidates know aged Alsace Riesling vs. aged German Riesling (structural differences: Alsace fuller, drier, higher ABV); (2) tests whether candidates separate Riesling from Chardonnay in an aged, mineral format; (3) Léon Beyer's cuvée name rather than Grand Cru labelling tests producer-level knowledge. "Further ageing": yes — Cuvée des Comtes is typically cited as capable of 15–25 years development.
