# Arbois Savagnin, Domaine de la Pinte, 2006

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold to amber; depending on whether this is ouillé (topped-up, non-oxidative) or sous voile (under veil, oxidative): if ouillé — rich, waxy, honey, quince, white peach; if sous voile — pronounced walnut, curry spice, dried apricot, oxidative aldehydic complexity reminiscent of Sherry. The 13.5% ABV and Savagnin variety are the key blind signals. Mouth-coating texture, long finish. A 2006 with 9 years of bottle age at exam time would show considerable development either way.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Savagnin (not Gewürztraminer — Jura's indigenous variety)
- Vinification: fermented in old oak barrels; Domaine de la Pinte is known for ouillé (topped-up) expressions of Savagnin, not the traditional sous voile/vin jaune style — a key differentiator vs oxidative Arbois
- Elevage: old oak barrels, topped-up to prevent flor development; length unknown for this vintage (Source needed)
- ABV: 13.5% / RS: dry / TA: elevated (Source needed — specific specs)
- Production: biodynamic estate, one of Arbois' leading domaines

## Vintage character (2006 Jura)
2006 was a challenging vintage in Jura — the region suffered variable weather with summer rains. Wines from reliable producers with low yields showed good concentration despite the difficult year; those with overcropped vines were dilute. By 2015 a well-made 2006 Savagnin would have developed considerable depth and complexity. Domaine de la Pinte's biodynamic viticulture and low-yield philosophy tends to produce wines with cellaring capacity. (Source needed — specific 2006 Jura vintage report)

## Producer style
Domaine de la Pinte, founded 1953 by Roger Martin; now managed by Pierre Martin. Based in Arbois, biodynamic certified. A pioneer of the ouillé (non-oxidative) Savagnin style in Jura, which deliberately positions the wine outside the traditional vin jaune/oxidative category. This approach — fresh, fruity, precise Savagnin — creates a wine that can mislead tasters expecting Gewürztraminer or oxidative Jura. High quality reputation, organic/biodynamic ethos, limited production. (Source needed — producer website inaccessible; TLS error)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 3 is the special styles paper but also includes still wines as curveballs. A Jura Savagnin ouillé at 9 years is a significant identification challenge: the variety mimics oxidative sherry if sous voile, or fat Alsatian Gewürztraminer if ouillé. The MW examiner tests whether candidates can distinguish Savagnin from Gewürztraminer and correctly identify the Jura appellation.
