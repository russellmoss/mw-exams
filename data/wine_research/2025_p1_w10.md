# Riesling Flussterrassen, Grans-Fassian, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with green-gold reflexes, typical of young Mosel Riesling. The nose offers white peach, pineapple, green apple, and grapefruit zest over a slate-mineral undertone. The palate is feinherb (off-dry, ~15 g/L RS) with racy acidity cutting through the residual sugar, delivering a juicy mid-palate of mirabelle plum and tropical fruit before a taut, mineral-driven finish. Low alcohol (11.5%) reinforces the impression of lightness and elegance characteristic of the Mittelmosel.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: River-terrace vineyards ("Flussterrassen" = river terraces) near Leiwen, south/east-facing slopes
- Soils: Grey Devonian slate
- Vinification: Selective hand harvest, gentle whole-cluster pressing, cool fermentation in stainless steel tanks
- Elevage: Stainless steel; no oak
- ABV / RS / TA: 11.5% / ~15 g/L / ~8.7 g/L
- Classification: VDP.Gutswein (estate-level wine in the VDP pyramid); Mosel QbA feinherb
- Closure: Screwcap
*(RS and TA figures sourced from German retailer listings for the 2022 vintage.)*

## Vintage character (2022 Mosel)
2022 was the driest year on record in Germany, with virtually no rain from May through July. The Mosel experienced extreme heat stress; harvest began unprecedentedly early on 20 August. September rains arrived late but provided some relief, giving Mosel producers an extended picking window compared to warmer southern regions. Yields were approximately 7% below average, with some estates losing far more. The resulting wines are lighter and more aromatic than the richer 2018-2020 vintages, with bright acidity and a steelier core reminiscent of 2020's delicacy. Virtually no BA/TBA was made due to lack of botrytis. (Source: Flatiron Wines; German Wine USA)

## Producer style
Weingut Grans-Fassian is a family estate in Leiwen on the Mittelmosel, continuously owned by the Grans family since 1624 -- now in its 13th generation under Catherina Grans (Geisenheim-trained). The estate cultivates ~12 hectares, 90% Riesling, with top parcels in Trittenheimer Apotheke, Leiwener Laurentiuslay, Piesporter Goldtropfchen, and Dhroner Hofberg. VDP member since 2001, with several vineyards classified as VDP.Grosse Lage. The house style is puristic and mineral-driven; 80% of production is dry. The Flussterrassen bottling sits at the Gutswein (entry) level of their range -- an accessible, feinherb expression designed to showcase classic Mosel Riesling character at a friendly price point. Wines are exported to 19 countries. (Source: grans-fassian.de)

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 10 sits in Question 4 of Paper 1 (2025): "Wines 10-12 are from three different European countries. For each wine: (a) Comment on the style of the wine with reference to the relative importance of human inputs versus natural factors. (b) Identify the grape variety and origin as closely as possible." This is a classic MW question testing the candidate's ability to read terroir versus winemaking. The Flussterrassen is an ideal vehicle: low-intervention stainless-steel winemaking lets the natural factors dominate (slate soils, cool Mosel microclimate, feinherb balance of RS against acidity). The candidate must recognise Mosel Riesling's signature -- low alcohol, racy acidity, off-dry balance, slate minerality -- and articulate that nature (steep slate slopes, continental-cool climate, unique river-terrace mesoclimate) is the primary driver here, with human inputs limited to careful viticulture and non-interventionist cellar work.
