# Moscato, Gallo Family Vineyard, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Very pale straw, possibly with a slight spritz; effusively aromatic nose of peach, apricot, orange blossom, lychee and grape — pure Muscat/Moscato aromatic profile. Light-bodied (9% ABV), noticeably sweet with soft, low acidity; fresh, easy, unchallenging palate. Designed for immediate enjoyment and mass-market appeal. The low alcohol, obvious sweetness and candied fruit character unambiguously signal commercial Moscato at the accessible end of the California market.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Muscat (likely Muscat Blanc à Petits Grains or Muscat of Alexandria)
- Vineyard: California AVA — large-scale sourcing typical of Gallo Family Vineyards portfolio
- Vinification: Early-harvested for low alcohol; arrested fermentation leaving residual sugar; tank-produced with CO2 preservation for freshness
- Elevage: Non-vintage or single vintage; tank-conditioned; early bottling
- ABV / RS / TA: 9% / sweet (approx 80-120 g/L RS typical for this style) / low acidity

## Vintage character (2019 California)
2019 was a moderate vintage for California with good natural acidity. For a mass-market Moscato at 9% ABV, vintage variation is minimal — the winemaking target (sweetness level, aromatics, low alcohol) overrides terroir expression. Source needed for specific 2019 production data.

## Producer style
Gallo Family Vineyards is a commercial sub-brand of E&J Gallo Winery, one of the world's largest wine producers (Modesto, California). The brand targets everyday drinking at accessible price points, with the Moscato range positioned squarely at the sweet, low-ABV consumer segment popularized post-2010. This is volume wine production at global scale — the antithesis of the artisan wines elsewhere in this paper.
