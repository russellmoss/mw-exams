# Nuits-St-Georges, 1er Cru Clos des Argillières, Domaine Rion, 2011. Burgundy, France

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with developed garnet-orange rim (6 years old at time of tasting). Nose of red cherry, dried strawberry, leather, forest floor, sous-bois, and earthy complexity; secondary development is evident. Medium body, refined tannins (Premier Cru quality), firm but integrated acidity, medium-long finish with savoury persistence. Nuits-St-Georges character: firmer, more structured and earthy than Gevrey-Chambertin but less floral than Chambolle-Musigny — the most "masculine" appellation of the Côte de Nuits.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: Clos des Argillières — a Premier Cru vineyard in Prémeaux-Prissey, the southernmost part of Nuits-St-Georges; "Argillières" = clay-rich soils; the "Clos" designation suggests a walled vineyard
- Soils: Clay-rich marl over limestone — "Argillières" literally translates to "clay-bearing"; heavier soils produce fuller, earthier Nuits wines
- Vinification: Traditional Burgundian fermentation; whole-cluster possible at Domaine Rion (Source needed)
- Elevage: French oak, proportion new oak appropriate for 1er Cru level (Source needed for exact % and duration)
- ABV: 13.0%

## Vintage character (2011 Côte de Nuits / Burgundy)
2011 was a good but uneven vintage in Burgundy — a warm spring and excellent July raised hopes, but cool August and some rain before harvest created pressure. The wines are generally elegant, accessible, and ready to drink relatively early — lighter and less structured than the dense 2010 or concentrated 2012. For NSG 1er Cru at 6 years old (when tasted in 2017), the 2011 would show attractive secondary development and approachable drinking.

## Producer style
Domaine Rion (Patrice Rion or possibly the associated Domaine Daniel Rion) is an established Burgundy family domaine based in Prémeaux-Prissey, Nuits-St-Georges. The family has produced wines from Nuits-St-Georges and surrounding appellations for generations. Clos des Argillières is one of their signature 1er Cru sites. They are known for a traditional, restrained Burgundian style. Source needed for confirmed current domaine status and owner (Patrice Rion vs. Domaine Daniel Rion).

## Why it's in this exam
Nuits-St-Georges 1er Cru sits directly alongside Côte de Nuits Villages (w9) to test the candidate's ability to assess Burgundy Pinot Noir at two quality levels: village appellation vs. Premier Cru. At Premier Cru, the expected differentiators are: greater complexity on the nose, finer more refined tannin quality, longer finish, and more evident terroir-specific character (the NSG earthy/mineral signature). Both wines at 13% ABV from the same vintage year would be the immediate comparison signal.
