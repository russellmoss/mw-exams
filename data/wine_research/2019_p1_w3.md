# Château La Louvière Blanc, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Medium lemon with a more layered, oak-shaped profile than the Sauvignon-dominant wines around it. Expect grapefruit, preserved lemon, lanolin, toast and light smoky notes over a fuller texture, moderate alcohol and fresh but not piercing acidity. The finish is dry, savoury and gently creamy.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Sauvignon Blanc and Sémillon blend
- Vineyard: Estate fruit from Pessac-Léognan
- Soils: Deep gravel with clay and limestone influence across the estate

## Vintage character (2013 Bordeaux)
The 2013 Bordeaux growing season was difficult, with a cool, wet cycle and uneven flowering reducing concentration across the region. Quality white wines could still perform well because producers harvested on freshness, but the vintage generally favoured earlier-drinking, acid-led styles over massive richness.

## Producer style
Château La Louvière, owned by André Lurton, is one of the reference estates of Pessac-Léognan and is especially respected for dry white Bordeaux. The blanc style is structured and polished, built on Sauvignon Blanc's aromatic lift with Sémillon adding breadth, and oak used to frame rather than dominate the wine.
