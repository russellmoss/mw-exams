# Zweigelt, Bergh, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Bright ruby. The nose should show crunchy dark cherry, wild berry jam, floral lift and a faint spicy-oaky note rather than deep extraction (source: https://serendipitywines.com/product/bergh-zweigelt/). On the palate this reads as a fresh, mid-weight, easy-drinking Austrian red: lively acidity, modest tannin, juicy red-black fruit and only a light oak framing, so the blind profile is more crunchy and gluggable than serious or structured (source: https://serendipitywines.com/sales-sheet/?default=1&wc_id=160374).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Zweigelt (source: https://serendipitywines.com/product/bergh-zweigelt/)
- Vineyard: In and around Roschitz in Niederosterreich; Bergh identifies Weinviertel as the family base (source: https://serendipitywines.com/product/bergh-zweigelt/)
- Soils: Loess (source: https://serendipitywines.com/product/bergh-zweigelt/)
- Vinification: Malolactic fermentation in partially used barriques and stainless steel (source: https://serendipitywines.com/product/bergh-zweigelt/)
- Elevage: 20% aged for 10 months in oak barrels, 80% in stainless steel (source: https://serendipitywines.com/product/bergh-zweigelt/)

## Vintage character (2021 Austria)
Austria's 2021 vintage was widely described as a high-quality year, with a long growing season and a favorable autumn delivering good ripeness with freshness rather than overripeness (source: https://www.meiningers-international.com/wine/sponsored-reports/2021-dream-vintage-austria). For a modestly oaked Zweigelt from Lower Austria, that supports a style with vivid fruit, bright acidity and moderate alcohol rather than plush warmth.

## Producer style
Bergh is a Gruber family project rooted in Roschitz, with family viticulture dating to 1814 and vineyards farmed organically in Weinviertel (source: https://serendipitywines.com/product/bergh-zweigelt/). The style here is intentionally accessible rather than ambitious: certified organic, fruit-led, lightly handled, and built to show Zweigelt's charm instead of density (source: https://serendipitywines.com/sales-sheet/?default=1&wc_id=160374).
