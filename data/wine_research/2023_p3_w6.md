# Oloroso VORS 30 Years, Bodegas Tradición, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep mahogany with amber-copper rim. Blind, this should announce long-aged dry Oloroso immediately: walnut, hazelnut, leather, noble wood, cocoa and balsamic lift, with a velvety but powerful palate, pronounced oxidative complexity and very long finish (source: https://elgustoporelvino.com/wp-content/uploads/TRADICION-OLOROSO-30-ANOS-VORS.pdf).

## Vintage character (NV / category character)
This is non-vintage and category-led. The exam-relevant issue is not harvest weather but understanding Oloroso: fortified base wine aged fully oxidatively, without flor protection, developing concentration, nutty aldehydic complexity and long cask-aged texture over decades.

## Producer style
Bodegas Tradición is known for very old Sherries and an intentionally classical, low-volume, heritage-driven commercial position rather than youthful, easy-drinking styles. That makes this bottle an archetype of premium traditional Sherry with strong educational value but narrower mainstream appeal than sparkling Cava or modern Garnacha.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 6 completes the all-Spain set in 2023 Paper 3, Question 2. It forces candidates to pivot from sparkling to mountain red to fortified oxidative wine, and to talk not just about quality but also commercial position inside a more specialist category.
