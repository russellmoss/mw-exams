# Torrontes, Crios, Susana Balbo, 2010

## Quick tasting profile
Aromatic and extrovert, with jasmine, grapey perfume, citrus and peach, but intended to finish dry rather than sweet. The palate is medium-light with briskness and aromatic lift, making it a classic exam candidate for Torrontés rather than Muscat or Gewürztraminer.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Torrontés
- Vineyard: High-altitude northern Argentine sourcing associated with Salta/Cafayate for this era
- Soils: Source needed
- Vinification: Clean, reductive aromatic white vinification without meaningful oak
- Élevage: Minimal and freshness-preserving
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2010 Salta)
High-altitude Salta typically preserves aromatic intensity and acidity despite strong sun. That combination gives Torrontés its distinctive exam profile: highly perfumed but not oily, floral yet usually dry and fresh on the palate.

## Producer style
Susana Balbo is one of Argentina’s best-known white-wine specialists, and Crios is a range aimed at delivering varietal clarity with strong commercial appeal. For Torrontés, that means fragrance, cleanliness and typicity rather than oak or sweetness tricks.

## Why it's in this exam
In Question 3 this is the aromatic New World curveball. It tests whether candidates can identify Torrontés confidently and avoid drifting toward Alsace Muscat, Gewürztraminer or Viognier when confronted with pronounced perfume.
