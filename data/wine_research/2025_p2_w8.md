# Touriga Nacional, Quinta do Vallado, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Deep young ruby-violet. Very concentrated nose of ripe black fruits (blackberry, black currant), cedar, anise, cinnamon, vanilla, violet, and balsamic notes. Broad, structured palate with velvety tannins, intense flavours, and harmonious balance. Medium to full-bodied with a long, fresh finish. Flavours of blackberry, black olive, chalky minerality, and black tea. Still youthful but approachable with 16 months of French oak ageing.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Touriga Nacional
- Vineyard: Estate vineyards near Régua in the Douro Valley

## Vintage character (2019 Douro)
A cooler vintage than 2018, characterized by fresher wines with purity of fruit. Below-average rainfall overall, but 130mm rain in April kickstarted the season. Moderate harvest temperatures with warm days and cool nights allowed uniform maturation and good acidity. Very fine vintage for reds: a fine combination of strength and freshness with velvety textures. Touriga Nacional found full expression with ripe tannins and maintained acidity. Not declared as a vintage port year.

## Producer style
Quinta do Vallado sits in the heart of the Douro near Régua, with winemaking references dating back to 1716 when it belonged to the legendary D. Antónia Adelaide Ferreira. Now sixth-generation family ownership. Produced Port exclusively until the 1990s, then expanded into dry table wines. Combines state-of-the-art winemaking with traditional methods (granite lagares). Organically farmed since 2009, certified since 2015. Recognized as a Wine & Spirits Top 100 Producer and one of the Top 100 Vineyards in the World.
