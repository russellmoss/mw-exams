# Colheita, Justino's, 1996

## Quick tasting profile
Amber-mahogany with orange-tawny rim; classic Maderisation — the combination of heat-aging and oxidation creates a unique palate profile impossible to fake. Nose: roasted coffee, dried apricot, orange marmalade, salted caramel, walnut, and the characteristic "Madeira bite" of high volatile acidity. Palate: rich, complex, long; sharp acid-sweetness interplay; persistent finish. The 1996 vintage date and 19% ABV, combined with the oxidative-heated character, should point firmly to Madeira Colheita.

## Vintage character (1996 Madeira)
As a Colheita, the single vintage character is preserved through the aging process. 1996 on Madeira was a normal harvest year. By 2015 the wine had 19 years of canteiro aging, developing full Maderised complexity. Colheita releases at this age are positioned between the standard 10-year tawny-equivalent tier and the true Frasqueira (vintage, 20+ years) tier. At the exam, the student sees a 19-year-old fortified wine with intense oxidative character.

## Producer style
Justino Henriques, founded 1870 in Funchal, is one of the largest Madeira producers by volume. Uses canteiro process (natural heat aging) for its premium wines — distinguishing it from cheaper estufagem (forced heating) wines. Exports globally. Positioned at mid-to-upper tier within Madeira. Colheita range covers multiple decades of single-vintage wines. (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madeira_wine)
