# Barolo, Diego Conterno, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-deep garnet with brick-edged rim developing. Aromatics of raspberry, sour cherry, rose petal, potpourri, incense, and licorice. Medium-to-full body with chewy, structured tannins and tall acidity. Juicy red fruit with savoury, mineral, and herbal notes. A traditionally made Barolo from Monforte d'Alba — powerful yet showing translucence. Needs time to unravel (recommended from 2024+).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Nebbiolo
- Vineyard: 7.5 ha in Monforte d'Alba (including 2 ha in Ginestra cru)
- Soils: Calcareous marl (typical Monforte Tortonian soils)
- Vinification: Indigenous yeast fermentation in neutral cement tanks, traditional extended maceration
- Elevage: Aged in large traditional oak botti (no new barriques)
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.5% / Source needed / Source needed
- Certified organic (since 2014), Demeter biodynamic from 2019 vintage onward

## Vintage character (2018 Barolo)
Challenging: wet winter, exceptionally rainy spring (25 consecutive days of rain in May), significant downy mildew and oidium pressure. Localized August hail. Despite difficulties, harvest in first three weeks of October was successful. Wines show finesse and charm with silk-like tannins and good balance of perfume, precision, and physical presence. Variable results producer-to-producer — vineyard management was critical. Not a blockbuster vintage; rewards careful producers. (source: Barolo Wine Club, Decanter, BBR)

## Producer style
Diego Conterno — a name synonymous with Monforte d'Alba — graduated from Umberto I Enological School and worked under Beppe Colla at Prunotto. Left Conterno-Fantino in 2000, took his Ginestra vineyard plots, and started fresh (first vintage 2003). Traditional winemaking: cement tank fermentation on indigenous yeasts, aging in large botti. Antonio Galloni describes the wines as blending "the energy and brawn of Monforte with the translucence of traditionally inspired winemaking." Organic since 2014, biodynamic (Demeter) from 2019. Small production (~7.5 ha total).
