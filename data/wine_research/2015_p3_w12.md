# Vin Santo del Chianti Classico, Isole e Olena, 2005

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber to dark amber; complex dried-fruit and oxidative nose — dried apricot, roasted almonds, toffee, orange marmalade, honey, beeswax. Palate: sweet but never cloying (as the producer describes it); very long finish with walnut and bitter-orange complexity from the extended barrel aging. The 13% ABV is moderate — this is not fortified, distinguishing Vin Santo from fortified oxidative wines. The 10-year-old profile at exam time would show full development.

## Technical specs
- Grape varieties: Malvasia and Trebbiano (traditional Tuscan varieties for white Vin Santo) — confirmed from producer website
- Vinification: grapes dried on racks for approximately 3 months ("Malvasia and Trebbiano grapes dry on racks for about three months; they are then soft-pressed and slowly fermented" — source: isoleeolena.it)
- Aging: 11 years in small sealed wooden vessels (caratelli) of various sizes — confirmed from producer website; wine maintains contact with madre (mother yeast) throughout aging
- ABV: 13% / RS: medium-sweet to sweet (exact g/L — Source needed) / TA: elevated from concentration and long aging
- Production: Isole e Olena estate in Barberino Val d'Elsa, Chianti Classico; Paolo De Marchi (now Marta De Marchi) family ownership
- Release date: 2005 vintage released 2016+ (11-year aging confirmed by producer)

## Vintage character (2005 Tuscany)
2005 was a good Tuscan vintage — relatively cool growing season with good acidity and aromatic expression. For Vin Santo grapes dried post-harvest, the vintage character of the base grapes is somewhat transformed by the drying and long barrel aging. By 2015 (10 years from vintage, still in barrel) this wine would not yet have been released — implying the exam bottle was either an early release or the producer released earlier than normal. ## Producer style
Isole e Olena is a biodynamic estate in Chianti Classico run by the De Marchi family (Paolo De Marchi for decades; now transitioning to daughters). Widely regarded as one of Chianti Classico's reference producers. Produces Cepparello (100% Sangiovese IGT), Chianti Classico, and the benchmark Vin Santo del Chianti Classico. The Vin Santo is one of Italy's most celebrated examples — 11 years in caratelli, maintaining the madre. "Sweet but never cloying" is the house descriptive. (Source: isoleeolena.it/en/wines/vin-santo/)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 3 includes passito/sweet wines. Isole e Olena Vin Santo is a benchmark example of this category. The MW examiner tests whether candidates can identify: dried-grape sweet wine (not fortified, not botrytised), Tuscan Vin Santo vs other oxidative sweets, and the nutty-dried fruit profile from 11 years in sealed caratelli. The 13% ABV (unfortified) is a key differentiator from Port-style or Sherry-style sweets.
