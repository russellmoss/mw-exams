# Pinot Noir, Dog Point Vineyard, 2012

## Quick tasting profile
Bright ruby with a more herbal, savoury profile than many plush New World Pinots: red cherry, wild herbs, dried flowers, and a faint smoky reduction. The palate is medium-bodied with ripe but not sweet fruit, brisk acidity, and fine-grained tannin, giving a taut, food-friendly style rather than a lavishly oaked one. In blind tasting it points to cooler-climate New World Pinot with a distinctly Marlborough savoury edge.

## Producer style
Dog Point is one of Marlborough's leading quality-focused estates, founded by former Cloudy Bay viticultural and winemaking figures. The house style tends toward low-cropping, organically farmed, distinctly savoury wines with less reliance on obvious fruit sweetness than many commercial Marlborough peers. Even across varieties, the producer has a reputation for texture, restraint, and ageworthiness.
