# Viognier Mon Grand-Père Était Limonadier, Julien Pilon, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
Medium lemon. Aromatically this should be recognisably Viognier, with apricot, citrus and floral lift, but it is fresher and less blowsy than many examples because the sites are higher, less sun-exposed plateau vineyards. The palate should be dry, medium-bodied, supple, and lees-textured, with ripe stone-fruit character moderated by genuine freshness rather than alcoholic weight (source: importer tech sheet).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Viognier (source: importer tech sheet)
- Vineyard: blend of four plots on plateaux overlooking the northern Rhone Valley (source: importer tech sheet)
- Soils: granite, sandy, poorly grounded soil (source: importer tech sheet)
- Vinification: hand harvested; each plot vinified separately; after pressing, musts strictly decanted; slow low-temperature alcoholic fermentation; full malolactic fermentation (source: importer tech sheet)
- Elevage: 10 months on lees in second-hand 225L and 400L barrels (source: importer tech sheet)
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.0% in the importer sheet, but the exam corpus lists 13.5%; RS and TA Source needed

## Vintage character (2020 Northern Rhone)
Berry Bros. & Rudd's 2020 Northern Rhone report describes a warm, dry year with stable heat, cool nights, early harvest dates, and whites that retained enhanced perfume, freshness, and texture. That is exactly the style logic behind this bottle: aromatic richness, but not flabbiness, with retained freshness in a warm year (source: BBR).

## Producer style
Julien Pilon is a small Northern Rhone producer near Condrieu who launched his own label in 2010 after working with leading regional figures. The cuvee name refers to his maternal grandfather's lemonade business, and the estate style, as presented by the importer, is artisanal, plot-specific and freshness-seeking rather than blockbuster or heavily oaked (source: importer tech sheet).
