# Château Léoville Barton, 2005

## Quick tasting profile
Deep garnet at maturity, with cassis, cedar, pencil shavings, cigar box, and cool graphite rather than plush sweetness. The palate is full-bodied and structured, still carrying firm Médoc tannin and fresh acid through a long, aristocratic finish. Blind, this is textbook classed-growth left-bank Bordeaux from a great vintage: Cabernet-led, restrained, and built for long evolution.

## Vintage character (2005 Bordeaux)
2005 is one of the benchmark modern Bordeaux vintages, combining ripe tannin, concentrated fruit, and the structure for long ageing. In the Médoc the best wines marry cassis depth with freshness and classical proportion rather than over-ripeness. This is exactly the kind of vintage that rewards candidates who can separate greatness of year from simple bottle age.

## Producer style
Léoville Barton is one of Bordeaux's classic overperforming classed-growth names, known for traditional, Cabernet-dominant Saint-Julien that often needs time in bottle. The estate's reputation rests on consistency, restraint, and a refusal to chase flashy ripeness. Compared with more polished modern styles, Léoville Barton usually reads as firm, cool, and structurally serious.
