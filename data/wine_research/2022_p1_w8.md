# Etna Bianco di Sei, Palmento Costanzo, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Expect a volcanic Mediterranean white with more mineral tension than overt fruit: citrus, fennel, white peach, herbs and a smoky-saline edge. The palate should feel leaner and more tensile than many southern Italian whites, with altitude and lava-derived terroir doing much of the work.

## Vintage character (2018 Etna)
2018 on Etna was not the easiest vintage, with more rain pressure than the warm, easy years, but better growers still preserved freshness and site definition. In blind tasting that can mean a cooler, more tensile expression with moderate alcohol and pronounced mineral length.

## Producer style
Palmento Costanzo is a quality-focused Etna producer rooted in old terraces and contrada-specific identity. Bianco di Sei is meant to translate Etna Bianco into a precise, terroir-marked style, relying on altitude and volcanic soils more than oak or simple fruit sweetness.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 8 completes the Italian trio in 2022 Paper 1 Question 3. It is the terroir-led southern Italian white that forces candidates to distinguish volcanic Etna Bianco from the lighter commercial Pinot Grigio and the overtly aromatic Alto Adige Gewurztraminer.
