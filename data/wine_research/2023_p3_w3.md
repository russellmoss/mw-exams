# Riesling Beerenauslese, Dr. Loosen, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Deep lemon to gold, intensely sweet but lifted. Blind, expect botrytis-inflected apricot, citrus peel, honey and light petroleum, with markedly low alcohol and enough Mosel acidity to keep the wine from feeling heavy (source: https://www.vinunic.se/sv/beerenauslese-5364-187ml.aspx/?pf=pdf).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling (source: https://www.vinunic.se/sv/beerenauslese-5364-187ml.aspx/?pf=pdf)
- Vineyard: Mosel; the importer material emphasizes old, largely ungrafted estate holdings in top vineyards, but does not assign this bottling to a single named site. Source needed.
- Soils: Mineral, stony Mosel vineyard soils are described at estate level, but no single-vineyard soil statement is published for this bottle. Source needed.
- Vinification: Slow cold maceration, cool fermentation in stainless steel, long lees contact, then fermentation stopped by chilling of the must (source: https://www.vinunic.se/sv/beerenauslese-5364-187ml.aspx/?pf=pdf)
- Elevage: Long lees contact with only one racking and filtration per the importer sheet (source: https://www.vinunic.se/sv/beerenauslese-5364-187ml.aspx/?pf=pdf)
- ABV / RS / TA: 7.5% / 139.5 g/L / 9.5 g/L (source: https://www.vinunic.se/sv/beerenauslese-5364-187ml.aspx/?pf=pdf)

## Vintage character (2018 Mosel)
2018 in Germany was warm and generous, which helps explain the ripe fruit tone, but Beerenauslese still depends on concentration and noble rot rather than simple ripeness. In exam terms, the key takeaway is not “hot vintage” but the combination of very high sweetness, low alcohol and bright acid retention that signals Prädikat sweet Riesling.

## Producer style
Dr. Loosen’s house style is built around old vines, site expression and restrained cellar handling (source: https://loosenbrosusa.com/wine/dr-loosen-riesling-beerenauslese/). Even in a sweet, accessible bottling, the estate identity is precision and acidity rather than syrupy weight.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 3 completes the Riesling trio in 2023 Paper 3, Question 1. It tests whether the candidate can move from sparkling to off-dry still to botrytized sweet Riesling while keeping the grape constant and shifting the discussion toward noble-rot sweet-wine production.
