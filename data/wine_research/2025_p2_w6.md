# Cornas Granit 30, Vincent Paris, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Deep purple-ruby. Haunting nose of black forest berries, violet, black pepper, liquorice, graphite, espresso, tobacco, and dry forest floor with wild herbs. Medium to full-bodied, lean and elegant with pronounced mineral vibrancy. Fine-grained tannins, medium-plus acidity, and long finish with savoury notes of dried meat, cedar, blackberry, and iodine. Incredibly expressive and precise — a modern-style Cornas with stacks of purity. Great Northern Rhône bargain.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Syrah
- Vineyard: Younger vines (approx. 15 years old) on granite slopes at 30-degree gradient — hence the name "Granit 30"
- Soils: Decomposed granite (arène granitique)
- Vinification: Traditional; no new oak; unfiltered
- Élevage: 3 months in old foudres — minimal oak influence, showcasing pure fruit and terroir
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2022 Northern Rhône)
The hottest and driest growing season since 1950. Despite this, Northern Rhône Syrah wines are well-built with reasonable alcohol (13–14%) and surprisingly fresh balances. The brightness of aromas is the vintage hallmark. Some blockages in ripening occurred around Cornas and Hermitage, making for lighter wines with moderate alcohol alongside more robust examples. Overall, delightfully elegant and structured wines.

## Producer style
Vincent Paris started making wine in the 1990s, initially working with his uncle, legendary Cornas winemaker Robert Michel, before establishing his own domaine. He leases and inherited vineyard plots on the Cornas slopes. His philosophy is minimal intervention: no new oak, unfiltered, letting the natural power of granite-grown Syrah speak. Granit 30 is the entry-level cuvée (younger vines), while Granit 60 comes from steeper, older-vine sites. Wines are increasingly recognized as some of the best-value Cornas available (average ~$40).
