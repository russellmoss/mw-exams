# Ried Lamm, 1, Grüner Veltliner, Schloss Gobelsburg, 2016. Kamptal, Austria.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with a green hue. Nose: white pepper (the hallmark GV signal), citrus peel, green herbs, peach, and mineral freshness. Palate: dry and precise — medium-plus body, crisp acidity, no oak, white-pepper finish. At 13.5% ABV the ripeness is evident but not over-ripe. The "1 STK" (Erste Lage / First Growth) designation signals a Burgundy-style classification system. Fine, taut, mineral, and age-worthy.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Grüner Veltliner
- Vineyard: Ried Lamm — a lower-lying vineyard site at Schloss Gobelsburg; sand and loess soils (source: wikipedia.org/wiki/Schloss_Gobelsburg)
- Vinification: Stainless steel fermentation; temperature controlled; no oak contact typical at this classification level
- Elevage: Stainless or large neutral wood; minimal intervention
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / bone dry / medium-high

## Vintage character (2016 Kamptal / Niederösterreich)
2016 was a good to very good vintage in Austrian wine — cooler than some recent years but with good balance and acidity retention. GV from Kamptal in 2016 showed classic green-herb-and-pepper intensity with fresh fruit. Erste Lage-level wines show concentration and aging potential. (Source needed: 2016 Austrian vintage report)

## Producer style
Schloss Gobelsburg is one of Austria's most historically important estates — the oldest winery in the Danube region with documented production since 1171. Located in Langenlois, Kamptal, it manages 39 hectares. Michael Moosbrugger, who took over in 1996, rebuilt the estate's reputation with the support of Willi Bründlmayer, applying near-monastic precision to vineyard and cellar work. Grüner Veltliner represents ~50% of production; Riesling ~25%. Karen MacNeil describes it as "one of the great wine estates of the world." (source: wikipedia.org/wiki/Schloss_Gobelsburg)

## Why it's in this exam
Included as the reference Austrian white-wine expression: GV with its diagnostic white-pepper character, dry/bone-dry, no oak, precise acid structure. Tests candidate recognition of Austria vs. Alsace, Germany, and other European whites. The Erste Lage classification (marked "1" on label) is an Austrian-specific premium signal. The Kamptal DAC is the key regional identifier for GV of this quality tier.
