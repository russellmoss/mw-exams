# Chianti Classico Riserva "Rancia", Felsina Berardenga, 2012. Tuscany, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-deep ruby with slight garnet evolution at 5 years. Nose of sour cherry (classic Sangiovese), dried strawberry, leather, iron-mineral, tobacco, and cedar from French oak; subtle floral violet note on entry. Medium-full body, firm but refined tannins (Castelnuovo Berardenga style, more Southern Chianti Classico), bright food-grade acidity, long persistent finish with savoury spice and dried red fruit. A structured, serious Riserva that rewards cellaring.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Sangiovese (source: felsina.it/en/wine/rancia/)
- Vineyard: Poggio a Rancia, Castelnuovo Berardenga — the southern edge of Chianti Classico zone, at 350–400 m asl (source: felsina.it)
- Soils: Alberese (clay-limestone-schist typical of southern Chianti Classico zone); 5,400 vines/ha planting density (source: felsina.it)
- Vinification: Destemming and crushing; fermentation at 28–30°C in stainless steel tanks; 16–20 days maceration (source: felsina.it)
- Elevage: 18–20 months in new French oak barrique; further 6–8 months bottle aging before release (source: felsina.it)
- ABV: 14.5% / First vintage: 1983

## Vintage character (2012 Chianti Classico / Tuscany)
2012 was a challenging but rewarding vintage in Tuscany — a dry, hot summer caused significant drought stress in some areas, but estates with good water retention or higher altitude sites produced concentrated, structured wines with excellent longevity. Chianti Classico 2012 wines are generally powerful, ripe, with higher-than-average alcohol and dense tannin. At 5 years old (as tasted in 2017), the 2012 Rancia would be showing early secondary development but with significant structure still present.

## Producer style
Felsina Berardenga was established in 1966 by the Poggiali family in Castelnuovo Berardenga — at the southern-most point of Chianti Classico on the border with Crete Senesi. The estate's focus is firmly on Sangiovese, producing the Rancia Riserva (from a single vineyard) as its flagship alongside Fontalloro (a 100% Sangiovese IGT), Berardenga Chianti Classico, and Colonia (Gran Selezione). The Rancia is known for its more structured, mineral style compared to more northern Chianti Classico estates. The use of 100% new French barrique (rather than Slavonian large cask) is a key fingerprint — unusual for traditional Chianti Classico, producing a more internationally-inflected style. Source: felsina.it.

## Why it's in this exam
Rancia represents the serious, single-vineyard Chianti Classico Riserva benchmark — used in P2 to distinguish Sangiovese-based DOCG from simple DOC Italian reds and from other Italian varieties. The high ABV (14.5%), new French oak barrique, and 2012 vintage power would be key discriminators under blind conditions.
