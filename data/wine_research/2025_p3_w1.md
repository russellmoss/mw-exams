# Riesling Sekt Extra Dry, Dr. Loosen, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with fine, persistent bubbles and delicate foam. The nose shows intensely ripe nectarine and tangerine edged by a veil of caramelized sugar. On the palate, zesty green apple and crisp stone fruits are electrified by spine-tingling acidity, with a hint of cookie crumbs on the mid-palate. Light-bodied, brisk and refreshing, with pure Riesling fruit character.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Estate vineyards in the Middle Mosel
- Vinification: Tank-fermented base wine, then bottle-fermented using the transfer method (not riddled)
- RS: 14 g/L
- TA: 6 g/L (source: Chambers Wines tech sheet)
- ABV: 11.5%

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage blend, drawing on multiple harvests from estate vineyards. The NV status allows blending for consistency of the house style — aromatic, off-dry, and refreshing.

## Producer style
Dr. Loosen is one of the Mosel's most internationally recognised estates, run by Ernst Loosen since 1988. The estate holds parcels in some of the Mosel's finest vineyards (Urziger Wurzgarten, Wehlener Sonnenuhr, Erdener Prälat) with ungrafted vines averaging 60+ years old. Loosen dramatically reduced crop sizes and stopped chemical fertilisation, pursuing a minimalist cellar approach with long, slow fermentations. The estate is a VDP member. The Sekt is the entry-level sparkling expression — well-priced and punching above its weight class.

## Why it's in this exam
Part of a Riesling trio (Q1, wines 1-3) spanning three radically different styles: sparkling (Sekt), still (Marlborough), and dessert (Beerenauslese). The candidate must identify Riesling as the common grape across these wildly different expressions — a classic Paper 3 exercise. The Sekt tests recognition of Riesling character through bubbles and residual sugar. Sharing a producer with Wine 3 (Dr. Loosen BA) adds a further layer for the examiner.
