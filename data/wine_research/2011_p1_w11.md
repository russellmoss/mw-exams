# Condrieu, De Poncins, Villard, 2009

## Quick tasting profile
Rich, full-bodied Viognier with apricot, white flowers, ripe orchard fruit and a creamy, nutty edge from élevage. It should feel more layered and mineral than simple varietal Viognier, with enough acidity and extract to sit comfortably as a high-quality Rhône white.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Viognier
- Vineyard: De Poncins vineyard in Condrieu
- Soils: Granite-based Condrieu hillside terroir is typical
- Vinification: Barrel élevage is part of the wine’s identity; Hachette notes eleven months in cask for the bottling line
- Élevage: Around 11 months in barrel according to Hachette reference
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2009 Northern Rhône)
2009 was a ripe, generous year in much of France, and in Condrieu that points toward richer fruit and a broad texture. A good producer should still retain floral lift and enough mineral tension to keep the wine from becoming blowsy.

## Producer style
François Villard is one of the leading modern names in the Northern Rhône, with a strong reputation for white wines and especially Viognier. De Poncins is one of his signature Condrieu bottlings and is often cited for combining breadth with tension and mineral length.

## Why it's in this exam
This is the premium Viognier reference in the mixed bag. It gives candidates a chance to show they can distinguish Condrieu from simpler aromatic whites and correctly link richness, floral lift and oak-polished texture to appellation-level quality.
