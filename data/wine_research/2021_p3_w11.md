# Full Rich, Henriques & Henriques, 3 y.o.

## Quick tasting profile
Tawny amber with some depth; nose showing the Madeira signatures — roasted coffee, caramel, dried orange peel, fig and that characteristic burnt sugar/rancio quality from the heating process (estufagem); sweet and rich on the palate (19% ABV), balanced by Madeira's defining high acidity; medium-length finish. The "Full Rich" style typically uses Malvasia (Malmsey) or Tinta Negra grapes and is the sweetest expression in the standard Madeira range — positioned at the accessible, commercial end of Henriques & Henriques' portfolio at 3 years of age.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Likely Tinta Negra (which comprises ~85% of Madeira production) or Malvasia/Malmsey for "Full Rich" style (Source: Wikipedia for Tinta Negra dominance; Full Rich = sweet style)
- Vineyard: Madeira island vineyards
- Vinification: Partial fermentation then fortification; then heated via estufagem (artificial heating in tanks or pipes at ~45°C for 3 months) for younger commercial Madeiras
- Elevage: Minimum 3 years (hence "3 y.o.") in old wood (oak or chestnut)
- ABV / RS / TA: 19% / sweet / very high acidity (Madeira's acid backbone is its defining structural feature)
- "Full Rich" is the sweetest commercial style in Henriques & Henriques' range

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage; "3 y.o." designation = minimum 3 years of aging in cask. Commercial base-level Madeira, produced consistently year-round for market supply.

## Producer style
Henriques & Henriques is one of Madeira's oldest and most important independent wine shippers, founded in 1850 in Câmara de Lobos. One of the few Madeira houses to still produce a complete range from 3-year commercial to single-vintage and Frasqueira (20+ year) wines. A quality-focused producer across all tiers.
