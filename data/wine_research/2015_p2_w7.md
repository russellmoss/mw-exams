# Chianti Classico, Felsina Berardenga, 2011. Tuscany, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby-garnet, moderately intense. Nose shows classic Chianti Classico Sangiovese: sour cherry, dried cranberry, leather, tobacco, earth, dried herbs, and a hint of cedar. On the palate the wine is medium-to-full-bodied, with firm, grippy tannins characteristic of Sangiovese (the variety's hallmark high tannin + high acidity combination), medium-high acidity, and a dry, persistent finish. At 14% the 2011 is slightly richer than the varietal average.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Sangiovese (source: felsina.it/en/wine/berardenga/ — confirmed)
- Vineyard: Castelnuovo Berardenga, southeastern Chianti Classico; vineyards at 320-420 metres elevation, southwest-facing slopes (source: felsina.it)
- Soils: Macigno (quartzose sandstone) and alberese (compact clay-limestone), alluvial deposits; complex heterogeneous mix (source: felsina.it)
- Vinification: Destemmed and crushed; fermentation 28-30°C; maceration 12-15 days in steel tanks with daily pump-overs and punch-downs (source: felsina.it)
- Elevage: 12 months in medium-capacity Slavonian oak botti plus a small percentage of second/third-use barriques; spring bottling with minimum 3 months bottle rest before release (source: felsina.it)
- ABV / RS / TA: 14% / dry / Source needed
- Critical score: Wine Advocate 92 points for 2011 (source: felsina.it product page cross-reference)

## Vintage character (2011 Tuscany/Chianti Classico)
2011 was a warm, precocious vintage in Tuscany with early budbreak and harvest. The wines are generally riper and more concentrated than the more classical 2010 or the cool 2014. At 14% ABV Felsina's 2011 reflects this warmth. Tannins are firm but accessible; the vintage is considered good-to-very good for Chianti Classico. By 2015 the wine would be approaching its primary drinking window. (Source needed for specific 2011 Tuscany vintage report URL.)

## Producer style
Felsina is one of Chianti Classico's most respected estates, located in Castelnuovo Berardenga at the southern edge of the denomination. The estate is known for its Sangiovese-focused approach (no international blending varieties at the estate level) and consistent quality across multiple tiers: the Berardenga Annata, the Riserva Rancia (single vineyard), and the Maestro Raro (100% Cabernet Sauvignon from non-DOCG designated wine). Winemaking is directed by Giuseppe Mazzocolin and winemaker Giovanni Cappelli. The Slavonian oak approach is traditional for the region, avoiding excessive barrique influence. Market position: premium Italian, widely exported.
