# Pinot Noir, Dutton-Goldfield, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
This is classic polished Russian River Pinot: generous but not blowsy, with dark bramble, plum, Bing cherry, rose petal and black tea over fine oak spice. The palate should be supple and silky but still structural, combining ripe California fruit with enough earth, acidity and tannin to keep it clearly premium and site-driven.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: a multi-site Dutton Ranch blend from Bush, Galante, Emerald Ridge, Goff, Jentoft and Ruxton
- Soils: Goldridge soils dominate Dutton Ranch, with Ruxton on Stoetz Ridge adding color and focus
- Vinification: hand sorted before destemming, gravity-fed to small open-top fermenters, 5-day cold soak, punch-down extraction, small-lot fermentation
- Elevage: 10 months in French oak, 40% new
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / Source needed / 6.4 g/L

## Vintage character (2022 Russian River Valley)
The 2022 season in Sonoma was uneven and fast-moving: dry spring conditions, frost issues, an unusually early harvest and a Labor Day heat spike. Dutton-Goldfield's own material stresses that the best wines came from rapid, strategic picking decisions that preserved acidity and fruit integrity. In blind tasting, expect a riper-fruited Pinot than in a cooler year, but one intentionally kept fresh and balanced.

## Producer style
Dutton-Goldfield was founded in 1998 by Steve Dutton and Dan Goldfield to make wines that express vineyard personality through brightness of fruit, complexity, structure and balance. The Dutton Ranch Pinot is their regional flagship: multiple certified-sustainable sites, cool Russian River fruit, restrained but real French oak, and a style aimed at elegance rather than maximal extraction. It is a benchmark commercial-premium Sonoma Pinot.
