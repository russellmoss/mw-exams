# Viognier, Yalumba, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with luminous clarity. Pure aromas of fresh apricots, lifted ginger, and fresh white flowers. Long, silky palate with stone fruit flavours, a hint of exotic saffron, and an alluring freshness. Finishes with textural richness from older barrel aging without overt oak character. Well-balanced and elegant at 13% — restrained for Viognier. Scored 93 points with a 2023-2031 drinking window.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Viognier

## Producer style
Yalumba, founded 1849, is Australia's oldest family-owned winery (Hill-Smith family). The Samuel's Collection range sits in the mid-tier (approx. $15-20 retail). Winemaker Louisa Rose is one of Australia's most respected white wine makers and a specialist in Viognier. Yalumba pioneered Viognier in Australia and is considered the country's benchmark producer of the variety. The Eden Valley sourcing provides higher altitude, cooler conditions than the Barossa Valley floor, yielding more restrained, elegant Viognier. Also in the corpus: Grenache Samuel's Collection Bush Vine 2019 (2023_p2_w8).
