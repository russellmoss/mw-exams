# Watervale Riesling, Mount Horrocks, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with a youthful, lime-driven nose showing white flowers, talc and wet stone. The palate is dry, light to medium-bodied and tightly wound, with high acidity and a distinctly Clare combination of citrus intensity and chalky drive. This is precise, ageworthy dry Riesling rather than an exuberantly fruity style.

## Vintage character (2018 Clare Valley)
The producer describes 2018 as a highly successful vintage for this wine, and the finished style supports that reading: intense fruit definition with no lack of acid line. In exam terms, the wine points to classic Clare dry Riesling with concentration and a long development curve.

## Producer style
Mount Horrocks is a respected small Clare Valley estate known for finely drawn, ageworthy Riesling and a strongly estate-led identity. The style favours purity, line and regional typicity over embellishment, making it a useful benchmark for modern Australian dry Riesling.

## Why it's in this exam
This appears in 2019 Paper 1 Question 2 as one of the Australian pairings. It provides the textbook lime-and-acid dry Riesling marker set, contrasted against the more oak-shaped, broader Chardonnay in wine 10.
