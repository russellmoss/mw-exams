# Château Coutet, 2013. Sauternes-Barsac, Bordeaux, France.

## Quick tasting profile
Bright golden yellow, less amber than the 2002. Nose: fresh apricot, peach, citrus peel, honey, vanilla, and clean Botrytis concentration — still clearly in its primary development phase at five years old (in 2018). Palate: sweet and structured, high acidity giving freshness and tension, vanilla oak integration ongoing, lower RS perception than the mature 2002. The 13.5% ABV vs. the 2002's 14% signals either a cooler year or a slightly less concentrated harvest.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 75% Sémillon, 23% Sauvignon Blanc, 2% Muscadelle (house blend; same as 2002)
- Vineyard: 38 hectares in Barsac
- Vinification: Selective botrytised harvest (tries); French oak fermentation
- Elevage: New French oak; ~4,500 cases grand vin/year
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / very high RS / high

## Vintage character (2013 Sauternes-Barsac)
2013 was a challenging vintage in Bordeaux generally — wet and cool — but Barsac/Sauternes had variable results. Some châteaux benefited from late September/October conditions with better Botrytis spread; others suffered from uneven ripening. 2013 is considered a minor to good vintage at best; estates that selected carefully made acceptable wines but it lacks the concentration of 2009 or 2002. By 2018, a 2013 Barsac is still young and primary. (Source needed: 2013 Sauternes vintage report)

## Producer style
Same as 2018_p3_w3 (Château Coutet): Premier Cru Classé Barsac, Baly family, 38 ha, 75/23/2 blend, Barsac style (lighter than Sauternes proper). See 2018_p3_w3 for full producer notes.

## Why it's in this exam
The younger vintage (2013) paired directly with the 2002 tests the candidate's ability to read maturity signals in Sauternes: colour (golden vs. amber), primary vs. secondary aromas, structural tightness vs. evolved softness, and lower vs. higher alcohol. The question should require sequencing the wines by vintage date and explaining the stylistic differences. Also tests knowledge of Barsac as a sub-appellation within Sauternes.
