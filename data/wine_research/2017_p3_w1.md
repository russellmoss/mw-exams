# White Zinfandel, Sutter Home, NV. California, USA

## Quick tasting profile
Pale salmon-pink colour with slight orange tint. Nose of strawberry candy, watermelon, melon, and confected red-berry sweetness; simple, forward, and immediately accessible. Light-bodied, noticeably sweet on the palate (typically ~20–30 g/L RS at this commercial tier), low acidity, very short finish. No tannin, no complexity. Textbook commercial off-dry rosé: sweet, fruit-forward, and approachable for wine novices.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Zinfandel (vinified as white/rosé by removing juice from skins immediately after crushing)
- Vineyard: Multi-regional California blend, primarily Central Valley sourcing at this commercial tier
- Soils: Source needed
- Vinification: Cold maceration then cold stabilisation; arrested fermentation to retain residual sugar; fining and sterile filtration
- Elevage: No oak; early release, stainless steel throughout
- ABV: 9.5% (low alcohol consistent with commercial White Zinfandel — arrested fermentation leaves RS and reduces ABV)
- RS: Approximately 20–30 g/L (Source needed for exact spec; Sutter Home website describes it as "noticeably sweet")

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage blend; no meaningful vintage character applicable. Produced for consistent commercial profile year-round.

## Producer style
Sutter Home is a large-volume California producer based in Napa Valley, owned by the Trinchero family. They claim to have created America's original White Zinfandel in 1972 — an accidentally sweet rosé that became the best-selling wine in the US during the 1980s and 1990s. White Zinfandel fundamentally changed American wine consumption patterns, bringing millions of consumers into wine for the first time via a sweet, accessible style. Source: sutterhome.com. This is unambiguously a commodity-tier, high-volume product with very wide supermarket distribution.

## Why it's in this exam
Sutter Home White Zinfandel is the P3 entry-level/commercial-sweet rosé benchmark — used to anchor the bottom of the quality spectrum in a mixed paper. Tests the candidate's ability to characterise off-dry rosé style, identify mass-market commercial positioning, low ABV, and distinguish from dry Provence rosé or sparkling rosé. The NV designation and California appellation (rather than an AVA) signal commercial sourcing. Key exam use: quality and commercial positioning analysis.
