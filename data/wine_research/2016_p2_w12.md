# Tributo Carménère, Caliterra, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple with ripe black plum, cassis, smoked paprika, green peppercorn, and sweet oak spice. The palate is full-bodied, dry, and plush-textured, with ripe tannins and a distinctly herbal edge that points toward Carménère rather than Merlot. In blind tasting the combination of dark fruit, soft richness, and leafy savour makes Chile a convincing call.

## Vintage character (2013 Colchagua)
The broad regional pattern in Colchagua was warm, ripe fruit with enough Pacific influence in better sites to preserve balance. For Carménère that usually means generous black-fruit character, supple tannins, and pronounced herbal complexity rather than severe greenness. ## Producer style
Caliterra occupies the polished premium-commercial space in Chile, producing clean, varietally clear wines with international appeal. The Tributo range sits above entry level and aims to show stronger site definition and more serious winemaking ambition, usually with oak support and riper tannin management. In MW practical terms it is a useful modern benchmark for varietal Carménère.
