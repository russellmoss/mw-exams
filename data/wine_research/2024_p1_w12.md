# Châteauneuf-du-Pape Blanc, Château de Nalys, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
Expect a full-bodied southern Rhône white with breadth, glycerol texture and warmth, but elevated by white flowers, citrus, stone fruit and a light toasty-vanilla edge rather than overt heaviness. The palate should be broad and layered, with Roussanne-Grenache Blanc richness, moderate acidity, clear oak influence and a long, noble finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 40% Roussanne, 40% Grenache Blanc, 18% Clairette, 2% Picardan
- Vineyard: fruit from four terroirs: Nalys, Bois Sénéchal, La Crau and Piedlong
- Soils: red sandstone and saffres at Nalys; ancient Rhône alluvial galets at Bois Sénéchal and La Crau; clay at Piedlong
- Vinification: manual harvest and sorting; pressing and clarification before vinification 70% in wood and 30% in stainless steel
- Elevage: 9 months, 70% in wood (barrels and demi-muids)

## Vintage character (2020 Châteauneuf-du-Pape Blanc)
2020 gave Nalys a wine the estate explicitly frames as one to keep, with concentration and complexity rather than simple early-drinking fruit. In blind tasting, this should point to a ripe southern profile with generous alcohol and texture, but the multi-terroir blend and partial steel vinification should keep the wine balanced enough to avoid flabbiness. It is a good exam bottle for testing whether the candidate can separate rich Mediterranean white blends from oak-shaped Bordeaux Blanc or Pyrenean whites.

## Producer style
Château de Nalys is one of Châteauneuf-du-Pape's oldest estates and has been owned by the Guigal family since 2017. The property farms 75 hectares across five distinct sectors and maintains the appellation's full cépage culture with particular seriousness. The grand vin blanc is built from four sites, dominated by Roussanne and Grenache Blanc, and uses significant but not total wood maturation to build density and complexity.
