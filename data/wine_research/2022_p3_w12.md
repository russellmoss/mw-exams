# Para 10 Year Grand Tawny, Seppeltsfield, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Tawny amber-mahogany with an olive-green rim (characteristic of oxidative tawny fortified wines in the Southern Hemisphere style); rich and nutty nose of dried fruit, Christmas cake, dates, toffee, orange peel, walnut, vanilla and a characteristic Australian rancio character; very sweet and concentrated (20.9% ABV — higher than Port), full-bodied with raisin and dried-fruit richness amplified by the Barossa heat; long, warming, complex finish. Broader and more richly fruited than Tawny Port at the same age designation; lacks Port's Douro-specific terroir expression.

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage fractional blend; the "Grand Tawny" range is Seppeltsfield's standard aged tawny expression across 10/20/30-year tiers. Barossa's hot climate produces rich, raisin-dominated fortified base wines with higher natural sugars than cooler regions, giving the final blend extra richness and concentration compared to equivalent Portuguese Tawny Port.

## Producer style
Seppeltsfield was founded in 1851 by Joseph Seppelt and remains one of Australia's most historic and iconic wine estates, particularly for its Para Tawny range. The estate is in the Barossa Valley's Seppeltsfield village and currently operates as a winery, cellar door and tourist destination. The 100-year Para Vintage Tawny (single vintage released annually) is one of Australia's most collectable and storied wines. The Para Grand Tawny 10-year represents the accessible entry point to the Para range. Source needed for current ownership details.
