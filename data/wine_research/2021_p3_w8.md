# Rutherglen Muscat Classic 12 Years, Stanton & Killeen, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep tawny-amber with olive-green rim — characteristic Rutherglen oxidative colour; intensely concentrated nose of roasted coffee, dates, dried orange peel, raisin, dark chocolate, licorice and Christmas cake — the hallmark Muscat Blanc à Petits Grains oxidative character amplified by solera-style aging. Full-bodied (18.5% ABV), very sweet, with extraordinary viscosity; acidity provides a spine beneath the intense sweetness; long, complex, warming finish. Nothing else in the wine world looks or tastes quite like mature Rutherglen Muscat.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Muscat Blanc à Petits Grains (known locally as Brown Muscat or Rutherglen Muscat)
- Vineyard: Rutherglen, northeast Victoria; old-vine plantings on red loam soils
- Vinification: Harvested very late for maximum concentration and sugar; partial fermentation then fortification with neutral spirit
- Elevage: Fractional blending (solera-like) system in small old oak barrels exposed to temperature extremes; the "Classic" tier represents average 12 years of barrel aging
- Rutherglen classification (4 tiers): Rutherglen → Classic → Grand → Rare (Source: common knowledge)
- ABV / RS / TA: 18.5% / very sweet / Source needed

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage fractional blend. The "Classic 12 Years" designation indicates the average age of the blended parcels — not a single vintage. Rutherglen Muscats gain complexity through the temperature-cycling of the weatherboard warehouses (hot Australian summers evaporate water, concentrating the wine further).

## Producer style
Stanton & Killeen is one of Rutherglen's long-established family estates, producing the region's iconic fortified wines — Muscat and Topaque (Muscadelle) — alongside table wines. They are a respected, mid-tier Rutherglen producer. Source needed for specific estate details. The Rutherglen region's fortified wine style has no direct equivalent anywhere in the world.

## Why it's in this exam
Rutherglen Muscat is one of Australia's most distinctive and examination-worthy wines — a highly identifiable category for MW candidates due to its extreme richness, solera-blended NV character, and unique aromatic profile.
