# Cuvée Juveniles, Torbreck, 2014. Barossa Valley, Australia

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple. Nose of ripe dark plum, blackberry jam, black olive, chocolate, white pepper (Shiraz), iron/mineral, and spice; generous Barossa fruit concentration with old-vine depth. Full-bodied, plush velvety tannins, warm integrated acidity, rich, long, spiced finish. Classic Barossa GSM (Grenache/Shiraz/Mataro) blend profile — the Rhône varieties suit this warm, arid valley floor terroir perfectly.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Grenache, Shiraz, and Mataro (Mourvèdre) blend — the classic Barossa/Southern Rhône GSM trinity; Juveniles cuvée is Torbreck's Grenache-dominant blend (Source needed for exact 2014 percentages; typically approximately 60% Grenache / 30% Shiraz / 10% Mataro)
- Vineyard: Old vine Barossa Valley fruit — Torbreck works with heritage vineyards, many pre-phylloxera, planted from 1860s–1930s
- Soils: Barossa Valley red-brown earth and clay soils over ironstone; old vine Grenache on Eden Valley and Barossa floor
- Vinification: Traditional; open fermenters; foot-stomping or minimal intervention; no fining (Source needed for Juveniles specific protocol)
- Elevage: Old large French oak foudres or older barriques; no new oak in Juveniles tier (consistent with Torbreck's stated "deliberately hands-off" style; Source needed for exact regime)
- ABV: 14.5%

## Vintage character (2014 Barossa Valley)
2014 was a generally warm vintage in the Barossa Valley, producing wines with concentrated dark fruit and full body — characteristic of Barossa in good years. Old-vine GSM from Torbreck in 2014 would show generous warmth and depth typical of the region and vintage.

## Producer style
Torbreck Vintners was founded in 1994 by David Powell (named after a Scottish forest) in the Barossa Valley, focused exclusively on Shiraz, Grenache, and Mataro from heritage old-vine vineyards. The estate's philosophy is "deliberately hands-off and traditional" — respecting the old vines' low-yielding character by non-interventionist winemaking. Flagship wines include The Laird (single-vineyard Shiraz, ~$850), RunRig (Shiraz/Viognier), and various Grenache-dominant blends including Juveniles. The brand is at the premium-prestige end of Barossa, though Juveniles is in the more accessible tier. Source: torbreck.com.

## Why it's in this exam
Cuvée Juveniles represents the benchmark Barossa Valley GSM blend in P3's red still wines section — used to test identification of Grenache/Shiraz/Mataro blending in the New World vs. southern Rhône analogues, and Barossa Valley regional recognition. The 14.5% ABV, old-vine concentration, and the distinctive warm-climate GSM profile are key signals. The name "Juveniles" is a tribute to a celebrated Paris wine bar (Willi's Wine Bar off-shoot) — a French connection that adds cultural depth.
