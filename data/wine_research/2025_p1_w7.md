# Muscadet Sèvre-et-Maine Monnières Saint-Fiacre l'Ancestrale, Vignobles Günther-Chéreau, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold to medium gold colour given extended lees ageing. Nose of orange marmalade, roasted white peach, honeysuckle, wax, and baking spice with smoky/gunflint mineral undertones. Palate is fleshy and creamy-textured with lemon-tinged richness, Asian pear, honeydew melon, and a long finish marked by subtle bitterness and saline minerality. Significantly more complex and weighty than standard Muscadet sur lie, yet retains freshness and dry precision at 12% ABV.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Melon de Bourgogne
- Vineyard: Cru Communal plots in Monnières-Saint-Fiacre; vines reportedly 100+ years old (source: Decanter review of 2015 vintage)
- Soils: Decomposed gneiss with clay, over geological faults linking Saint-Fiacre, Monnières, and Maisdon
- Vinification: Hand-harvested; 24 hours skin contact before pressing; fermentation with indigenous yeasts in temperature-controlled stainless steel; no oak (source: De Maison Selections importer sheet)
- Elevage: Extended lees ageing in underground cement/stainless tanks -- minimum 24 months sur lie required by the Cru Communal rules, but l'Ancestrale typically sees significantly longer (reportedly 4+ years on lees for the 2015; likely 3-4 years for the 2018) (source: Decanter)
- ABV / RS / TA: 12% / dry / Source needed
- Yields: Maximum 45 hl/ha for Cru Communal status

## Vintage character (2018 Loire / Pays Nantais)
2018 was a generous, warm vintage across the Loire. A successful spring flowering was followed by June mildew pressure from heavy rains, but a hot, dry summer allowed full recovery. Harvest conditions were excellent, with growers picking at leisure. Whites tend to show slightly lower acidity than average but compensate with ripe aromatics and generous body. For Muscadet, 2018 produced balanced, aromatic wines with good concentration -- well suited to the extended lees ageing demanded by Cru Communal bottlings. (source: Wine-Searcher 2018 Loire vintage report; Winedoctor Loire 2018)

## Producer style
Vignobles Günther-Chéreau is a three-generation family domaine run by Véronique Günther-Chéreau and her daughter Aurore, spanning 75 hectares across three properties: Château du Coing (Saint-Fiacre, 45ha), Château de la Gravelle (Gorges, 17ha), and Grand Fief de la Cormeraie (Monnières, 8ha). Converting to certified organic since 2010. The estate vinifies each plot separately in underground vats, emphasizing terroir expression through extended lees contact rather than oak. Their range spans simple sur lie Muscadets through to premium Cru Communal bottlings like l'Ancestrale, which represents the top of their hierarchy. Soils across the properties include two-mica gneiss, gabbro, and micaschist, producing wines ranging from floral/fruity (sur lie) to smoky/gunflint (cru communal). Market positioning is mid-premium for Muscadet -- l'Ancestrale retails around $39 USD / under GBP 20, offering notable value for its complexity. (source: vgc.fr; thewinedoctor.com)

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 7 in 2025 Paper 1, Question 3: "Wines 7-9 are from the same country and different single grape varieties." Paired with Riesling (Rolly Gassmann, Alsace) and Chenin Blanc (Domaine Huet, Vouvray demi-sec), all from France. The question asks candidates to identify origin closely with reference to grape variety, comment on production method, and assess style/quality/maturity. Melon de Bourgogne is a curveball variety -- rarely encountered outside Muscadet, and this Cru Communal expression with extended lees ageing will present as significantly richer and more complex than commodity Muscadet, potentially misleading candidates toward Burgundy Chardonnay or aged Chenin. The low 12% ABV and bone-dry finish are the key tells for Melon/Muscadet.
