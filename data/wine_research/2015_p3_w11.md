# Tokaji Aszú 5 Puttonyos, Royal Tokaji Wine Company, 2008

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber-gold with orange highlights; complex botrytis-driven nose of apricot jam, dried orange peel, honey, beeswax, saffron, ginger, and oxidative notes from long cask aging. Palate: rich and sweet but lifted by very high acidity (the Furmint contribution); the 5-puttonyos level (~150–180 g/L residual sugar) delivers concentrated sweetness without treacle-like heaviness. Long finish of dried apricot and mandarin. 11.5% ABV reflects the very high sugar content limiting fermentation.

## Technical specs
- Grape varieties: botrytised Furmint (primary), Hárslevelű, and/or Muscat de Lunel (aszú paste); base wine from non-botrytised Furmint/Hárslevelű
- Vinification: aszú berries individually hand-harvested; trampled into paste (göncölszekér historically); must or base wine macerated on the paste; transferred to göncii oak barrels for fermentation and aging
- Classification: 5 puttonyos = ~150–180 g/L RS; minimum 120 g/L total dry extract per current regulations; minimum aging 3 years (2 in oak)

## Vintage character (2008 Tokaj)
2008 was a classic Tokaji vintage — warm, humid autumn promoting botrytis development across the Tokaj-Hegyalja hillsides. Late harvest conditions produced excellent aszú berries with concentrated sugars. The Royal Tokaji 2008 5 Puttonyos has been well-regarded in critical reviews as a typical great Tokaj vintage expression. By 2015 (7 years) this wine was drinking in its early maturity window. ## Producer style
Royal Tokaji Wine Company founded 1990 by Hugh Johnson, Peter Vinding-Diers, and other investors — one of the first private Tokaji producers after Communist-era collectivisation. Based in Mád. Pioneered single-vineyard Aszú bottlings (First Growth classifications). Positioned as the prestige benchmark for modern Tokaji internationally. The 5 Puttonyos is the mid-tier of the range (above 3-puttonyos, below 6-puttonyos and Eszencia). (Source: general knowledge — producer website inaccessible, royaltokaji.com was a parking page)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 3 includes sweet wines. Tokaji Aszú is one of the world's canonical botrytised sweet wines alongside Sauternes and TBA/Beerenauslese. The 5-puttonyos level has high RS with very high acidity — distinguishable from Sauternes by the Furmint's elevated acid, from German TBA by the Hungarian oak aging character, from Vouvray Moelleux by the deeper colour and botrytis intensity.
