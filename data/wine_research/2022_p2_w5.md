# Chianti Classico Brolio, Barone Ricasoli, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Expect sour cherry, red plum, dried herbs, iris and savory oak spice with bright acidity and medium tannin. The wine should feel more lifted, angular and food-shaped than Brunello, with less depth but more immediate territorial clarity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Sangiovese 80%, Merlot 15%, Cabernet Sauvignon 5%
- Vineyard: estate vineyards from five geological areas between about 290 and 500m
- Soils: varied, all with abundant stony material
- Vinification: stainless steel fermentation at 24-27C with 12-16 days on skins
- Elevage: about 9 months in second- and third-passage tonneaux
- ABV / RS / TA: exam corpus lists 13.5%; Source needed / Source needed

## Vintage character (2017 Chianti Classico)
Ricasoli describes 2017 as an atypical year marked by April frost, drought and prolonged heat, followed by relieving late-summer rain that enabled good ripening. In tasting terms that points to concentration and warmth, but also to producers needing to preserve freshness and protect fruit from sun stress.

## Producer style
Barone Ricasoli is one of Chianti Classico's historic names, rooted in Brolio and strongly associated with the region's modern identity. Brolio is not the estate's most elevated bottling, but it is a serious regional expression designed to show polished, contemporary Chianti Classico structure.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 5 starts 2022 Paper 2 Question 2, where two Sangiovese-based wines from Tuscany but different sub-regions are compared. Brolio provides the Chianti Classico reference point: fresher, more acid-driven and less massive than the single-vineyard Brunello that follows.
