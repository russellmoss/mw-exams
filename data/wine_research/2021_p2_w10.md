# Cotes du Rhone, Guigal, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby with blackberry, black cherry, pepper and spice, framed by polished oak. The palate is dry, medium to full-bodied and smoothly commercial, with ripe fruit and supple tannins. It reads clearly southern Rhône but at a more accessible, less profound level than top cru bottlings.

## Vintage character (2017 Cotes du Rhone)
Guigal notes that 2017 was harder to source than 2016, implying lower availability but still enough good fruit for the blend. Stylistically the wine should be generous, ripe and ready earlier than more tannic or more concentrated Rhône vintages.

## Producer style
Guigal is one of the Rhône's defining houses, with exceptional prestige at the top and reliable brand recognition at entry and mid tiers. The regional Côtes du Rhône bottling is a global ambassador for the house style: ripe fruit, polished élevage and broad appeal.
