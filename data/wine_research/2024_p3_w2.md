# Cava Reserva Brut Nature, Avinyó, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Straw-yellow with soft golden reflections. Fine, persistent bead. Clean, balanced nose with apple, citrus, white peach notes and tertiary tones of brioche and almond. Palate is light to medium-bodied, dry (Brut Nature — zero dosage), with refreshing acidity, subtle lees-aging nuances, and a mineral, slightly saline finish. Modest autolytic complexity compared to longer-aged sparkling wines.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Macabeo 60%, Xarel-lo 25%, Parellada 15% (source: Delectable/importer)
- Vineyard: Can Fontanals estate, organically certified. Vines planted 1945–2017, at 250–275m elevation (source: De Maison Selections)
- Soils: Deep, well-drained, loamy-silty texture with calcium carbonate accumulations (source: Avinyó)
- Vinification: Traditional method (méthode traditionnelle). Estate-grown, produced, and bottled.
- Élevage: Minimum 24 months on lees (Reserva category); many bottlings receive 36+ months. Brut Nature — zero dosage.
- ABV / RS / TA: 12% / ~0 g/L (Brut Nature) / Source needed

## Vintage character (2018 Penedès)
Winter and spring of 2018 were exceptionally dry. Rain at end of August and beginning of September brought freshness to the wines. The vintage produced Cavas with good fruit expression and refreshing acidity.

## Producer style
Avinyó is a small family estate in Avinyonet del Penedès, established by a family that has lived there since 1597. They sold grapes to large Cava houses until the 1970s, bottling their first Cava in 1979. All production is from organically certified estate-grown grapes. Traditional Cava varieties (Xarel-lo, Macabeo, Parellada) dominate, with some Pinot Noir for rosé/blanc de noirs. All wines qualify as de Guarda Superior (vintage-dated, 18+ months lees aging). Artisanal positioning — independent, terroir-driven, contrasting with industrial Cava.
