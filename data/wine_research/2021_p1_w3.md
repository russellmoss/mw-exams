# Inopia Blanc, Rotem & Mounir Saouma, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold with some concentration; aromatic and generous nose with white Rhône character — Grenache Blanc and/or Clairette and Viognier-influenced profile showing peach, apricot, garrigue herbs, white flower and possibly some oak spice. Full-bodied and broad on the palate, with the warm 2018 vintage amplifying richness at 14% ABV; moderate acidity, textured mid-palate, medium-long finish. This is a high-quality, atypical Côtes du Rhône-Villages white that challenges candidates expecting simpler Rhône Blanc.

## Vintage character (2018 Southern Rhône)
2018 was a warm, generous vintage across the Southern Rhône, producing ripe, full-bodied whites with high alcohol. Yields were good and disease pressure low following a warm dry summer. White Rhône wines of 2018 are rich and immediate with tropical stone-fruit character and good weight, though acidity can be lower than cooler years.

## Producer style
Rotem & Mounir Saouma are Lebanese-origin négociant-producers who source and vinify wines from various appellations including Châteauneuf-du-Pape, Côtes du Rhône and Rhône-Villages. They are known for making premium, terroir-focused wines at AOC village level that punch above their appellation status.
