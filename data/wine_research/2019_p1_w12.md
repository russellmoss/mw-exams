# Vecchio Samperi, Marco de Bartoli, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber with a powerful oxidative nose of walnut, rancio, orange peel, curry spice and salted caramel, but without sweetness in the finish. The palate is dry, concentrated and long, with high savoury intensity and a complex, maderised character. Blind, it can resemble old dry sherry or traditional Marsala, but with its own Sicilian identity.

## Vintage character (NV / oxidative ageing wine)
This is not a vintage-dated wine in the ordinary sense, so classic annual weather commentary matters less than the long oxidative ageing regime. The wine's identity comes from Grillo, cask ageing, concentration and the producer's revival of historical Marsala practice.

## Producer style
Marco de Bartoli is one of Sicily's iconic artisanal producers and a key figure in the restoration of traditional Marsala-region wine culture. Vecchio Samperi is among the estate's signature wines: uncompromising, oxidative, terroir-marked and aimed squarely at serious drinkers rather than broad commercial sweetness.

## Why it's in this exam
In 2019 Paper 1 Question 3, this wine serves as the second high-distinctiveness outlier beside the Georgian Chinuri. It tests recognition of oxidative ageing, old-cask development and dry fortified-adjacent styling without relying on an obvious modern DOC label cue.
