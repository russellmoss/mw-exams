# Clos du Bourg Demi-Sec, Domaine Huet, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
Light gold with quince, ripe apple, yellow plum, chamomile, and chalky mineral notes rather than overt botrytis or fortification markers. The palate should show noticeable residual sugar, but balanced by firm Chenin acidity and phenolic grip, making it taste demi-sec rather than fully dessert-sweet. In blind tasting it should feel tense, structured, and ageworthy.

## Vintage character (2020 Vouvray)
The 2020 bottling is presented by merchants as concentrated and still youthful, which is consistent with a ripe Loire Chenin year that nonetheless retained Huet's hallmark tension. The key exam point is balance: this is a wine with real sugar, but also enough acidity and structure to avoid the lush texture of Sauternes or Icewine.

## Producer style
Domaine Huet is one of Vouvray's benchmark estates and Clos du Bourg one of its canonical sites. Merchant notes across multiple vintages stress chalk-rooted tension, precision, and long ageing capacity, while the demi-sec style sits at the estate's classic middle point between fully dry and moelleux expressions.
