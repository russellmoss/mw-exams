# Margaux, Chateau Rauzan-Segla, 2005

## Quick tasting profile
Fully mature but still structured claret. Expect cassis, cedar, graphite, cigar box, dried flowers and savoury earth over fine but persistent tannin. The palate should be long, poised and aristocratic rather than massive, with the perfume and finesse expected of top Margaux.

## Vintage character (2005 Bordeaux)
2005 is one of the benchmark modern Bordeaux vintages: dry, sunny and evenly ripened, producing concentrated wines with ripe tannin, freshness and serious longevity. That profile explains why this bottle was ideal exam material even with bottle age, combining tertiary development with classical structure.

## Producer style
Rauzan-Segla is a Second Growth estate in Margaux owned by Chanel since 1994. The house emphasis is on precision, elegance and ageworthiness from Margaux's gravelly terroirs, aiming for perfume and refinement without sacrificing depth.

## Why it's in this exam
In 2023 Paper 2 Question 2, it served as the mature fine-wine reference point: a classified-growth Bordeaux where oak, bottle evolution and market position all matter, contrasting sharply with commercial new-world or less ageworthy wines in the same question.
