# Château Coutet, 2002. Sauternes-Barsac, Bordeaux, France.

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber-gold, viscous, highly aromatic. Nose: apricot jam, orange marmalade, crème brûlée, vanilla oak, dried mango, and a honeyed Botrytis complexity. By 2018 (16 years on), secondary notes of toffee, nuts, and dried fruit are prominent. Palate: full, sweet, and rich but with the Barsac style — lighter body and greater freshness than Sauternes proper (Coutet has more Sauvignon Blanc than some Sauternes châteaux, giving acidity). High alcohol (14%) is a key structural element. Very long finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 75% Sémillon, 23% Sauvignon Blanc, 2% Muscadelle (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Château_Coutet)
- Vineyard: 38 hectares in Barsac; location between Garonne and Ciron rivers supports autumn mist and Botrytis development
- Vinification: Botrytis-affected harvested grapes; multiple selective passes (tries); fermented in French oak
- Elevage: New French oak barrels; extended aging; approximately 4,500 cases/year for the Grand Vin
- ABV / RS / TA: 14% / very high RS (>100 g/L) / high
*(Premium cuvée Madame produced in exceptional years: ~700 cases)*

## Vintage character (2002 Barsac/Sauternes)
2002 was a classic year for Sauternes and Barsac — wet autumn conditions following a dry summer provided ideal Botrytis spread. Quality is consistently cited as excellent. The wines have good concentration and balancing acidity. By 2018, a 2002 Barsac Premier Cru is in full secondary maturity, showing marmalade and dried fruit complexity alongside integrated oak. (Source needed: dedicated 2002 Sauternes vintage report)

## Producer style
Château Coutet is a Premier Cru Classé in Barsac, described by David Peppercorn as a "twin" to Château Climens. The Baly family has owned it since 1977 following Henry-Louis Guy's 1923 purchase. The estate is Barsac's second estate after Climens in the hierarchy, and is noted for slightly lighter body and more freshness than Sauternes AOP proper — the Barsac style. Second wine: Chartreuse de Coutet. Exceptional years produce Cuvée Madame (~700 cases). (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Château_Coutet)

## Why it's in this exam
Paired with the 2013 (w4) in a classic vintage comparison exercise — the question asks candidates to identify both as Sauternes-Barsac Premier Cru, distinguish the mature (2002) from the younger (2013) expression, and comment on structural differences. The 14% ABV of 2002 vs. 13.5% for 2013 is one signal. The deeper amber colour of the mature wine is another. Coutet's Barsac style (lighter than Yquem) adds a regional specificity test.
