# Chateau Ducru-Beaucaillou, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-black with ripe cassis, violet, cedar, graphite and sweet spice. The palate is fuller, richer and more seamless than the 2013, with abundant but ripe tannin, concentrated dark fruit and a long cedar-lined finish. This is classical Saint-Julien scaled up by a warm, successful vintage.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 95% Cabernet Sauvignon, 5% Merlot
- Vineyard: Estate fruit from Saint-Julien gravel ridges
- Soils: Gunzian gravel with marl and stone
- Vinification: Cabernet harvested in the first week of October after a long ripening cycle
- Elevage: Raised in 100% new barrels
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2015 Saint-Julien)
2015 is widely regarded as a strong Left Bank vintage, combining ripe tannins with freshness and concentration. That profile aligns perfectly with this wine's richer fruit core and more complete structure than the 2013 while still remaining clearly Médoc rather than overripe.

## Producer style
Ducru-Beaucaillou's signature is luxurious but disciplined Cabernet-based Saint-Julien, usually with high-quality new oak and a silky tannic frame. In strong years like 2015, that style becomes especially complete, marrying perfume, power and ageing potential.

## Why it's in this exam
In the 2021 Paper 2 Question 1 vertical, this functions as the youthful high-quality modern benchmark. It sits between the accessible 2013 and the mature 2005, making vintage and maturity comparison central to the answer.
