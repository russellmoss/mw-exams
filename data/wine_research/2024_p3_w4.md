# L'Etoile, Domaine de Montbourgeau, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold. Complex, oxidative nose — ripe apricot, white peach, nectarine, toasted walnuts, chestnuts, dried flowers (acacia, chamomile), white button mushrooms, major salinity. Palate is textured and rich with apple skin, crushed hazelnuts, mountain herbs, chalk, lemon/lime juice, salty mineral finish. Not full Vin Jaune character but displays clear oxidative influence from non-topped barrel aging. High ABV (14.5%) contributes to weight and richness. Long, nutty, saline finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: ~95% Chardonnay, ~5% Savagnin (interplanted; exact proportions vary by vintage)
- Vineyard: 9 hectares total at domaine; L'Etoile Chardonnay from ~40-year-old organic vines
- Soils: Clay and limestone with fossilised starfish (pentacrinites) — characteristic of L'Etoile
- Vinification: Hand-harvested. Fermented in stainless steel cuves with indigenous yeasts.
- Élevage: Racked into old foudres, demi-muids, and small barrels (virtually no new oak). Never racked during aging. **Not topped off** (non-ouillé) — wines develop partial voile (flor). Aged 24–30 months. No fining or filtration mentioned.
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2018 Jura)
2018 was a warm, generous vintage across France. In the Jura, it produced ripe, rich, concentrated whites with lower acidity than cooler years. Ideal conditions for the oxidative aging regime — the higher-alcohol, richer base wines develop well under voile. Source needed for specific L'Etoile 2018 vintage details.

## Producer style
Domaine de Montbourgeau has produced traditional Jura wines since Victor Gros first planted in 1920. Third-generation Nicole Deriaux has run the estate since 1986. The 9-hectare domaine in L'Etoile is planted mostly to Chardonnay with 1.7 ha of Savagnin, plus some Trousseau and Poulsard. Strictly traditional winemaking: whites are aged non-ouillé (not topped up), developing partial oxidative character without being full Vin Jaune. Organic viticulture. All vinified in cellars beneath the family home. US import: Rosenthal Wine Merchant. A benchmark for L'Etoile — the less well-known but high-quality Jura appellation.

## Why it's in this exam
Paired with Wine 3 (Touraize Chardonnay 2021) in Paper 3 Q2: "Wines 3 and 4 are from the same region." Both are Jura whites, but they represent opposite winemaking philosophies: Wine 3 is ouillé (topped-up), fresh, and reductive; Wine 4 is non-ouillé, oxidative, and nutty. The candidate must identify the Jura region and compare/contrast the two winemaking approaches. The higher ABV (14.5% vs 12.5%) and the oxidative character are key differentiators. Tests understanding of ouillé vs sous-voile and their impact on Jura white wine style.
