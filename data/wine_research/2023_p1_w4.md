# Semillon Granito, Julio Bouchon, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Medium lemon. This should show a broader, more textural Semillon shape than Hunter Valley examples: ripe citrus, orchard fruit, subtle oak seasoning, and a dry but layered palate with moderate body, moderate acidity, and a savoury granite-derived feel. Blind, it reads as barrel-influenced white from a warm, dry climate rather than a neutral, razor-acid expression (source: producer and importer tech sheets).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Semillon (source: tech sheet)
- Vineyard: Cuartel 1 of the Batuco vineyard in Maule secano interior, around 30 km from the Pacific at 183 m, from unirrigated old vines planted in the 1940s (source: producer page)
- Soils: granite-derived site; exact soil wording on the cited pages is limited, but the cuvee name and producer framing are granite-led (source: producer page)
- Vinification: whole-bunch pressing in a vertical press; fermented in French oak at 15-18C for 14 days with native yeast (source: tech sheet)
- Elevage: 70% in 2,500L foudre and 30% in French barrels for 12 months (source: tech sheet)
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / 1.23 g/L / 6.3 g/L (source: tech sheet)

## Vintage character (2019 Maule / Chile)
Bibendum's Chile 2019 harvest report described the vintage as one to explore for regionality, with strong quality and complexity in several Chilean regions. In this context, Maule's old-vine, dry-farmed material could deliver concentration without losing identity, which fits a serious, terroir-led white rather than a simple fruity export style (source: Bibendum).

## Producer style
Julio Bouchon is a Maule-focused family producer working old vines in the secano interior, with a strong emphasis on dry farming, coastal influence, and heritage vineyards. The Granito range is explicitly site-driven, built around old unirrigated vines and elevage choices that add texture while still presenting the wine as a vineyard expression (source: producer page and tech sheet).

## Why it's in this exam
This is wine 4 in 2023 Paper 1 Question 1, paired with Tyrrell's Semillon. It is the richer, oak-shaped, warmer-climate half of the Semillon pair and tests whether candidates can still see the variety through a very different textural and winemaking lens.
