# Torrontés, Susanna Balbo, 2016. Valle de Uco, Mendoza, Argentina.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw with golden highlights. Nose: explosively aromatic — rose petal, lychee, peach, apricot, and Muscat-like floral character. Initially may suggest off-dry sweetness but the palate reveals dry (RS <3 g/L), fresh, moderate acidity, medium body at 13%. The Muscat-like aromatics are intrinsic to the variety, not from RS. Finish is clean but relatively short. This is the Argentina signature white grape.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Torrontés (likely Torrontés Riojano, the most aromatic and widely planted Argentine type)
- Vineyard: Valle de Uco, Mendoza — high-altitude subregion (800-1100m); significant diurnal temperature variation preserves aromatics and acidity
- Vinification: Cold fermentation in stainless steel to preserve aromatics; no oak
- Elevage: No oak; early bottling for freshness; designed for immediate consumption
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% / dry / medium

## Vintage character (2016 Mendoza / Valle de Uco)
2016 was a good vintage in Mendoza with balanced conditions — not the extreme heat of 2015 in the US. Valle de Uco's altitude provides natural freshness. Torrontés at this elevation retains perfumed aromatics with firm acidity — better balance than lower-altitude Mendoza. (Source needed: 2016 Mendoza vintage report)

## Producer style
Susanna Balbo is one of Argentina's pioneering winemakers — the first woman to graduate as an oenologist from Universidad Nacional de Cuyo, Mendoza. Her Dominio del Plata winery (established 1999) produces multiple tiers; the Crios de Susana Balbo brand (note: different from the Susanna Balbo Wines label on this wine) was her entry-level range. This 2018 exam wine carries the "Susanna Balbo" signature label, positioning it above the Crios tier. The estate focuses on authentically Argentine varieties and styles. The linked wine 2016_p1_w9 is "Crios de Susana Balbo Torrontés, Dominio del Plata, 2015" — a slightly earlier vintage of a related expression. (Source needed: Susanna Balbo Wines producer website)

## Why it's in this exam
Torrontés from Argentina is one of the most distinctive aromatic whites globally. Its Muscat-like floral explosion on the nose is unmistakable. The Valle de Uco provenance is relevant (altitude freshness vs. lower Mendoza). This is a test of variety identification — can the candidate distinguish Torrontés from Muscat, Gewürztraminer, and Viognier, all of which share floral/lychee character but differ in structure, origin, and acid profile?
