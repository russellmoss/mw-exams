# Chambolle-Musigny, Domaine Roumier, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Transparent ruby with red cherry, rose, spice, and fine earth. The palate should be precise and silky rather than powerful, with fine tannins, high but polished acidity, and the perfume and finesse that make Chambolle-Musigny distinct from firmer neighboring villages.

## Vintage character (2014 Burgundy reds)
2014 red Burgundy is generally regarded as fresh, pure, and classically proportioned. The better wines show bright fruit, aromatic lift, and balance more than sheer scale, which suits Chambolle especially well.

## Producer style
Domaine Roumier is one of Burgundy's benchmark domaines, known for precision, perfume, and transparency to site. Even at village level, the wines are expected to show refinement and ageworthy structure rather than obvious extraction.

## Why it's in this exam
This was one of the classic Western European benchmarks in 2018 paper 2. It tests fine-boned Pinot recognition and whether the taster can place Chambolle's texture and perfume within the Cote de Nuits hierarchy.
