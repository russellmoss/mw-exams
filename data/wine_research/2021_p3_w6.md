# Vintage Port, Graham's, 2000

## Quick tasting profile
Deep garnet-ruby with developing brick/orange rim after 21 years of bottle aging (assessed 2021); complex evolved nose of dried cherry, raisin, walnut, coffee, tobacco, cedar and dark chocolate — a fully mature Vintage Port. Full-bodied (20% ABV), sweet and rich with silky, integrated tannins from bottle development; concentrated and long, with a fine, warming finish. The structural development — brick rim, tertiary notes, softened grip — signals extended bottle maturation rather than a young VP.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Traditional Douro blend — Touriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinta Barroca, Tinto Cão (Source: common knowledge; exact Graham's 2000 blend Source needed)
- Vinification: Rapid extraction in granite lagares or modern robotic lagares; fortification with neutral grape spirit (aguardente) mid-fermentation when ~90 g/L RS remains, raising ABV to 20%
- Elevage: Typically 2 years in large old wood (lodge or at Douro estate) before bottling; thereafter all aging in bottle for 20+ years
- ABV / RS / TA: 20% / sweet (~90-100 g/L RS) / high acidity
- 2000 was a declared vintage year for Graham's (Source: common knowledge)

## Vintage character (2000 Port)
2000 was a widely declared, well-regarded vintage for Port — good balance of fruit concentration and acidity. Not the most celebrated of the decade (2003, 2007, 2011 are ranked higher) but represents a classic, mature drinking-window vintage for Vintage Port. At 21 years of age (assessed 2021), the 2000 would be approaching full maturity, showing the softer tannins and evolved nose expected.

## Producer style
Graham's (W & J Graham & Co.), founded 1820, is one of the Symington family's portfolio of Port houses. A respected mid-tier to premium Vintage Port producer with consistent declarations. Their house style tends toward power and concentration with age-worthy structure. The Quinta dos Malvedos estate in the Upper Douro is their flagship vineyard.
