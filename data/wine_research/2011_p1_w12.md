# Monopole, CVNE, 2008

## Quick tasting profile
Dry white Rioja with apple, citrus and a faint savory note, likely broader and more evolved than neutral stainless whites of similar age. Depending on élevage, it may show subtle nutty or waxy development, but the main read should still be dry, medium-bodied and traditional rather than overtly modern aromatic.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Primarily Viura/Macabeo is the classic Monopole model
- Vineyard: Rioja fruit from the CVNE network and estate holdings around Haro/Rioja Alta and Rioja Alavesa

## Vintage character (2008 Rioja Blanco)
By the time of the exam, this wine would likely have developed some secondary complexity while remaining structurally straightforward. That made it useful as a classic but not ultra-oxidative white Rioja reference in a mixed-identification set.

## Producer style
CVNE is one of Rioja’s historic houses, and Monopole is an especially important label because it dates back to the early 20th century and became one of Spain’s famous dry white brands. The wine matters less as a luxury icon than as a historical and stylistic benchmark for dry white Rioja.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 12 closes the mixed bag with a classic Old World white that is easier to miss than its famous name suggests. It tests whether candidates can identify white Rioja from texture and development rather than relying only on overt oxidative markers.
