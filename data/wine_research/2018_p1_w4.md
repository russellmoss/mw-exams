# Hunting Hill Chardonnay, Kumeu River, 2015. Auckland, New Zealand.

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold. Nose: concentrated white peach, toasted hazelnut, gentle Burgundian-influenced oak with struck-flint mineral character. Palate: full-bodied (14% ABV), creamy texture, savoury lees complexity, firm backbone of acidity. Single-vineyard precision distinguishes this from the broader estate blend — more focused and taut than some Napa peers. Persistent, complex finish. Style deliberately Burgundian in aspiration.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Hunting Hill Vineyard, Kumeu — single vineyard site; clay and alluvial soils
- Vinification: Whole-cluster pressing; barrel fermentation in French oak; full malolactic fermentation typical of the house
- Elevage: Extended lees aging with periodic bâtonnage; Burgundian methods applied
- ABV / RS / TA: 14% / dry / medium-high

## Vintage character (2015 Auckland / Kumeu)
2015 was a warm, ripe vintage in Kumeu with good sugar accumulation and full phenolic maturity. Auckland's humid maritime climate can present disease pressure; 2015 was generally favourable. Single-vineyard wines like Hunting Hill show more textured, concentrated fruit in warm years like 2015. (Source needed: 2015 New Zealand vintage report)

## Producer style
Kumeu River, founded by the Brajkovich family, is New Zealand's most celebrated Chardonnay producer. Michael Brajkovich MW was awarded a 2024 New Zealand Winegrowers Fellowship in recognition of his contributions to winemaking. The estate makes multiple single-vineyard Chardonnays (Rays Road, Maté's Vineyard, Hunting Hill) each representing distinct terroir expressions. Their winemaking philosophy is explicitly modelled on Burgundy — whole-cluster pressing, barrel fermentation, full malo, lees aging. They are regularly described as producing New Zealand's finest Chardonnay and frequently command prices in the $40-80 NZD range at release. (source: kumeuriver.co.nz)

## Why it's in this exam
Included as a premium New World Chardonnay with a Burgundian soul — a test of whether candidates can differentiate New Zealand terroir from Burgundy and Napa when all three styles are in close proximity. At 14% ABV with pronounced lees character, it sits between Shafer (opulent California) and William Fèvre (austere Chablis). The single-vineyard Auckland provenance is the distinctive key signal.
