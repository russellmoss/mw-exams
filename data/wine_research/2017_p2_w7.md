# Barolo, Pio Cesare, 2012. Piemonte, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-deep garnet with orange-brick tints at 5 years of age. Nose of tar, roses (Rosa centifolia — quintessential Nebbiolo descriptor), dried cherry, leather, tobacco, violets, and complex earthy-mineral notes; tertiary leather and forest floor emerging. Full-bodied, astringent, firm tannins (hallmark of Nebbiolo/Barolo — among the highest tannin of any red variety), high acidity, long persistent finish. The 2012 vintage would give additional warmth and ripeness to Pio Cesare's already generous house style.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Nebbiolo
- Vineyard: Multi-village blend (Pio Cesare's standard Barolo sources from multiple Barolo MGA communes including Serralunga d'Alba, Barolo, La Morra); sourced across the appellation (Source needed for 2012 specific vineyard sourcing)
- Soils: Mix of Tortonian (more compact, higher tannin) and Helvetian (lighter, more aromatic) MGA soils depending on parcel (Source needed)
- Vinification: Pio Cesare uses a blend of traditional long maceration and modern rotary fermenter techniques — the house sits stylistically between traditional and modernist Barolo; significant maceration for tannin management
- Elevage: Mix of large Slavonian oak casks and smaller French barriques (Pio Cesare's signature hybrid approach); approximately 24 months total (Source needed for exact 2012 regime)
- ABV: 14.0%

## Vintage character (2012 Barolo / Piemonte)
2012 was a warm, challenging vintage in Piemonte — a severe drought in summer caused water stress in many vineyards, producing wines of high alcohol, concentrated dark fruit, and ripe, powerful tannins. It is considered a controversial vintage: some producers delivered spectacular, deeply concentrated wines; others struggled with over-ripeness and coarseness. Pio Cesare's multi-source Barolo in 2012 would show the warmth and richness of the year but benefit from the house's structured approach. Not universally celebrated as a great vintage.

## Producer style
Pio Cesare is one of Barolo's most storied houses, founded in 1881 in Alba's historic centre (built on ancient Roman walls) and now in its fifth generation. The estate produces around 80 hectares of vineyards and is known for a carefully balanced style between traditional (large Slavonian oak, long maceration) and modernist (barriques, rotofermenters) approaches. The result is wines that are approachable earlier than strict traditionalists but with the backbone for long ageing. Flagship wines include the multi-commune Barolo, single-vineyard Ornato (Serralunga), and Mosconi (Monforte). Source: piocesare.it.

## Why it's in this exam
Pio Cesare Barolo represents the prestige DOCG Nebbiolo benchmark — used in P2's Italian flight to test Barolo variety and appellation identification. Key blind signals: tar-and-roses aroma, extreme tannin, high acidity, garnet-orange colour (even at 5 years), and the distinctive Piemontese structure. The 2012 vintage adds warmth vs. cooler Barolo archetypes.
