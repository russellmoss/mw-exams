# 40 Year Tawny Port, Kopke, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber with a greenish rim. This should be unmistakably aged tawny: walnut, fig, raisin, caramelized dried fruit, vanilla and honey, but also notable freshness and length rather than mere sweetness (source: https://kopke1638.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/KOPKE_40YOTawny-1.pdf). In blind terms the oxidative maturity, cask-derived complexity and lifted acidity separate it clearly from ruby-style Port.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Traditional Douro grape varieties (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/)
- Vineyard: Douro Valley vineyards up to 600m (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/)
- Soils: Schist-sandstone with some granite (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/)
- Vinification: Traditional Port fermentation with maceration in lagares, then fortification with grape spirit (source: https://kopke1638.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/KOPKE_40YOTawny-1.pdf)
- Elevage: Blend of different harvests matured in oak casks for varied periods; the age on the label is the average age of the blend (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/; https://kopke1638.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/KOPKE_40YOTawny-1.pdf)
- ABV / RS / TA: 20.0% / 147 g/dm3 / 5.36 g/dm3 (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/)

## Vintage character (NV / category character)
This is non-vintage, so age indication matters more than harvest year. Decanter notes that 10, 20, 30 and over-40-year tawny categories are defined by the IVDP and are shaped by gradual oxidative ageing in seasoned casks, with the blender balancing older and younger components to maintain house style (source: https://www.decanter.com/wine/wine-regions/port/understanding-tawny-port-347710/). Kopke's own sheet echoes that logic exactly.

## Producer style
Kopke, founded in 1638, is the oldest Port house and markets this bottle as a flagship historical style rather than an everyday fortified wine (source: https://kopke1638.com/store/port-wines/aged-tawny-white/kopke-40-year-old-tawny-port/). Commercially it sits high in the aged-tawny ladder: expensive, niche, luxurious and reliant on blending reserves and patient cask management rather than vintage declaration.
