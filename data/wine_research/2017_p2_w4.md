# Rubicon, Meerlust, 2013. Stellenbosch, South Africa

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-garnet with a fine, clear rim. Nose shows cassis, plum, pencil shavings, cedar, a hint of violets and dried herbs; secondary savoury notes of leather and earth. Full-bodied, firm but fine-grained tannins, firm integrated acidity, long finish with mineral and dark-fruit persistence. Cool-influence Stellenbosch character (ocean proximity to False Bay) yields a more structured, austere profile than the Paarl or Swartland.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Cabernet Franc, and Petit Verdot (Bordeaux blend; Source needed for 2013 exact percentages — typically Cabernet Sauvignon dominant ~55-60%)
- Vineyard: Meerlust estate, 15 km south of Stellenbosch towards False Bay; predominantly Hutton and Clovelly soils (deep, well-drained) (source: Meerlust website)
- Soils: Hutton and Clovelly deep well-drained loam over decomposed granite; supports slow, even ripening
- Vinification: Separate fermentation by variety and parcel; maceration; blending after barrel ageing
- Elevage: 18 months in French oak barriques (Source needed for exact % new oak)
- ABV: 14.0%

## Vintage character (2013 Stellenbosch)
2013 was a generally warm vintage in the Western Cape, producing well-structured, concentrated reds. Stellenbosch delivered wines with good depth and aromatic complexity. No major weather disruptions recorded. Rubicon 2013 would be approachable but built for medium-term cellaring (10–15 years from vintage).

## Producer style
Meerlust is one of South Africa's most prestigious wine estates, owned by the Myburgh family since 1756 and currently managed by Hannes Myburgh (eighth generation). The estate is located near False Bay with ocean breezes cooling the vineyards. The Rubicon blend — launched with the 1980 vintage — was one of South Africa's first Bordeaux-style blends and remains the estate's flagship, consistently among SA's most cellarworthy reds. Meerlust also produces Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, rare estate variety breadth for Stellenbosch. Source: meerlust.co.za.

## Why it's in this exam
Rubicon represents the premium South African Bordeaux-style blend — used in P2 to test identification of the Cape's top-tier red category against Bordeaux originals and other New World Cabernet blends. The challenge: distinguishing Cape Bordeaux blend from actual Bordeaux or from other New World examples. Key signals are the distinctive Stellenbosch mineral/herb character, ocean-influence structure, and SA winemaking signature.
