# Corton Charlemagne, Domaine Xavier Monnot, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
This should show grand cru white Burgundy scale: concentrated citrus and stone fruit, smoky reduction, toast, struck flint and a more vertical mineral drive than the Meursault. The palate is likely denser, more forceful and longer, with oak integrated into a much more architectural frame.

## Vintage character (2019 Burgundy)
2019 gave Burgundy whites real concentration and ripe fruit, but the better sites retained energy and definition. In grand cru format that often translates into both power and precision, so candidates should expect more volume, extract and persistence than the village wine without losing freshness.

## Producer style
Domaine Xavier Monnot is known for polished but not overdone white Burgundy, with site expression carrying the wines more than overt oak sweetness. Corton-Charlemagne is one of the estate's prestige whites and naturally occupies a far higher qualitative and commercial tier than its Meursault village bottling.
