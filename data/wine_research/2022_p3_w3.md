# Rosé Brut, Bollinger, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Delicate salmon-pink to light copper colour with persistent fine mousse; characteristically Bollinger nose — Pinot Noir dominant, showing red fruit (cherry, strawberry, raspberry), brioche/toast from barrel-aged reserve wines, and a slight nutty depth; full-bodied for a rosé Champagne (12% ABV), with the house's characteristic richness and Pinot structure; brut dosage (4-9 g/L); long, fine finish. Immediately distinguishable from New World rosé sparkling by the autolytic complexity and textural depth.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Pinot Noir dominant (house signature), with Chardonnay and Pinot Meunier; red wine addition (typically 5-8% Pinot Noir still wine from Bollinger's village parcels) for rosé colour
- Vineyard: Bollinger owns ~160 hectares sourcing 60%+ of their needs; parcels across Montagne de Reims, Côte des Blancs
- Vinification: Traditional method; barrel fermentation of the base wines in old oak (unusual among Champagne houses); bottle fermentation for secondary
- Elevage: Reserve wines aged 5+ years in barrel; full disgorgement process; Bollinger hand-riddles prestige cuvées; dosage 4-9 g/L (brut)
- ABV / RS / TA: 12% / brut (4-9 g/L) / high acidity
- James Bond film association since 1973 (Source: Wikipedia)

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage; special cuvée blends current vintage (~85-90%) with reserve wines (up to 10-15%). The Bollinger NV style is deliberately rich and Pinot-dominant — more substance than most NV Champagnes.

## Producer style
Bollinger, founded 1829 in Aÿ by Hennequin de Villermont, Paul Renaudin, and Jacques Bollinger, is one of Champagne's few remaining independent family houses. Pinot Noir-dominant style; barrel fermentation in old oak; Special Cuvée (NV) and Grande Année (vintage) are the main expressions, plus the rare R.D. and Vieilles Vignes Françaises. "Classic, Pinot-dominated Champagnes of great potential" that "tend to go toasty" (Tom Stevenson). Positioned at the prestige independent-house level, above NV commodity Champagne. (Source: Wikipedia)

## Why it's in this exam
The Champagne benchmark in the rosé sparkling flight alongside Prosecco Rosé (w1), Lambrusco Rosato (w2), and Cremant de Bourgogne Brut Rosé (w4). Tests the candidate's ability to identify traditional method Champagne rosé versus Charmat-method sparkling and French Cremant, and to recognise Bollinger's distinctive Pinot Noir-dominant, barrel-influenced house style within the Champagne category.
