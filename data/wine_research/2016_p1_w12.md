# Ried Schütt Dürnsteiner Riesling, Emmerich Knoll, 2012

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-deep gold with developing colour; a Smaragd-level (highest Wachau classification, 12.5%+ ABV) Riesling showing pronounced concentration and structure. Nose: white peach, apricot, mineral slate, developing petrol (kerosene) notes at 4 years, floral lime. Palate: full-bodied for Riesling (13.5% ABV is high — Smaragd level), dry, with broad, waxy texture contrasting with high acid. The Wachau descriptor "fuller bodied, higher extract style, more similar to Alsatian expressions than German counterparts" (confirmed: Wikipedia) applies. Long, mineralic finish. No oak influence.

- Classification: Smaragd (13.5% ABV confirms Smaragd: 12.5%+ ABV; named after the emerald lizard) — the richest Wachau expression

## Vintage character (2012 Wachau)
2012 was an excellent vintage in the Wachau — warm, dry growing season with optimal Riesling ripeness. Smaragd Rieslings from 2012 showed concentration, extract, and exceptional depth. At 4 years in 2016, a Smaragd Riesling of this calibre would be just entering its ideal drinking window — primary fruit fading into complexity. (Source needed — specific 2012 Wachau vintage report)

## Producer style
Emmerich Knoll, based in Unterloiben, Wachau; one of the region's most prestigious estates. The Knoll family has farmed the Wachau for generations. Emmerich Knoll III and now Emmerich Knoll IV (Emmerich the younger) manage the estate. Multiple highly regarded vineyard holdings including Ried Schütt (Dürnstein), Pfaffenberg, and Loibenberg. Positioned as one of the top three Wachau producers alongside F.X. Pichler and Alzinger. Very low production; sought-after allocation. (Source needed — producer website inaccessible during research)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 1 white wines. Wachau Riesling from a top producer at Smaragd level is an important MW identification challenge. The 13.5% ABV and full-bodied Riesling style creates risk of confusion with Alsace Riesling or mature German Auslese. Key differentiators: no oak, no malo, dry, Austrian mineral precision, gneiss terroir character. Emmerich Knoll is among Austria's canonical names — recognisable at the premium tier.
