# Old Vine Chenin Blanc, Raats, 2017. Stellenbosch, South Africa.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium gold. Nose: ripe quince, yellow peach, guava (tropical emphasis characteristic of Cape Chenin), with waxy honeysuckle and possible lees-derived creamy note. Palate: full-bodied for the variety at 13.5%, dry, with a textured mid-palate from old vine concentration, moderate to high acidity, and good length. Not an off-dry Loire style — distinctly Southern Hemisphere in fruit expression but with genuine structural complexity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc (Steen)
- Vineyard: Old vine Chenin Blanc sites in Stellenbosch; vine age not specified from public sources but "Old Vine" designation implies 35+ years (South African Old Vine Project criteria)
- Vinification: Likely combination of stainless and oak fermentation; possibly some skin contact for texture
- Elevage: Possible partial barrel maturation; lees contact probable
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / dry / medium-high

## Vintage character (2017 Stellenbosch)
2017 was affected by the Western Cape drought — the worst in decades, with severe water restrictions in Cape Town. Crop volumes were reduced but quality was concentrated. Chenin from Stellenbosch in 2017 shows intense, ripe fruit with lower natural acidity than cooler years. Good structure despite drought conditions. (Source needed: 2017 South Africa vintage report)

## Producer style
Raats Family Wines is led by Bruwer Raats, one of South Africa's foremost champions of Chenin Blanc as a premium variety. The winery focuses almost exclusively on Chenin Blanc (and Cabernet Franc). Bruwer Raats is a leading voice in the Old Vine Project — the South African initiative to identify and preserve pre-1985 plantings. Their wines are positioned as premium-quality, terroir-expressive Cape Chenin, priced $25-40, and widely used as reference examples in wine education. (Source needed: Raats Family Wines producer website)

## Why it's in this exam
Old Vine Chenin Blanc from Stellenbosch is a deliberate South African curveball in a white wine paper. Tests candidate recognition of Cape Chenin's tropical-fruit-led style vs. Loire Chenin (Vouvray/Savennières). The 13.5% ABV and Stellenbosch provenance contrast with the leaner, lower-alcohol Loire style. Old vine concentration adds structural complexity. Raats is a benchmark producer — if the examiner wanted the candidate to identify South African premium Chenin, this is the wine to use.
