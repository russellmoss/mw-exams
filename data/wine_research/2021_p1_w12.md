# Pinot Gris, Ponzi, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium gold; restrained and varietal nose with pear, green apple, subtle melon and a crisp, refreshing character — Oregon Pinot Gris styled in the dry, Alsatian tradition rather than the sweet Italian style. Medium body (13.5% ABV), clean and bright acidity, dry palate with stone-fruit and citrus tones; medium-length finish. More restrained than Alsace Pinot Gris with less aromatic intensity and lower residual sugar — a food-friendly, dry expression typical of Oregon's approach to the variety.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Gris

## Vintage character (2019 Willamette Valley)
2019 was a warm, excellent vintage in the Willamette Valley — one of Oregon's best recent years, with ideal ripening conditions and low disease pressure. Pinot Gris in 2019 achieved full physiological ripeness while maintaining the valley's characteristic bright acidity. The wine shows more generosity and fruit weight than the typical lean, cooler-year Oregon style.

## Producer style
Ponzi Vineyards is one of Oregon's founding estate wineries, established in 1970 by Dick and Nancy Ponzi — pioneers of Willamette Valley viticulture alongside Adelsheim and Eyrie. The estate is now run by the second generation (Luisa Ponzi as head winemaker). Ponzi produces Pinot Noir and Pinot Gris as their core range, positioned at premium Oregon quality levels. Their Pinot Gris is styled dry, in the Alsatian tradition, and is one of the benchmark examples of the variety in the state. Source needed for specific current estate acreage and pricing tier data.
