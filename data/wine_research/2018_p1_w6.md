# Viña Gravonia, Lopez de Heredia Viña Tondonia, 2007. Rioja, Spain.

## Quick tasting profile
Deep amber-gold, showing significant oxidative development. Nose: wax, dried apple, camomile, almond, hazelnut, and lanolin with a distinctive woody-spice character from extended large American oak aging. Not a fresh-fruit profile at all. Palate: dry, taut acidity, savoury and almost sherried complexity, bone-dry, textured. At 12.5% ABV and 11 years old by exam date (2018), this is profoundly unusual. A curveball for any candidate expecting a standard white.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: ~100% Viura (Macabeo) with possible small Malvasia inclusion
- Vineyard: Gravonia vineyard in Rioja Alta
- Vinification: Traditional press; fermented in large American oak vats
- Elevage: Extended aging in large American oak casks (NOT small French barriques) — Lopez de Heredia ages its whites far beyond legal Crianza/Reserva minimums; this 2007 was likely released around 2014-2016 with several years in large cask plus bottle aging
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / dry / high
*(Lopez de Heredia ages wines including whites for decades before release, using large American oak rather than small French barrels per Wikipedia)*

## Vintage character (2007 Rioja)
2007 was a warm, ripe vintage in Rioja with good phenolic maturity. For a wine designed for extended aging like Viña Gravonia, the 2007 base fruit provides sufficient concentration to survive long oak and bottle maturation. The oxidative character in the finished wine reflects intentional winemaking rather than vintage weakness. (Source needed: 2007 Rioja vintage report)

## Producer style
Lopez de Heredia, founded 1877, is one of Spain's most tradition-bound bodegas. Located in Haro, Rioja Alta, the estate manages approximately 420 acres and produces roughly 25,000 cases per year. They are famous for resisting modernisation — still using large American oak barrels (not small French), maintaining cobwebbed old-vintage reserves, and releasing wines years after legal minimums. The Zaha Hadid-designed 2006 showroom entrance (shaped like a wine decanter) is the sole concession to modernity. Viña Gravonia is their white wine; Viña Tondonia and Viña Bosconia are the reds and rosé. (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/López_de_Heredia)

## Why it's in this exam
Viña Gravonia is one of the most distinctive blind tasting wines possible: an oxidative, heavily aged Spanish white from Viura that looks and smells nothing like any other white wine in Paper 1. It is an explicit test of whether a candidate can identify extended wood aging in a white wine, the Viura variety, and the Lopez de Heredia Rioja Blanco style. This is a textbook MW curveball requiring specific producer/regional knowledge.
