# Etna Rosso, Pietradolce, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium ruby with a lifted, volcanic style: red cherry, dried herbs, flowers and stony mineral notes rather than dense black fruit. The palate should be dry, agile and savory, with fine tannins, bright acidity and a lightly smoky, mineral finish that makes it feel more alpine than southerly.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Nerello Mascalese
- Vineyard: northern slopes of Mount Etna around Solicchiata, about 800m
- Soils: sandy soils rich in volcanic rock
- Vinification: hand harvested; 14 days skin maceration, then gentle pressing
- Elevage: 3 months in fine-grain oak barrels with natural malolactic fermentation
- ABV / RS / TA: official sheet gives 14.5%; exam corpus lists 13.5% / Source needed / Source needed

## Vintage character (2020 Etna)
Decanter's Etna Rosso panel report says 2020 was the strongest of the younger recent vintages, with bright fruit, charm, aromatic delicacy and less angularity than 2019. That is a useful guide for this bottling: 2020 Etna Rosso should still be nervy and mineral, but more open and seductive than a sterner year. In blind tasting, that can make Etna look deceptively easy at first before the volcanic savoriness kicks in.

## Producer style
Pietradolce frames itself as a family estate rooted on Etna's northern slopes, farming native varieties organically and privileging artisanal methods tied closely to the volcano's landscape. The vineyard page emphasizes altitude, contrada diversity and sandy mineral soils, while the cellar description highlights concrete fermentation spaces and oak ageing for the reds. The result is a modern Etna fine-wine producer whose style aims for territorial precision more than cellar weight.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 4 closes 2023 Paper 2 Question 1. After Barolo, Valpolicella and Taurasi, it tests whether the candidate can still place a lighter-colored, aromatic red correctly inside Italy and explain its ageability, volcanic origin and less forceful but still serious production model.
