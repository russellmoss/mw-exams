# Taylor's Vintage Port, 1985. Douro, Portugal.

## Quick tasting profile
By 2018 (33 years old), this is deep tawny-ruby with an amber rim — extensive colour evolution from the original opaque purple. Nose: secondary complexity dominates: dried fruit (fig, prune, raisin), walnut, leather, tobacco, chocolate, and a distinctive Douro mineral character. No fresh berry fruit remains. Palate: full, sweet, still firm tannins (vintage port tannins are legendary for longevity), very high alcohol (20.5%), long dried-fruit finish. A wine at its peak drinking window, 30+ years of development visible.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Traditional Douro field blend including Touriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinta Barroca, Tinta Cão
- Vineyard: Multi-quinta blend; Taylor's estate vineyards in the Douro Valley
- Vinification: Fermentation halted by addition of grape spirit (aguardente) at approximately 77 g/L RS; colour and tannin extraction via treading (lagares) or automated vinification
- Elevage: 2 years in large oak pipes in Vila Nova de Gaia before bottling; then decades of bottle aging (design for 20-50 year cellaring)
- ABV / RS / TA: 20.5% / very sweet (>77 g/L) / medium-high

## Vintage character (1985 Douro / Vintage Port)
1985 is considered one of the great Vintage Port years — widely declared by most major houses. Hot, dry conditions in the Douro produced intensely concentrated, tannic fruit. By 2018, the 1985 is fully mature — the tannins have softened and the wine is at or approaching its peak drinking window, though Taylor's finest vintage ports can drink for 40+ years. ## Producer style
Taylor Fladgate (founded 1692) is one of the most prestigious Vintage Port houses. The company describes itself as "one of the largest port wine houses" and was the creator of Late Bottled Vintage (LBV) port. Their Vintage Ports are consistently among the most age-worthy and critically acclaimed. The Taylor Fonseca Group also owns Fonseca, Croft, Delaforce, and Krohn. (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taylor_Fladgate)

## Why it's in this exam
Paired with the 2009 (w6) in an explicit vintage comparison of Taylor's Vintage Port. The 1985 is a fully mature, iconic vintage; the 2009 is a much younger wine. Tests: identification of Vintage Port as a style, recognition of the Douro as origin, assessment of maturity differences between a 33-year and 9-year-old port, and commentary on what makes each vintage significant. The identical 20.5% ABV on both wines is a deliberate examiner signal.
