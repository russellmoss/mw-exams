# Single Vineyard Tannat, Bodega Garzón, 2017. Maldonado, Uruguay

## Quick tasting profile
Very deep purple-ruby, nearly opaque. Nose of blackberry, dark plum, leather, dark chocolate, iron/mineral and tobacco — intense and brooding. Palate is full-bodied with substantial, firm tannins (hallmark of Tannat), high acidity, concentrated dark fruit and a savoury, long finish. Structurally imposing; built for ageing. At Garzón level, likely uses micro-oxygenation or careful oak management to soften Tannat's trademark tannins.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Tannat
- Vineyard: Single vineyard parcel within Bodega Garzón estate in Maldonado Department, southeastern Uruguay, near Atlantic Ocean
- Soils: Granitic and rocky soils at altitude (unique for Uruguay); Source needed for specific soil description

- Elevage: French oak barriques, approximately 20 months (consistent with Wikipedia notation of ~20-month oak ageing for Tannat — source: Wikipedia Tannat)

## Producer style
Bodega Garzón was founded by Uruguayan entrepreneur Alejandro Bulgheroni and opened in 2016 (first commercial vintage 2013). The winery — designed by architect Alberto Kalach — is a cutting-edge facility positioned as a world-class wine tourism destination, with restaurant run by Francis Mallmann (source: producer website). Garzón has rapidly become Uruguay's most internationally visible winery, winning IWSC International Winery of the Year accolades. The Single Vineyard Tannat is their flagship red, showcasing Uruguay's national variety from granitic high-elevation terroir. Market position: premium ($40–80).

## Why it's in this exam
Bodega Garzón Tannat is the definitive MW curveball for Uruguay: a wine the candidate must identify as high-quality Tannat from an unexpected southern hemisphere source. The massive tannin structure, dark fruit profile and firm acidity point away from both Bordeaux and conventional New World. Recognising Tannat (Madiran/Uruguay) from tannin level and fruit profile is the key exam skill.
