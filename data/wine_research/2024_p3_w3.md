# Chardonnay En Flandre, Domaine de la Touraize, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold. Sharp, electric nose — lemony citrus, Granny Smith apple, smoky/reductive popcorn notes, light herbal greenness, touch of creamy oak. Palate is crisp, dry, and precise with medium body: ripe lemon citrus, tart apple, leesy tones, vanilla yogurt, salty liquorice, wet rock minerality. Bracing acidity provides structure and energy. Long, tangy finish. This is a ouillé (topped-up) Jura Chardonnay — no flor influence, showcasing bare-knuckled minerality.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: En Flandre lieu-dit, 1 hectare, ~15 years old (planted ~2007), NE exposure, 310m altitude
- Soils: Calcareous-clay
- Viticulture: Organic/biodynamic. Guyot cane pruning. Copper and sulphur only, no pesticides. Multiple annual ploughings.
- Vinification: Manual harvest into 300kg crates. Pneumatic whole-bunch pressing, static gravity settling. Spontaneous fermentation with indigenous yeast. Malolactic in 350L barrels.
- Élevage: 12–24 months on fine lees in 350L barrels with weekly topping (ouillé). Light plate filtration. Unfined. Total SO2: ~33 mg/L.
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2021 Jura)
2021 was a challenging vintage across France with late frosts and cool, wet conditions. In the Jura, the growing season was difficult, producing wines with pronounced acidity and moderate ripeness — potentially favouring the region's naturally high-acid, mineral whites. ## Producer style
Domaine de la Touraize has been in the Morin family for 8+ generations since 1704. André-Jean Morin began estate-bottling in 2011 after selling some land to build his own cellar. Self-taught winemaker — learned through family tradition rather than formal study. Natural wine approach: indigenous yeasts, minimal SO2, unfined/unfiltered. Most whites are ouillé (topped-up), emphasising minerality without oxidative notes. The two sous-voile Savagnins are the exception. Represented by Rosenthal Wine Merchant in the US.

## Why it's in this exam
Placed in Paper 3, Q2 with Wine 4 (L'Etoile, Domaine de Montbourgeau 2018): "Wines 3 and 4 are from the same region." Both are Jura whites, but the Touraize is a modern ouillé Chardonnay (fresh, reductive, mineral) while the Montbourgeau is likely a more traditional, potentially oxidative Jura style. Tests the candidate's ability to identify the Jura and contrast the two philosophical winemaking approaches within the same region.
