# Condrieu Coteau de Vernon, Georges Vernay, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Full-bodied and aromatic, with apricot, honeysuckle, tangerine oil, and a faint toasted note from elevage. The palate is dry but rich, low in sharp acidity, and expansive in texture, with the oily, floral profile that places it squarely in top-end Viognier territory.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Viognier
- Vineyard: Coteau de Vernon, one of Condrieu's great slopes
- Soils: Granite-derived hillside soils

## Vintage character (2014 Northern Rhone)
2014 was a cooler and more classical Northern Rhone vintage than 2015, often emphasizing freshness over sheer power. In Condrieu that can be an advantage, keeping Viognier's exotic fruit from turning heavy and allowing the site to show more clearly.

## Producer style
Georges Vernay is one of the defining names of Condrieu, associated with painstaking hillside viticulture and serious, cellar-worthy Viognier. Coteau de Vernon is the estate's flagship expression, marrying aromatic generosity with real structure.

## Why it's in this exam
This sat in the 2017 paper 1 European mixed-variety section. It is an archetypal fine-wine Viognier, useful for testing whether the candidate can distinguish genuinely high-quality Condrieu from simpler aromatic whites.
