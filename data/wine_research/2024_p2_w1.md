# Syrah Outer Limits, Montes, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-red with violet tones. Cool-climate Syrah character: red fruits (strawberry, sour cherry, fresh plum) with cassis and sweet spice. Palate shows vibrant acidity, silky near-imperceptible tannins, and juicy texture with good volume. A cool-climate New World Syrah that leans toward Northern Rhone restraint rather than Barossa power.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Syrah
- Vineyard: Zapallar, 45 ha, 7-11 km from Pacific coast, 120-150 m altitude
- Soils: Clay-loam with granite
- Vinification: Hand-picked early morning in 12 kg boxes, hand-sorted, 5-day cold maceration at 6-8C, fermented at 26-28C for 10 days with selected yeasts, 5 further days on skins
- Elevage: 65% concrete eggs with lees contact, 35% second-use French oak barrels, 12 months (source: Liberty Wines). Note: some sources report 55% oak / 45% concrete split — discrepancy may reflect vintage variation.
- ABV: 14%

## Vintage character (2022 Chile)
Cold, dry winter followed by cool spring with frost. Low yields (7-22% drop depending on valley). Grapes struggled with ripeness until late March warmth. Exceptional for concentrated, ageworthy reds — some producers compared it to the acclaimed 2018. Aconcagua particularly dry (75 mm rainfall at Sena), giving higher fruit concentration. (source: Decanter, The Drinks Business)

## Producer style
Montes is a leading Chilean producer founded in 1988. The Outer Limits range explores cool-climate, coastal and marginal sites beyond their traditional Colchagua base. The Zapallar vineyard (Aconcagua Costa) is their maritime-influenced frontier site. Sustainability-focused farming. The brand bridges premium quality with accessible pricing. Montes also appears in the 2019 MW exam (Carmenere, Montes Alpha — 2019_p2_w7).
