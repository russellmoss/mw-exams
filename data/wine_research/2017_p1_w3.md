# Pouilly-Fume Les Griottes, Jean-Pierre Bailly, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with a lifted, varietal Sauvignon profile: gooseberry, citrus, elderflower, and the flinty, smoky edge that often pushes tasters toward Pouilly-Fume rather than generic Loire Sauvignon. The palate is dry, linear, and tense, finishing on stone, citrus pith, and fresh-cut herb notes.

## Vintage character (2015 Central Loire)
2015 brought warmth and healthy ripeness to the Loire, often giving Sauvignon Blanc a slightly broader fruit profile than cooler years. The better wines still kept freshness, but the fruit tone typically shifts from austere citrus toward riper orchard and stone-fruit accents.

## Producer style
Jean-Pierre Bailly reads as a classically regional producer rather than an overtly international one. The cue here is typicity: bright Sauvignon fruit, flint/smoke, and an unadorned, dry finish.

## Why it's in this exam
This completes the 2017 paper 1 Loire trio. It tests whether the candidate can identify Sauvignon Blanc and place it in the Central Loire rather than confuse it with New World Sauvignon or Chenin-based Loire whites.
