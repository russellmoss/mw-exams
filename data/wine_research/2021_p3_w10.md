# Matusalem Oloroso Dulce 30 Years, González Byass, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep mahogany-brown with greenish rim (30 years of oxidative aging); extraordinary nose of coffee, dark chocolate, raisins, figs, orange peel, toffee, vanilla, leather and roasted nuts — the most complex and evolved aromas of the Oloroso category; very sweet on the palate (blended with Pedro Ximénez to achieve sweetness) but balanced by the deep oxidative structure; full-bodied (20.5% ABV), very long, warming finish with chocolate and dried-fruit persistence. One of Spain's most celebrated fortified wines.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Palomino Fino (Oloroso base) + Pedro Ximénez (for sweetening — PX is dried on mats to concentrate sugars)
- Vineyard: Albariza soils of the Jerez triangle
- Vinification: As per Oloroso (flor suppressed, oxidative aging); then blended with PX for sweetness
- Elevage: 30+ year average age in criadera-solera in old American oak; VORS (Vinum Optimum Rara Signatum) category = "Very Old Rare Sherry" min average 30 years
- ABV / RS / TA: 20.5% / very sweet (PX-sweetened; approx 180-220 g/L RS) / Source needed
- Matusalem is named after Methuselah (the biblical figure of great age)

## Vintage character (NV solera)
Non-vintage 30-year solera. VORS status is independently verified by the Consejo Regulador — certifying average age ≥30 years. The extraordinary age concentrates and integrates all elements, producing a wine of unique complexity in the sweet fortified category.

## Producer style
González Byass, founded 1835 by Manuel María González Angel (with English agent Robert Blake Byass), is one of Spain's most famous sherry houses. Known primarily for Tío Pepe Fino, they also produce exceptional aged Sherries including Matusalem (VORS Oloroso Dulce), Noé (VORS PX) and Apostoles (VORS Palo Cortado). The family regained sole control in 1988. A prestige, internationally-distributed premium producer. (Source: Wikipedia)
