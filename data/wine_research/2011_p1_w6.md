# Chardonnay, Saintsbury, 2008

## Quick tasting profile
Ripe but not blowsy Carneros Chardonnay, combining orchard and citrus fruit with toast, cream and subtle spice from barrel work. Compared with the Chablis it should feel rounder and more luxurious; compared with the Wolf Blass, more precise and less obviously commercial.

## Vintage character (2008 Carneros)
Carneros usually delivers cool-climate California Chardonnay with ripe fruit but better acidity than warmer inland sites. In 2008 the result is likely a balanced, coastal style with enough concentration to carry malo and oak without losing freshness.

## Producer style
Saintsbury is best known for Pinot Noir but has long produced Carneros Chardonnay with a thoughtful, vineyard-sourced approach. This places it comfortably above simple branded Chardonnay and makes it a strong mid-to-upper-tier New World benchmark in a same-variety comparative set.
