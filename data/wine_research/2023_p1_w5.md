# Riesling Kabinett, Wehlener Sonnenuhr, JJ Prüm, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
Very pale lemon. The wine should be intensely aromatic but featherweight in body, showing lime, white peach, slatey lift and a touch of sweetness carried by piercing acidity. At just 8% alcohol and with no oak influence, this is a benchmark Mosel Kabinett: light, translucent, long, and built on the tension between residual sugar and electric acid rather than density (source: The Wine Society).

## Vintage character (2020 Mosel)
Max Ferd. Richter's 2020 vintage report described 2020 as an early, warm, mostly healthy season with little severe disease pressure, an early harvest, and clean fruit. The report specifically says the wines show well-balanced fruit and acid and that 2020 places a strong emphasis on the Kabinett category, which is highly relevant for understanding why a 2020 Mosel Kabinett could be both expressive and sharply defined (source: Max Ferd. Richter).

## Producer style
JJ Prüm is one of the canonical Mosel estates: steep vineyard holdings, meticulous low-yield viticulture, long cool fermentations, and very late bottling. The Wine Society's producer profile frames the estate as the premier exponent of the region, and the signature style is exactly what MW candidates must recognise: purity, site transparency, and ageworthy sweetness balanced by acidity rather than heavy extraction (source: The Wine Society).

## Why it's in this exam
This is wine 5 in 2023 Paper 1 Question 2, a set of four different single grape varieties where candidates had to identify the grape, then focus on acidity, texture, and ageing potential. It is a classic test wine because its sweetness is obvious, but the deeper point is how acidity and low alcohol create finesse rather than heaviness.
