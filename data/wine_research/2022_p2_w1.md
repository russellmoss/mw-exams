# Chinon Cuvee Terroir, Charles Joguet, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
This should show Cabernet Franc in a cooler, more savory frame than Napa Cabernet or plush Chilean Merlot: red and black currant fruit, graphite, violet, earth and fresh herb notes with firm but refined tannin. The palate should feel medium-bodied, linear and distinctly Loire rather than lush.

## Vintage character (2019 Chinon)
2019 in the central Loire gave ripe Cabernet Franc with more generosity than cooler years, but good producers still retained freshness and fine tannins. In Chinon that means dark-fruited depth without losing the leafy-mineral signature that anchors varietal identity.

## Producer style
Charles Joguet is one of Chinon's benchmark domaines and is known for treating Cabernet Franc through a terroir-led, ageworthy lens rather than as simple bistro red. Even when this specific cuvee is less documented than the single-vineyard wines, the estate style points to precision, freshness and site clarity.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 1 begins 2022 Paper 2 Question 1, where wines 1-3 are single-variety reds from different countries and wine 4 blends those same varieties. Joguet's Chinon functions as the Cabernet Franc marker: cooler, less opulent and more site-driven than the wines built on Cabernet Sauvignon and Merlot.
