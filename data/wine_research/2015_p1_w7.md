# Albariño, Pazo de Villarei, 2013. Rías Baixas, Spain

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon-green; fresh, aromatic nose of citrus (lemon, grapefruit), white peach, apricot and a distinctive botanical/herbal quality. Palate shows bright, high acidity, light-medium body, fresh stone fruit and citrus with a subtle saline or oceanic note characteristic of Atlantic Galician viticulture. Clean, stainless steel fermented — no oak. Dry finish with bracing acidity. Textbook young Albariño from Rías Baixas.

## Vintage character (2013 Rías Baixas)
2013 was a challenging vintage in Galicia — a wet, cool growing season with high disease pressure (Botrytis, mildew), requiring careful canopy management and selective harvesting. The typical pergola (parral) training in Rías Baixas helps air circulation, but 2013 required significant viticultural attention. Wines from successful producers show good natural acidity and crisp, focused aromatics, sometimes with slightly lower alcohol than warmer years. ## Producer style
Pazo de Villarei is a Rías Baixas estate producing Albariño in the classic, internationally accessible style: stainless steel fermented, no oak, fresh and aromatic, designed for the restaurant and retail market. The term "pazo" refers to a traditional Galician stone manor — many Rías Baixas estates use this designation to signal terroir-rooted identity. Pazo de Villarei is a commercially distributed brand in the entry-to-mid premium tier of Albariño production. The 2013 vintage at 13% ABV is consistent with a cooler year.
