# Reserve Sauvignon Blanc, Honig, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Medium lemon. The aromatics should move beyond grassy Sauvignon into honeysuckle, orange blossom, peach, exotic citrus and a light vanilla-oak accent. The palate is full-bodied and textural, with ripe fruit, moderate-to-high acidity, clear lees/oak breadth, and a long mineral finish, making this a distinctly Californian, premium, ageable Sauvignon Blanc rather than a stainless-steel style (source: Honig wine notes).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 96% Sauvignon Blanc, 4% Semillon (source: Honig wine notes)
- Vineyard: select blocks from Honig's sustainably farmed estate vineyard in Rutherford, Napa Valley (source: Honig wine notes)
- Soils: Source needed
- Vinification: 62% fermented in stainless steel; the balance fermented in new French oak barrels and puncheons; the barrel-fermented portion underwent malolactic fermentation (source: Honig wine notes)
- Elevage: 68% aged on lees for 8 months in French oak puncheons; 32% aged on lees for 8 months in new French oak barrels and puncheons (source: Honig wine notes)

## Vintage character (2021 Napa Valley)
Napa Valley's 2021 season was drought-shaped and low-yielding, with early ripening in drier hillside and water-limited sites. Napa Valley Vintners described the vintage as small but high quality, with concentrated fruit and clean harvest conditions, which fits the density and ripeness seen here (source: Napa Valley Vintners).

## Producer style
Honig is a Rutherford estate known for sustainability and polished, market-aware Napa styling. The winery's trade materials foreground both sustainability and a Sauvignon Blanc program that uses barrel fermentation, lees ageing, and selective malo to build texture without losing freshness, positioning this bottling above simple varietal Sauvignon Blanc (source: Honig trade info and wine notes).
