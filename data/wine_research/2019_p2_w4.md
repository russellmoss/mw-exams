# "V", Vergelegen, 2012. Stellenbosch, South Africa

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple with brick hints developing after seven years. Nose of blackcurrant, black cherry, cedar, tobacco leaf, dark chocolate and graphite — a classic Bordeaux-inspired profile. Palate is full-bodied with firm, ripe tannins, good supporting acidity, and concentrated fruit core; well-integrated new oak adds structure and spice. Long, savoury finish. A flagship Cape Bordeaux blend showing serious ageing potential.

## Vintage character (2012 Stellenbosch)
2012 in Stellenbosch was a warmer year with concentrated, structured wines. Favourable ripening conditions through summer produced rich, full-bodied reds with good tannin development. At Vergelegen's elevated sites, heat was partly moderated. Wines from this vintage have aged well and are now in a secondary drinking window.

## Producer style
Vergelegen is one of South Africa's most historically significant and prestigious estates, founded in 1700. Anglo American purchased it in 1987 and relaunched commercial production from 1992. The estate became a quality benchmark for Cape Bordeaux blends with its "V" icon wine, winning international recognition including "New World Winery of the Year" from Wine Enthusiast (2005-2006). The V is positioned at the very top of South African red wine — a direct competitor to the finest Cape blends. Vergelegen holds BWI Champion status for conservation (source: producer website).

## Why it's in this exam
"V" is a key benchmark in the MW context: a high-quality Cape Bordeaux blend used to test whether a candidate can distinguish premium South Africa from Bordeaux or Napa, and identify Stellenbosch's characteristic warm-climate, granite-driven profile. The high ABV (14.5%) is a structural cue pointing away from Old World.
