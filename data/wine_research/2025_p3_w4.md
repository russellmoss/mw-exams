# Cava Brut Reserva "Essential Purpura", Juve y Camps, 2021. Penedes, Spain

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw-gold with fine, persistent bubbles. Pleasant aromas of fruit — juicy red apple, slightly ripe pineapple — over a light floral background. On the palate: creamy, fresh, and very pleasant with good complexity. Full body for a Cava, serious yet approachable. The 24+ months of lees ageing brings autolytic notes (brioche, biscuit) alongside crisp acidity.

## Technical specs
- Grape varieties: Macabeo, Xarel-lo, Parellada (traditional Cava blend)
- Vinification: Each variety fermented separately in stainless steel vats. Malolactic fermentation completed. Blended, then second fermentation in bottle (methode traditionnelle).
- Elevage: Minimum 24 months on lees in bottle (Reserva classification)
- ABV: 12% / RS: Source needed (Brut dosage, likely <12 g/L)
- Organic viticulture

## Producer style
Juve y Camps have been winegrowers in Penedes since 1796 and sparkling wine producers since 1921. Now led by Meritxell Juve (fourth generation), the house is committed to organic farming, manual harvesting and artisanal winemaking. All grapes are hand-harvested. The Essential Purpura (formerly Cinta Purpura) is their mid-range Brut Reserva — a well-regarded benchmark for quality Cava. The house also appeared in the MW exam in 2023 (Reserva de la Familia, a higher-tier Brut Nature Gran Reserva).

## Why it's in this exam
Part of the Spanish trio (Q2, wines 4-6): Cava sparkling, Garnacha from Gredos, and Oloroso VORS Sherry. Three radically different Spanish wine styles testing the candidate's breadth. The Cava represents traditional-method sparkling at Reserva level — the candidate must identify origin, discuss methode traditionnelle winemaking, and assess commercial position within the Cava hierarchy (Reserva vs Gran Reserva vs Paraje Calificado).
