# Cuvée Spéciale, Domaine de Montbourgeau, 2012. L'Étoile, Jura, France

## Quick tasting profile
Deep golden-amber colour (oxidative character, not from botrytis). Nose of walnut, yellow apple, dried apricot, curry/spice, beeswax, honey and a distinctive oxidative nuttiness — the unmistakable character of a sous voile (vin jaune-influenced) Jura white. Palate is rich, textured, with high acidity, savoury oxidative depth, dried fruit and nut flavours; long and complex. Aged in old barrels without topping — similar to vin jaune technique but without the full 6 years and 3 months requirement of strict vin jaune.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Chardonnay (L'Étoile AOC permits Chardonnay, Savagnin, and Poulsard — source: Wikipedia L'Étoile AOC); "Cuvée Spéciale" at Montbourgeau likely a Chardonnay aged sous voile (with some oxidative exposure) or a blend including Savagnin
- Vineyard: Domaine de Montbourgeau estate in the L'Étoile commune, southern Jura
- Soils: Blue lias limestone typical of the L'Étoile appellation (the "star-shaped" crinoid fossils that give the name)
- Vinification: Barrel fermentation; likely aged sous voile (without topping) to develop oxidative character without being a formal vin jaune; duration for "Cuvée Spéciale" longer than standard (Source needed for exact protocol)
- Elevage: Ageing in old barrels; "Cuvée Spéciale" suggests extended ageing versus basic L'Étoile (Source needed)

## Vintage character (2012 Jura)
2012 in the Jura was a challenging vintage: spring frosts and summer rain created difficult conditions. Yields were down significantly. However, for oxidative styles like sous voile whites, the vintage character becomes partially masked by the extended barrel ageing and oxidation process. By 2019, a 2012 Jura white with sous voile ageing would be showing significant development and complexity.

## Producer style
Domaine de Montbourgeau is one of the leading L'Étoile producers, located in the commune of L'Étoile in the southern Jura. The estate focuses almost exclusively on L'Étoile AOC wines across multiple cuvées. The "Cuvée Spéciale" is their premium oxidative expression — likely aged for an extended period in old Jura barrels under partial veil (sous voile), producing a wine with vin jaune character without being labelled vin jaune. The domaine is well-regarded among Jura specialists and natural wine enthusiasts. (Source needed for specific founding date and production size.)

## Why it's in this exam
Cuvée Spéciale is a Paper 3 curveball: an oxidative Jura white that challenges candidates to identify the sous voile/vin jaune character and correctly place it in L'Étoile (or more broadly, Jura). The walnut-curry-apple oxidative profile is distinctive and unlike any other wine style globally. This wine tests specialist Jura knowledge and the ability to identify oxidative white wine styles from appearance (amber), nose, and palate.
