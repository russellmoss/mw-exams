# Rioja Reserva, Bodegas Muga, 2010. Rioja, Spain

## Quick tasting profile
Medium garnet with slight orange rim development at 5 years. Nose shows classic Rioja Reserva: red cherry, strawberry, vanilla, coconut from American oak, dried herbs, leather, slight cedar. Medium-bodied, soft tannins, medium acidity, long finish with characteristic oak-influenced sweetness. Approachable but age-worthy — a textbook Rioja Reserva.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Predominantly Tempranillo, plus Garnacha, Mazuelo (Carinena), and Graciano (source: bodegasmuga.com/en/muga/ — confirmed for current releases; blend proportions are typical across vintages)
- Vineyard: Rioja Alta, calcareous clay soils from the tertiary period, Haro region; sheltered by Montes Obarenes and Sierra de Cantabria mountains (source: bodegasmuga.com/en/muga/)
- Vinification: Manual harvesting; fermentation in temperature-controlled wooden vats; traditional pump-over and pump-down regime
- Elevage: 24 months in Muga's own oak casks (80% French, 20% American origin), made in-house by the bodega's own cooper workshop; minimum 12 months in bottle before release (source: bodegasmuga.com/en/muga/)

## Vintage character (2010 Rioja)
2010 is widely regarded as an outstanding Rioja vintage: a cool growing season with excellent diurnal variation preserved freshness and acidity while producing ripe, well-structured wines. Critics consistently rate 2010 among the top recent Rioja vintages for structure and longevity. The wines at 5 years would be well into their drinking window but still showing considerable freshness. ## Producer style
Bodegas Muga is one of the great traditional Riojan producers, founded in 1932 and still family-owned. Located in Haro's prestigious Station District (Barrio de la Estacion), alongside peers Lopéz de Heredia, CVNE, and La Rioja Alta. Muga's defining characteristic is operating the only in-house cooperage in Spain, with a master cooper and three barrel makers (source: bodegasmuga.com/en/the-bodega/). This allows complete control over oak character. Their wines use both French and American oak in varying proportions. The Reserva is the core of their range, sitting at mid-to-premium commercial positioning. Higher up the range sits the Prado Enea Gran Reserva and the single-vineyard Torre Muga. Natural clarification with egg whites rather than fining agents.

## Why it's in this exam
Rioja Reserva represents the Old World international red benchmark for Tempranillo at premium quality. Placed alongside Burgundy and Italian reds to test regional identification. The distinctive oak profile (French and American mix), the grape blend incorporating Garnacha and Mazuelo, the terracotta-tinged garnet colour, and the vanilla/coconut note are all diagnostic for an experienced blind taster.
