# Brachetto d'Acqui, Contero, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
Bright ruby-pink colour with gentle frizzante bubbles; vibrantly aromatic nose of strawberry, raspberry, rose petal, cherry and red berry candy — Brachetto's distinctive floral-fruity signature. Light-bodied (5.5% ABV), gently sweet, with soft, low tannins and refreshing red-fruit acidity; the low alcohol makes this feel almost like a sophisticated cordial. Short, fruity, pleasant finish. Unmistakably sweet and sparkling red — a category with few lookalikes in a blind tasting.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Brachetto
- Vineyard: Acqui Terme area, Alessandria province, Piedmont; can overlap into Asti province
- Vinification: Charmat (tank) method for spumante style, or natural refermentation for frizzante; arrested fermentation preserves sweetness and low alcohol
- Elevage: Tank; early bottling; consumed fresh
- ABV / RS / TA: 5.5% / sweet (approx 70-100 g/L RS) / medium-high acidity (for balance)
- DOCG since 1996; must be 100% Brachetto (Source: Wikipedia)
- Minimum harvest: 8 tonnes/ha; minimum ABV potential 12% for spumante (Source: Wikipedia)

## Vintage character (2020 Piedmont)
2020 was generally a warm, good-quality vintage in Piedmont. For Brachetto d'Acqui at 5.5% ABV, vintage variation is minimised by the winemaking process (fermentation arrest), so the character is driven primarily by Brachetto's aromatics and the house style rather than vintage weather.
