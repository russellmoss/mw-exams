# La Garnacha de Mustiguillo, Toni Sarrión, 2015. El Terrerazo, La Mancha, Spain.

## Quick tasting profile
Ruby-crimson. Nose: red cherry, raspberry, rose petal, elegant and delicate for 14% ABV — cold maceration and pigeage-only extraction have preserved aromatic freshness. Hints of dried herbs (garrigue-adjacent). Palate: medium-bodied, fresh acidity (5.4-6.1 g/L), fine tannins, elegant and food-friendly rather than hedonistically extracted. The high-altitude site (800m) and dolomitic limestone soils contribute mineral freshness and restrained power.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Garnacha (Grenache)
- Vineyard: El Terrerazo at 800m elevation; dolomitic limestone soils; dry-farmed; 1.9 kg/plant yield
- Vinification: Cold maceration in harvest bins; 20% whole bunches; 10-day primary fermentation with pigeage only; 8-day post-fermentation maceration; gravity-fed racking of free-run juice only
- Elevage: 8 months combined in French oak barriques and large oak vats
- ABV / RS / TA: 14% / dry (<1.5 g/L RS) / 5.4-6.1 g/L
- pH: 3.52-3.55
*(Source: mustiguillo.com producer page — technical data confirmed)*

## Vintage character (2015 El Terrerazo / La Mancha)
2015 was a hot, dry summer across Spain, with the Meseta (central plateau) experiencing intense heat. At 800m elevation, El Terrerazo has natural protection against the most extreme temperatures. The 2015 La Garnacha shows ripe fruit at 14% ABV while retaining the freshness characteristic of altitude sites. (Source needed: 2015 La Mancha/Terres Castille VT vintage report)

## Producer style
Mustiguillo is a pioneering estate in El Terrerazo (Valencia-La Mancha border), led by Toni Sarrión. The winery operates under Vino de la Tierra Terres Castille (VT) rather than DOP because their focus on Bobal and Garnacha as primary varieties sits outside DO Utiel-Requena's Bobal-dominated regulations. Mustiguillo is internationally recognised as one of Spain's most interesting artisan producers, working high-altitude dryland vineyards on dolomitic limestone. Toni Sarrión's winemaking philosophy emphasises minimal intervention, terroir expression, and elegance over extraction. Market positioning: $25-45, widely listed in good European wine merchants. (source: mustiguillo.com)

## Why it's in this exam
La Garnacha de Mustiguillo is a curveball in the purest sense: Spanish Garnacha from a VdlT outside the canonical Priorat/Rioja/Navarra/Aragon framework — at 800m elevation in inland Valencia/La Mancha. The candidate must identify: the Garnacha variety (common in Spain, France, Australia), the high-altitude elevation freshness, the VdlT classification, and distinguish it from Côtes du Rhône or Priorat Garnacha. The cold-maceration technique and pigeage-only approach produce a lighter, more elegant profile than expected for 14% Spanish red.
