# Chablis 1er Cru Côte de Lechet, Domaine Defaix, 2010

## Quick tasting profile
At 15+ years of age, expect deep gold with silver reflections at the rim. The nose shows salted lemon peel, bosc pear, wet mineral, white tea leaves, blanched almond, crushed oyster shell, and an attractive leesy/autolytic complexity from extended lees contact. The palate is voluminous with bracing acidity, racy mineral persistence, and a saline finish — classic aged Chablis from a traditionalist producer who releases wines only at maturity. (source: wineaccess.com tasting note)

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Côte de Lechet Premier Cru, 3.5 ha owned by the family within the "Clos des Moines" section; drastically steep 45% gradient slopes overlooking the Serein valley, south-east exposure (source: Rosenthal Wine Merchant; Vinfolio)
- Soils: Kimmeridgian limestone/marl (Jurassic calcareous), shallow and rocky (source: chablisdefaix.com)
- Vine age: ~45 years (source: Rosenthal)
- Vinification: Soft pressing over 3 hours using only first-run juice; month-long fermentation with indigenous yeasts at 18C; full malolactic (can take up to 2 years to complete in the cold cellar). All stainless steel — no oak. (source: ofwineandwhimsy.co.uk)
- Elevage: Minimum 18 months on fine lees in stainless steel, but 1er Cru wines typically age 6-9 years on lees before release. Batonnage without air exposure or added sulfur. No fining, no filtration, no cold stabilization. (source: Vinfolio; ofwineandwhimsy.co.uk)
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2010 Chablis)
2010 was a triumphant vintage in Chablis — Decanter awarded it 5/5 stars. Cool weather extended the growing season, producing wines with great purity, powerful acidity, and dense structure. Poor flowering limited yields, concentrating flavors. The wines are similar in racy character to the 2008s but with a touch more weight. An outstanding vintage for mineral-driven, age-worthy Chablis, and this wine — released after extended lees aging — is drinking within its window (suggested through 2030). (source: Decanter vintage guide; wineaccess.com)

## Producer style
Domaine Daniel-Etienne Defaix is one of the oldest estates in Chablis, family-owned since 1610 (16th generation). The house style is radically traditionalist: extended lees aging in stainless steel (6-12 years for Premier/Grand Cru), indigenous yeast fermentation, no oak, no fining, no filtration. This approach — revived by Daniel-Etienne in 1978 from his grandfather's methods — produces sapid, gourmand wines with autolytic complexity that are only released at maturity. Holdings span ~28 ha across village, three Premier Crus (Les Lys, Vaillon, Côte de Lechet), and Grand Cru (Blanchot/Grenouilles). Market positioning is mid-tier: approximately GBP 40-50 for Premier Cru. The estate is a "Source needed" for formal organic certification status, though they practice organic viticulture with biodynamic influences. (source: Vinfolio; ofwineandwhimsy.co.uk)

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 3 sits in 2025 Paper 1, Question 2: "Wines 3-6 are from the same single grape variety" (Chardonnay). This is the oldest, most terroir-driven, unoaked expression in the flight — a cool-climate, mineral, mature Chablis Premier Cru at 13% ABV designed to contrast with a Monterey Chardonnay (w4, 14%), an Elgin Highlands Chardonnay (w5, 12.5%), and a Margaret River Chardonnay (w6, 14%). The examiner is testing the candidate's ability to distinguish unoaked, lees-aged, high-acid Old World Chardonnay from oaked and/or warmer-climate New World expressions, and to assess maturity in a wine with 15 years of age.
