# Sauvignon Blanc, Southern Right, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
This is not the loud Marlborough style. Expect a dry, mineral Sauvignon Blanc with citrus, herbs and subtle stone or saline notes, where texture and length matter more than pungent thiol fireworks. The palate should be brisk and tensile, with moderate body, bright acidity and a lightly structural mid-palate.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Sauvignon Blanc
- Vineyard: fruit from Hemel-en-Aarde Valley, Upper Hemel-en-Aarde Valley and other Walker Bay sources
- Soils: 51% clay-rich shale-derived soils and 49% Table Mountain sandstone-derived soils
- Vinification: natural and endemic yeasts are emphasized in the producer background
- Elevage: 9% matured in neutral barrels
- ABV / RS / TA: official sheet 13.32%; exam corpus rounds to 13.5% / 2.10 g/L / 6.86 g/L

## Vintage character (2021 Walker Bay / South Africa)
Southern Right's own 2021 sheet describes a later, cooler year with wetter winter conditions, delayed harvest and exceptional acidity, leading to wines of layered elegance, purity and mineral liveliness. WoSA's national 2021 report broadly agrees, highlighting slow ripening, low pH and high natural acidity. In blind tasting, this should therefore read as a cool-climate, origin-led Sauvignon rather than a simple varietal style.

## Producer style
Southern Right is a small specialist property founded by Anthony Hamilton Russell in 1994, with a stated aim of making site-driven wines in a cool maritime setting. The background note stresses that the Sauvignon Blanc is made to avoid excessive varietal caricature and instead foreground minerality, texture and place. That makes it a useful MW exam wine because it asks candidates to distinguish noble restraint from under-expression.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 12 completes 2023 Paper 1 Question 3. It gives the examiner a third grape variety from the same country, but in a style that can wrong-foot candidates expecting overt Sauvignon markers. The key is not just naming Sauvignon Blanc, but explaining why this market-facing wine still behaves like serious regional fine wine.
