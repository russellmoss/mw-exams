# Corton-Charlemagne, Domaine Louis Jadot, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-deep gold. Complex, multi-dimensional nose of citrus zest, freshly baked bread, beeswax, buttered popcorn, vanilla pod, confit citrus, and hints of petrol. Full-bodied, satiny, and layered on the palate — rich and muscular with a concentrated, structured core of fruit girdled by racy acids and chalky dry extract. Explosive on the palate at not much more than 14.5%, with a long, resonant finish. CellarTracker community average 93.1 pts.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: 4.94-acre (2-ha) parcel on the Corton hill adjacent to Les Pougets, purchased 1914
- Soils: Calcareous marl, limestone scree
- Vinification: Manual harvest, gentle pressing; fermentation in barrels from Jadot's own cooperage
- Élevage: 18 months in barrel, approximately one-third new oak
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.5% / dry / Source needed
*(Malo sometimes partially blocked to retain acidity and finesse — source: Kobrand tech sheet.)*

## Vintage character (2019 Burgundy whites)
2019 was generous with amazing grapes but small yields. Nearly perfect weather throughout the growing season. Wines are gracious, delicate, and full of freshness despite the warm year. The southern half of the Corton hill produced extraordinary wines — electric and collectors-worthy. Sunny but not over-ripe; wines reward patience, best after 2027.

## Producer style
Louis Jadot, founded 1859, is one of Burgundy's largest and most respected négociant-éleveur houses, also holding significant domaine vineyards (Héritiers Louis Jadot, Domaine André Gagey). Philosophy of restraint with oak — aim to let terroir and vintage speak. Winemaking under the long tenure of Jacques Lardière (1970-2012), now Frédéric Barnier. The Corton-Charlemagne is one of the house's crown jewels. Also appears in the corpus as Moulin À Vent Château des Jacques (2016_p2_w5).

## Why it's in this exam
Paired with wine 4 (Yellow Tail Chardonnay) as a Chardonnay pair in Q1. This is the ultimate quality contrast: a Grand Cru Burgundy at the apex of Chardonnay production versus a mass-market commodity wine. Tests ability to identify the same variety across radically different quality tiers, and to comment on maturity and capacity to age.
