# Brut Rosé, Simonnet-Febvre, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Pale salmon-pink with fine, persistent bubbles (traditional method); fresh and appealing nose of red berry fruit, citrus, white flowers and a subtle brioche/yeasty note from lees aging; lighter in body than Champagne rosé (12% ABV), clean and crisp palate with brut dryness and refreshing finish. More linear and less complex than Bollinger Rosé (w3) but showing traditional method quality above the Charmat wines — a good-value Crémant expression of Burgundy's rosé sparkling credentials.

- Grape variety: Minimum 30% Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Pinot Blanc or Pinot Gris required; remainder typically Aligoté (Source: Wikipedia)
- Hand-harvested with controlled yields per Crémant regulations

Non-vintage; Crémant de Bourgogne NV blends harvests from multiple years, typically with Pinot Noir sourced from Burgundy's main growing zones (Côte d'Or, Yonne, Mâconnais).

## Producer style
Simonnet-Febvre is one of Burgundy's oldest négociant houses, founded in 1840 in Chablis. They are the leading producer of Crémant de Bourgogne and among Chablis' most important négociants.
