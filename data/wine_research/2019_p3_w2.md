# Brut Grand Cru Millésime, Egly-Ouriet, 2007. Champagne, France

## Quick tasting profile
Deep golden colour with fine, mature mousse. Nose is complex and evolved at 12 years on disgorgement (tasted 2019): rich autolytic notes of toasted bread, brioche, hazelnut; layered fruit of poached apple, quince, dried apricot, lemon curd; savouriness and a hint of oxidative complexity. Palate is full, textured, with creamy mousse, pronounced depth, high intensity and a long, complex finish. High-end Pinot Noir-dominant Grand Cru Champagne at peak maturity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Predominantly Pinot Noir from Grand Cru villages; possibly with Chardonnay; Grand Cru vineyards in Ambonnay, Verzenay and Bouzy (70% Pinot Noir / 30% Chardonnay in vineyard — source: Wikipedia Egly-Ouriet)
- Vineyard: Grand Cru Montagne de Reims hillside vineyards (Ambonnay, Verzenay, Bouzy) — plus Vrigny premier cru for Pinot Meunier
- Soils: Chalk bedrock with argilo-calcaire (clay-chalk) topsoil
- Vinification: Extended barrel ageing — Egly-Ouriet uses 3–4 years lees ageing, exceeding house standards significantly (source: Wikipedia). Fermentation partly in barrel
- Elevage: 3–4 years minimum on lees in bottle (source: Wikipedia); later disgorgement than standard; very low dosage

## Vintage character (2007 Champagne)
2007 Champagne was a challenging but underrated vintage. Cool growing season with some rot pressure; rigorous selection needed. The wines that succeeded show bright acidity, freshness and mineral precision — not the opulence of 2008 or 2012, but elegant, classically styled Champagnes that have aged gracefully. At 12 years of age by the 2019 exam, Egly-Ouriet 2007 would be showing exceptional maturity and autolytic complexity.

## Producer style
Egly-Ouriet is a grower-producer (RM) based in Ambonnay, fourth generation under Francis Egly (source: Wikipedia). Rated three stars in La Revue du Vin de France; ranked among top five Champagne producers by Andrew Jefford in "The New France." Extended barrel ageing (3–4 years) and very low dosage are house hallmarks — producing wines of exceptional complexity and ageing potential. Considered the definitive "wine-geekiest grower Champagne" (source: Wikipedia). Market position: premium grower ($100–200+). In 2024, became Singapore Airlines business class supplier — confirming prestige positioning.

## Why it's in this exam
Egly-Ouriet Millésime is the artisanal grower Champagne benchmark contrasting with Perrier-Jouët Grand Brut. It tests the candidate's ability to identify vintage complexity, autolytic depth, low dosage, and grower-producer style. The 2007 vintage at 12 years demonstrates exceptional development and longevity — a direct MW lesson in Champagne ageing.
