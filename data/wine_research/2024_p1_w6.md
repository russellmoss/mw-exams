# Sauvignon Blanc Section 94, Dog Point, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Medium gold (deeper than typical Marlborough SB). Complex nose of gunflint, blackcurrant leaf, hazelnut, savoury toast, struck match, honeysuckle, chamomile, spicy marmalade, and ripe peach. Fleshy, smooth palate with praline-like flavours, crisp white stone fruit, rounded citrus, dried mango, and candied ginger. A touch of lanolin. Full-bodied, textural, with racy acidity and immense complexity. Scores: Bob Campbell MW 96, Cameron Douglas MS 96, James Suckling 94.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Sauvignon Blanc
- Vineyard: Section 94, single-site old vines
- Soils: Source needed (Marlborough alluvial)
- Vinification: 100% wild/native yeast fermentation; whole-cluster pressing
- Élevage: 18 months fermentation and aging in older French oak barrels (Pouilly-Fumé style)
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.5% / dry / Source needed
- Bottled without fining, minimal filtration

## Producer style
Dog Point was founded by James Healy (winemaker) and Ivan Sutherland (viticulturist), both formerly of Cloudy Bay. They were among the first in Marlborough to ferment Sauvignon Blanc in barrel with wild yeasts. The philosophy is non-interventionist: extended barrel aging with virtually no intervention, no fining, minimal filtration. The estate farms biodynamically. Section 94 is their flagship white — a barrel-fermented Sauvignon Blanc in the Pouilly-Fumé style that challenges the typical aromatic Marlborough paradigm. Also in the corpus: Pinot Noir 2012 (appears in an earlier year).

## Why it's in this exam
Paired with wine 5 (Cowrie Bay Sauvignon Blanc 2023) as a Sauvignon Blanc pair in Q1. This is the high-quality, atypical anchor: a wild-fermented, barrel-aged, 14.5% ABV Sauvignon Blanc that many candidates might not recognise as Marlborough SB at all. The contrast tests understanding of winemaking impact on style, quality assessment, and ageability (drinking window 2024-2034 for this wine).
