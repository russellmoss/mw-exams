# Pinot Noir, Rockburn, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Expect a generously fruited but still cool-climate New Zealand Pinot: vivid red and black cherry, plum, mocha and spice, with silky tannins and a long, polished finish. Compared with the Sonoma wine, this should read a little more lifted and fragrant; compared with Baden, more openly fruit-led; compared with Niagara, less ferrous and more immediately accessible.

## Vintage character (2022 Central Otago)
Rockburn describes 2022 as yielding beautiful fruit across its sites, with the winery taking a minimal-handling approach to preserve a pure Central Otago expression. The estate's own regional notes stress that Central Otago contains meaningful internal sub-regional differences, with Gibbston tending toward perfume and earth while Lowburn/Bannockburn zones give darker, fuller fruit. This bottling blends Parkburn and Gibbston, so in blind tasting expect both fragrance and generous fruit.

## Producer style
Rockburn positions itself as a premium Central Otago specialist and emphasizes the region's cool-climate varietal intensity. The estate's story highlights its spread across different Central Otago "appellations" and its belief that Pinot Noir changes significantly by sub-zone. The 2022 estate Pinot is made in a deliberately hands-off style, with moderate new French oak and a smooth, elegant texture aimed at broad appeal without losing regional identity.
