# Chinuri, Iago, 2016

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold to amber rather than pale straw, with quince, dried herbs, tea, orchard fruit skin and a lightly earthy note. The palate is dry, textured and phenolic, carrying moderate alcohol, firm grip and a savoury finish. In blind terms this points clearly to skin-contact white wine rather than conventional reductive white production.

## Producer style
Iago Bitarishvili is one of the emblematic figures of Georgia's natural wine movement and a leading advocate for Chinuri in Kartli. The wines are rooted in traditional qvevri practice, emphasizing texture, savoury complexity and indigenous Georgian identity over international white-wine polish.

## Why it's in this exam
