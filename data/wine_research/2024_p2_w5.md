# Brunello di Montalcino Amore e Magia, Podere Le Ripi, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Bright ruby with deep core developing brick tints. Beguiling nose of dusty violets, exotic spices, cedar, clove, black cherries, sweet cherry, wild rose, lavender bud, candied orange, and iron ore. Concentrated yet weightless palate with juicy black cherry, raspberry jam, licorice, and espresso. Plush textures, tart wild berry interplay with minerals and minty herbs. Late-arriving tannins lead to a structured, classically dry finish with blood orange and wild strawberry nuances. Critic average ~93 points.

## Vintage character (2017 Brunello)
Four-star vintage, below the heights of 2016. Hot, dry growing season with early budbreak (2 weeks ahead). Late April frost damaged some parcels. July-August extremely hot and dry, causing hydric stress and some berry shrivelling. Saving grace: September rains with cool nights slowed ripening and preserved freshness. Variable results — higher-altitude estates fared better. Wines show concentration and high alcohol but can lack the elegance of cooler years. Best producers made balanced, aromatic wines. Drinking window: next 5-8 years for most. (source: Decanter, Wine Enthusiast)

## Producer style
Founded 1997 by Francesco Illy (of the coffee family). Certified organic and Demeter biodynamic. 12 ha of Sangiovese. Distinctive features include an experimental "Bonsai" vineyard at 62,500 vines/ha and a spiral cellar built to Golden Ratio principles. Integrated farming with synergic vegetable gardens, orchards, beehives, and animals since 2018. "Amore e Magia" is their top Brunello expression. Also appears in the 2018 MW exam as Brunello Riserva Lupi di Serene (2018_p2_w1) — the IMW clearly values this producer for its biodynamic credentials and distinctive approach.

## Why it's in this exam

The warm 2017 vintage character (ripe, high-ABV) combined with biodynamic production makes for rich exam discussion. At 14.5% it shares the same ABV as the Barolo and Saint-Emilion, testing whether candidates can distinguish by variety and terroir.
