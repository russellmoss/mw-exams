# Vigna d'Alceo, Castello dei Rampolla, 2012. Tuscany, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple with minimal garnet at edge. Nose of blackcurrant, dark cherry, cassis, violets, cigar box and graphite with a firm structural backbone — unmistakably Cabernet Sauvignon-led. Palate is full-bodied, with dense but fine-grained tannins, good acidity, concentrated fruit and a long finish. New oak integration adds vanilla and spice. Serious ageing potential; a benchmark Super Tuscan.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Predominantly Cabernet Sauvignon with Petit Verdot (reportedly ~85% Cabernet Sauvignon, ~15% Petit Verdot — source: widely cited trade sources, Source needed for exact 2012 split)
- Vineyard: Single vineyard "Vigna d'Alceo" within the Castello dei Rampolla estate in Panzano in Chianti, Chianti Classico zone
- Soils: Galestro schist and alberese limestone-clay typical of high-altitude Panzano
- Vinification: Gravity-fed winery; biodynamic viticulture (Rampolla has been biodynamic since the early 2000s); fermentation in concrete or wood
- Elevage: Aged in French barriques (new and second-fill); duration approximately 18–24 months (Source needed for exact 2012 protocol)
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2012 Tuscany)
2012 in Tuscany was a warm, relatively dry year producing wines of concentration and extraction. Harvest occurred in ideal conditions after a dry summer — wines tend toward density and depth rather than freshness. For Panzano's high-altitude sites, the heat was partially mitigated, yielding powerful but structured reds with good tannic backbone.

## Producer style
Castello dei Rampolla is one of the historic estates in Panzano in Chianti, owned by the di Napoli Rampolla family. Long associated with biodynamic viticulture — one of the earliest adopters in Tuscany. Their flagship wines are Vigna d'Alceo (IGT Super Tuscan, Cab Sauv/Petit Verdot) and Sammarco (Cabernet Franc/Sangiovese blend). The estate is small-production and artisanal, with prices placing it firmly in the premium Tuscan tier. Vigna d'Alceo has been described as one of Italy's finest Cabernet-based wines.

## Why it's in this exam
This wine tests the candidate's ability to identify a high-quality Cabernet Sauvignon-dominated Italian red as a Super Tuscan rather than a Bordeaux. The Panzano terroir imparts distinctly Italian character (higher acidity, more herbal lift) versus a Médoc equivalent. In a Paper 2 context, it likely anchors a question on premium Italian reds or Old World versus New World Cab.
