# Pelorus Rosé, Cloudy Bay, NV. Marlborough, New Zealand

## Quick tasting profile
Pale salmon-pink with persistent fine mousse and a delicate bead. Nose of fresh strawberry, cherry, brioche, and subtle autolytic notes (lees contact from traditional method). Dry to off-dry on the palate, crisp Marlborough acidity, light-medium body, clean finish with red fruit persistence. Elegant and food-friendly sparkling rosé in the European Champagne-influenced style, though with brighter, fresher fruit than Champagne counterparts.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Predominantly Pinot Noir and/or Chardonnay blend (standard traditional method rosé base; Source needed for exact Pelorus Rosé blend percentages)
- Winemaking method: Traditional method (méthode traditionnelle / méthode champenoise) — secondary fermentation in bottle; lees ageing; disgorgement
- Vineyard: Marlborough, South Island, primarily from Cloudy Bay's Wairau Valley estate vineyards
- Soils: Stony free-draining Wairau Valley alluvial soils
- Vinification: Base wines fermented separately; blending; tirage (addition of sugar + yeast for secondary fermentation); bottle fermentation; extended lees contact; disgorgement; dosage (dry style, likely brut dosage)
- ABV: 12.5%

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage blend designed for consistency year to year. Marlborough provides the fresh, high-acidity base wines essential to high-quality traditional method sparkling.

## Producer style
Cloudy Bay is Marlborough's most internationally recognised wine brand, established 1985 by David Hohnen and now owned by LVMH (Moët Hennessy). Known primarily for Sauvignon Blanc, the Pelorus range is their traditional-method sparkling line, developed as a premium add-on to the main portfolio. Pelorus wines are produced in both NV (non-vintage) and vintage formats, in still white and rosé styles. The brand targets premium restaurant and on-trade positioning globally. ## Why it's in this exam
Cloudy Bay Pelorus Rosé sits in P3's "pink wine" flight alongside Sutter Home White Zinfandel and Pradel Billette Rosé — the three represent three fundamentally different styles of pink wine: sweet still Californian White Zinfandel, bone-dry still Provence rosé, and dry traditional-method sparkling New Zealand rosé.
