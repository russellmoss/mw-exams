# Chateau Larrivet Haut-Brion, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
This should read clearly as a Bordeaux blend: cassis, cedar, tobacco leaf, graphite and dark plum with firmer structure and more restraint than the New World wines in the same question. The palate should combine ripe 2018 fruit with dry extract, oak seasoning and a classical savory finish.

## Vintage character (2018 Bordeaux)
2018 combined disease pressure early in the season with a very warm, dry, successful run into harvest, producing concentrated reds with ripe tannins and notable power. In Pessac-Leognan that can mean both plush fruit and serious structure, especially in well-handled estates.

## Producer style
Larrivet Haut-Brion is a serious but not first-growth Pessac-Leognan estate, which makes it useful in exams because it offers recognizable claret structure without requiring candidates to infer a trophy label. The house style tends to emphasize balance between ripe fruit, oak polish and classical Bordeaux lines.

## Why it's in this exam
