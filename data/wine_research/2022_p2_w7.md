# Bourgogne Hautes Cotes de Beaune, Domaine Frederic Esmonin, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
This should be fragrant and comparatively light for Burgundy Pinot Noir: red cherry, raspberry, earth and gentle spice with moderate alcohol and fresh acidity. The wine is more about drinkability and regional charm than grand cru depth.

## Vintage character (2019 Burgundy)
2019 brought concentration and ripe fruit to Burgundy, but cooler elevated sites could still preserve freshness. In Hautes Cotes terms that means a bit more generosity than very lean years, while staying clearly lighter and less layered than grand cru Cote de Nuits.

## Producer style
Domaine Frederic Esmonin is a Burgundian producer whose wines span modest and more serious appellations. Here the important point is typicity: this is the simpler regional/subregional Burgundy in the pair, meant to contrast with the scale and structure of Clos de Vougeot.
