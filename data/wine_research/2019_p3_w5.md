# Rosé, Château Miraval, 2018. Côtes de Provence, France

## Quick tasting profile
Pale salmon/peach-pink, brilliant clarity, classic Provence rosé colour. Nose of red berry (strawberry, raspberry), white peach, dried herbs (garrigue), light floral notes and a stony minerality. Palate is dry, fresh and crisp with medium-light body, bright acidity, a refreshing texture and a clean, slightly savoury finish. Benchmark pale Provence rosé: restrained, elegant, precise.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Classic Provence blend including Cinsault, Grenache, Syrah, and possibly Rolle (Vermentino) and others; exact 2018 blend proportions Source needed
- Vineyard: Château Miraval estate in Correns (Var), Côtes de Provence — "30 hectares on the valley floor devoted to organic vines" (source: Wikipedia Château Miraval)
- Soils: Calcareous limestone and clay-limestone soils typical of Provence
- Vinification: Direct pressing (saignée method not typically used in premium Provence rosé); temperature-controlled fermentation; no oak
- Elevage: Stainless steel; early bottling to preserve freshness
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2018 Provence)
2018 was a warm, generous year in Provence — wines show good fruit ripeness and aromatic depth while the earlier harvest preserved the freshness needed for quality rosé. Generally considered an excellent vintage for Côtes de Provence rosé: balanced, aromatic, with good structure.

## Producer style
Château Miraval is located in Correns, Var. Owned jointly (controversially) by Brad Pitt (continuing operator) after Angelina Jolie's sale to Stoli Group's Tenute del Mondo (2021 — source: Wikipedia). The wine gained fame from the Pitt-Jolie ownership from 2011 and critical acclaim from Wine Spectator. Organic viticulture; 30 hectares (source: Wikipedia). Also collaborates with Péters on Fleur de Miraval rosé Champagne. Price positioning: premium for Provence rosé ($20–30). Style: deliberately Provence classic — pale, dry, precise, garrigue-inflected.

## Why it's in this exam
Miraval is the flagship Côtes de Provence benchmark: a celebrity-profile wine that is also genuinely high quality. In a Paper 3 rosé group, it anchors the Old World dry Provence rosé type. Key identification signals: pale colour, dry, restrained red fruit/herb/mineral profile, moderate ABV, Provence appellation character.
