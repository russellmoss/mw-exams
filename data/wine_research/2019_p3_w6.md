# Rosado, Cune, 2018. Rioja, Spain

## Quick tasting profile
Medium pink, brighter and deeper than Provence rosé — more vibrant salmon-red. Nose of strawberry, red cherry, dried herbs, some white spice and a slight earthy rusticity typical of Rioja. Palate is fresh, medium-bodied, dry but with fuller flavour than Provence style; some grip and body. Higher ABV (13.5%) signals warmer southern European production. Described by the producer as "a fresh, perhaps somewhat rustic, rosé, whose flavour reflects the typical character of rosés from Haro, Rioja Alta."

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Tempranillo (source: CVNE producer website)
- Vineyard: Hand-harvested; grapes chilled in stainless steel at 10–12°C to prevent premature fermentation (source: CVNE)
- Soils: Calcareous clay typical of Rioja Alta around Haro
- Vinification: Short skin contact (24–48 hours at 10–12°C until desired pink colour develops), then juice drained off; fermentation at controlled 16–18°C to preserve aromatics (source: CVNE)
- Elevage: Stainless steel only; early bottling

## Vintage character (2018 Rioja)
2018 was a warm, generous vintage in Rioja. Rosado wines from this year show good fruit ripeness with higher natural alcohol; the freshness depends on careful harvest timing. At CVNE's quality level, the 2018 Rosado is expected to be ripe and aromatic with characteristic Tempranillo strawberry fruit character.

## Producer style
CVNE (Compañía Vinícola del Norte de España) was founded in 1879 in Haro, Rioja Alta — one of Rioja's oldest and most respected bodegas. The Cune label (CVNE's commercial range) is widely distributed internationally. CVNE also owns Imperial and Viña Real brands. The Rosado is a classic Spanish pink wine made from Tempranillo, showing the savoury, grippy character typical of Haro-style rosados versus the delicate French rosé style. Market position: value-to-mid tier ($12–18).

## Why it's in this exam
CVNE Rosado provides the Spanish counterpoint to Provence rosé in the Paper 3 rosé comparison.
