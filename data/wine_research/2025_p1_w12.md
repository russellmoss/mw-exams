# Vin Santo del Chianti Rufina, Selvapiana, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Amber to slightly tawny colour with pronounced intensity. The nose is intoxicating: restrained green orange peel, celery, dried figs, apricot jam, walnuts, and vermouth-like complexity. Full-bodied on the palate with layered apricot and candied orange flavours, rich and velvety texture, but sustained by remarkable acidity that maintains tension and freshness. Outstanding, lingering finish with notes of toasted nuts and honey. A Vin Santo driven by crystalline purity and acidity rather than mere viscosity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Trebbiano Toscano (source: Vinous / Selvapiana website)
- Vineyard: Estate vineyards in Rufina, Pontassieve, and Pelago municipalities; up to 350m elevation
- Soils: Source needed (Chianti Rufina generally features galestro/alberese calcareous clay soils)
- Vinification: Grapes harvested end of September, hung from bamboo rods to dry (appassimento) for approximately five months. Pressed and fermented in traditional small caratelli (50-litre barrels), mostly French and Slavonian oak with some chestnut. No selected yeasts used since 1992.
- Elevage: Six years in sealed caratelli with the madre (yeast lees culture); oxidative environment from headspace in partially filled barrels

- Bottle size: 500ml

## Vintage character (2015 Tuscany)
The 2015 Tuscan vintage was excellent despite a variable growing season. A wet, cold winter transitioned to a temperamental spring; sizzling daytime temperatures were tempered by cool nights, preserving freshness. August rains and some hail reduced yields but concentrated flavours. Harvest was slightly earlier than usual. The resulting wines combine power with elegance -- richly fruited with balanced structure. For Vin Santo, the warm vintage likely produced grapes with high sugar concentration at harvest, contributing to the wine's richness and weight.

## Producer style
Fattoria Selvapiana is a benchmark Chianti Rufina estate, family-owned since 1827 when banker Michele Giuntini acquired the property -- originally a medieval watchtower and summer villa for the bishops of Florence. Now run by fifth-generation Federico and Silvia Giuntini Masseti (adopted children of former estate manager Franco Masseti). The estate spans 250 hectares (58ha vines, 36ha olives, 140ha woodland) across Rufina, Pontassieve, and Pelago. Organic certified (pioneered with Fornace vineyard in 1990, fully converted thereafter). Consultant oenologist Franco Bernabei has worked with the estate since 1978. Known for pioneering 100% Sangiovese Chianti Riserva (1978) and single-vineyard Bucerchiale (1979). The Vin Santo is a flagship traditional wine -- aged six years in caratelli -- with a reputation for acidity-driven purity rather than cloying sweetness. Wines reflect the cooler Rufina microclimate near the Sieve River: fresh acidity, finesse, and age-worthiness. Market positioning is mid-to-upper tier ($38-73 USD per 500ml for the Vin Santo). James Suckling awarded the 2015 vintage 95-97 points, ranking it #59 in the Top 100 Wines of Italy 2024.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 12 in 2025 Paper 1, Question 4: "Wines 10-12 are from three different European countries... comment on the style of the wine with reference to the relative importance of human inputs versus natural factors... identify the grape variety and origin as closely as possible." The Vin Santo is the Italian entry alongside a German Riesling (Mosel) and a Spanish Rioja Blanco Gran Reserva. It tests the candidate's ability to identify a traditional, artisanal winemaking style (Vin Santo) where human inputs (appassimento, caratelli aging, madre culture, six-year elevage) are paramount and arguably outweigh terroir expression -- a direct answer to the question's framing about human vs. natural factors. Also tests recognition of an unusual wine in a white wine paper (Vin Santo appearing among still whites) and knowledge of Trebbiano as a variety.
