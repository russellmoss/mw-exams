# Single Harvest Boal, Henriques & Henriques, 2000

## Quick tasting profile
Deep tawny-amber to mahogany with greenish rim after 21 years of canteiro aging (assessed 2021); extraordinary complexity on the nose — roasted nuts, dried apricot, orange peel, beeswax, coffee, caramel and the distinctive rancio/aldehyde quality of very old Madeira; medium-sweet (Boal is medium to medium-sweet), balanced by Madeira's searingly high acidity making it feel fresh and vital despite 21 years in barrel/bottle; extremely long finish with sweet-sour tension. One of the most complex fortified wines in any exam category.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Boal (also spelled Bual) — Madeira's medium-sweet grape variety; the second sweetest of the four noble varieties (Malvasia > Boal > Verdelho > Sercial)
- Vineyard: Madeira island, sourced from 2000 harvest
- Vinification: Partial fermentation; fortification with grape spirit; aged via canteiro (passive natural warming) rather than estufagem — the premium aging method
- Elevage: Single Harvest (Colheita) requires minimum 5 years aging; premium bottlings age much longer in old wood under canteiro; this 2000 would have been aged 20+ years
- ABV / RS / TA: 20% / medium-sweet / extremely high acidity
- "Single Harvest" = Colheita = single vintage, aged in cask

## Producer style
Henriques & Henriques (see w11 for producer detail) is one of the few houses maintaining production of single-vintage and Frasqueira Madeiras alongside commercial ranges. Their Single Harvest range represents their premium single-vintage tier, using canteiro natural aging in sun-exposed lodges — the highest quality production method for Madeira. This wine represents the apex of the Henriques & Henriques range.
