# Riesling, Giesen, 2015. New Zealand

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon-green, clear and bright. Nose of lime, green apple, white peach, lemon zest, and a hint of petrol (even in youth at 2 years — Riesling's TDN character emerges quickly at this commercial tier). Light-medium body, off-dry (the Giesen range includes both dry and slightly off-dry styles; the low ABV of 10% is a strong indicator of residual sweetness and/or arrested fermentation). Crisp acidity, lively, fresh palate. Approachable commercial Riesling.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Giesen sources from Canterbury and Marlborough in New Zealand; their home estate is in Canterbury but Marlborough fruit is used for the main range (Source needed for 2015 exact sourcing)
- Soils: Free-draining stony South Island soils (Source needed for specific vineyard)
- Vinification: Early harvest for freshness; temperature-controlled fermentation; some residual sugar retained (consistent with 10% ABV — arrested fermentation or chaptalization unlikely in NZ)
- Elevage: Stainless steel; no oak; early release
- ABV: 10.0% — significant indicator of off-dry style (residual sugar balance)

## Vintage character (2015 New Zealand Riesling)
2015 was a good vintage in New Zealand — a warm year that produced ripe, generous wines. For commercial Riesling, the warmth would have delivered good fruit intensity with the refreshing acidity characteristic of New Zealand's cool South Island sites. At 10% ABV and commercial tier, vintage variation has moderate impact.

## Producer style
Giesen is a large, family-owned New Zealand wine producer (three brothers: Alex, Marcel, and Theo Giesen) based in Canterbury with significant Marlborough sourcing. The winery is one of New Zealand's major commercial producers, known particularly for Sauvignon Blanc but also for Riesling across a broad price range. The estate Riesling is typically dry or lightly off-dry. At 10% ABV this is toward the lighter end of the range, suggesting a deliberately restrained, fresh style. ## Why it's in this exam
Giesen Riesling at 10% ABV represents the commercial off-dry New World Riesling tier in P3's mixed flight. The low ABV and fresh, accessible style contrast sharply with the Late Harvest Gewürztraminer (w6) and fortified wines later in the paper. Key exam signals: low ABV suggesting off-dry style, New Zealand Riesling's lime/TDN character, no oak, no complexity.
