# La Bruja, Comando G, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Transparent ruby, pale for central Spain, with lifted red cherry, wild herbs and stony perfume rather than jam or extraction. Blind, this should signal high-altitude Garnacha from granite: aromatic, tensile and fine-boned, with acidity and delicate tannin carrying the wine more than body or new oak (source: https://www.europeancellars.com/wine/la-bruja/; https://www.europeancellars.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Comando-G-Bruja-1.pdf).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Garnacha (source: https://www.europeancellars.com/wine/la-bruja/)
- Vineyard: Several vineyards around Las Rozas / the Cebreros zone in the Sierra de Gredos, around 900-1000m altitude (source: https://www.europeancellars.com/wine/la-bruja/; https://www.europeancellars.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Comando-G-Bruja-1.pdf)
- Soils: Sandy granite with silt; broader estate material also mentions granite, slate and quartz in the mountain sites (source: https://www.europeancellars.com/wine/la-bruja/; https://www.europeancellars.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Comando-G-Bruja-1.pdf)
- Vinification: Hand-harvested; plot-separated fermentations in open oak vats with indigenous yeast and long maceration (30 days average on current importer page; 40-60 days on the PDF, likely reflecting changing releases) (source: https://www.europeancellars.com/wine/la-bruja/; https://www.europeancellars.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Comando-G-Bruja-1.pdf)
- Elevage: 8-9 months in large neutral oak vats/foudre, 15-60 hL (source: https://www.europeancellars.com/wine/la-bruja/; https://www.europeancellars.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Comando-G-Bruja-1.pdf)

## Vintage character (2022 Sierra de Gredos)
The 2022 vintage is shown by importer reviews as a strong release, but the more important exam point is site expression: altitude and decomposed granite keep Garnacha fragrant and comparatively light on its feet. This is Spain through a mountain, Atlantic-leaning lens rather than a warm, heavy Mediterranean one.

## Producer style
Comando G, led by Fernando García and Dani Landi, is central to the modern re-framing of Gredos Garnacha as site-driven, pale, perfumed and Burgundian in texture rather than bulk Mediterranean red (source: https://www.europeancellars.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Comando-G-Bruja-1.pdf). La Bruja is the more accessible village/appellation wine within that philosophy.
