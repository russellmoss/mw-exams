# Pinot Gris Spiegel Grand Cru, Schlumberger, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Deepish lemon-gold, broad and textural, with ripe stone fruit, honeyed spice and a faint smoky bitterness on the finish. Blind, this should point toward rich Alsace Pinot Gris with some residual sugar and Grand Cru breadth rather than a lean, crisp aromatic white (source: https://www.domaines-schlumberger.com/wine/pinot-gris-grand-cru-spiegel-2019/).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Pinot Gris (source: https://www.domaines-schlumberger.com/wine/pinot-gris-grand-cru-spiegel-2019/)
- Vineyard: Grand Cru Spiegel between Guebwiller and Bergholtz, 260-315m, east-facing (source: https://www.domaines-schlumberger.com/the-estate/the-vineyard-and-its-terroirs/the-grand-cru-spiegel/)
- Soils: Sandy-clay (source: https://www.domaines-schlumberger.com/the-estate/the-vineyard-and-its-terroirs/the-grand-cru-spiegel/)
- Vinification: Hand-picked fruit, whole-bunch pressing, static settling, fermentation in temperature-controlled tuns (source: https://www.domaines-schlumberger.com/wine/pinot-gris-grand-cru-spiegel-2019/)
- Elevage: 8 months on lees (source: https://www.domaines-schlumberger.com/wine/pinot-gris-grand-cru-spiegel-2019/)

## Producer style
Domaines Schlumberger is a long-established, quality-focused Alsace estate with a strong Grand Cru identity and a willingness to let Pinot Gris carry richness and some sweetness when the fruit supports it. Spiegel is presented as a distinct terroir with recurring method and élevage rather than a highly manipulated cellar wine.
