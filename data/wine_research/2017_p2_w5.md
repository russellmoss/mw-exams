# Montepulciano d'Abruzzo, La Piuma, 2015. Abruzzo, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple, youthful vivid colour. Nose of fresh dark plum, blackberry, morello cherry, violet, and a hint of earthy farmyard character; low aromatic complexity but good freshness. Medium-full body, juicy acidity (characteristic of the variety), grippy but not harsh tannins, medium finish. The variety's naturally deep colour and jammy fruit profile are key blind-tasting signals. Relatively soft and approachable at 2 years old.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Montepulciano (the Abruzzese variety; unrelated to the Tuscan town)

## Vintage character (2015 Abruzzo)
2015 was a warm vintage across much of Italy, including Abruzzo. The year produced ripe, generous Montepulciano with deep colour, full fruit character and moderate tannin. Generally considered a good-to-excellent vintage for the region. At entry commercial level, the warm season is reflected in the exuberant fruit and soft acidity.
