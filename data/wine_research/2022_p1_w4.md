# Chardonnay, Pierro Vineyards, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
This should show concentrated Margaret River Chardonnay with ripe stone fruit, grapefruit, cashew, struck match and creamy oak-derived texture. Expect more body and malo-derived breadth than the Grosset Riesling, but still enough acidity and line to avoid heaviness.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: predominantly Chardonnay, about 95% Gin Gin clone
- Vineyard: three Chardonnay blocks on the estate in Willyabrup
- Soils: rocky soils on slopes down to Willyabrup Brook
- Vinification: 100% malolactic fermentation

## Vintage character (2019 Margaret River)
2019 in Margaret River was warm and dry enough to deliver ripe flavors, but top sites still retained freshness and composure. In Chardonnay this often translates into generous stone-fruit concentration, nutty barrel complexity and a firm acid spine.

## Producer style
Pierro is one of Margaret River's foundational fine-wine estates, with Chardonnay its signature wine. Michael Peterkin's comments on the estate page emphasize Gin Gin-clone material, texture from full malolactic fermentation and a house style built on richness, flavor intensity and refined oak rather than simple fruitiness.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 4 belongs in the Australian trio of 2022 Paper 1 Question 2. It acts as the Chardonnay contrast to Riesling and aged Marsanne, testing whether candidates can recognize modern premium Margaret River barrel-fermented Chardonnay and describe winemaking as well as quality.
