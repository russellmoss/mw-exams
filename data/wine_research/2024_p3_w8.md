# Vidal Icewine, Inniskillin, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Lustrous gold with explosive peach, mango, apricot, candied citrus, and orange-peel aromas. The palate should be viscous and very sweet, but lifted by sharp, vivid acidity, preventing heaviness. In blind tasting, the combination of low alcohol, tropical fruit, and icy concentration points strongly toward Canadian Vidal Icewine rather than botrytised European sweet wine.

## Vintage character (2021 Niagara Peninsula)
The core stylistic point matters more than a subtle vintage nuance here: 2021 still presents as classic Niagara Icewine, where naturally frozen Vidal delivers intense sweetness with high acidity and exotic fruit. Inniskillin's own trade material and retail notes stress vivid tropical fruit and freshness rather than oxidative or caramelised development.

## Producer style
Inniskillin is the benchmark name in modern Canadian Icewine, founded in 1975 and internationally propelled by its pioneering Vidal Icewine success at Vinexpo in 1991. It remains a reference point for premium Niagara sweet wine and a standard-bearer for the category in export markets.
