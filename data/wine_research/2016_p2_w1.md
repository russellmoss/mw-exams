# Morgon, Domaine Lapierre, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Transparent ruby rather than dense purple, with lifted cherry, raspberry, peony, and a lightly feral, stemmy edge typical of traditionally made cru Beaujolais. The palate should be medium-bodied, bright, and sapid rather than oaky, with crunchy red fruit, fine but persistent tannin, and a stony finish. In blind tasting this sits firmly in the serious Gamay camp rather than simple Beaujolais Nouveau.

## Vintage character (2014 Beaujolais)
2014 was generally regarded as a cleaner, more classical Beaujolais vintage than the difficult 2013s, giving wines with fresh fruit, moderate body, and good drinkability rather than massive concentration. For Morgon that usually means floral lift, red-cherry fruit, and enough structure to age over the medium term. Source needed for a producer-specific 2014 vintage note.

## Producer style
Lapierre is one of Morgon's benchmark minimal-intervention estates and a reference point for the Beaujolais "Gang of Four" generation. The domaine is closely associated with organic and low-intervention farming, native-leaning fermentations, and vivid, transparent expressions of Gamay rather than glossy, oaked reds. Even the entry Morgon is treated as serious cru Beaujolais.
