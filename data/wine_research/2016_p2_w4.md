# Blauschiefer Spätburgunder, Meyer-Näkel, 2014

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium ruby, with cranberry, sour cherry, rosehip, and smoky mineral notes rather than overt sweet fruit. The palate should be dry, brisk, and finely structured, with crunchy acidity, light-to-medium body, and a slight earthy reduction that marks cool-climate German Pinot. Blind, this is a clear Spätburgunder candidate: delicate in colour but serious in texture and mineral tension.

## Vintage character (2014 Ahr)
2014 was not a blockbuster German red vintage, so the best producers relied on site selection and careful cellar work to preserve freshness and shape. In the Ahr that often means lighter-coloured, higher-acid Spätburgunder with tension and savoury detail rather than plush fruit weight. Source needed for a citable Ahr-specific 2014 report.

## Producer style
Werner Näkel is widely regarded as a pioneer of modern dry red winemaking in the Ahr, especially for Spätburgunder raised with Burgundian ambition rather than simple, soft German red styling. Meyer-Näkel's wines aim for precision, minerality, and serious ageing potential, often using French oak while keeping alcohol and fruit profile restrained. Blauschiefer sits below the top cuvées but still signals the estate's polished, cool-climate red philosophy.
