# Côte de Nuits Villages, Vieilles Vignes, Domaine David Clark, 2012. Burgundy, France

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-light ruby with some garnet-orange evolution at 5 years. Nose of red cherry, strawberry, dried flowers (rose), light earthiness, and a faintly gamey sous-bois character; subtle and restrained (old-vine CdNV Pinot character). Light-to-medium body, fine silky tannins, fresh Burgundy acidity, relatively short finish. The "Vieilles Vignes" designation signals old vine concentration, which adds a degree of depth vs. standard village Burgundy.

## Vintage character (2012 Côte de Nuits / Burgundy)
2012 was a challenging but rewarding vintage in Burgundy — severe frost in April decimated yields (50%+ losses in some appellations), and hail hit some areas. The surviving grapes were concentrated due to low yields. The resulting wines are often highly regarded: structured, complex, with good depth despite the difficult season. At village level (CdNV), the 2012 character would deliver more concentration than usual due to low yields.
