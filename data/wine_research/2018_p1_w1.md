# Chardonnay, Yellow Tail, 2017. South East Australia.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw with green tints. Nose: simple peach, melon, and light tropical fruit with minimal oak influence. Palate: soft, round, low-acid entry, uncomplicated stone fruit, clean finish at 13%. Style is distinctly mass-market: fruit-forward, low complexity, high approachability. No detectable phenolic bitterness or significant minerality.

## Vintage character (2017 South East Australia)
2017 was warm and dry across most of South East Australia, producing ripe, fruit-forward wines with moderate to high alcohol. Volume brands in SE Australia had consistent yields owing to irrigation. No significant quality differentiation at this tier between years.

## Producer style
Casella Family Brands, founded 1969 by Sicilian immigrants in Yenda NSW, launched [yellow tail] in the US market in 2001. By 2022 it was the largest family-owned winery in Australia and the country's largest wine exporter by volume, with [yellow tail] representing ~17% of all Australian exported wine. The brand is engineered for mass-market accessibility: soft acids, residual sweetness perception, early-drinking fruit profile. No vintage variation signal intended. (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casella_Family_Brands)
