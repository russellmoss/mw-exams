# Riesling Icewine, Inniskillin, 2012

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to deep gold; intensely aromatic — mango, apricot, lychee, tropical notes, honey, and a distinctive Riesling petrol undercurrent. Palate: viscously sweet, extremely concentrated, residual sugar ~180–320 g/L, but electric acidity prevents cloy. The high acid/sugar tension is uniquely Canadian icewine — less botrytised character than German or French equivalents, more pure-fruit intensity. 9.5% ABV is typical: the frozen grapes yield very little juice, which ferments slowly and partially. Finish extraordinarily long.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vinification: grapes naturally frozen on vine at ≤−8°C; harvested and pressed while frozen; the water remains frozen, yielding concentrated juice (typically ≤35° Brix minimum by VQA regulations); slow cold fermentation
- ABV: 9.5% / RS: ~180–280 g/L (typical range; exact 2012 Inniskillin — Source needed) / TA: typically 10+ g/L
- VQA Ontario designation; Canadian Icewine requires natural freezing and −8°C minimum harvest temperature (confirmed: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icewine)
- Ontario accounts for ~90% of Canadian icewine production; Inniskillin Niagara is the flagship facility

## Vintage character (2012 Ontario)
2012 was a good icewine year in Ontario — cold snaps arrived in December–January allowing harvest of naturally frozen grapes. Riesling icewines from 2012 showed classic Ontario profile: intense citrus-tropical fruit, very high acidity, clean and pure. Inniskillin pioneered Canadian icewine with their 1989 Vidal winning Grand Prix d'Honneur at Vinexpo 1991 — establishing the international benchmark. By 2015 the 2012 icewine was 2–3 years old, still primary and showing peak fruit intensity. (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inniskillin for 1991 Vinexpo win)

## Producer style
Inniskillin founded 1974 by Donald Ziraldo and Karl Kaiser in Niagara-on-the-Lake; first winery licence issued in Ontario since 1929. Pioneered Canadian icewine production commercially by 1984. Now owned by Constellation Brands (acquired 2006 via Vincor International). Positioned as the prestige benchmark for Canadian icewine internationally. The Riesling Icewine sits at the premium end of the range (Vidal Icewine is the flagship/entry; Riesling is higher tier for those who appreciate its more complex, acidic profile). (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inniskillin)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 3 includes sweet wines. A Canadian Riesling Icewine at 9.5% ABV is a key identification challenge: very sweet, high acid, cold-climate, non-botrytis. The MW examiner tests whether candidates can distinguish icewine from Trockenbeerenauslese (Germany) and Sélection de Grains Nobles (Alsace). Key differentiators: harvested frozen, not botrytised; very pure fruit; Ontario provenance.
