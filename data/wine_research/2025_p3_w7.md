# Cote de Provence Cru Classe, Chateau Galoupet, 2023. Provence, France

## Quick tasting profile
Pale salmon-pink with a brilliant sheen. Rich, very expressive nose with summer fruit, yellow peach, floral notes, exotic touches (mango, fresh pineapple), blood orange, lime zest, and strawberry. Subtle roasted aromas and a hint of pink cookie add complexity. On the palate, fruit expression deepens with fine balance between exotic and Mediterranean aromas, supported by a silky structure with juicy acidity, liquorice, white nougat, and a complex, saline finish. Full-bodied for a rose. 94 Falstaff points.

## Producer style
Chateau Galoupet was listed as one of 18 Cru Classe estates of Provence in the original 1955 classification. Acquired by Moet Hennessy (LVMH) in 2019 — the first Provence rose estate in the LVMH portfolio. Under LVMH ownership, the estate has undergone significant investment in sustainability: certified organic in 2023, and subsequently awarded Regenerative Organic Certified (ROC) status. The estate maintains 100 beehives in partnership with the OFA beekeeping research organization. Agroforestry and cover crops encourage biodiversity. This is a gastronomic, premium rose positioned well above commodity Provence pink.
