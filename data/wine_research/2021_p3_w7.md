# Maury, Domaine Pouderoux, Grande Reserve, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep garnet-ruby with potential orange-tawny rim depending on aging period; concentrated nose of dried dark fruits — black cherry, fig, prune — with notes of dark chocolate, coffee, spice and the characteristic Grenache sweetness of Maury; some oxidative notes (chocolate/mocha) from aging. Full-bodied (15.5% ABV), sweet, with firm but integrated tannins; medium-high acidity (from Grenache); long finish. The dark-fruit concentration and French VDN context distinguish this from Port (higher acid, more freshness at same ABV) and Maury Sec.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Minimum 75% Grenache Noir (since 2000 regulations); possible addition of Grenache Gris, Grenache Blanc, Macabeu or Carignan
- Vineyard: Agly valley, Pyrénées-Orientales; schist, slate and granite soils in the Maury commune
- Vinification: Partial fermentation arrested by mutage — addition of neutral spirit mid-fermentation (VDN = vin doux naturel method, developed by Arnaldus de Villa Nova in late 13th C)
- Elevage: Aged in large old oak or concrete; "Grande Reserve" NV suggests extended aging/blending — potentially oxidative aging component
- ABV / RS / TA: 15.5% / sweet / medium-high acidity
- AOC designation 1936 (Source: Wikipedia)

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage; Grande Reserve implies blended from multiple vintages with extended aging, building complexity and oxidative character over time. The Roussillon climate is warm-Mediterranean, producing reliably ripe Grenache for VDN production.

## Producer style
Domaine Pouderoux is a small family estate based in Maury. Source needed for specific producer details. The Maury appellation is dominated by small domaines producing traditional VDN from Grenache Noir on schist soils — a niche category with strong terroir identity but limited international distribution. The "Grande Reserve" designation indicates premium-tier within their range.

## Why it's in this exam
Maury VDN is the French fortified dark-wine benchmark — tested alongside Graham's 2000 VP (w6), Rutherglen Muscat (w8), Olorosos (w9, w10) and Madeiras (w11, w12). Tests candidate ability to identify French Roussillon VDN versus Port, Maury's dark-fruit Grenache character, and VDN production method (mutage). An important but under-studied category that typically forms a curveball in Paper 3.
