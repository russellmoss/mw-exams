# Tokaji Aszú 5 Puttonyos, Sauska, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold to amber with bronze highlights; intensely complex botrytis nose — honey, apricot, quince, orange blossom, dried mango, beeswax and a distinctive Hungarian varietal note (Furmint/Hárslevelű contribution of phenolic grip and high acid); full-bodied (11.5% ABV — low alcohol indicating high RS), concentrated and luscious sweetness (minimum 120 g/L RS for 5 puttonyos as per current regulations) countered by Tokaj's defining high acidity; extraordinary length and freshness despite the intensity. One of the world's great dessert wine archetypes.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Predominantly Furmint (60% of Tokaj vineyards), likely with Hárslevelű; botrytis-affected grapes (aszú berries) mashed into a paste (aszú dough) and added to base wine
- Vineyard: Tokaj-Hegyalja UNESCO World Heritage Site; volcanic/loess/clay soils
- Vinification: Aszú berries hand-selected; traditional dough maceration method or modern gentle pressing technique; the paste is macerated in wine or fermenting must for 24-36 hours
- Elevage: Minimum 2 years in barrel (including minimum 1 year in Gönc oak barrels, 136L) + minimum 1 year in bottle before release
- 5 puttonyos = minimum 120 g/L RS (current PDO regulations since 2013; former 3-6 puttonyos scale now simplified to Aszú minimum 120 g/L)
- ABV / RS / TA: 11.5% / very sweet (120+ g/L RS) / very high acidity

## Vintage character (2017 Tokaj)
2017 was a warm, excellent vintage in Tokaj with outstanding botrytis conditions. Warm, dry September and October with morning mists along the Bodrog and Tisza rivers created ideal noble rot development. The 2017 Tokaji Aszús are regarded as rich, concentrated and well-structured with the warmth of the vintage showing in ripe, approachable fruit character while retaining Tokaj's characteristic acidity. Sauska is a modern, investment-driven Tokaj estate established by a Hungarian entrepreneur, positioned at the quality premium tier of Tokaj production.
