# Riesling, Framingham, 2023. Marlborough, New Zealand

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold in the glass, fragrant and complex. The nose shows mandarin pith, wet stone, beeswax, honeysuckle and jasmine with a touch of quinine. The palate is generous and mouth-filling with ripe yellow/orange citrus flavours, stone fruit and a hint of beeswax richness. Vibrant, intense and focused, held together by juicy acidity and elegant texture. Subtle creaminess adds viscosity. Off-dry in style.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Framingham estate, all four sections; vines planted 1981 — among the oldest Riesling vines in New Zealand
- Soils: Gravelly silt loams
- Vinification: Handpicked, whole-bunch pressed. Predominantly fermented in stainless steel with a small portion in acacia barrels. Aged on full ferment lees without SO2 for ~5 months prior to blending and bottling.
- ABV: 12% / RS: Source needed / TA: Source needed
- Certified organic (since 2014)

## Vintage character (2023 Marlborough)
The 2022/23 growing season had some unusually humid periods to navigate in late February, but weather conditions settled through harvest, allowing picking at targeted ripeness and flavour levels. A generally successful vintage for aromatic whites in Marlborough.

## Producer style
Framingham's Riesling vines, planted in 1981, are among the oldest in Marlborough. Founded by Rex Brooke-Taylor, the winery was named after his ancestral home near Norwich, England. First wine released under the Framingham name was a 1994 Riesling. Jancis Robinson MW has described Framingham as "one of the finest producers of Riesling, both in and outside Germany." The 19.5-hectare estate vineyard has been certified organic since 2014. The Classic Riesling is their flagship — generous, aromatic, off-dry.

## Why it's in this exam
Part of the Riesling trio (Q1, wines 1-3). This is the still, New World expression — off-dry Marlborough Riesling with aromatic intensity and textural richness. Contrasts with the German sparkling (w1) and German dessert (w3). The candidate must recognise Riesling character in a warmer-climate, still wine context and identify Marlborough's signature aromatic intensity and acidity.
