# Pol Roger Brut Vintage, 2012

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with fine, persistent bubbles; more complex and evolved than a standard NV — brioche, toasted almonds, citrus zest, white cherry, chalk minerality and developing secondary/tertiary notes after ~9 years on lees (or post-disgorgement). Full-bodied for Champagne (12.5% ABV), fine acidity, and a long, precise, chalky finish. The vintage Champagne structure is immediately apparent: more depth, more autolytic complexity, more tension than NV.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Approximately 40% Chardonnay, 60% Pinot Noir (standard Pol Roger Vintage blend; can vary)
- Vinification: Traditional method; extended lees aging on lees
- Elevage: Pol Roger ages vintage wines a minimum of 6 years on lees (Source: house practice, common knowledge); disgorged for release, with some late-disgorged releases available
- Dosage: Brut (approx 8-10 g/L RS) — Source needed for exact dosage
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / brut / high acidity

## Vintage character (2012 Champagne)
2012 was a challenging but ultimately excellent vintage in Champagne — a difficult growing season with spring frosts and summer storms reduced yields significantly. Those who managed their vineyards carefully produced wines of great concentration and freshness. The best 2012s are linear, precise, mineral and long-lived, showing the structure of a great year rather than an opulent one. Pol Roger 2012 is regarded as one of the house's finest recent vintages.

## Producer style
Pol Roger, founded in 1849 in Épernay, is a family-owned (descendants of the founder) prestige Champagne house producing approximately 110,000 cases annually. Their standard vintage Brut blends ~40% Chardonnay and 60% Pinot Noir. The house is historically associated with Winston Churchill (his favourite Champagne since 1908; they placed black borders on UK-bound labels after his 1965 death). The Cuvée Sir Winston Churchill is their prestige cuvée. The house style emphasizes elegance, precision and aging potential rather than power or richness. (Source: Wikipedia)

## Why it's in this exam
Pol Roger 2012 vintage is the benchmark vintage Champagne in this Paper 3 sparkling flight alongside NV Moët (w1) and California NV Brut Quartet (w3).
