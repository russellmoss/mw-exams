# Nuits-Saint-Georges Vieilles Vignes, Domaine Robert Chevillon, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Translucent ruby. Ripe black cherry and dark berry fruit with spice and earth, more serious and tannic than a generic Bourgogne but not heavily oaked. The palate is concentrated and savory, with sweet tannins, notable structure and a long village-level Côte de Nuits finish.

## Vintage character (2019 Burgundy)
2019 produced ripe, concentrated reds with good tannin maturity and freshness after a warm, dry season. This wine fits that profile: generous fruit, real structure and less fragility than cooler years, while still retaining village Burgundy shape.

## Producer style
Robert Chevillon is one of Nuits-Saint-Georges' benchmark domaines. The domaine is noted for old vines and unusually silky texture for an appellation more often associated with firmness and tannic authority, making it especially instructive in an exam setting.

## Why it's in this exam
In 2023 Paper 2 Question 3, wines 9-12 asked candidates to identify two grape varieties across two countries and discuss style and commercial position. This bottle was the classical Pinot Noir anchor: Old World, site-specific, cellarworthy and premium rather than mass-market.
