# Riesling Burgstall Federspiel, F.X. Pichler, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw with green hints; pristine and intensely aromatic nose of green apple, white peach, lime zest, slate minerality and white pepper; marked reduction possible at a young stage (classic FX Pichler signature). Medium-light body (12.5% ABV — top of Federspiel range), very high acidity, bone dry, with a precise, focused, mineral palate and long saline finish. The Federspiel tier indicates a lighter, more elegant style within FX Pichler's range — contrasted directly with the Smaragd Kellerberg (w9) on the same paper.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Burgstall vineyard, Wachau; terraced gneiss and granite slopes above the Danube
- Vinification: Temperature-controlled stainless steel fermentation; no new oak; preserving aromatic precision
- Elevage: Stainless steel; bottled relatively early to preserve freshness
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / bone dry / very high acidity (Source needed for TA)
- Federspiel = Vinea Wachau classification for wines 11.5–12.5% ABV (Source: Wikipedia)

## Vintage character (2018 Wachau)
2018 was a warm, early vintage in Austria — harvest began in August for some varieties, among the earliest on record. The Wachau Rieslings of 2018 are riper and more generous than typical cool years, with higher natural sugar levels. Even the Federspiel tier in 2018 shows more fruit weight and body than comparable wines from 2016 or 2014. Good acidity is maintained by the dramatic Danube valley temperature swings. ## Producer style
F.X. Pichler (Franz Xaver Pichler) is widely considered Austria's most prestigious white wine producer, based in Oberloiben, Wachau. The estate produces benchmark Riesling and Grüner Veltliner across the Federspiel, Smaragd tiers with the famous "M" Smaragd at the apex. Their wines are known for extreme precision, reductive freshness and long aging potential. Both w8 (Federspiel) and w9 (Smaragd Kellerberg) from the same estate allow direct tier comparison.
