# Sourgal Moscato d'Asti, Elio Perrone, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Very pale lemon with a delicate, frothy mousse. Intensely aromatic nose of orange blossom, peach, grape must and fresh sage. The palate is sweet but airy, with low alcohol, gentle spritz and bright acidity keeping the finish light rather than cloying.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Moscato Bianco
- Vineyard: Estate vineyards around Castiglione Tinella
- Soils: Calcareous marl and sandy components typical of the zone; Source needed on exact parcel mix
- Vinification: Temperature-controlled tank fermentation stopped early to retain natural sweetness and low alcohol
- Elevage: Bottled young to preserve primary aromatics and CO2
- ABV / RS / TA: 5.5% in exam reference; producer technical sheet shows 5% real alcohol and 12.7% potential alcohol / Source needed / Source needed

## Vintage character (2017 Piedmont)
Justerini's 2017 Piedmont report describes a hot, dry growing season that reduced yields and accelerated ripening. For aromatic Moscato, that kind of year tends to amplify perfume and fruit generosity, provided enough freshness is retained through early harvest and careful cellar work.

## Producer style
Elio Perrone is a specialist Moscato producer whose Sourgal bottling is treated seriously rather than as a novelty sweet wine. The style is precise, fragrant and light-footed, emphasizing purity of aromatics, fine sparkle and balance rather than simple sugary sweetness.

## Why it's in this exam
This is the second Italian wine in 2019 Paper 1 Question 2 and provides a clear contrast in grape, sweetness level and production method. It tests whether the candidate can identify Moscato d'Asti from its low alcohol, floral aromatics, sweetness and gentle mousse.
