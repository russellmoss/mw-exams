# Muscat D'Alsace, Rolly Gassmann, 2007

## Quick tasting profile
Deeply aromatic, old-school Alsace Muscat with grapey, floral and citrus-peel lift rather than overt sweetness. On the palate it should read dry to just off-dry in feel, broad rather than razor-sharp, with texture from ripe fruit and a gently spicy finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Muscat d'Alsace, almost certainly Muscat Blanc à Petits Grains and/or Muscat Ottonel
- Vineyard: Estate fruit from around Rorschwihr
- Soils: Mixed Alsace terroirs; the domaine bottles many named lieux-dits from calcareous marl, Muschelkalk and red marl sites around Rorschwihr
- Vinification: Traditional still white vinification; no clear evidence of new oak

## Vintage character (2007 Alsace)
2007 in Alsace was generally generous and ripe, giving aromatic whites with early charm and accessible fruit. For Muscat this points to expressive perfume, softer acidity than the most severe vintages, and a style that is more about varietal exuberance than tension.

## Producer style
Rolly Gassmann is a long-established Rorschwihr family domaine with roots going back centuries and roughly 55 hectares under vine. The house style is unapologetically full-flavored and terroir-driven, often with notable dry extract and a tendency toward richer, more textural expressions than the leanest school of Alsace.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 1 sits in a three-wine Alsace set asking the candidate to identify one region expressed through different single varieties. This bottle is the aromatic outlier in the trio and tests whether the candidate can recognize dry Muscat in a mature Alsace context without confusing perfume with sweetness.
