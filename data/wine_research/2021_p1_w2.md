# Corton-Charlemagne, Domaine Antonin Guyon, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium gold; closed and mineral on the nose with hints of green apple, white peach and toasty new oak, plus the appellation's characteristic cinnamon spice and flinty reduction. Full-bodied palate with high alcohol (13.5%), very high acidity for the vintage, and the power and breadth typical of the Corton hill. 2017's warm, dry conditions give more generous fruit than austere years. Long, chalky-mineral finish.

## Vintage character (2017 Burgundy)
2017 opened with damaging spring frosts (April) that reduced yields severely across the Côte d'Or — some estates lost 50-80% of their crop. The summer was warm and dry, producing small, concentrated berries. The resulting whites are rich and forward with good acidity, already approachable but with structure to age. For Corton-Charlemagne, 2017 produced generous, textured whites that show the appellation's power early.

## Producer style
Domaine Antonin Guyon is a Côte de Beaune négociant-domaine based in Savigny-lès-Beaune, with over 45 ha of vineyards across multiple appellations. They hold Grand Cru parcels in Corton-Charlemagne and other Corton sites. The house style aims for fruit-forward, oak-framed Burgundy in the modern negociant register — clean and commercially styled rather than reductively artisan.
