# Tokaji Édes Szamorodni, István Szepsy, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
Golden, sweet and botrytis-scented, but not as massively concentrated as full Aszú. Blind, expect apricot, honey, citrus peel and spice, with marked acidity keeping the wine tense and buoyant despite substantial residual sugar; the style should feel classic Tokaj rather than late-harvest softness (source: https://wineloverswebshop.com/szepsy-edes-szamorodni-2017-05l-15945; https://winesofhungary.hu/grape-varieties/white-grape-varieties-white-wine-styles/tokaji-szamorodni).

## Vintage character (2017 Tokaj)
Commercial references to the 2017 release describe it as a warm Tokaj year with early botrytis development. For exam purposes, the useful implication is ripe, generous fruit supporting a sweet Szamorodni style without sacrificing the region’s high-acid backbone.

## Producer style
István Szepsy is one of Tokaj’s reference-point names, associated with precision, low yields and top-end sweet wines. Even without a full official tech sheet for this bottling, the producer name itself signals a premium benchmark rather than an entry-level regional sweet wine.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 9 sits squarely in the residual-sugar flight of 2023 Paper 3, Question 4. It tests whether the candidate can distinguish Szamorodni from Aszú and explain a classic Tokaj sweet-wine category that is botrytis-derived but structurally different from the most famous puttonyos wines.
