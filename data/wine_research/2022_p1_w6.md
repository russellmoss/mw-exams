# Pinot Grigio delle Venezie, Gianni Tessari, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
This should read as clean, light-to-medium-bodied northeastern Italian Pinot Grigio: pear, citrus peel, white flowers and a faint almond note with crisp acidity and no oak. The style is meant to be immediate and commercial, though a good producer can add more texture and precision than supermarket examples.

2020 in northeastern Italy was generally even and competent for fresh white production, with enough balance to preserve varietal clarity. For a wine like this, vintage expression matters less than maintaining freshness, straightforward fruit and clean execution.

## Producer style
Gianni Tessari is a quality-minded Veneto producer better known in fine-wine circles for Soave and volcanic terroir bottlings than for simple Pinot Grigio. That matters in the glass: the wine should still be commercially pitched, but likely a touch more exact and better balanced than anonymous category examples.
