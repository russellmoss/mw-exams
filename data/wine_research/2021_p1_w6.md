# Clos du Papillon, Domaine des Baumard, 2007

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold with amber highlights after 14 years' bottle age (wine assessed 2021); complex and layered on the nose — beeswax, quince, dried apricot, roasted hazelnuts, lanolin and flint — Chenin Blanc in its mature, grand terroir expression. Full-bodied (14.5% ABV) with extraordinary acidity that remains vibrant despite age; very long, saline, mineral finish. The power and complexity of aged Savennières makes this one of France's most distinctive dry whites. Markedly different from the cooperative Anjou Chenin (w5) in the same paper.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc
- Vineyard: Clos du Papillon — a monopole parcel within Savennières; schist and volcanic soils on the northwest-facing slopes of the Loire; old vines

## Vintage character (2007 Loire)
2007 was a challenging vintage in the Loire with a cool, wet summer, disease pressure and late harvest. Savennières in 2007 produced wines of powerful acidity and moderate fruit weight — initially austere and tannic in structure. With 14 years of bottle age at time of exam, the 2007 Clos du Papillon would have evolved beautifully toward the honeyed, waxy, mineral profile characteristic of mature Savennières. ## Producer style
Domaine des Baumard, established 1634, is Savennières' most internationally recognized estate, currently run by Florent Baumard. The 40 ha domaine spans both banks of the Loire. Florent balances "technical rigor from his father with exploration of varietal expression." The house philosophy prioritizes fruit purity via careful pressing and clean winemaking, with sustainable viticulture since the late 1950s. Clos du Papillon is their flagship Savennières cuvée — a monopole expressing the pinnacle of Anjou Chenin on schist slopes. (Source: baumard.fr)

## Why it's in this exam
Domaine des Baumard Clos du Papillon is one of the benchmarks for aged dry Chenin Blanc from great terroir, used here in contrast to 2021_p1_w5 (commercial Anjou Chenin). Savennières is a classic MW exam appellation for testing knowledge of Loire quality hierarchy, Chenin Blanc maturation and the powerful/mineral dry white style from schist soils.
