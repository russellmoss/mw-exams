# Chardonnay, Hyde Vineyard, Patz & Hall, 2016

## Quick tasting profile
Medium to deep gold; opulent and expressive nose with new oak framing — creamy vanilla, toasted hazelnut, ripe peach, nectarine, pineapple and some lemon curd. Full-bodied (14.5% ABV), very rich and broad palate with creamy texture from full malolactic fermentation; well-integrated oak and an opulent, long finish. High-quality California Chardonnay in the rich, New World style — the high alcohol, full malo and new oak all place this unmistakably in premium California territory.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Hyde Vineyard — a celebrated Carneros site in southern Napa, farmed by Larry Hyde; known for its cool bay climate and consistently producing high-quality Chardonnay fruit for multiple top producers
- Vinification: Whole-cluster pressing; barrel fermentation in French oak; typically 50-100% new oak; full malolactic fermentation; lees stirring (battonage)
- Elevage: ~10-12 months in French oak barrels; unfined or minimally filtered

## Vintage character (2016 Carneros/Napa)
2016 was a very good vintage in California — moderate to warm growing season with excellent ripening conditions and good natural acidity. Carneros benefited from bay cooling to moderate the heat. The 2016 Carneros Chardonnays show concentration and balance — ripe fruit with enough acidity to provide structure. Considered a high-quality vintage with aging potential.

## Producer style
Patz & Hall is a premium Sonoma/Napa Chardonnay and Pinot Noir producer founded in 1988, known for single-vineyard expressions from top contracted vineyards including Hyde. The house style features rich, full-malo, oak-influenced Chardonnay with site-specific character. They are positioned at the upper tier of California single-vineyard Chardonnay production.
