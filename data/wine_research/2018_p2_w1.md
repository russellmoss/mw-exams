# Brunello di Montalcino Riserva Lupi e Sirene, Podere Le Ripi, 2011

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby moving toward garnet, with sour cherry, dried rose, balsam, leather, and savory spice. The palate is dry, full, and firmly tannic, with Sangiovese's bright acid line keeping the wine lifted despite the warm-year fruit.

## Vintage character (2011 Montalcino)
2011 was a warmer, earlier, softer vintage than 2010, often giving generous fruit and less severe structure. The best producers preserved balance, but the year generally reads as more open-knit and earlier drinking than truly classic vintages.

## Producer style
Podere Le Ripi is associated with organic/biodynamic farming and a more artisanal, site-conscious reading of Montalcino. Even so, Lupi e Sirene sits firmly in the serious Brunello register rather than a modern, fruit-first one.
