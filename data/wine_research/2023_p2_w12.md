# Malbec, Catena Alta, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Deep purple-ruby. Highly aromatic but serious nose of black fruit, violet and mountain freshness over well-integrated French oak. The palate is concentrated and structured, with fine tannin, bright natural acidity and a long, polished, high-altitude finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Malbec
- Vineyard: Lots from Angelica, La Piramide, Nicasia, Angelica Sur and Adrianna vineyards
- Soils: Alluvial sites with combinations of loam, clay, rounded stones, gravel and limestone
- Vinification: Whole berries in small fermentation bins and 225-500L barrels; cold maceration; wild yeasts; 28-30 day fermentation and maceration; malolactic in barrel with active batonnage
- Elevage: 18 months in French oak
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.8% / dry / 6.2 g/L

## Vintage character (2018 Mendoza)
The multi-vineyard, high-altitude Catena Alta style in 2018 emphasizes freshness and precision as much as power. With sites reaching up to 1450m, the wine reads as cooler and more lifted than a simple warm-valley Malbec, while still delivering full varietal depth.

## Producer style
Catena Zapata is one of Argentina's benchmark fine-wine estates, central to the modern prestige of high-altitude Mendoza Malbec. Catena Alta sits in the estate's upper tier and is built from historic rows in top family vineyards, aiming for aromatic complexity, elegance and long ageing.

## Why it's in this exam
In 2023 Paper 2 Question 3, this was the Argentine Malbec reference against Humberto Canale Pinot Noir. It tested both grape recognition and the ability to distinguish the concentration, oak handling and status cues of premium Mendoza Malbec.
