# Reserve Pinot Noir, Argyle, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with red cherry, cranberry, baking spice, and a touch of cola and forest floor. The palate is dry, medium-bodied, and polished, balancing New World fruit generosity with fresher acid structure than the Russian River wine.

## Vintage character (2015 Willamette Valley)
2015 in Oregon was a warm vintage that delivered ripe fruit and relatively open textures. The stronger examples kept enough freshness to avoid heaviness, producing attractive, generous wines with broad appeal.

## Producer style
Argyle is a major Oregon quality brand with strong trade recognition, known for polished, dependable wines rather than highly rustic or minimalist ones. Reserve Pinot Noir sits in the premium but accessible segment of the market.

## Why it's in this exam
This completed the 2018 paper 2 Pinot Noir trio. It offers a middle position between Californian richness and Central Otago tension, making it a useful test of regional nuance within New World Pinot Noir.
