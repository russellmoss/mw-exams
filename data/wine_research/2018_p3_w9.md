# Saint-Romain, Sous le Château, Olivier Leflaive, 2015. Burgundy, France.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium gold. Nose: classic Burgundy Chardonnay — citrus blossom, green apple, lemon curd, subtle vanilla from oak contact, flinty mineral. Saint-Romain's cool side-valley position produces a leaner, higher-acid style than Meursault or Puligny. Palate: medium-bodied, crisp, fresh, with village-level precision but without Premier Cru depth. 2015's warm vintage gives slightly more generous fruit than typical Saint-Romain. Clean, dry finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: "Sous le Château" — a named premier-ish location below the castle ruins in Saint-Romain; the AOC contains no Premiers Crus (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint-Romain_AOC)
- Soils: Limestone and clay; varied orientations across the 96+ hectare vineyard area
- Vinification: Barrel fermentation; Olivier Leflaive style: moderate oak, clean Burgundian technique
- Elevage: 12 months French oak, predominantly older wood; bâtonnage
- ABV / RS / TA: 13% / dry / medium-high
*(Minimum potential alcohol requirement: 11% for village classification)*

## Vintage character (2015 Burgundy)
2015 was a warm, generous Burgundy vintage — excellent natural ripeness, lower yields from the very warm summer, rich and ripe wines with good balance. Saint-Romain whites in 2015 are fuller than usual for the appellation. By 2018 the wine is drinking well — 2-5 years is the typical window for village-level Saint-Romain. (Source needed: 2015 Côte de Beaune vintage report)

## Producer style
Olivier Leflaive Frères is a well-regarded Burgundy négociant house based in Puligny-Montrachet. Run by Olivier Leflaive (from the Domaine Leflaive family), the maison sources fruit from across the Côte d'Or and Côte Chalonnaise, vinifying in their own cellars. They are positioned as a quality-focused négociant producing clean, technically precise Burgundian whites at accessible-to-premium pricing. Saint-Romain is one of their village-level offerings. (Source needed: Olivier Leflaive Frères producer website)

## Why it's in this exam
Saint-Romain is a deliberate examiner test of Burgundy hierarchy knowledge: the candidate must identify a village-level Chardonnay from a village with no Premiers Crus or Grands Crus, distinguish it from the higher-quality tier white Burgundies that may appear elsewhere in the exam, and identify Olivier Leflaive as a négociant. The Côte de Beaune side-valley position — west of the main escarpment — gives a cooler, slightly leaner profile. "Sous le Château" tests familiarity with this less-famous appellation's named sites.
