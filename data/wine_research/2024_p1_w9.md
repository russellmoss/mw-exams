# Gewurztraminer Furstentum Grand Cru, Domaine Weinbach, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Broad, aromatic, and unmistakably Gewurztraminer: rose petal, honeysuckle, quince, candied orange, exotic fruit, and sweet spice rather than citrus-led restraint. The palate is rich and creamy with noticeable sweetness of fruit, but 2021's freshness keeps it from becoming blowsy; this should read as opulent and Grand Cru rather than simple varietal perfume.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Gewurztraminer
- Vineyard: Grand Cru Furstentum, on the communes of Kientzheim and Sigolsheim
- Soils: shallow, stony, filtering limestone-clay and sandstone / marno-calcaro-greseux soils
- Vinification: whole-cluster pressing in pneumatic presses; indigenous-yeast fermentation in old oak vats
- Elevage: 10 months in old foudre (neutral oak)
- ABV / RS / TA: exam corpus lists 13.0%; the 2021 tech sheet lists 15.0% / 24 g/L / 4.6 g/L

## Vintage character (2021 Alsace)
2021 was one of the hardest recent Alsace vintages at Weinbach: very wet May through July, intense mildew pressure, and sharply reduced yields. Furstentum's limestone sites were hit hard, but August and September turned dry enough to ripen the surviving fruit well, preserving aromatic richness and unusually high acidity. For blind tasting, expect classic Gewurz breadth with more lift than in a hotter year.

## Producer style
Domaine Weinbach is one of Alsace's benchmark white-wine estates, founded in 1612 and owned by the Faller family since 1898. Catherine Faller has run the domaine with her sons Eddy and Theo since 2016. Weinbach has farmed all 38 hectares biodynamically since the 2005 vintage, with Ecocert and Demeter certification. The house style is terroir-led and textural, with fermentation commonly in old oak rather than flashy new wood.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 9 sits in 2024 Paper 1 Question 2, where wines 7-9 are different single varieties and candidates must comment on production method, style, quality, commercial position, and identify grape and origin. This Weinbach is the high-quality aromatic benchmark in that trio: a serious Alsace Gewurztraminer Grand Cru that tests whether the candidate can distinguish noble-aromatic opulence from simpler commercial examples.
