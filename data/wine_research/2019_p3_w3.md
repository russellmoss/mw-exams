# Prosecco Superiore, Giustino B, Ruggeri, 2017. Valdobbiadene, Veneto, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw, fine and persistent perlage. Nose is aromatic and expressive: fresh pear, green apple, white peach, acacia blossom, and a light floral-yeasty freshness. The millesimato status adds a vintage-specific aromatic depth described as "extraordinarily broad and complex" by the producer. Palate is light, refreshing and gently off-dry with a creamy mousse, clean acidity and a clean, short-to-medium finish. Extra Dry designation means slightly perceptible sweetness (RS ~17 g/L).

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Minimum 85% Glera; up to 15% other permitted local white varieties (source: Ruggeri tech sheet and Wikipedia Prosecco)
- Vineyard: Valdobbiadene DOCG hillside vineyards; Ruggeri estate sources
- Soils: Steep hillside with clay-limestone and sandy soils typical of Valdobbiadene (Conegliano Valdobbiadene UNESCO heritage landscape)
- Vinification: Off-skin pressing; primary fermentation at 16°C; secondary fermentation (Charmat/Martinotti method) in sealed stainless steel tanks at 14–15°C with approximately 3 months lees contact (source: Ruggeri tech sheet)
- Elevage: ~3 months lees ageing in autoclave tanks; no oak (source: Ruggeri)
- ABV / RS / TA: 11.5% / approximately 17 g/L / Source needed; bottle pressure: 5.5 atmospheres (source: Ruggeri)

## Vintage character (2017 Valdobbiadene)
2017 was a warm, dry year across northeastern Italy — producing wines with good ripeness and aromatic concentration. For Prosecco, this translates to richer, more fruit-forward expression than average, with slightly lower natural acidity. The millesimato character adds vintage freshness and aromatic precision versus a non-vintage blend.

## Producer style
Ruggeri is a respected Valdobbiadene producer established in 1950. Their Giustino B. is named in honour of Giustino Bisol, one of the founders; it is their flagship millesimato (vintage-dated) Extra Dry. Positioned at premium-tier for Prosecco (awarded Gold at Berlin Wine Trophy, Mundus Vini; 90–93 points in Italian wine guides — source: Ruggeri website). Ruggeri focuses on quality DOCG Prosecco with terroir expression, contrasting with commodity DOC producers.

## Why it's in this exam
Ruggeri Giustino B. represents premium DOCG Prosecco alongside the commodity DOC La Marca (wine 4). This pair likely anchors a quality comparison question: identifying the difference between DOCG Valdobbiadene millesimato and standard DOC Prosecco — the key indicators being aroma complexity, perlage quality, and structural finesse. Both are Extra Dry style with perceptible sweetness.
