# Carmes de Rieussec, Chateau Rieussec, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold with youthful brightness. Expect botrytis signatures of apricot, marmalade, saffron, pineapple, and honey, but in a less massive, less layered form than the grand vin. The palate should be unctuous and sweet, yet supported by enough acidity to keep it from tasting flat, making it a clear Sauternes rather than an oxidative or fortified sweet wine.

## Vintage character (2018 Sauternes)
Decanter describes 2018 in Sauternes and Barsac as a strong sweet-wine year after a difficult growing season, with heat and dryness later in the cycle helping concentration and botrytis quality. For blind tasting, that usually means generous fruit, richness, and ample sweetness, but still enough freshness to define it as high-quality noble-rot wine rather than merely sugary dessert wine.

## Producer style
Rieussec is one of the historic leading names of Sauternes and has been owned by Domaines Barons de Rothschild Lafite since 1984. The estate's official profile emphasizes both dry and sweet wines, but its reputation is built on botrytised Sauternes from a major classified growth. Carmes functions as the more accessible, earlier-drinking sweet expression of the property.
