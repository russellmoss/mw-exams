# Château Rieussec R de Rieussec, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Light yellow-green with silver reflections. Aromatic nose — exotic fruits (mango, papaya), citrus (lime, passion fruit), meadow herbs, pear, subtle tobacco, well-integrated oak. Palate is rich yet fresh: smooth texture, lemony acidity, subtle fruitiness, salty mineral finish. Light-footed aperitif style. Good length. A dry white Bordeaux from Sauternes terroir — Sémillon brings weight, Sauvignon Blanc brings freshness.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: ~50% Sauvignon Blanc, ~50% Sémillon (approximate; the 2021 has been described as 60% Sémillon / 40% Sauvignon Blanc in some sources)
- Vineyard: Estate vineyards in Fargues commune, Sauternes. 93 hectares total estate; R de Rieussec from selected plots
- Soils: Gravel, clay, and limestone on the high hill of Fargues
- Vinification: Sauvignon harvested early for freshness/thiol aromas; Sémillon harvested at full ripeness for structure. Sémillon fermented largely in new and one-wine oak barrels; Sauvignon mainly in stainless steel at low temperature, small portion in one-wine barrels. ~20% barrel-fermented overall.
- Élevage: Source needed for specific duration. Likely 6–8 months in barrel.
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / dry / Source needed
- Score: 91 Falstaff points (2024 review)

## Vintage character (2021 Bordeaux Blanc)
2021 was a challenging vintage in Bordeaux — late frost, cool and wet conditions. Dry whites from 2021 tend to show bright acidity and moderate alcohol, with more freshness and less opulence than warmer years like 2018 or 2020. Source needed for specific Sauternes/Fargues dry white vintage notes.

## Producer style
Château Rieussec is a 1er Cru Classé Sauternes (1855 Classification), owned since 1984 by Domaines Barons de Rothschild (Lafite). The estate sits on the highest point of Fargues, adjacent to Yquem. 93 hectares planted predominantly to Sémillon (90%), Sauvignon (7%), Muscadelle (3%) for the sweet wine. R de Rieussec is the estate's dry white, produced from parcels not used for the Grand Vin Sauternes or the second sweet wine (Carmes de Rieussec). Positioned as an accessible, quality dry Bordeaux blanc from prestigious terroir.
