# Cornas Brise Cailloux, Matthieu Barret, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Deep, intense colour. Nose of crushed dark berries, violet, lavender, leather, coffee, and pepper with smoky notes. Palate is powerful yet has a delicate texture thanks to the 2021 vintage freshness; well-integrated tannins, high acidity, and a long spicy finish with licorice and blackcurrant. A natural-leaning, granite-inflected Cornas with notable purity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Syrah
- Vineyard: Blend of young and old vines from Gore terroir and clay-silt hillside plots in Cornas; biodynamic cultivation
- Soils: Granite (the name "Brise Cailloux" = "rock breaker")
- Vinification: Minimal intervention, indigenous yeasts (implied by biodynamic approach), whole-cluster component likely. Very low yields ~25 hl/ha
- Elevage: 18 months in oval concrete fermenters and 500-litre barrels (no new oak). No conventional oak barrique program — cellar is notably free of standard barriques

- Very low sulphur additions

## Vintage character (2021 Northern Rhone)
Severe frost April 7-9 devastated Cornas, Saint-Peray, and Crozes-Hermitage — first major April frost in 40 years. Losses up to 90% on flat terrain (Domaine Clape). Late summer warmth arrived August 10-15. Those who picked before early October rains produced structured, fresh, classic wines. 2021 is a return to classicism after the heat of 2018-2020: high acidity, firm tannins, lower alcohol. (source: Berry Bros. & Rudd, Decanter)

## Producer style
Matthieu Barret is a seventh-generation vigneron who joined his grandfather in Cornas in 1997 after studying viticulture in Beaune. Biodynamic practitioner following Steiner/Thun principles. ~20 ha across steep Cornas terraces. Distinctive for eschewing oak barrels entirely in favour of concrete vats and clay amphorae. Yields as low as 20 hl/ha. Very low sulphur. Part of the new wave of natural/biodynamic Cornas producers raising the appellation's profile. "Brise Cailloux" is his more accessible cuvee; "Les Terrasses du Serre" is the top wine.

## Why it's in this exam
The classic Northern Rhone Syrah benchmark in a trio with Chilean (Wine 1) and Australian (Wine 3) Syrah/Shiraz. At 12.5% ABV — notably low — it exemplifies cool-vintage, low-intervention Northern Rhone character. The question on "styles and consumer appeal" invites contrast between this artisanal, terroir-driven wine and the more commercially positioned alternatives. The biodynamic, minimal-intervention approach is a key winemaking discussion point.
