# Lagrein, Castel Turmhof, Tiefenbrunner, 2014. Südtirol / Alto Adige, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple, almost inky — Lagrein characteristically produces very deep colour. Nose of dark plum, blackberry, violet, bitter chocolate, leather, and a distinctive herbal/alpine note; mineral freshness typical of Alto Adige. Medium-full body, firm velvety tannins, fresh acidity, medium-long finish with bitter-chocolate and mineral persistence. The very deep colour alongside firm tannins and fresh acidity is distinctively Lagrein.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Lagrein (indigenous Südtirol/Alto Adige variety, related to Teroldego; genetic parent unknown; thrives in the Bolzano basin's warm days/cool nights)
- Vineyard: Tiefenbrunner estate vineyards, centred on the historic Castel Turmhof estate in Kurtatsch (Cortaccia) on the Weinstrasse; 77 ha total, 210–1000m elevation range (source: tiefenbrunner.com)
- Soils: Alluvial gravels and sandy-loam typical of the Adige Valley floor; Lagrein traditionally grown at lower altitudes in Bolzano and around (Source needed for Tiefenbrunner specific Lagrein vineyard soils)

- ABV: 13.0%

## Vintage character (2014 Alto Adige / Südtirol)
2014 was a cooler, difficult vintage in Northern Italy. Alto Adige's altitude and the Adige Valley's diurnal range helped maintain acidity even in warmer years; in a cooler year like 2014, the wines would show pronounced freshness and acidity with less concentrated fruit. Lagrein's naturally high colour and firm tannins persisted regardless of vintage warmth.

## Producer style
Tiefenbrunner (Schlosskellerei Turmhof) is one of Alto Adige's most established estates, based at the historic Castel Turmhof (over 800 years old) in Kurtatsch/Cortaccia on the Südtirol Weinstrasse. The family has operated for five to six generations. Their 77-ha estate produces 28 different wines across 12 grape varieties at 210–1000m elevation — one of the broadest varietal portfolios in the region. They operate four quality tiers from fresh to complex, with a particular strength in white wines (80% of production). The estate is known for quality across a wide range, with Lagrein representing a signature indigenous red variety. Source: tiefenbrunner.com.

## Why it's in this exam
Lagrein is a distinctly regional variety representing the northern Italian Alto Adige/Südtirol identity — a curveball wine testing knowledge beyond mainstream Italian reds. Its very deep colour (deeper than Barolo or Sangiovese), firm tannins, violet/plum/chocolate profile, and alpine mineral freshness are diagnostic. At 13% ABV, it shows northern Italian restraint. The bilingual appellation (Südtirol/Alto Adige) and German-speaking cultural context are also relevant for origin identification.
