# Maury "Grande Réserve", Domaine Pouderoux, NV. Roussillon, France

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-tawny with mahogany-brick rim (indicating significant oxidative ageing in old wood). Nose of dried black fruit (prune, fig, raisin), dark chocolate, coffee, vanilla, toffee, and a distinctive "rancio" character from extended oxidative ageing; complex and rich. Full-bodied, sweet but balanced by rancio dryness on the finish, high alcohol warmth (15.5%), long concentrated finish. The contrast between fresh Maury (which is deeply fruity and young) and aged "Grande Réserve" is significant — this cuvée has been through extended barrel/solera-style ageing.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Grenache Noir (Maury AOC requires minimum 75% Grenache; quality examples are typically mono-cépage; Source needed for Pouderoux exact composition)
- Vineyard: Maury appellation, Roussillon, centred on the village of Maury in the Agly Valley; the region's distinctive black schist soils (schistes noirs) are fundamental to Maury's character
- Soils: Black schist (schistes ardoisiers) — volcanic/metamorphic rock, poor fertility, extreme drainage; gives wines their distinctive dark fruit and mineral character
- Vinification: Mutage — arrested fermentation by addition of grape spirit (neutral alcohol) to approximately 95 g/L potential RS retained; fortified at ~6–8% ABV during fermentation
- Elevage: Extended oxidative ageing in old barrels, demi-muids, or bonbonnes (glass demijohns exposed to oxygen and temperature variation) — the "Grande Réserve" designation implies significant ageing, likely 10+ years average; NV solera-style blending
- ABV: 15.5%
- RS: Approximately 80–120 g/L (high; Source needed for exact Pouderoux spec)

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage; blended from multiple years of ageing stock. The "Grande Réserve" cuvée suggests selection from the oldest, most complex reserve wines in the Domaine's cellar.

## Producer style
Domaine Pouderoux is a small family-owned estate in Maury, Roussillon, specialising in the Vin Doux Naturel wines of the appellation. The estate produces both young, fresh Maury (typically deep ruby, unoaked, concentrated) and aged, oxidative "Grande Réserve" cuvées that develop tawny-rancio character. Source needed for confirmed producer details. Maury is one of France's smallest AOC appellations for VDN and produces exclusively from Grenache.

## Why it's in this exam
Domaine Pouderoux Maury Grande Réserve represents the French VDN (Vin Doux Naturel) oxidative sweet fortified wine category in P3 — paired alongside Valdespino Palo Cortado (w11) to test the candidate's ability to distinguish two oxidative fortified wine styles: Spanish dry Sherry vs. French sweet VDN. Key discriminators: Maury is sweet (vs. dry Palo Cortado), red-grape-based (vs. white Palomino), French/Roussillon origin (vs. Andalucia/Jerez), and at 15.5% ABV rather than 20%.
