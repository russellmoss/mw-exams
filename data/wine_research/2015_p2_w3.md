# Gevrey-Chambertin 1er Cru Les Corbeaux, Domaine Bruno Clavelier, 2009. Burgundy, France

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-deep ruby; 2009 Cote de Nuits produces wines with more colour depth and richness than cooler years. Nose is classic Gevrey: dark cherry, cassis, forest floor, earthy smoke, fine spice, with characteristic savory grip. On the palate, full-bodied for Pinot Noir with the vintage's ripe, broad tannins, medium-high acidity, and a long, structured finish. 2009's warmth gives more opulence than the tight 2010 across the Cote de Nuits.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: Les Corbeaux — located at the northern end of Gevrey-Chambertin's premier cru sites, on the border with Brochon; higher elevation, north-facing aspects give fresher character within the Gevrey profile
- Soils: Brown calcareous limestone with clay, colluvium over hard limestone bedrock
- Vinification: Traditional: whole-cluster or partial whole-cluster; open-top fermenters; long maceration typical for Gevrey; Bruno Clavelier is known for biodynamic or low-intervention approach
- Elevage: French oak barriques, elevated proportion of older wood (Clavelier is not a high new-oak producer); approximately 16-18 months

## Vintage character (2009 Burgundy)
2009 was a landmark warm vintage in Burgundy, producing opulent, rich, deeply coloured reds with broad, generous tannins and ripe dark fruit. The wines were immediately accessible and highly praised by critics (Parker scored many 98-100). The Cote de Nuits produced wines of exceptional concentration. By 2015, the 2009s were entering their first drinking window with primary fruit still dominant but emerging secondary complexity. Gevrey-Chambertin's appellation produces the most structured and tannic wines of the Cote de Nuits, amplified further in the 2009 vintage. ## Producer style
Domaine Bruno Clavelier is a small Vosne-Romanee-based domaine farming approximately 8 hectares across multiple premier cru and village appellations in the Cote de Nuits, including Vosne-Romanee, Gevrey-Chambertin, and Chambolle-Musigny. Clavelier works biodynamically and uses native yeasts. The house style is precision-oriented, terroir-driven, with minimal new oak. The estate is considered artisan-level, well-regarded by Burgundy specialists but not widely distributed internationally. (Source needed for specific domaine profile URL.)

## Why it's in this exam
Used as the Cote de Nuits premier cru anchor in a Burgundy-focused question set, paired against the Cote de Beaune Monthelie (w1).
