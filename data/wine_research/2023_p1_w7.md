# Chardonnay, La Crema, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Medium lemon. This should show a modern Californian Chardonnay profile: citrus blossom, apple, nectarine, lemon meringue and caramel, with a rich texture balanced by fresh acidity. Blind, it reads as ripe but not overblown Russian River Chardonnay, where cool-climate fruit definition supports a broad, creamy mid-palate rather than flabby sweetness (source: La Crema).

## Vintage character (2021 Russian River Valley)
Jordan's 2021 Russian River Chardonnay report calls 2021 a welcome return to normalcy after difficult prior years, with drought but strong spring-to-fall weather, mild temperatures, and fruit that achieved both intensity and bright acidity. That summary aligns well with the style here: concentrated fruit, but still enough freshness to keep the wine in balance (source: Jordan).

## Producer style
La Crema focuses on cool-climate appellations and positions Russian River Valley as one of its flagship Chardonnay regions, stressing maritime influence, slow ripening, and expressive elegance. Even in an accessible regional bottling, the style is polished and commercially confident: ripe fruit, texture, and broad appeal, but still anchored by acidity and regional credibility (source: La Crema region and product pages).
