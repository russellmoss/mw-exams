# Samuel's Collection Bush Vine Grenache, Yalumba, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Bright ruby with lifted red and black berry fruit, plum and spice. The palate is supple and fleshy rather than heavy, with juicy red fruit, velvety tannin and a generous but not over-oaked finish. Blind, it reads as ripe old-vine Australian Grenache with freshness preserved.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Grenache
- Vineyard: Old bush-vine Barossa parcels including the Schiller family vines, Habermann family vines and Yalumba's Tri-Centenary Vineyard
- Soils: Sandy soils, sandy loam, and red-brown earth over red clay
- Vinification: Source needed
- Elevage: 7 months in older French and Hungarian hogsheads
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.0% / Source needed / 5.23 g/L

## Vintage character (2019 Barossa)
Yalumba reports a cool winter with lighter-than-average rainfall followed by mild, dry growing-season weather and a sunny, dry summer. The vintage gave flavour concentration with vibrant Grenache fruit, which matches the wine's ripe but energetic profile.

## Producer style
Yalumba, founded in 1849, remains one of Australia's major family-owned fine-wine producers. Samuel's Collection honours founder Samuel Smith and generally sits in the upper-premium, regionally expressive tier: more detailed and vineyard-aware than commodity Barossa reds, but still accessible.
