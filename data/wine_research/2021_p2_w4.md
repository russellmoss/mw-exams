# Pinot Noir, Flowers Winery, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with lifted red cherry, raspberry, rose petal and forest-floor notes over subtle coastal spice. The palate is dry, medium-bodied and finely textured, with bright acidity and savoury mineral lift rather than plush sweetness. This is a cool-edge Sonoma Coast Pinot Noir, not a broad inland California style.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: Sonoma Coast estate and grower vineyards, including far-coast sourcing
- Soils: Rocky, well-drained coastal soils
- Vinification: 100% native yeast according to the producer sell sheet
- Elevage: 12 months in 80% French oak and 20% stainless steel
- ABV / RS / TA: 14.1% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2018 Sonoma Coast)
Published Sonoma Coast 2018 summaries describe a long, even growing season with balanced ripening under cool maritime influence. That fits the wine's combination of aromatic precision, moderate body and freshness.

## Producer style
Flowers is one of the reference names for far Sonoma Coast Chardonnay and Pinot Noir, built around coastal exposure, elevation and restrained extraction. The house style leans elegant and site-expressive rather than heavily worked or sweetly oaked.

This opens Question 2 in 2021 Paper 2, where each wine is a different single variety from a different continent. It is the cool-climate New World Pinot benchmark, testing both variety recognition and a precise coastal California call.
