# Syrah, Te Mata Estate, 2016

## Quick tasting profile
Mid-ruby with black pepper, violet, blueberry, and a distinctly cool-climate olive note. The palate is dry and medium-bodied, with bright acidity, moderate tannin, and a savory finish that points more to Northern Rhone shape than to ripe Australian Shiraz.

## Vintage character (2016 Hawke's Bay)
2016 was a clean and generally successful New Zealand vintage, giving good ripeness without losing freshness. In Hawke's Bay Syrah, that often shows as peppery aromatic lift, moderate alcohol, and well-defined structure.

## Producer style
Te Mata is one of New Zealand's oldest prestige estates, with a broad fine-wine portfolio and strong export identity. Its Syrah style usually favors fragrance and structure over brute force.
