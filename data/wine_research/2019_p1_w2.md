# Clifford Bay Reserve Sauvignon Blanc, Villa Maria, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Pale lemon with a pungent, lifted Marlborough nose of passion fruit, lime, snow pea and fresh-cut herbs. The palate is dry, bright and emphatically aromatic, with a high-acid, citrus-driven finish and the salty, greener edge typical of Awatere Valley fruit.

## Vintage character (2018 Marlborough)
Wine Spectator reported an excellent 2018 New Zealand harvest, with warm, settled conditions and generous ripeness in many regions. In Marlborough that translated into expressive fruit and full flavour development, which fits this wine's ripe but still sharply defined Sauvignon Blanc profile.

## Producer style
Villa Maria is one of New Zealand's flagship producers, combining large-scale commercial reach with strong regional specialization. The Clifford Bay / Reserve tier aims at a more serious Marlborough expression than entry-level bottlings, emphasizing site definition and concentration while retaining the winery's clean, stainless-steel, fruit-led style.

## Why it's in this exam
This is one of the 2019 Paper 1 Question 1 wines built around Sauvignon Blanc. It serves as the archetypal New World reference point in the quartet, especially useful against Sancerre and lower-alcohol Marlborough variants for examining region, ripeness and winemaking choices.
