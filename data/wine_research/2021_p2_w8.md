# Terrunyo Carmenere Block 27, Concha y Toro, 2018

## Quick tasting profile
Deep ruby-purple with black fruit, roasted peppercorn, herbs, cocoa and smoky oak accents. The palate is full-bodied and dry, with plush dark fruit, ripe tannin and the leafy-spicy edge that marks serious Carmenère. It is richer and more polished than value-tier Chilean examples.

## Vintage character (2018 Cachapoal)
The producer positions Terrunyo as a pure expression of Peumo Carmenère, and 2018 is presented in market notes as a strong vintage for concentration and typicity. That matches the wine's ripe black fruit and polished but still herbal shape.

## Producer style
Terrunyo is part of Concha y Toro's upper tier, designed to show specific high-performing sites rather than the company's broad commercial range. In Carmenère, Peumo is one of Chile's benchmark zones, giving flesh, ripeness and a relatively refined vegetal signature.

This is the second Americas wine in 2021 Paper 2 Question 3. It asks the candidate to distinguish premium single-vineyard Chilean Carmenère from California Petite Sirah through tannin feel, herbaceousness and site expression.
