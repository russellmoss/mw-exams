# Riesling Smaragd, Ried Kellerberg Dürnsteiner, Weingut Knoll, 2007

## Quick tasting profile
Powerful dry Wachau Riesling with ripe stone fruit, citrus oil, stony extract and a broad, forceful palate. Compared with the Hunter Semillon it is bigger, more obviously dry-extractive and more site-saturated, with alcohol and concentration contributing to its authority.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Ried Kellerberg in Dürnstein
- Soils: Gneiss/primary-rock influenced steep Wachau site
- Vinification: Dry still Riesling under the Smaragd category; no meaningful new-oak signature expected
- Élevage: Source needed
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / dry / Source needed

## Vintage character (2007 Wachau)
2007 in the Wachau produced ripe but balanced wines, often with excellent concentration. In Smaragd Riesling that usually translates into generosity of fruit, palpable dry extract and considerable aging potential without losing site definition.

## Producer style
Emmerich Knoll is one of the Wachau’s reference estates, known especially for serious Grüner Veltliner and Riesling from sites such as Kellerberg, Schütt and Loibenberg. The Smaragd tier signals the fullest, most powerful dry style in the Vinea Wachau hierarchy.

## Why it's in this exam
This is the Austrian heavyweight in the mixed-bag Question 3. It tests whether candidates can distinguish mature Semillon-like toastiness from genuinely dry, powerful Riesling and then place that wine in the Wachau rather than Alsace or Germany.
