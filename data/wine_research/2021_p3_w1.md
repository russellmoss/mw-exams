# Brut Imperial, Moët & Chandon, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with persistent, fine bubbles; welcoming and yeasty nose of brioche, green apple, pear, citrus and subtle toast — classic non-vintage Champagne nose with good autolytic development. Medium-bodied, well-balanced palate with fresh acidity and 9-12 g/L dosage keeping it approachable; the fruit is primary and clean. The quintessential benchmark entry-level NV Champagne — anonymous in style, designed for recognition and consistency rather than complexity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Blend of Pinot Noir, Pinot Meunier and Chardonnay (typical Moët composition leans toward Pinot Noir and Meunier dominant)
- Vinification: Traditional method (méthode champenoise); cuvée + taille press fractions; secondary fermentation in bottle
- Elevage: Minimum 15 months on lees (NV requirement is 12 months; Moët typically meets this minimum or slightly exceeds)
- Dosage: Brut (6-12 g/L RS) — Source needed for current production dosage
- ABV / RS / TA: 12% / brut (approx 9-10 g/L RS) / high acidity

## Vintage character (NV Champagne)
Non-vintage Champagne does not have a single vintage character; the blend typically draws on a base year (the most recent harvest) plus up to 20-25% reserve wines from earlier harvests. Moët's NV Brut Imperial is styled for consistency year-on-year — one of the world's most consistent wine blends.

## Producer style
Moët & Chandon, founded in 1743 (Épernay), is the world's largest Champagne house and part of the LVMH luxury group. They own 1,190 ha of vineyards and produce approximately 28 million bottles annually. Brut Imperial, introduced in the 1860s, is their flagship and best-selling expression — the global face of non-vintage Champagne and a major on-trade/airline standard. The house is known for consistency, accessibility and global marketing rather than artisan complexity. (Source: Wikipedia)
