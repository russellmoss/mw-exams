# Vouvray, Marc Brédif, 2013. Loire, France

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium lemon-gold; nose of honeysuckle, beeswax, quince, bruised apple and white flowers — classic cool-climate Chenin Blanc. Palate shows the 2013 vintage's tendency toward off-dry character: a hint of residual sugar (likely demi-sec or tender sec) balanced by the variety's naturally high acidity. Lighter body than W5 (South African Chenin); fresh, mineral, with a slightly wet stone quality from Loire tuffeau soils. Clean, no oak. Crisp finish.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chenin Blanc (also known as Pineau de la Loire in Vouvray)
- Vineyard: Tuffeau (chalky clay-limestone) soils typical of Vouvray AOC, east of Tours along the Loire River
- Soils: Tuffeau — soft chalky limestone with clay; contributes mineral freshness and good natural acidity (source: Wikipedia Vouvray)
- Vinification: Stainless steel fermentation; minimalist approach typical of Vouvray — neutral vessel, no MLF to preserve acidity; sweetness level determined by harvest date and fermentation management (source: Wikipedia Vouvray — "minimalist approach, often using neutral fermentation vessel such as stainless steel")
- Elevage: Stainless steel tank or large older vessel; bottled to preserve fruit freshness
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5% / likely 6–15 g/L (off-dry/demi-sec range) / high acidity (Source needed: confirmed RS for this specific vintage)

## Vintage character (2013 Loire / Vouvray)
2013 was a cool, rainy vintage in the Loire Valley — one of the more challenging recent years. Cool temperatures throughout the season encouraged high natural acidity in Chenin Blanc; botrytis was present but selective hand-harvesting was required. The resulting wines (particularly in Vouvray) tend toward the off-dry or demi-sec register rather than fully dry; the naturally high acidity of 2013 Chenin Blanc is a distinctive marker. Many Vouvray producers found the vintage suited demi-sec production rather than sec. The 12.5% ABV supports a cooler, less ripe vintage character. ## Producer style
Marc Brédif is one of the oldest and most historically significant négociant-éleveur houses in Vouvray, dating to the late 19th century. The house was acquired in 1980 by the de Ladoucette family (owners of Baron de L in Pouilly-Fumé), and is now run as a quality-focused traditional Vouvray operation. Brédif produces the full range of Vouvray styles: sec, demi-sec, moelleux, and pétillant. The house is particularly known for their extraordinary old vintage library, including some of the oldest commercially available Vouvray in existence, which demonstrates the variety's legendary longevity in the Loire. The house style leans classical: stainless steel or large old oak, minimal intervention, emphasising Chenin's natural acidity and age-worthiness. Commercial positioning: broadly available, mid-premium tier. (Source needed: Marc Brédif producer website unavailable during research; general reputation from MW study knowledge.)

## Why it's in this exam
W6 pairs with W5 (Tierhoek Piekenierskloof Chenin Blanc, South Africa) in Q2: same variety, different countries. Key structural discriminators for blind tasting: (1) Loire Vouvray is leaner, higher acid, lower ABV (12.5%) versus South African (14%); (2) fruit profile diverges — Vouvray shows honeysuckle/quince/bruised apple versus South African's tropical pineapple/guava; (3) Vouvray likely off-dry/demi-sec in 2013, giving a subtle sweetness that South African dry Chenin may lack; (4) Loire mineral/tuffeau character is distinct from Piekenierskloof's granite/altitude freshness. Marc Brédif is also a curveball — not as well-known as Huet or Foreau, testing producer-level knowledge.
