# Roussanne, Domaine du Tunnel, 2020

## Quick tasting profile
This should be richer and more textural than the other wines in the four-wine set: white flowers, grapefruit, almond, apricot-blossom and a lightly brioched edge. The palate should be broad and mouthfilling but still saline and fresh enough to point to Saint-Peray rather than an oak-heavy Chardonnay.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Roussanne
- Vineyard: Domaine du Tunnel holdings in Saint-Peray
- Soils: granitic soils
- Vinification: fermented in a combination of stainless steel and older barriques
- Elevage: 8 to 10 months, with Liberty noting nine months on lees
- ABV / RS / TA: exam corpus lists 14.0%; Source needed / Source needed

## Vintage character (2020 Saint-Peray)
2020 in the northern Rhone gave many white wines both ripeness and aromatic generosity, especially where freshness could be preserved. In Roussanne from Saint-Peray that often means ample texture and floral richness, balanced by mineral lift rather than sharp acidity.

## Producer style
Domaine du Tunnel, run by Stephane Robert, is a small northern Rhone family estate that vinifies Saint-Peray seriously rather than as a curiosity. The white style is concentrated but elegant, using older wood and lees ageing to build texture without obscuring site and grape character.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 12 closes 2022 Paper 1 Question 4. It is the richer, less internationally obvious single-variety white in the lineup and tests whether candidates can identify Roussanne and a northern Rhone origin even when the wine shows power, texture and discreet oak handling.
