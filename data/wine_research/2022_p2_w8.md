# Clos Vougeot, Domaine Gros Frere et Soeur, 2017

## Quick tasting profile
This should be a major step up in scale from the Hautes Cotes wine: darker cherry fruit, rose, spice, forest floor and oak sweetness over broader tannins and deeper extract. Even if youthfully generous, it should still signal grand cru ambition through intensity and persistence.

## Vintage character (2017 Burgundy)
2017 in Burgundy gave accessible, charming reds with less sheer mass than 2015 but good definition and earlier appeal. In grand cru Cote de Nuits, that can still mean serious structure, but often with more openness and less austerity than tougher years.

## Producer style
Gros Frere et Soeur is a historic Burgundy domaine with notable grand cru holdings. In an exam context the wine's importance is its hierarchy and sub-region: it is the high-status Cote de Nuits grand cru foil to a simpler Hautes Cotes de Beaune bottling.
