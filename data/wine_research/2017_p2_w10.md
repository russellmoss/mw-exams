# Spätburgunder, Martin Wassmer, 2014. Baden, Germany

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby, clear and translucent (typical of Pinot Noir/Spätburgunder). Nose of red cherry, strawberry, raspberry, rose petal, light earthy-mineral notes, and subtle spice from French oak; elegant and refined rather than primary-fruity. Medium body, silky fine-grained tannins, fresh acidity, medium length. The Baden style is fuller and riper than Burgundy or Pfalz Spätburgunder due to warmer climate; 13.5% ABV signals warmth but within European parameters.

- Grape variety: 100% Spätburgunder (Pinot Noir)

## Vintage character (2014 Baden / Germany)
2014 was a variable vintage in Germany — a warm start with some cool spells. Baden, being Germany's warmest region, generally produced well-structured reds. Spätburgunder 2014 from Baden showed good fruit concentration with fresh acidity. Not the most celebrated recent Baden vintage (2015 is generally rated higher) but produced solid, drinkable wines.

## Producer style
Martin Wassmer is widely considered one of Germany's finest Spätburgunder (Pinot Noir) producers, based in Schlatt in the Markgräflerland sub-region of Baden. A VDP member, Wassmer produces GG (Grosses Gewächs, the VDP's top dry wine designation) and Erste Lage Spätburgunder from named vineyard sites. His wines are known for combining Burgundian elegance with Baden richness — an increasingly internationally recognised benchmark for German Pinot Noir.
