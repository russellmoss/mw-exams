# Nebbiolo delle Langhe, Castello di Verduno, 2013. Piemonte, Italy

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-light ruby with an orange-brick rim developing even at 2 years of age — Nebbiolo's characteristic rapid colour evolution. Nose shows classic Nebbiolo: dried cherry, rose petal, tar, tobacco, anise, autumn leaves. The palate is medium-bodied (lighter than Barolo), with firm, drying tannins and elevated acidity — Nebbiolo's signature tannic grip even at the Langhe DOC level. The finish is long and bitter-edged.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Nebbiolo
- Vineyard: Verduno commune, Barolo DOCG zone; Langhe DOC designation means these are young vines, sub-appellation parcels, or declassified grapes not meeting Barolo requirements

## Vintage character (2013 Piemonte)
2013 was a cool, late-harvest vintage in Piemonte — widely considered excellent for Barolo and Barbaresco, producing wines of classic structure, great freshness, and longevity potential. The wines show marked acidity, firm tannins, and aromatic purity. For a Langhe Nebbiolo 2013, this vintage produces a wine with excellent definition but at 2 years old (exam time) would still be quite youthful and tannic. ## Producer style
Castello di Verduno is a historic Piemontese estate based in Verduno, a commune within the Barolo DOCG. The estate is one of a handful of producers keeping alive the Pelaverga Piccolo grape of Verduno, alongside their Barolo, Barbaresco, and Langhe Nebbiolo. The estate is family-run (Burlotto/Sordo families historically; currently the Burlotto/Bel Colle connection). The winery is traditional-leaning, producing structured, age-worthy wines. The Langhe Nebbiolo serves as the entry-level wine in the range, made from younger vines or plots not classified into Barolo.
