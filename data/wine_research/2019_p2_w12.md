# Villányi Franc, Vylyan Winery, 2015. Villány, Hungary

## Quick tasting profile
Medium-deep ruby. Nose of ripe black cherry, dark plum, dried tobacco, graphite, dark chocolate and subtle spiced herbal notes — more southern and ripe than Loire Cabernet Franc. Palate is medium-to-full bodied with ripe, velvety tannins, moderate acidity and a smooth, persistent finish. Villány's warm, southerly climate produces a fuller, riper expression of Cabernet Franc than its Loire counterpart — a key identification challenge at blind tasting.

## Vintage character (2015 Hungary/Villány)
2015 was a warm year across Central Europe. In Villány — already Hungary's warmest and most southerly wine region — this produced ripe, concentrated reds. Cabernet Franc from this vintage shows greater fruit ripeness and softer tannins than average, reducing pyrazine character and enhancing dark fruit expression.

## Producer style
Vylyan Winery is one of Villány's leading estates, located in Kisharsány. The winery produces wines across multiple tiers (Classicus, Premium, Variation, Selection, Magnum) using both stainless steel and small barrel ageing (source: producer website). Villány is Hungary's benchmark region for Bordeaux-variety reds, particularly Cabernet Franc and Cabernet Sauvignon. Vylyan is a respected mid-to-premium producer with export markets. The "Villányi Franc" name explicitly designates the variety and regional identity.

## Why it's in this exam
Villányi Franc represents the ultimate curveball in a Paper 2 context: a wine that tastes Cabernet Franc but comes from an unexpected origin (Hungary). The warm climate character (riper fruit, softer tannins versus Loire) combined with the unusual geography tests whether a candidate can look beyond France for Cab Franc. The IMW uses Eastern European wines specifically to challenge assumptions.
