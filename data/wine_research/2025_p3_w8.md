# Pinot Gris Spiegel Grand Cru, Schlumberger, 2022. Alsace, France

## Quick tasting profile
Golden yellow with light green reflections. The nose is overripe and candied — yellow fruits, quince, mirabelle plum, honey, and light smokiness, with Golden apple and subtle acacia flower notes developing with air. On the palate, moderately ample onset, semi-liqueurish, with vivid yellow fruit, mirabelle plum, quince, and sophisticated smokiness. Good acidity provides structure ("sharp liveliness"). Balance is very nice with concentration mastered by the "cold year" and terroir. Finish of 8-9 caudalies. Off-dry to medium in sweetness impression.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Gris
- Vineyard: Grand Cru Spiegel, 18 hectares at 260-315m altitude, east-facing mid-slope across communes of Guebwiller and Bergholtz
- Soils: Sandy soils on a marly-sandstone base, limestone-rich
- Vinification: Fermented and aged in large oak tuns (traditional Alsatian foudres used for decades)
- ABV: 13.5% (actual measured at 12.26% per one source) / RS: Source needed / TA: 4.39 g/L
- Production: Yields 50% lower than Alsace average (~80,000 cases total estate production vs 160,000 allowed)

## Vintage character (2022 Alsace)
2022 was generally warm in Alsace but the Spiegel's elevation and orientation moderated ripening. Described as having concentration "mastered by the cold year and the terroir" — suggesting the site retained freshness despite regional warmth. Source needed for broader 2022 Alsace vintage report.

## Producer style
Domaines Schlumberger is the largest Grand Cru landowner in Alsace, operating 140 hectares of vines with half spread across 4 Grands Crus (Kitterlé, Kessler, Saering, Spiegel). Exclusively estate-grown and produced. Production is limited to 50% of legal yields for exceptional concentration. All wines are fermented and aged in large, traditional oak tuns. The house style balances richness with minerality — particularly suited to the marly-sandstone terroir of Spiegel, which is considered especially propitious for Gewurztraminer and Pinot Gris.
