# Monthelie 1er Cru Clos Les Champs Fulliot, Bouchard Pere et Fils, 2010

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with some garnet development at 5 years. Nose shows Cote de Beaune Pinot Noir character: red cherry, raspberry, dried herbs, earthy sous bois, and a hint of spice from oak. On the palate the wine is medium-bodied, with fine-grained tannins, medium-high acidity, and a focused, moderately long finish typical of a village-adjacent premier cru rather than a prestige Beaune site.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: Clos Les Champs Fulliot monopole; hillside site adjacent to Volnay, south-facing
- Soils: Calcareous clay with oolitic limestone, similar to Volnay terroir
- Vinification: Traditional Burgundian: destemming (partial or full), fermentation in open-top wooden cuves, pumping over and/or pigeage, gravity transfer
- Elevage: 12-16 months in French oak, predominantly older barrels (Bouchard house style at 1er Cru level uses low new oak percentage, typically 20-30%)
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / dry / Source needed
*(Bouchard uses a largely neutral-oak approach for village and premier cru reds to preserve fruit character.)*

## Vintage character (2010 Burgundy)
2010 is widely regarded as an exceptional red Burgundy vintage: a late harvest after a cool, dry summer produced wines with excellent natural acidity, deep colour, concentrated fruit, and significant aging potential. The wines are more structured and less opulently ripe than 2009, with tighter tannins and classical Pinot Noir aromatics. At 5 years old (when presented in 2015), they would still be showing primary fruit alongside emerging secondary complexity. ## Producer style
Bouchard Pere et Fils is one of Burgundy's largest and most historic negociants, founded 1731 in Beaune. Acquired by Champagne Henriot in 1995, the house underwent a significant quality upgrade. The estate owns approximately 130 hectares including prestigious grand and premier cru parcels across the Cote d'Or. Bouchard is simultaneously a domaine (estate wines) and negociant. Their style across reds is classically elegant: restrained new oak, terroir-driven, built for medium-to-long aging. Monthelie is a lesser-known Cote de Beaune appellation sandwiched between Volnay and Meursault; the 1er Cru Fulliot is arguably its best site.

## Why it's in this exam
Monthelie serves as the lesser-known Cote de Beaune benchmark in a Burgundy-focused question cluster, testing whether candidates can identify Pinot Noir from a second-tier appellation alongside more recognizable Gevrey. The 2010 vintage offers structural contrast to the riper 2009 wines elsewhere in the paper.
