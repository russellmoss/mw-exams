# 10 Year Tawny Port, Quinta do Noval, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Tawny amber with orange-brick rim — classic 10-year tawny oxidative colour; inviting nose of dried fig, walnut, toffee, orange peel and subtle vanilla; medium-sweet (sweeter than dry Sherry, lighter than Graham's Vintage Port), lighter-bodied than Vintage Port, with the nutty, oxidative character of prolonged barrel aging; high alcohol (19.5% ABV); clean, warming, medium-long finish. The tawny colour and nutty, dried-fruit-dominated nose are the key identifiers versus ruby/vintage Port.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: Traditional Douro blend (Touriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, etc.)
- Vinification: Standard Port production — grape spirit fortification mid-fermentation leaving residual sugar
- Elevage: Fractional blending (criadera-solera analogy) in small old oak pipes (550L); oxidative aging; "10 Year" = average age of the blended parcels (Source: Wikipedia — "age designations in 10-year increments representing average time spent in wood")
- ABV / RS / TA: 19.5% / sweet (approx 90-110 g/L RS) / medium acidity
- EU law: Only Port from Portugal can be labelled "port" (Source: Wikipedia)

## Vintage character (NV)
Non-vintage blended average. Quinta do Noval is based in the Cima Corgo sub-region of the Douro with their estate at Pinhão — one of Portugal's finest Port estates. Their 10-year Tawny is a benchmark example of aged Port from a top house.

## Producer style
Quinta do Noval, founded in the 18th century, is one of Portugal's most prestigious Port estates. Known for the legendary Nacional Vintage Port (from ungrafted pre-phylloxera vines). Acquired by AXA Millésimes (French insurance group) in 1993. Positioned at the premium quality tier of Port production, with distribution across fine-wine channels.
