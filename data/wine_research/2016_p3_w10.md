# Château Guiraud, 2001

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold to amber with apricot confit, marmalade, saffron, lanolin, vanilla, and toasted nuts. The palate is rich and unctuous, but the best 2001 Sauternes retains balancing acidity and noble bitterness, giving lift through a long, botrytis-laden finish. Blind, this is classic mature top-flight Sauternes: opulent, oaked, and aristocratic.

## Vintage character (2001 Sauternes)
2001 is widely regarded as one of the great modern Sauternes vintages, delivering exceptional botrytis concentration with enough acidity and definition to support long ageing. The best wines show both density and precision, and the year has near-iconic status in blind tasting. That makes Guiraud 2001 a strong exam reference for quality and maturity.

## Producer style
Château Guiraud is a premier cru classé estate known for serious, ageworthy Sauternes with a strong botrytis signature and evident oak support. The style combines richness and honeyed breadth with freshness and bitterness that prevent heaviness. Compared with Tokaji, it is broader, oakier, and more texturally enveloping.

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2016 Paper 3 Question 3 opposite Royal Tokaji 6 Puttonyos. It tests whether the candidate can contrast two noble-rot sweet wines from different countries, focusing on blend, botrytis expression, oak, alcohol, and structural balance.
