# Chardonnay, Iona, 2022. Elgin Highlands, South Africa.

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with green tinge. The nose is Burgundian in character: bright citrus (lemon cordial, grated lime), white stone fruit, oyster-shell minerality, subtle struck-flint reduction, hints of brioche and hazelnut. The palate is lean and shapely with concentrated marzipan, citrus, and almond notes, carried by bright fresh acidity and well-integrated oak. Medium-bodied with a long, seamless finish. Notable for achieving weight and complexity at just 12.5% ABV -- a hallmark of cool-climate Chardonnay.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Four blocks (45% Kloof, 26% Fynbos, 22% Kraal, 7% Block F4), planted 2003-2009, north-facing, ~420m elevation (source: topwinesa.com)
- Soils: Gravel, sandstone, silica quartz with clay base; post-glacial alluvial soils (source: wineanorak.com / topwinesa.com)
- Vinification: Hand-picked, whole-bunch pressed, 100% wild fermented in French oak (20% new, remainder 2nd-4th fill) (source: bushvines.co.uk)
- Elevage: 11 months on lees; 10-12% malolactic fermentation (source: topwinesa.com / bushvines.co.uk)
- ABV / RS / TA: 12.5-12.9% / 1.7 g/L / 6.9 g/L / pH 3.4 (source: topwinesa.com)

## Vintage character (2022 Elgin)
The 2022 South African vintage was "small but mighty" -- about 5.5% smaller than 2021 but of exceptional quality. A largely cool season with cold winters (including snow in Elgin), above-average rainfall, and moderate summer temperatures led to slower-than-usual ripening, delayed harvest by 10-14 days, and smaller berries with concentrated flavours. Extended ripening from January to April produced grapes with high natural acidity and phenolic ripeness -- ideal conditions for Elgin Chardonnay. Harvest dates for this wine: March 18, 23, and 24. (source: thedrinksbusiness.com / topwinesa.com)

## Producer style
Iona was founded in 1997 by Andrew Gunn on a former apple farm in the Elgin Highlands, named after the Scottish island linked to his ancestry. The estate sits at 420m elevation, just 3 km from the Atlantic -- making it one of South Africa's highest and coolest vineyard sites. Vineyards total ~40 hectares across two properties planted to Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Syrah, Mourvedre, and Viognier. Farming follows organic and biodynamic principles with minimal intervention in the cellar (indigenous yeast, limited sulphur, no commercial enzymes, minimal filtration). Geese provide natural vineyard pest management. The house style prioritises elegance, terroir expression, and moderate alcohol over power -- a Burgundian philosophy applied to South African cool climate. Iona's Sauvignon Blanc is their flagship, but the Chardonnay is increasingly recognised: the 2022 was the highest-rated Chardonnay in Platter's 2025 South African Wine Guide. Scores: Greg Sherwood MW 94 pts, Tim Atkin MW 93 pts. (source: wineanorak.com / bushvines.co.uk / topwinesa.com)

## Why it's in this exam
Used in 2025 Paper 1, Question 2: "Wines 3-6 are from the same single grape variety" (Chardonnay). The four-wine flight spans aged Chablis Premier Cru (Defaix 2010), Californian Monterey (Old Stage 2019), this Iona from Elgin (2022), and Margaret River Moss Wood (2023). Iona serves as the cool-climate New World representative with a distinctly Burgundian, restrained profile -- at 12.5% ABV it stands in sharp contrast to the richer, warmer-climate Californian and Australian examples. Its low alcohol, bright acidity, and mineral character test the candidate's ability to distinguish cool-climate South African Chardonnay from Old World Burgundy and other New World expressions. The question asks candidates to comment on style, quality, maturity, and to identify origin as closely as possible.
