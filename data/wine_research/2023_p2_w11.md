# Pinot Noir, Humberto Canale, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby-garnet. Fresh strawberry, cherry and spice with subtle vanilla and candied-fruit notes. The palate is light-to-medium-bodied, supple and smooth, with gentle tannin and enough freshness to keep the style lifted rather than sweetly confected.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Pinot Noir
- Vineyard: General Roca, Alto Valle del Rio Negro, Patagonia
- Soils: Source needed
- Vinification: Fermented in concrete vats at 25-27C for about 20 days with periodic rack-and-return; malolactic completed
- Elevage: 15-20% of the volume aged in American and French oak casks for 7 months, then 6 months bottle ageing
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.7% on current estate tech sheet; exam lists 13.5% / 2.68 g/L / 4.72 g/L

## Vintage character (2021 Rio Negro)
Specific 2021 harvest commentary was not found, but the Alto Valle's cool, dry Patagonian conditions typically support Pinot with bright aromatics, moderate body and measured ripeness. The wine's delicate structure and red-fruited profile are consistent with that regional frame.

## Producer style
Bodega Humberto Canale is one of Patagonia's historic producers and an important name for Río Negro Pinot Noir. The house style here leans elegant and accessible, using only partial oak to add polish without overwhelming the cooler-climate fruit.

## Why it's in this exam
In 2023 Paper 2 Question 3, wines 11 and 12 formed the Argentine pair. This bottle tested whether candidates could recognize Pinot Noir from a cool Patagonian origin and contrast its lighter frame with Catena Alta Malbec from Mendoza.
