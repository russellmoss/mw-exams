# Roero Arneis, Cornarea, 2016

## Quick tasting profile
Pale to medium lemon with floral, pear, fennel, and white peach notes. The palate is dry, gently textural, and softer in acidity than Sauvignon or Muscadet, with the slightly herbal, almond-edged finish that often gives Arneis away.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Arneis
- Vineyard: Roero
- Soils: Sandy Roero soils are central to the appellation's aromatic profile
- Vinification: Producer material indicates stainless-steel vinification to preserve freshness
- Elevage: Neutral vessel; no strong oak cue from the cited sources
- ABV / RS / TA: 13.5% / Source needed / Source needed

## Vintage character (2016 Piemonte)
2016 was a highly regarded northern Italian vintage, with freshness and structure alongside good ripeness. In aromatic whites from Piemonte, that typically means clean fruit definition and enough acidity to keep the mid-palate from turning broad.

## Producer style
Cornarea is closely associated with Arneis and is often treated as a reference-point specialist rather than a broad portfolio house. The style is varietally clear and regionally rooted, avoiding oak signatures that would blur the grape's delicate profile.

## Why it's in this exam
This wine functions as a classic Arneis benchmark in the 2017 paper 1 mixed-variety section. Its value is diagnostic: lower acidity than many peers, floral pear fruit, and a gently bitter finish that helps anchor Roero Arneis blind.
