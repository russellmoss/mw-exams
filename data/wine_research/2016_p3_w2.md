# Blanc de Blancs, Pol Roger, 2006

## Quick tasting profile
Pale gold with fine, persistent mousse, showing lemon curd, chalk, green apple, white flowers, and brioche. The palate is dry and tensile but fuller than a young non-vintage blanc de blancs, with bottle-age complexity beginning to layer over a classical, precise Champagne line. In blind tasting this reads as high-quality, traditionally made Chardonnay Champagne with less oxidative cellar signature than Selosse.

## Vintage character (2006 Champagne)
2006 gave ripe fruit and relatively generous texture in Champagne, especially compared with stricter, acid-driven years. In blanc de blancs that can mean a broad, creamy mid-palate over a still-chalky frame, often making the wines approachable earlier while still ageworthy. ## Producer style
Pol Roger is one of Champagne's most established grandes marques, known for polished, classical wines that balance precision with breadth. Compared with Selosse, the style is cleaner, less oxidative, and more house-blend oriented, with mousse and autolysis integrated into a refined rather than provocative whole. That makes it a strong reference point for "classical luxury Champagne" in MW tasting.
