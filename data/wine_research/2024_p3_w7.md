# Chateau Rieussec, 2007

## Quick tasting profile
Full gold to ambering at the rim, with developed aromas of orange marmalade, saffron, apricot compote, caramelised citrus, and botrytis spice. The palate should be dense and sweet, but also polished and long, with tertiary complexity already emerging. Compared with Carmes, this should feel deeper, more layered, and more complete.

## Vintage character (2007 Sauternes)
Decanter's 2007 Sauternes guide describes a year that produced successful sweet wines despite broader Bordeaux difficulties, with conditions favoring botrytis and giving wines of purity and definition. That is the key exam signal: 2007 Sauternes can show real pedigree and freshness, not just age and sweetness, and mature examples often combine richness with impressive precision.

## Producer style
Rieussec is a first growth of Sauternes in the 1855 classification and one of the flagship sweet-wine estates of Bordeaux. Under DBR Lafite ownership, it sits in the premium end of the market and is associated with ambitious, ageworthy wines rather than inexpensive dessert bottlings. The official estate profile stresses both historical prestige and technical rigor.

## Why it's in this exam
Wine 7 is the mature prestige anchor in 2024 Paper 3 Question 3. Against the dry R de Rieussec and the younger Carmes de Rieussec, it tests whether the candidate can identify not only Sauternes, but also maturity level and hierarchy within one producer's range.
