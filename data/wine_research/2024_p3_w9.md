# Beerenauslese Cuvee, Kracher, 2019

## Quick tasting profile
Bright gold with intense peach, apricot, orange blossom, pineapple, and honeyed botrytis notes. The palate should be lusciously sweet but not thick or oxidative, with acidity and aromatic lift carrying the finish. In blind tasting it reads as a modern, cleanly made noble-sweet wine rather than fortified Muscat or oxidative Vin Santo.

## Vintage character (2019 Burgenland)
Kracher's own trade sheet for the current Beerenauslese Cuvee frames the wine as a pure, fruit-driven botrytis style, and the 2019 bottling pages show that same sleek profile. The exam takeaway is stylistic: Neusiedlersee noble-sweet wine often shows bright tropical and stone-fruit expression with high sweetness but cleaner, less wood-shaped presentation than many Sauternes or Vin Santo examples.

## Producer style
Kracher is one of Austria's iconic sweet-wine producers, based in Illmitz near the Neusiedlersee. The estate is strongly associated with botrytis wines and with polished, export-facing modern sweet-wine presentation. This cuvee is positioned below the top single-vineyard or TBAs, but remains a serious benchmark in the Beerenauslese category.
