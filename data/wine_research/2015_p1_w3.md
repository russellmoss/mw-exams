# Bâtard-Montrachet Grand Cru, Domaine Paul Pernot et Ses Fils, 2012

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold; nose of white peach, ripe citrus, hazelnut, brioche and vanilla with a subtle creamy oak frame. Palate is full-bodied, unctuous texture, broad with glycerol weight, ripe stone fruit and nutty lees complexity. High but ripe acidity balanced by extract; long, mineral finish with toasty oak integration. Clearly oaked Côte de Beaune Grand Cru Chardonnay — structurally very different from the Chablis wines in this flight.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Chardonnay
- Vineyard: Bâtard-Montrachet spans 11.24 hectares across Puligny-Montrachet and Chassagne-Montrachet communes; Paul Pernot holds parcels primarily in the Puligny side
- Soils: Calcareous marl, limestone with brown clay on the lower slope — richer in limestone than Bienvenues-Bâtard to the north; base yield 40 hl/ha (source: Wikipedia Bâtard-Montrachet)
- Vinification: Whole-cluster pressing; fermentation in French oak barriques (typically 30–50% new oak at Grand Cru level for Paul Pernot); full malolactic fermentation; lees stirring (bâtonnage) (Source needed: confirmed production details for Pernot)
- Elevage: 12–18 months in French oak barriques; bottled unfined and unfiltered

## Vintage character (2012 Côte de Beaune whites)
2012 was a trying vintage in the Côte de Beaune; April frost and July hail reduced yields by up to 40% in some villages, including Puligny-Montrachet. The hail in particular affected vine canopies and required careful sorting. Despite difficulties, the warm September produced wines with good natural alcohol and balanced acidity. 2012 whites from the Côte de Beaune tend to be leaner and more angular than the flamboyant 2009s or the richer 2010s — they reward patience. Grand Cru sites such as Bâtard-Montrachet, with natural drainage and optimal exposition, fared relatively well. (Source needed: specific 2012 vintage meteorological data for Puligny-Montrachet.)

## Producer style
Domaine Paul Pernot et Ses Fils is a reference-quality family estate based in Puligny-Montrachet, holding exceptional parcels in some of Burgundy's most prized whites: Bienvenues-Bâtard-Montrachet, Bâtard-Montrachet, Puligny-Montrachet Premier Crus (Les Pucelles, Les Folatières, Champ Canet, Champ Gain, La Garenne), and Meursault Premier Cru Blagny. The style is traditional Côte de Beaune: barrel fermentation, partial new oak, full malolactic fermentation, bâtonnage. Wines are regarded for combining power and tension — not over-extracted or overoaked. Market positioning is prestige/trophy category for Grand Crus.
