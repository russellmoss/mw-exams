# Matusalem Oloroso Dulce Viejo, González Byass, NV

## Quick tasting profile
Deep mahogany-amber with green rim; viscous, legs coat the glass. Nose: complex and old — dried fig, walnut, toffee, dark caramel, Christmas cake spice, orange peel, dried apricot. Palate: concentrated, rich, sweet but balanced by oxidative bitterness and rancio character. Very long finish. The combination of 20.5% ABV, oxidative development (no flor), and intense sweetness points to aged sweet Oloroso from Jerez. At NV with a very old solera average, this is a VORS-equivalent (or close) wine.

## Technical specs
- Grape varieties: Palomino Fino for the base wine; sweetened with Pedro Ximénez or arrope (must concentrate) — exact blend ratio (Source needed)
- Vinification: originally fermented dry as Oloroso without flor development; fortified to 20.5% ABV; blended with sweetening component
- Aging: very old criadera y solera system; "Matusalem" name (after the Biblical Methuselah — lived to 969 years) refers to extreme age
- Solera average age: reportedly 30+ years; categorised as VOS/VORS equivalent (Source needed — exact VORS certification status)
- ABV: 20.5% / RS: ~200–350 g/L (very sweet; exact spec — Source needed)
- González Byass owns some of the world's largest sherry bodegas: 1972 Las Copas facility holds 80,000 butts (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gonzalez_Byass)

## Vintage character (NV)
As a solera wine there is no single vintage. The solera for Matusalem is among the oldest in the González Byass range. The combination of extreme oxidative age, sweetening, and fortification creates a very stable wine with profound complexity. "Matusalem" sits alongside "Noé" (PX) and "Apostoles" (Palo Cortado) as the flagship aged Sherry tier.

## Producer style
González Byass established 1835 by Manuel María González Angel; joined by English agent Robert Blake Byass. Now fully family-owned (4th and 5th generation González family as of Wikipedia). The largest Sherry producer in Jerez, best known for Tío Pepe Fino. The Matusalem range sits at the top of the premium aged Sherry portfolio. Company fame extends beyond wine — introduced polo to Spain, first grass tennis court, electricity and running water (source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gonzalez_Byass). La Concha bodega designed by Gustave Eiffel (1862).

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 3 includes Sherry. Matusalem as a sweet Oloroso at 20.5% ABV tests the candidate's ability to distinguish oxidative sweet fortified (sweet Oloroso) from biologically aged (fino/manzanilla), and from PX (which would be even darker and denser). The very high ABV and oxidative-sweet character should read as Jerez, sweet Oloroso, premium aged tier.
