# JMK Shiraz VP, Kalleske, 2012

## Quick tasting profile
Dense inky purple-black; virtually opaque. Nose: massively concentrated dark fruit — blackberry, dark plum, mulberry — with spirit warmth, dark chocolate, and characteristic Barossa Shiraz pepper/spice. Palate: full-bodied, rich and viscous, fortified sweetness, grippy but ripe tannins; 18.5% ABV shows as warmth on finish. At 3 years of age in 2015, still young and primary — could age 10–20+ years. This is a VP (Vintage Port-style) expression, not a tawny or muscat.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Shiraz from certified organic/biodynamic Barossa Valley estate vineyard
- Vinification: fermented to partial dryness then fortified with grape spirit to arrest fermentation; extraction maximised for VP style
- Elevage: limited oak aging before release (specific duration — Source needed)
- ABV: 18.5% / RS: ~90–130 g/L (typical VP-style; exact Kalleske spec — Source needed)
- Certification: Kalleske is certified organic and biodynamic (verified from winery pages)

## Vintage character (2012 Barossa Valley)
2012 was a good-to-excellent vintage in the Barossa following the challenging 2011 vintage. The growing season saw warm, dry conditions with harvest in optimal ripeness. Shiraz from 2012 showed concentrated fruit with good structural tannin and natural sugar levels well suited to fortification. The Kalleske estate in the Eden Valley/Barossa has old-vine material contributing intensity. (Source needed — specific 2012 Barossa vintage report)

## Producer style
Kalleske is a family-owned, seventh-generation estate in Greenock, Barossa Valley, certified organic and biodynamic. Known for single-vineyard Shiraz and Grenache at high quality. The JMK Shiraz VP is a tribute to family heritage and a nod to Australia's traditional fortified wine culture. The winery is small-production, premium-positioned, exported internationally. Style: bold, concentrated, terroir-driven. (Source: kalleske.com general pages — product details not accessible)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 3 includes fortified wines. A high-ABV Australian Shiraz VP is a curveball vs. Port — both are fortified red wines but the grape variety (Shiraz vs Touriga blend), origin (Barossa vs Douro), and stylistic differences (pepper/eucalyptus vs tobacco/iron) should differentiate. Tests whether candidates can move beyond "vintage Port" as the default answer for sweet fortified reds. The 18.5% ABV signals fortification; the Shiraz character points to Australia.
