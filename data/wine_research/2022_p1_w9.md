# Torrontes, Colome Estate, 2021

## Quick tasting profile
This should be intensely aromatic but dry: jasmine, orange blossom, white peach and grapey lift over a bright, medium-bodied palate. The key to recognizing good Salta Torrontes is that despite the perfume, the finish should stay dry, fresh and not overtly soapy.

## Vintage character (2021 Salta)
High-altitude Salta often preserves acidity and aromatic intensity even in sunny years, and 2021 gave Argentina many very good white wines. In Torrontes that means explosive floral expression supported by enough natural freshness to keep the wine dry and defined.

## Producer style
Bodega Colome is one of Argentina's iconic high-altitude estates and has long been associated with Salta's distinctive aromatic whites as well as serious reds. Its Torrontes tends to sit above entry-level examples in precision and balance, showing perfume without collapsing into simple grapey softness.
