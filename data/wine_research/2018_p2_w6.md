# Pinot Noir, DuMOL, 2015

## Quick tasting profile
Medium ruby with ripe black cherry, plum, baking spice, and subtle forest-floor notes. The palate is dry, supple, and fruit-rich, with relatively generous alcohol and oak sweetness by Burgundy standards but enough acidity to keep shape.

## Vintage character (2015 Russian River Valley)
2015 in Sonoma was low-yielding and early, often giving concentrated fruit and smaller crops. In Russian River Pinot Noir that usually means darker fruit, plusher mid-palate weight, and immediate appeal.

## Producer style
DuMOL is a benchmark modern California fine-wine producer, known for rich yet site-conscious Chardonnay and Pinot Noir. The house style tends to be generous, polished, and oak-literate rather than austere.

## Why it's in this exam
This started the 2018 paper 2 Pinot Noir trio. Its role is to show the ripe, glossy Californian end of the spectrum against Central Otago and Oregon expressions of the same grape.
