# Rioja Gran Reserva 890, La Rioja Alta, 2010

## Quick tasting profile
Medium garnet-red with iodine tinge on the rim — showing development. Classic tertiary Rioja nose: cherry jam, black plum compote, tobacco leaf, cinnamon, vanilla, pepper, chocolate powder, truffles, dried orange, forest floor, cocoa, cigar box, and cedar wood. Beautifully refined palate with vertical, velvety tannins and gentle freshness. Elegant, complex, and decadent. One of the finest vintages of this iconic wine. 97-98 points from Parker, Suckling, and Decanter (Sarah Jane Evans).

## Producer style
La Rioja Alta S.A. was founded in 1890 and is one of Rioja's most traditional and prestigious producers. The 890 is their ultimate expression — named for the founding year. Hallmark is extended American oak aging (they make their own barrels from American oak staves). The house style is classically oxidative Rioja: the long barrel aging develops tertiary complexity (leather, tobacco, spice) rather than primary fruit. They release wines only when deemed ready, resulting in extraordinarily long lead times. This 2010 was released around 2023 — 13 years after vintage. Epitomizes the "classical school" of Rioja winemaking.

## Why it's in this exam
Wines 4-7 are from "classic European origins." The 890 is perhaps the most classically European wine in this flight — a traditional Rioja Gran Reserva with 6 years in American oak and 7 years in bottle, released over a decade after vintage. The question asks for vintage identification and drinking window, which is particularly challenging given the extended aging regime (the wine appears much older than its calendar age). At 14% ABV and the only wine from 2010 in this flight, the tertiary development and American oak signature are key identifiers. The contrast with the younger, more primary-fruited Barolo, Brunello, and Bordeaux tests the candidate's ability to read maturity signals.
