# Riesling Ürziger Würzgarten Kabinett, Dr. Loosen, 2022

## Quick tasting profile
Pale straw with green reflexes. Intensely aromatic nose of crushed wild thyme, crunchy green apple, and peachy sweetness over slatey minerality. The palate is lemony, vivid, and light at just 8% ABV, with mid-palate CO2 adding levity. Mouth-watering finish with ripe lemon and spicy undertones. Off-dry with ~33-38 g/L RS beautifully balanced by tangy acidity.

## Technical specs
- Grape variety: 100% Riesling
- Vineyard: Ürziger Würzgarten, red volcanic slate and sandstone soils with high iron content
- Soils: Weathered red slatey volcanic rock, distinctive along the Mosel
- Vinification: Selection from parcels of 10- to 100-year-old vines at 78° Oechsle; fermented in stainless-steel tank
- Élevage: Stainless steel
- ABV / RS / TA: 8.0% (some sources say 8.5%) / 33 g/L / Source needed

## Vintage character (2022 Mosel)
2022 was the driest year on record in the Mosel, with no precipitation between May and July. Harvest began earlier than ever (August 20). The vintage produced wines with crunchy acidity and yellow/green fruit, with terroir expression varying dramatically by producer. Dr. Loosen's Kabinett succeeded with playful fruit and vibrant acidity, though some 2022 Mosels showed textural issues with jagged acidity.

## Producer style
Dr. Loosen, led by Ernst Loosen since 1988, is one of Germany's benchmark Riesling estates. The philosophy is terroir over technology: crop yields nearly half of legal limits, minimal organic fertilizers, old-fashioned cellar practices. The estate holds parcels in the Mosel's finest sites including Wehlener Sonnenuhr, Erdener Prälat, and Ürziger Würzgarten. VDP member committed to origin-based classification.

## Why it's in this exam
Paired with wine 2 (Hans Wirsching GG from Franken) as a Riesling pair in Q1. The pairing contrasts a light, off-dry, low-alcohol Kabinett from the Mosel with a dry, full-bodied Grosses Gewächs from Franken — testing the candidate's ability to recognise Riesling across radically different German styles and regions.
