# Chardonnay McCrea Vineyard, Kistler Vineyards, 2013

## Quick tasting profile
Deep gold; concentrated and complex. Nose: stone fruit (white peach, nectarine), toasted hazelnut and creamy French oak, lemon curd, mineral tension. Full malo creates a rich, silky texture but at a higher quality threshold than Clos du Bois — the underlying fruit concentration and acid structure are markedly superior. Palate: full-bodied, rich, long, with remarkable fruit-oak integration and a persistent mineral finish. At 3 years of age in 2016, still primary but showing development.

## Vintage character (2013 Sonoma Mountain)
2013 was part of California's drought cycle — warm, dry year with early harvest. For a cool-elevated Sonoma Mountain site like McCrea, the reduced precipitation and warm temperatures concentrated sugars while the elevation maintained some acid. Resulting wines are full, ripe, but structured relative to valley floor California Chardonnay. At 3 years in 2016, this wine would be drinking well. ## Producer style
Kistler Vineyards established 1978; Steve Kistler (studied at Stanford) was the founding winemaker until retirement. Sold majority stake to Bill Price (owner of Durell Vineyard) in 2008; Jason Kesner became head winemaker by end of 2017. Multiple single-vineyard Chardonnays and Pinot Noirs. "Biannual release... one of the most sought after by wine enthusiasts" (Food & Wine Magazine per Wikipedia). Positioned at the top of Californian Chardonnay — Burgundy-inspired, single-vineyard, low production, allocated. Price tier: USD 60–80+. (Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kistler_Vineyards)

## Why it's in this exam
Paper 1 white wines. Kistler McCrea is placed as the premium California Chardonnay counterpart to Clos du Bois (w3 — commodity tier). Together they test whether candidates can distinguish quality tiers within California Chardonnay. Additionally, at 14.1% ABV with French oak and elevated site character, Kistler risks being misidentified as White Burgundy — an important discrimination test.
